# Sector: Mobile Ad Tech / Programmatic Advertising

> Ultima actualizacion: 2026-02-18
> Status: INFRAPONDERAR (regulatory headwinds + valuation extremes + platform dependency)

## Resumen Ejecutivo

Mobile ad tech encompasses companies that provide the infrastructure for mobile app advertising -- mediation platforms, demand-side platforms (DSPs), supply-side platforms (SSPs), and AI-driven optimization engines. The sector sits at the intersection of two megatrends: the shift of advertising budgets to mobile (now >70% of digital ad spend) and the application of AI/ML to ad targeting and optimization.

The sector is currently in a precarious position. On the positive side, mobile ad spending continues to grow at 14-15% CAGR and programmatic automation is driving efficiency gains. On the negative side, privacy regulation is tightening globally (Apple ATT, potential Google policy reversals, EU Digital Markets Act, US state privacy laws), creating structural headwinds for data-dependent business models. The industry's largest pure-play company, AppLovin, is under SEC investigation and facing multiple short-seller allegations regarding its data-collection practices.

My thesis on the sector is CAUTIOUS: the TAM is real and growing, but the economics favor the walled gardens (Google, Meta, Amazon) over independent ad tech. Independent players like AppLovin, Unity, and Digital Turbine must navigate platform dependency risk (Apple/Google control the ecosystem), privacy headwinds, and increasing competition from AI-native alternatives. Valuations in the sector are extreme (APP at 72.8x EV/EBIT) despite these structural risks, reflecting momentum and AI narrative rather than fundamentals.

## Estructura Competitiva

**Concentracion:** Oligopoly at the top (Google/Meta = 50%+ of digital ad market), fragmented below

**Top players by mobile ad market share:**
- Google: ~28% of global digital ads
- Meta: ~22% of global digital ads
- Amazon: ~10% and growing
- AppLovin: #1 in mobile gaming ad mediation (~80% mediation market), attempting e-commerce expansion
- Unity/IronSource: #2 in mobile gaming, struggling post-merger

- Platform approval (Apple/Google) required
- BUT: New entrants like CloudX threaten with LLM-based automation

## Ciclo y Sensibilidad

| Factor | Sensibilidad | Notas |
|--------|--------------|-------|
| Tipos de interes | Baja | Ad tech companies are asset-light, low debt |
| Recesion | Alta | Ad budgets are discretionary, cut in downturns. Mobile gaming ads more resilient than brand |
| Inflacion | Baja | Digital pricing largely inflation-neutral |
| Privacy regulation | MUY ALTA | Apple ATT, Google policy, GDPR, US state laws directly impact targeting capability |
| Platform policy (Apple/Google) | MUY ALTA | Both control mobile OS and app stores. Policy changes can eliminate business models overnight |
| AI disruption | Alta | LLM-based ad optimization (CloudX) could commoditize what AXON does |

**Mejor fase del ciclo:** Mid-cycle (ad spending grows with economy but gets cut in recession)
**Beta tipico:** 1.5-2.5x (high beta sector, especially pure-play names)
