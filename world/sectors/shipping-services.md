# Sector: Shipping Services (Shipbroking & Maritime Advisory)

> Ultima actualizacion: 2026-02-19
> Status: NEUTRAL (selective quality only)

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The global shipbroking industry intermediates the chartering, sale & purchase, and newbuilding of vessels worldwide. Brokers earn commissions (typically 1-2% of transaction value) on shipping contracts without taking principal risk, making it asset-light with high margins. The industry is cyclical because commission revenue tracks freight rates, vessel values, and S&P transaction volumes, which correlate with global trade. However, top brokers with diversified segment coverage and recurring data/research businesses demonstrate meaningful through-cycle resilience.

The sector is fragmented but consolidating. Clarkson PLC dominates with ~25% market share and revenue 4.5x the #2 player (Braemar). The brokerage model is relationship-intensive and data-driven, creating barriers for new entrants but limited protection from shipping cycle downturns. Digitalization is a modest long-term threat (platform disintermediation), but the complexity of global shipping transactions, regulatory requirements, and relationship nature of brokerage work protects incumbents for the medium term.

Valuation is reasonable for the leader. CKN.L trades at P/E ~16.8x, EV/EBIT ~8.6x with net cash. H1 2025 showed revenue decline (-4%) and profit decline (-23%), reflecting softer dry bulk and tanker markets. The forward order book ($231M) provides partial visibility. The sector is NOT cheap enough for speculative names -- only the dominant player (CKN.L) offers quality+value.

**Barreras de entrada:** Medium-High

- Proprietary data networks (CKN's SIN database, fleet registers)
- Regulatory knowledge (sanctions, compliance)
- Global office network required (24 countries, CKN)
