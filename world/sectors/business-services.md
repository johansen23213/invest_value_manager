## Resumen Ejecutivo

El sector Business Services es extremadamente heterogeneo, abarcando desde BPO/contact centers hasta information services, testing/inspection/certification (TIC), professional consulting, y specialty business services. El factor comun es que son servicios B2B o B2B2C que no producen bienes fisicos.

**Tesis principal ACTUALIZADA (2026-02-13)**: El sector esta TRIFURCADO:

1. **Information Services / Data Analytics** (WIDE moat, high ROIC, recurring) -- Verisk, RELX, Wolters Kluwer, Gartner, FactSet, S&P Global, Moody's. Estos son los verdaderos quality compounders del sector. Capital-light, datos propietarios, switching costs. Muchos estan 40-70% por debajo de 52w highs por el SaaSpocalypse selloff (AI miedo + multiple compression). AQUI esta la oportunidad.

2. **Testing/Inspection/Certification (TIC)** (medium moat, steady growth) -- Bureau Veritas, SGS, Intertek, Eurofins. Regulacion crea demanda estructural. Crecimiento organico mid-single-digit + M&A. Barreras altas por acreditaciones y reputacion. BVI.PA ya en quality universe (QS 69).

3. **BPO/Staffing/Value Traps** (low moat, AI disruption) -- Teleperformance, Concentrix, staffing agencies. Estas son las empresas "baratas" por P/E pero con riesgo estructural de AI disruption. Vendimos TEP.PA y FUTR.L por esta razon. HRB sigue en portfolio pero con IRS Direct File risk.

**Posiciones actuales**: EDEN.PA (employee benefits, Tier B), HRB (tax services, en portfolio pero no en quality universe).

**Cambio clave vs version anterior**: El screening revela que LOS MEJORES negocios del sector no son los "baratos" (TEP, HRB) sino los "caros" (VRSK, IT, WKL.AS) que ahora estan en selloff. Las empresas de datos e informacion tienen ROIC 20-50%, FCF margins 20-40%, y moats basados en datos propietarios que son practicamente irreplicables.

## Quality Analysis Summary

### Tier A Candidates (QS >= 75)

**1. VRSK (Verisk Analytics) -- QS 80 tool / 85 adj, Tier A -- R1 COMPLETE**
- Insurance data analytics near-monopoly. ISO statistical agent for US P&C insurance.
- ROIC 38.3% with +30.5pp spread over WACC. FCF margin 32%. GM 69%.
- Revenue CAGR +5.4% (insurance data is steady, not hyper-growth).
- At $179, it is 45% below 52wH of $323. P/E 27.5x.
- WIDE moat: proprietary actuarial data, switching costs, regulatory mandates.
- **FV $260, Entry $150. Added to quality universe. Earnings Feb 18.**
- AI is tailwind (data is input to AI models). Failed AccuLynx acquisition is net positive.
- CEO insider ownership only 0.3% -- weak alignment vs other Tier A.
- Risk: multiple compression continues, weather-dependent claims revenue volatility.

**2. ROL (Rollins Inc) -- QS 79, Tier A**
- Global pest control leader (#1 US). Route-based moat with recurring revenue (80%+ recurring).
- ROIC 23.4%, +14.8pp spread. FCF expanding. Revenue CAGR +11.8%.
- Insider ownership 38.3% (Rollins family). Low leverage 1.1x.
- At $59, only 11% off 52wH of $66. P/E 53.8x -- VERY expensive.
- Near-zero AI disruption risk. Defensive, recession-resistant.
- **Priority: MEDIUM. Outstanding quality but valuation is prohibitive at current levels. Monitor for pullback.**

**3. WKL.AS (Wolters Kluwer) -- QS 77, Tier A**
- Professional information services for legal, tax, healthcare, financial.
- ROIC 24.3%, +19.8pp spread. GM 72.5%. FCF 22.7%.
- Revenue CAGR +7.4%. EPS CAGR +17.6%.
- At EUR 62, it is 66% below 52wH of EUR 180. P/E 13x. Yield 4.0%.
- NOTE: The 66% decline from 52wH is extraordinary for a Tier A compounder. This warrants investigation -- could be a data issue or a legitimate selloff.
- WIDE moat: embedded in professional workflows, switching costs.
- **Priority: HIGH. Needs immediate investigation of why it is so far from highs.**

### Tier A (adjusted) - R1 COMPLETE

**4. IT (Gartner) -- QS 73 tool / 80 adj, Tier A -- R1 COMPLETE**
- #1 research and advisory firm for IT decision-makers. 6x larger than nearest competitor.
- ROIC 52.1% -- highest in the group. +43.4pp spread over WACC.
- Revenue CAGR +9.8%. EPS CAGR +20%. No dividend (all cash to buybacks, $2B in 2025).
- At $154, it is 70% below 52wH of $519. P/E 15.9x.
- QS adjusted +7 for market position (0->7/8 manual correction). Tier A at 80.
- Selloff driven by: (1) SaaSpocalypse AI fears, (2) DOGE US federal contract losses, (3) Weak 2026 guidance.
- Wallet retention 103% confirms clients NOT leaving. Research segment (82% rev) stable.
- Consulting segment (-12.8%) is genuinely impaired but only 9% of revenue.
- **FV $245, Entry $140-150, ADD $130. WATCHLIST -- needs R2 (devil's advocate) before commitment.**
- Key risks: per-seat pricing erosion from AI, GM declining, CEO net seller.

## Estructura Competitiva

### Information Services
**Concentracion:** Oligopolio per subsector (each niche has 1-3 dominant players)
**Barreras de entrada:** VERY HIGH
- Proprietary databases built over decades (ISO data, credit histories, legal precedents)
- Regulatory mandates (FICO scores, insurance statistical agents)
- Embedded in customer workflows (switching costs)
- Network effects (analyst consensus, benchmark indices)

### TIC
**Concentracion:** Top 3 (SGS, BVI, ITRK) hold ~23% global share
**Barreras de entrada:** HIGH
- Accreditations and lab infrastructure
- Reputation and trust in certification marks
- Regulatory requirements for third-party testing
- Global laboratory networks (thousands of locations)

**Narrativa dominante (info services):**
"AI will disintermediate research, advisory, and analytics. Per-seat pricing models are dead. SaaS multiples must compress to reflect lower TAM."

**Mi contra-tesis:**
1. Los datos propietarios de VRSK, MORN, FDS son INSUMOS para AI, no victimas de AI. Los modelos necesitan datos limpios y estructurados.
2. El selloff ha comprimido multiples de empresas con ROIC 20-50% a niveles de empresas ciclicas (P/E 13-16x). Esto no esta justificado para negocios con 90%+ recurring revenue.
3. La durabilidad de switching costs en workflows profesionales (legal, tax, insurance) es subestimada.
4. Gartner e IT a 70% de su 52wH con ROIC 52% es una anomalia que merece investigacion.
