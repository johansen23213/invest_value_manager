## Resumen Ejecutivo

The global live entertainment market is valued at ~$810B (2025), growing ~4% CAGR to ~$1.2T by 2035. Post-COVID "experience economy" has been a secular tailwind, with consumers prioritizing live events over goods. The ticketing sub-segment is dominated by Ticketmaster (Live Nation subsidiary) with ~80% US primary ticketing market share.

The sector faces unprecedented regulatory scrutiny. The DOJ filed antitrust suit against Live Nation/Ticketmaster in May 2024, with trial starting March 2, 2026. 40 state AGs pursue independently. The Google antitrust precedent (2024-25) resulted in behavioral remedies only, establishing a pattern that may apply here.

Key sector dynamics: (1) ticketing is a natural monopoly due to venue exclusivity, (2) Concerts are low-margin (2.8% AOI) loss leaders that feed ticketing fees, (3) premium/VIP represents growing share (40%+ of stadium ticket sales), (4) international expansion accelerating (OCESA/Mexico, Japan, APAC).

## Drivers y Riesgos

**Positivos:**
- Experience economy secular trend (post-COVID consumer preference shift)
- Premium/VIP pricing expansion (40%+ of stadium tickets)
- International expansion (OCESA, Japan, India opening up)
- Stadium pipeline (60% higher in 2025 vs 2024)
- Deferred revenue growth (+37% YoY — leading indicator)

**Negativos:**
- DOJ antitrust trial March 2, 2026 (existential for TM monopoly)
- 40 state AG coalition pursuing independently
- Consumer backlash against service fees (political risk)
- All-in pricing mandates reducing fee opacity
- Low structural margins (Concerts at 2.8% AOI)
