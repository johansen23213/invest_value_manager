# Sector: UK Adviser Investment Platforms

> Ultima actualizacion: 2026-02-20
> Status: SELECTIVO - Quality business models but revenue margin compression headwind. Proprietary tech platforms preferred.

## Resumen Ejecutivo

UK adviser investment platforms provide technology infrastructure for financial advisers to manage client portfolios. The sector benefits from structural tailwinds: ageing population needing advice, pension freedoms increasing SIPP usage, and digital transformation of wealth management. Revenue is driven by assets under administration (basis points on FUA), creating a natural leverage to rising markets.

The UK adviser platform market has approximately GBP 700-800bn in total FUA across major platforms. The market is oligopolistic with high barriers to entry (regulatory capital, FCA compliance, technology investment, adviser relationships). Key differentiator: proprietary technology vs. outsourced infrastructure (FNZ-based platforms). Proprietary tech platforms (Transact, Hargreaves Lansdown) have higher margins and more control.

Revenue margin compression is the dominant headwind: pricing pressure from competition and natural tiering (larger client portfolios pay lower bps). Most platforms have seen revenue margins compress from 25-30bps to 20-23bps over the past 5 years. This is structural but slow-moving. The key question: can volume growth (more FUA) outpace margin compression (fewer bps per FUA)?
