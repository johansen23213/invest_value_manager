## Resumen Ejecutivo

1. **REGIME CHANGE: From "soft landing" to "stagflation risk."** The US-Israel attack on Iran (Feb 28) and Hormuz closure represent the largest oil supply disruption in history (~20% global supply). WTI surged from $62 to $96+ in 12 days. Feb NFP showed -92K jobs (first negative print in months). This is a textbook 1970s-style stagflation setup: rising energy costs + weakening labor market + central banks unable to cut rates. Yardeni stagflation odds at 35%. Oxford Economics warns $140 oil for 2 months = global recession.

2. **Central banks PARALYZED.** Fed, ECB, and BoE all face an impossible dilemma: oil-driven inflation demands higher rates, but weakening growth demands cuts. Result: everyone holds. FOMC Mar 18 = hold (92%+). ECB hawkish pause with "probability of hike has increased" language. BoE hold (was 50/50 cut pre-war). This freezes the "rate cut catalyst" that was supporting equity valuations across multiple sectors.

3. **Duration is everything.** The economic impact is ENTIRELY dependent on how long Hormuz stays closed. Moderate scenario (reopens in weeks): GDP trimmed 0.3pp, inflation spike transitory, markets recover. Severe scenario (months-long closure): $140 oil, eurozone/UK/Japan in contraction, guaranteed global recession per energy analysts. Iran's new leader (Mojtaba Khamenei) vows to keep Hormuz closed. IEA 400M barrel release is stabilizing but insufficient if disruption persists beyond 120 days.

4. **Safe haven rotation underway.** Gold $5,073 (+18% in 3 months), DXY 99.7 (strengthening on safe-haven flows -- reversing the weak-dollar trend from Feb), defense stocks +5-6%. Risk assets selling: S&P -2.5%, Stoxx 600 worst week since October, tech/consumer discretionary underperforming. Flight from risk = headwind for our equity-heavy portfolio.

5. **Portfolio implications SEVERE.** Our 52.4% cash position, previously seen as "too high," is now our greatest asset. The oil shock has turned the rate-cut narrative upside down, eliminated near-term BoE cuts (negative for 5 UK positions), and created stagflation risk that hits consumer discretionary (LULU, DOM.L) and growth stocks hardest. Cash preservation is now alpha. Standing orders at deeply discounted levels may become ACTIONABLE if crisis deepens.

6. **Tariffs: secondary concern now.** Turnberry Deal stalled after EU Parliament postponed vote on Trump's 15% global tariff threat. But the war and oil shock dominate all trade considerations. Steel/aluminum and EU-US trade are on the back burner until Hormuz resolves.
