### Sector Analysis

**Winner Characteristics:** The best companies in this sector have formulation lock-in (once a flavor/fragrance is designed into a product, switching costs are enormous), high gross margins (>40%), and recurring revenue characteristics. The problem is the market knows this -- Givaudan and Novonesis trade at 28-48x P/E.

**Where value exists:**
- **Kerry Group (KYGA.L)** -- hitting NEW 52-week low on Feb 18 after FY2025 results showed revenue decline (FX impact) despite 3% volume growth and 7.5% EPS expansion. EBITDA margin expanding to ~18%. Insider buying on Feb 17 (756 shares at GBP 65.15). Market cap ~EUR 12B. This is the most actionable opportunity in the sector.
- **Fuchs SE (FPE3.DE)** -- Overlooked German specialty lubricants leader. Record EBIT 2024, guiding EUR 460M EBIT for 2025. ROIC ~16% is best-in-class for this sector. 23 consecutive dividend increases. P/E 17x reasonable for quality. Market cap EUR 5B.
- **Arkema (AKE.PA)** -- Deep cyclical trough. Forward P/E 13x, EBITDA EUR 1.25-1.30B. Specialty chemicals pivot away from commodity. Cheap but lower quality.

**Avoid:**
- Givaudan, Novonesis: premium permanently baked in
- Victrex: payout ratio 187% signals fundamental deterioration
- Treatt: micro-cap, -56% indicates structural issues

### Sector Analysis

**Winner Characteristics:** Asset-light models with network effects (freight forwarding), or niche dominance with pricing power (cold chain, contract logistics). The pure freight forwarders (DSV, K+N) have proven the asset-light model but trade at premium multiples.

**Where value exists:**
- **STEF (STF.PA)** -- This is the standout hidden gem. EUR 3.1B market cap cold chain logistics leader in France. Revenue EUR 5.1B (hit target a year early). P/E 16x, div yield 3.4%. Cold chain has structural tailwinds (food safety regulation, frozen food growth, last-mile cold delivery). Dominant position with 250 sites and 5,500 refrigerated vehicles, yet <15% market share = room to grow. This is a quality compounder hiding in plain sight.
- **Logista (LOG.MC)** -- Already in our quality universe (QS 64, scored). EUR 3.9B, P/E 14x, ROIC ~25% (!), div yield ~5%. The "boring" tobacco/pharma distribution monopoly in Southern Europe. Very high returns on capital.
- **DFDS (DFDS.CO)** -- Cyclically cheap at P/E ~10x, -40% from highs. Ferry + logistics operator (Denmark/UK/Baltics). Risks: Brexit trade friction, energy costs. But at this valuation, a lot of bad news is priced in.

**Avoid:**
- PostNL, bpost: structural mail decline, low ROIC, sell consensus
- DSV: quality but at 30x P/E post-Schenker acquisition, too expensive
- ID Logistics: 47x P/E, priced for perfection
