## 1. WILSON PROXY FIGHT

**Classification: MATERIAL -- APPROACHING CRITICAL (Mar 13 deadline in 9 days)**

### What happened Feb 27

Wilson published a formal shareholder update (PR Newswire) escalating demands significantly:

- Demands **more than three directors** be replaced (previously three nominees)
- Demands **immediate declassification** of staggered board (board offered only phased de-staggering over multiple years)
- Demands creation of **Brand Product Committee** -- rejected by board
- Characterizes board response as "weak and insufficient"
- Board's primary concrete proposal was a **non-disparagement agreement** -- Wilson calls this prioritizing "protecting their own reputations" over shareholder interests
- Filed definitive proxy statement + "GOLD" universal proxy card

### Wilson's three nominees

1. **Marc Maurer** -- Former Co-CEO of On Holding AG (nearly quadrupled revenue)
2. **Laura Gentile** -- Former ESPN Chief Marketing Officer, founded espnW
3. **Eric Hirshberg** -- Former CEO of Activision Publishing

### Board response

Lululemon stated they "engaged with Mr. Wilson in good faith over past months" including "numerous meetings" but disagreed with his characterization. They claim Wilson has been "unwilling to have a constructive dialogue toward a reasonable resolution."

### March 13 deadline -- status as of Mar 4

**NO RESOLUTION reached.** Despite engagement starting Feb 24 (after 70+ days of silence), fundamental disagreements remain on pace and scope of change. Wilson: "If the Board engages as shareholders expect, there is no reason to believe we cannot have this resolved before March 13."

### Kill Condition Assessment

KC#5 (Wilson gains board control and implements disruptive changes) remains **ELEVATED**. The proxy fight is escalating, not de-escalating. However, Wilson seeking board seats is different from "gaining control" -- his three nominees would not constitute a majority even if all elected.

### Thesis SELL trigger check

SELL trigger: "Wilson proxy deadline Mar 13 without resolution AND stock <$165"
- Resolution: NO (not resolved)
- Stock: ~$172 (ABOVE $165 threshold)
- **SELL trigger NOT activated** at current prices, but the proxy side is negative.

## 3. Q4 FY2025 EARNINGS (Mar 17)

**Classification: MATERIAL -- PRE-EARNINGS CONTEXT**

### What we know

**ICR Conference update (Jan 12):** Company guided Q4 revenue and EPS toward HIGH END of prior ranges:
- Revenue: high end of $3.500B - $3.585B
- Diluted EPS: high end of $4.66 - $4.76
- Full-year FY2025: GAAP EPS $12.92 - $13.02, revenue $10.96B - $11.05B

**No subsequent pre-announcement or guidance change** since Jan 12.

### Key metrics to watch (kill condition-relevant)

| Metric | Kill/SELL Trigger | Thesis Assumption | ICR Guide |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Global comps** | Negative global comps = SELL | US negative, intl positive | Not specified |
| **Gross margin** | GM <55% = SELL | 59.2% FY2024 | -580 bps guide (tariffs -410 bps) |
| **FY2026 guidance** | E[CAGR] <0% = SELL | Rev +2-4% | Not yet given |

### Gross margin risk -- SIGNIFICANT

Management guided Q4 for **-580 bps gross margin decline**, with tariffs accounting for -410 bps. This would imply Q4 GM of approximately **53-54%** (vs 59.2% in FY2024).

**IMPORTANT:** Our SELL trigger is GM <55% Q4. The -580 bps guidance from management implies GM could come in BELOW our 55% trigger. However, the thesis notes (from S109 update) already corrected that 59.2% was the FY2024 figure -- Q3 FY2025 GM was 55.6%. So Q4 at ~53-54% after a 580 bps decline from an FY2024 Q4 base would need to be checked against the actual Q4 FY2024 base.

**Q4 FY2024 GM was approximately 59.4%.** A -580 bps decline would imply Q4 FY2025 GM of ~53.6%, which is BELOW our 55% SELL trigger.

This is a SIGNIFICANT finding: **if management's own -580 bps guidance materializes, GM will breach our 55% kill condition.** The question is whether this tariff-driven compression is structural or temporary.

### FY2026 guidance risk

Management has flagged $320M tariff impact for FY2026. CFO outlined mitigation via product creation, activation, and enterprise efficiency. If FY2026 guide implies revenue flat or declining AND margin compression continues, E[CAGR] will be deeply negative.

### Consensus estimates (for reference, not anchor)

- Average analyst price target: ~$213-$221 (22 analysts, "Hold" consensus)
- Q4 EPS consensus: ~$4.70 (in line with guided high end)
