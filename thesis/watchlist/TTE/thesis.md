# TotalEnergies SE (TTE)

> Updated: 2026-01-31
> Status: **CONDITIONAL BUY**
> Confidence: **MODERATE-HIGH**

## TL;DR

TotalEnergies is a diversified energy major (oil, gas, LNG, power, renewables) with the best ROACE among European majors (14.8%), production growth of +3%/yr, and a 6% dividend yield at ~10x P/E. Independent DCF valuation yields a conservative fair value of EUR 78-82, implying a margin of safety of 22-26% at EUR 61. This is on the borderline of our 25% threshold. The stock passes on Morningstar/Alpha Spread estimates (26.4% MoS) and on our DCF moderate case, but is tight on the conservative base case. **Recommendation: BUY at max EUR 1,130 position** -- the combination of 6% yield, 3% production growth, buybacks, and geopolitical hedge provides sufficient total return even if capital appreciation is modest. Entry below EUR 59 would provide clearer margin.

## The Business

### Model

TotalEnergies operates across the full energy value chain:

1. **Exploration & Production**: Oil and gas production >2.55 Mboe/d (Q1 2025), growing +3%/yr through 2030 from projects in US offshore, Brazil, Iraq, Uganda, Qatar LNG
2. **Integrated LNG**: Major global LNG player, benefiting from Qatar NFE and Malaysia Jerun projects
3. **Integrated Power**: 46 GW gross capacity target by 2030, ROACE 10%, cash flow $2.6B in 2024 (+19% YoY)
4. **Downstream**: Refining, petrochemicals, marketing -- $3.5B adjusted net operating income in 2024

**What differentiates TTE from Shell/BP**: TotalEnergies is the only European major actively growing production AND investing meaningfully in low-carbon ($4.5B/yr, ~26% of capex). Shell is more disciplined on returns but flat production. BP is cutting back on everything. TTE is the "growth major."

### Capital Allocation

**CEO Patrick Pouyanne** -- long tenure, pragmatic operator:
- Shareholder returns >40% of CFFO (target 50% at $70/bbl)
- 2024: $8B buybacks + EUR 3.22/share dividend (+7% YoY)
- 2025: Dividend raised to EUR 0.85/quarter (+7.6%), $2B/quarter buybacks
- 2026 guidance: Buybacks $0.75-1.5B/quarter at $60-70 Brent
- Capex REDUCED to ~$16B in 2026 (from $17-18B), $7.5B savings program 2026-2030
- Share count declining ~3%/yr from buybacks (2.297B shares mid-2025, down 5% from 2023)
