## Executive Summary

Domino's Pizza Group is the exclusive master franchisee for Domino's in the UK and Ireland, operating a 100% franchised, asset-light model with ~1,381 stores and 54% UK pizza delivery market share (3.5x nearest competitor). The stock has fallen ~48% from its 52-week high due to a CEO departure (Nov 2025), a profit warning (Aug 2025, FY25 EBITDA guided to GBP 130-140M vs GBP 143M FY24), and weak UK consumer sentiment.

At 182.5p, the stock trades at 9.1x trailing P/E and ~12% FCF yield -- levels not seen in over a decade for a franchise business of this quality. The bear case (CEO vacuum, consumer weakness, cost pressures) is substantially priced in. The asset-light model generates GBP 85M+ FCF annually with minimal capex (GBP 13-19M), supporting a 6.1% dividend yield and aggressive buybacks (GBP 500M returned since 2021).

**Verdict: BUY at 182.5p. Conservative fair value 260p, base case 335p. Margin of safety 42-84%. Exceeds Tier B 25% MoS requirement.**

## Business Model & Competitive Position

### Business Model
- **Master Franchisee** exclusivo para UK e Irlanda de la marca Domino's
- **100% franquiciado**: no opera tiendas directamente, ultra asset-light
- Revenue streams: royalties, supply chain (masa, ingredientes, equipamiento), alquiler locales
- CapEx mantenimiento <1% revenue, working capital negativo
- ~1,370-1,381 tiendas operativas, objetivo 1,600 para 2028, 2,000 para 2033
- 9.5M clientes en app, nueva plataforma eCommerce
- 12.1% stake en Domino's Pizza Poland (opcion de crecimiento)
- Nuevo Profitability and Growth Framework (PGF) 2025-2030, aprobado unanimemente por franquiciados

### Revenue Trends (GBP M)
| Year | Revenue | Growth |
|------|---------|--------|
| FY2021 | 560.8 | - |
| FY2022 | 600.3 | +7.0% |
| FY2023 | 679.8 | +13.2% |
| FY2024 | 664.5 | -2.3% |
| TTM | 669.2 | ~+0.7% |

Revenue CAGR (2021-2024): ~5.8%. FY2024 decline reflects UK consumer weakness, not structural deterioration.

### Profitability
- EBITDA FY24: GBP 143.4M (margin ~21.6%)
- FY25 guidance (lowered Aug 2025): GBP 130-140M EBITDA
- Underlying PBT FY24: GBP 107.3M (vs 98.9M FY23, +8.5%)
- Operating margin: ~15.5%, Net margin: ~11.6%
- EPS growth FY24: +13.3% (including buyback benefit)

### Free Cash Flow
- FCF TTM: GBP 87.3M (OCF 100.4M - CapEx 13.1M)
- FCF conversion: excellent (87% of OCF, asset-light model)
- 5-year FCF: consistently GBP 65-100M range
- FCF/share: 22.9p (vs 11.2p dividend = 49% payout, conservative)

### Balance Sheet
- Net Debt: GBP 265.5M (FY24), guided GBP 280-300M end FY25
- Net Debt/EBITDA: 1.93x (within 1.5-2.5x target, down from 2.16x at H1 2024)
- **Negative equity**: result of cumulative aggressive buybacks, NOT losses
- This is common in asset-light franchise models (McDonald's, DPZ US have same structure)
- Does NOT indicate insolvency but limits balance sheet flexibility in severe stress
- Use Net Debt/EBITDA (1.93x) as primary leverage metric, not D/E

### Capital Allocation
- Dividend: GBP 11.2p/share (6.1% yield), growing 3-5% annually
- Buybacks: GBP 20M programme completed 2025 at avg 203p (above current price)
- Since 2021: GBP 500M+ returned to shareholders
- Policy: return excess FCF via dividend + buyback

## Moat Assessment: NARROW (trending toward Wide)

### Moat Sources

1. **Master Franchise Exclusivity (Strong):** Legal monopoly on Domino's brand in UK & Ireland. Long-term agreement, extremely costly to break. No competitor can open a Domino's.

2. **Scale Advantage (Strong):** 54% market share = 3.5x nearest competitor. 1,381 stores while competitors close locations. Avg store sales GBP 1.2M vs ~600K for competitors. EBITDA margin 13% vs lower for peers. Payback period 4 years.

3. **Captive Supply Chain (Strong):** ~67% of revenue from supply chain (franchisees MUST buy all ingredients/equipment from DPG). Recurring, locked-in, high-margin revenue stream.

4. **Brand Recognition (Moderate):** #1 recognized pizza delivery brand in UK. 13.5M active customers. But brand alone is not a wide moat in food.

5. **Technology/Digital (Moderate):** 90%+ digital ordering. 9.5M app customers. Proprietary platform. But replicable over time.

### Why Narrow, Not Wide
- Depends on single brand and single geography (UK/Ireland)
- DPZ parent relationship: royalty terms could change
- UK pizza delivery market growth is low (~0.5% CAGR)
- Aggregator platforms provide alternatives for consumers
- Limited pricing power (competes on value/deals)

### Moat Durability: 8-10 years minimum
Master franchise + supply chain lock-in + scale are durable. Competitors are contracting.

## Bear Case (Why NOT to Buy)

1. **CEO vacuum could last 6-12 months.** Strategic direction unclear.
2. **FY25 is a down year.** EBITDA guided 130-140M (vs 143M FY24).
3. **Negative equity limits flexibility.** GBP 551M debt, no equity buffer.
4. **UK consumer outlook poor.** Energy bills, mortgages, cost of living.
5. **Aggregator platforms structural threat.** Uber Eats/Deliveroo give consumers alternatives.
6. **DPZ parent royalty risk.** Permanent cost, could change terms.
7. **Value trap potential.** Market may be right that growth is over.

### Counter-arguments:
- CEO departures less impactful in franchise businesses (model runs itself)
- FCF is real, recurring, not growth-dependent
- Competitors CLOSING stores while DOM opens them
- 6.1% dividend + buybacks = ~16% total shareholder return yield -- paid to wait
- Historical P/E 18-22x means modest re-rating to 14x = 285p (+56%)
- Q3 2025: delivery orders -3.4% but collection orders growing, overall revenue resilient
