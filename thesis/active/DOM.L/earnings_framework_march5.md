## Special Factors for This Report

### 1. New CEO Dominic Paul (starts Apr 6 — AFTER results)
- FY25 results reflect PRIOR management execution
- Board will provide FY26 guidance pre-CEO (likely conservative)
- Watch for: any strategic commentary from Paul pre-start, or if board defers guidance to new CEO
- Risk: new CEO may announce strategic review/investment phase that temporarily depresses near-term earnings outlook
- Positive: Costa Coffee track record (strong consumer/franchise background, led GBP 3.9B sale to Coca-Cola 2019)

### 2. New CFO Andy Andrea (starts Mar 16 — 11 days after results)
- Current CFO presents FY25 results (last act)
- Both C-suite positions changing simultaneously — creates both opportunity (clean slate) and risk (disruption)
- Watch: any change in capital allocation philosophy (buyback, dividend, reinvestment)

### 3. NLW/NIC Impact (GBP ~3M direct)
- National Living Wage: GBP 12.21 (Apr 2025) → GBP 12.71 (Apr 2026, +4.1%)
- Employer NIC: 13.8% → 15% (Apr 2025)
- Direct DPG cost: ~GBP 3M/yr (<2.5% of EBITDA — manageable)
- BUT: indirect impact through franchisee margin squeeze is bigger
- Watch: commentary on franchisee profitability and willingness to open new stores

### 4. Store Opening Pipeline
- FY24: 54 new stores (exceptional)
- FY25E: mid-twenties (~25, -54% YoY) — the BIGGEST CONCERN
- Q3 update: 18 YTD, targeting mid-twenties
- Watch: FY26 store opening guidance (if <30 → growth engine stalling)
- Bear case: NLW makes franchisee economics marginal, store growth permanently lower

### 5. FCF Conversion
- FV v3.0 was cut 18% largely because FCF conversion fell from 61% to est. 43%
- If actual FCF > GBP 65M → v2.0 may have been partially right
- If actual FCF < GBP 50M → further FV cut needed
- Watch: working capital movement, lease payments, capex

### 6. Market Share Trend
- 53.7% (+560bp YoY at H1 2025) — very strong
- If FY25 full-year shows continued gains → moat thesis strengthened
- If share gains decelerate → competitive pressure may be returning
