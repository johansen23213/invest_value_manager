## 3. Top 3 Critical Risks

### 1. Deal Builder Dealer Revolt + April 2026 Pricing Event (CRITICAL)

- **Category:** Fundamental
- **Description:** AutoTrader mandated adoption of Deal Builder in November 2025, triggering the most serious dealer backlash in the company's history. 165 dealers cancelled contracts (IMDA count; AutoTrader says "fewer than 100"). 70% of surveyed dealers reduced/downgraded packages. The CEO was forced to publicly apologize on the Car Dealer Podcast. Now the critical test: the April 2026 pricing event (5.5% increase, effective April 1) arrives into this hostile environment. Jefferies specifically identifies this as the "tent pole" risk -- Deal Builder was meant to be the product justifying the price increase.
- **Evidence:**
  - IMDA survey: 165 cancellations, 700+ respondents, 70% downgraded
  - AutoTrader's own count: "fewer than 100" cancellations (still the most in company history)
  - Jefferies downgrade from Buy to Hold, target slashed from 895p to 650p
  - Berenberg slashed target from 830p to 665p, forecasting ARPR growth of just 5.2% for FY27 (down from 7.5% prior)
  - CEO Nathan Coe admitted AutoTrader "got things wrong"
  - Only 4,000 of 14,020 dealers have signed up for Deal Builder (29% adoption)
  - Dealers complaining about forced account creation reducing leads, "well under 50% conversion" rate
- **Probability:** Alta -- The April pricing event is 7 weeks away. Dealer sentiment is at a nadir.
- **Impact if materializes:** If April pricing event causes further cancellations or significant downgrades:
  - ARPR growth could fall to 2-3% (vs 5% current), reducing revenue growth to 2-3%
  - At 2-3% revenue growth, P/E of 14-15x is fully valued, not undervalued
  - Stock could retest 400-450p (further 10-18% downside from here)
  - Estimated impact: -10% to -20% from current price
- **Mitigante:** AutoTrader introduced "Reservation Request" option; launched Customer Advisory Groups; CEO engaging directly. The 5.5% price increase is LOWER than last year's ~8%.
- **Kill condition?** YES -- If ARPR growth turns negative in FY2027 reporting.

### 2. CMA/Competition Law Risk (Rightmove Precedent) (HIGH)

- **Category:** Regulatory/Legal
- **Description:** Dealers are directing formal CMA complaints against AutoTrader with "specialist lawyers" who believe they have "an excellent case." The Rightmove precedent is directly relevant: Rightmove (70% profit margin, monopoly property portal) faces a GBP 1 billion class action funded by Innsworth, led by former CMA panel member Jeremy Newman, alleging abuse of dominant market position via excessive subscription fees. AutoTrader's business model is structurally IDENTICAL: dominant UK classifieds platform (75% share), 62% operating margin, annual price increases above inflation, dealers have no realistic alternative. If the Rightmove case succeeds, AutoTrader faces identical litigation risk.
- **Evidence:**
  - Rightmove class action filed November 2025; GBP 1B damages sought; opt-out basis
  - Led by former CMA panel member (credible, not frivolous)
  - IMDA dealers openly discussing CMA complaint
  - AutoTrader and Rightmove have identical market structures: monopoly platform, above-inflation pricing, captive customer base
  - Rightmove's 70% margin vs AutoTrader's 62% margin -- similar "excessive pricing" argument applies
- **Probability:** Media -- No formal CMA investigation yet. Class actions take years. But the Rightmove case creates a blueprint.
- **Impact if materializes:**
  - CMA formal investigation would cap pricing power immediately (market would price in 0-2% ARPR growth)
  - Class action damages of GBP 500M-1B would be material vs GBP 4.1B market cap
  - Even without damages, the THREAT constrains future pricing
  - Stock impact: -25% to -40% if formal investigation announced
- **Mitigante:** CMA has limited resources; may not prioritize; Rightmove case outcome years away. AutoTrader could proactively moderate price increases to avoid scrutiny.
- **Kill condition?** YES -- If CMA opens formal investigation into AutoTrader's pricing practices.

## 4. Deal Builder Deep Dive

### The Thesis Says: "Noise, not signal" (165 out of 13,500 = 1.2%)

### My Assessment: THE SIGNAL IS STRONGER THAN THE THESIS ADMITS

**What actually happened:**
1. AutoTrader mandated Deal Builder in November 2025
2. Deal Builder lets consumers reserve cars for GBP 99, marks car as "reserved" (off sale)
3. Dealers reported: forced account creation reduced leads dramatically; "well under 50%" conversion from reservations; lost control of sales process
4. IMDA organized mass cancellation campaign; surveyed 700+ dealers
5. 165 dealers cancelled (IMDA) vs "fewer than 100" (AutoTrader's count) -- significant discrepancy
6. 70% of surveyed dealers reduced or downgraded their packages
7. CEO Nathan Coe appeared on Car Dealer Podcast, admitted they "got things wrong", apologized
8. AutoTrader added "Reservation Request" option (concession), launched Customer Advisory Groups
9. Only 4,000 of 14,020 dealers (29%) have signed up for Deal Builder

**Why the thesis underestimates this:**

1. **The CANCELLATION count is misleading.** The REAL damage is in DOWNGRADES. If 70% of the 700 IMDA respondents downgraded packages, that is ~490 dealers paying less per month. At GBP 2,994/month average ARPR, even a 20% downgrade across 490 dealers = GBP 3.5M annual revenue impact. If the sentiment is representative of the broader 14,000 dealer base (not just IMDA respondents), the impact scales dramatically.

2. **The April 2026 pricing event is THE TEST.** AutoTrader announced a 5.5% price increase effective April 1. This is LOWER than last year's ~8% (itself an admission of pricing pressure). If dealer pushback to this more modest increase is still fierce, the pricing power narrative breaks.

3. **Low Deal Builder adoption is concerning.** Only 29% of dealers have signed up. If this is the "tent pole" product for the pricing event, and 71% of dealers haven't adopted it, the product value proposition is not landing.

4. **Historical precedent (Rightmove) is NOT reassuring.** The thesis says "the same pattern happens with Rightmove." But Rightmove now faces a GBP 1B class action. The pattern of monopoly platform extracting above-inflation fees from captive customers is EXACTLY what creates legal and regulatory liability. The "they can't leave" argument works until it becomes a CMA case.

5. **Facebook Marketplace is no longer zero-threat.** Marketplace Pro launched AI-powered listing software for car dealers on Facebook Marketplace in February 2026. While Facebook lacks AutoTrader's trust and finance integration, it is FREE. Disgruntled dealers seeking ANY alternative now have one. This did not exist 12 months ago.

**My probability assessment:**
- Probability that April 2026 event causes NO additional friction: 20%
- Probability of moderate friction (some more cancellations/downgrades, ARPR growth 3-4%): 50%
- Probability of severe friction (ARPR growth <2%, dealer count declines): 30%

## META-REFLECTION

### Dudas/Incertidumbres
- The true dealer sentiment is hard to gauge from trade press alone. The IMDA represents independent dealers (smaller operators), not the large dealer groups (Lookers, Pendragon, Sytner) that contribute most of AutoTrader's revenue. If large dealer groups are satisfied, the IMDA backlash may indeed be "noise." But I could not find evidence that large groups are supportive.
- The CMA complaint risk is genuinely uncertain. Dealers SAY they are filing complaints, but no formal investigation exists. The Rightmove class action is a leading indicator but not a guarantee.
- I was unable to access AutoTrader's investor presentation PDF directly to verify all financial claims. The H1 FY2026 data comes from secondary sources.

### Risks that Could Be Underestimated
- The ARPR deceleration risk may be MORE severe than I scored. If the UK economy weakens further (GDP growth 1.2% in 2026), dealers under margin pressure could cancel at higher rates.
- The Facebook Marketplace threat is emerging, not current. But the trajectory matters: if even 5% of classified minutes shift to Facebook within 2 years, AutoTrader's pricing power narrative weakens significantly.
- The Rightmove class action is the most important risk to monitor. If it SUCCEEDS, AutoTrader faces identical exposure. Even the filing of a similar suit against AutoTrader would be material.

### Discrepancies with Thesis
- **QS 79 vs my 68-72**: The thesis overrates Quality Score by approximately one full tier. Financial Quality is inflated by goodwill-distorted ROIC. Moat Evidence is overrated because it ignores the Deal Builder backlash and emerging competitive threats.
- **FV 688p vs my 580p**: The thesis overestimates Fair Value by ~16%. The growth assumption of 8% is too high vs 5-7% guidance. The peer comparison ignores that Rightmove's premium is being challenged by a class action.
- **MoS 29% vs my 16%**: Significantly less margin of safety than claimed. For a Tier B company, 16% is barely adequate.
- **"Noise not signal"**: The thesis dismisses Deal Builder as noise. I classify it as the PRIMARY risk, especially given the correlated cluster of 5 HIGH+ risks emanating from dealer relationship deterioration.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema
- Create a "platform monopoly risk" assessment template for any business that derives pricing power from captive customers (AUTO.L, RMV.L, MONY.L). The pattern is consistent: annual price increases -> customer resentment -> organized pushback -> regulatory risk.
- Monitor Rightmove class action outcome as a LEADING INDICATOR for AutoTrader risk.
- For UK platform businesses, always check trade press (Car Dealer Magazine, AM Online, Motor Trader) for customer sentiment -- this is not captured in financial data.
