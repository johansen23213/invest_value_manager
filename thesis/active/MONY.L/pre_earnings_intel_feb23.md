### 1.5 UK Regulatory Framework for AI Insurance Advice

| Finding | Detail | Source |
|---------|--------|--------|
| FCA approach | Technology-neutral, principles-based, outcomes-focused | FCA (Tier 1) |
| No new AI-specific regulations planned | Relies on existing Consumer Duty + SM&CR + operational resilience | FCA (Tier 1) |
| Consumer Duty requirements | Firms must provide "meaningful information" about how AI impacts outcomes | FCA (Tier 1) |
| AI Live Testing program | FCA launched initiative (Sep 2025) for firms to test AI with regulator support | FCA (Tier 1) |
| Bias monitoring | FCA explicitly watching for AI pricing bias (race, ethnicity) in insurance | FCA (Tier 1) |
| Guidance expected 2026 | On audit trails and human-in-the-loop protocols | FCA (Tier 1) |
| GDPR + UK DPA | Creates friction for AI agents handling sensitive financial data | UK law (Tier 1) |

**Classification: MODERATE IMPACT (net POSITIVE for MONY)**
**Assessment:** The FCA is NOT blocking AI in insurance, but it is NOT clearing a fast lane either. The principles-based approach means AI insurers must comply with Consumer Duty (transparency, fair outcomes) -- which is exactly what Berenberg argues creates a "moat for PCWs." However, the AI Live Testing program shows the FCA is actively facilitating AI adoption, not just tolerating it. This is a nuanced signal: the regulatory barrier protects MONY for now (2-3 years) but is designed to ENABLE AI adoption, not prevent it permanently.
