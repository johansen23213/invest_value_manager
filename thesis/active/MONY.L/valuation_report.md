## CHALLENGE OF THESIS ASSUMPTIONS

Before computing fair value, I address each question raised in the adversarial mandate.

### 1. Is 5-7% revenue growth realistic?

**Verdict: Thesis growth assumption is OPTIMISTIC. My estimate: 2-4% base case.**

Evidence:
- FY2024 revenue growth was +1.6% (down from +11.3% in FY2023)
- H1 2025 revenue growth was +1% (further deceleration)
- Insurance segment (52% of revenue) was -2% in H1 2025
- UK price comparison market is mature; TAM growth is 3% at best
- MONY is #2 with stable (not growing) market share
- 4-year CAGR (2020-2024) was 6.5%, but heavily distorted by COVID recovery bounce in 2022 (+20%)
- Normalised organic growth (excluding COVID recovery): ~2-3%

The thesis uses "TAM +3%, pricing +1-2%, mix shift +1-2% = 5-7%". But actual results show only 1-1.6% growth in the most recent periods. The pricing power and mix shift assumptions are not materialising in reported numbers. I use 3% base case (reflecting UK inflation + modest mix improvement).

### 2. Is WACC of 9% appropriate?

**Verdict: WACC should be 9.5-10%, not 9%.**

The thesis calculates:
- Ke = 4.5% + 0.92 x 5.0% = 9.1%
- WACC = 9.0% (since minimal debt)

Issues:
- UK 10Y gilt is currently ~4.5% (correct)
- Beta of 0.92 is reasonable for a UK digital business
- BUT: UK Equity Risk Premium should be 5.0-5.5% given current macro uncertainty
- A #2 player facing AI disruption risk deserves a risk premium above pure CAPM
- I use WACC of 9.5% base (reflecting #2 market position + AI uncertainty)

### 3. DCF of 333 GBp vs OEY of 240 GBp -- which is more reliable?

**Verdict: Neither. Both are too high. The DCF at 333 GBp is particularly unreliable.**

The DCF tool uses 5% growth default, which I've shown is optimistic. With corrected assumptions:
- DCF at 3% growth, 9.5% WACC, 2.5% terminal = 309 GBp (still high)
- DCF at 3% growth, 10% WACC, 2% terminal = 251 GBp (more reasonable)
- DCF at 0% growth, 9.5% WACC, 2% terminal = 235 GBp

The OEY method in the thesis implicitly assumes the market should pay 8% OEY for MONY, yielding 240 GBp. But what OEY should the market demand?
- AUTO.L (monopoly, 50% FCF margin): FCF yield ~5-6% -- lower yield demanded because higher quality
- RMV.L (near-monopoly): FCF yield ~5%
- MONY.L (duopoly #2, AI risk): should command HIGHER yield (lower valuation) than monopolists

An OEY of 9-10% is more appropriate for a #2 player with AI headwinds. This implies FV of 194-215 GBp, NOT 240 GBp.

### 4. Reverse DCF: What growth does the market price?

**The thesis claims "market prices 0-1% growth." This is INCORRECT.**

My reverse DCF calculation shows:
| WACC | Terminal Growth | Implied FCF Growth |

| 9.0% | 2.0% | -8.5% |
| 9.5% | 2.0% | -7.1% |
| 10.0% | 2.0% | -5.7% |
| 9.5% | 2.5% | -8.1% |

The market is pricing NEGATIVE FCF growth of -5% to -8%. This is much more pessimistic than "0-1% growth." This changes the thesis materially:
- The thesis argues the market prices zero growth when the company can do 5-7% -- a huge gap
- Reality: the market prices material FCF decline, which is consistent with AI disruption fears and the declining gross margin trend
- The question is not "will MONY grow?" but "will MONY's FCF decline?"

### 5. Gross margin declining -- should assumptions be lower?

**Verdict: YES. This is material and the thesis underweights it.**

| Year | Gross Margin | Change |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 2022 | 67.8% | - |
| 2023 | 67.6% | -0.2pp |
| 2024 | 66.2% | -1.4pp |

This is a -2.9pp decline over 2 years, accelerating. The thesis dismisses this as "noise" (only 1.5pp in 1 year). But with TTM data showing further deterioration to 64.9%, this is a clear TREND, not noise. Possible causes:
- More competitive pricing to maintain share vs Compare the Market
- Higher provider costs / lower commission rates
- Mix shift toward lower-margin products

If gross margin continues declining at -1pp/year, by 2027 it would be ~63%, which compresses earnings even if revenue grows modestly.

### 7. What P/E multiple does a #2 player deserve?

**Verdict: Significant discount to monopolists.**

| Peer | P/E | Position | Moat | FCF Margin |
|------|-----|----------|------|------------|
| AUTO.L | 14.7x | Monopoly (75% share) | Wide | ~50% |
| RMV.L | 17.2x | Near-monopoly | Wide | ~60% |

AUTO.L deserves premium: monopoly position, 75% market share, 50% FCF margin, pricing power proven.

A fair discount: MONY should trade at 70-80% of AUTO.L's multiple.

**The current P/E of 11.1x may already be FAIR for a #2 player with declining margins and AI risk.** This directly contradicts the thesis claim that P/E should re-rate to 14-15x.
