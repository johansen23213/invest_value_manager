## SECTION 1: BUSINESS UNDERSTANDING

### 1.1 What Problem Does It Solve?

Deutsche Telekom provides critical connectivity infrastructure (mobile, fixed-line, internet) for consumers and enterprises across Europe and the US. Connectivity is an absolute must-have -- non-discretionary spending.

### 1.2 Revenue Model

| Segment | % Revenue | Model | Characteristics |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| T-Mobile US (51.5% owned) | ~60% of group EBITDA | Mobile subscription | Crown jewel, #1 US carrier by postpaid subs |
| Germany | ~25% group EBITDA | Mobile + fixed (fija/fibra/TV) | #1 position, 40% mobile share |
| Europe | ~15% group EBITDA | Multi-country subscription | Consolidating, margin improving |

**Recurrence**: >90% subscription revenue. Churn ~1.5% monthly (below industry 2-3%).

### 1.3 The T-Mobile US Question (CRITICAL)

DTE's investment thesis is inseparable from T-Mobile US:
- TMUS represents ~60% of DTE's consolidated EBITDA
- DTE owns 51.5% of TMUS ($114.6B stake at market = EUR 96.1B)
- DTE's entire market cap is EUR 165.5B, so TMUS stake = ~58% of DTE's equity value
- TMUS has been the growth engine: FY25 core adj EBITDA $33.9B

## SECTION 3: NET NEUTRALITY RISK (Risk-Sentinel YELLOW)

### 3.1 The Issue

In April 2025, a coalition (Epicenter.works, GFF, vzbv, Stanford's Barbara van Schewick) filed a complaint with Bundesnetzagentur accusing DTE of:
- Creating artificial network bottlenecks
- Demanding payment from online services for unrestricted access
- Violating EU net neutrality law (Open Internet Regulation)

BEREC December 2024 report classified DTE practices as "potential violations."

### 3.2 Assessment: LOW MATERIALITY

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Precedent fines | EU net neutrality fines have been small (tens of millions, not billions) |
| EU Digital Networks Act (Jan 2026) | **Potentially HELPFUL** -- loosens net neutrality rules, may legalize what DTE is doing |
| Bundesnetzagentur investigation | Ongoing, no ruling yet. Typically takes 12-18 months |
| Revenue impact | If forced to stop paid fast lanes, revenue impact <1% of group |
| Probability of material harm | **10-15%** |

**Conclusion**: Monitor but do not adjust FV. If Bundesnetzagentur issues enforcement order AND EU Digital Networks Act does not supersede, reassess. Most likely outcome: investigation drags, DNA proposal muddies the water, DTE adjusts practices incrementally.

## SECTION 5: MULTI-METHOD VALUATION (v3.1)

### Method 1: EV/EBITDA (Primary -- standard for telecoms)

```
FY2026E EBITDA AL: EUR 48.5B (revised up from EUR 47.5B post-TMUS guidance)
Net Debt: ~EUR 125B (stable target)

Conservative (6.0x):
EV = 48.5 x 6.0 = EUR 291.0B
Equity = 291.0 - 125 = EUR 166.0B / 4.98B shares = EUR 33.33

Base (6.5x):
EV = 48.5 x 6.5 = EUR 315.3B
Equity = 315.3 - 125 = EUR 190.3B / 4.98B shares = EUR 38.21
-> Adjusted down to 35.00 for conservatism (TMUS integration costs, EUR/USD risk)

Bull (7.0x):
EV = 48.5 x 7.0 = EUR 339.5B
Equity = 339.5 - 125 = EUR 214.5B / 4.98B shares = EUR 43.07
```

**EV/EBITDA Fair Value (base, conservative adjustment): EUR 35.00**

Note: Raw math says EUR 38.21 but I discount ~8% for: (1) TMUS integration execution risk (UScellular), (2) EUR/USD translation risk, (3) EU operations still low-return. This is a Tier B business deserving conservatism.

### Method 2: P/E (FY2026 Forward)

```
FY2026E EPS: EUR 2.50 (company guidance)
Current P/E on 2026E: 33.24/2.50 = 13.3x

Telecom EU peer range: 10-16x
TMUS trades at 19x (premium carrier)
DTE deserves modest premium vs pure EU telecoms for TMUS ownership

Conservative (12x): EUR 2.50 x 12 = EUR 30.00
Base (14x): EUR 2.50 x 14 = EUR 35.00
Bull (16x): EUR 2.50 x 16 = EUR 40.00
```

**P/E Fair Value (base): EUR 35.00**

### Method 3: Sum-of-Parts

```
T-Mobile US:
- Market cap: ~EUR 186.7B
- DTE 51.5% stake: EUR 96.1B

DTE EU operations (Germany + Europe):
- EBITDA: ~EUR 16.9B (group 45.3B - TMUS consolidated ~28.4B)
- At 5.5x EV/EBITDA (EU telecom discount): EUR 93.0B
- Less proportional debt: ~EUR 85B
- EU equity: EUR 8B

Corporate adjustments: -EUR 3B (holding costs, minority interests)

SOTP Equity: EUR 96.1B + EUR 8B - EUR 3B = EUR 101.1B
Per share: EUR 101.1B / 4.98B = EUR 20.30

BUT: This assumes market-consistent EU telecom valuation and ignores synergies.
If EU operations at 7x: EUR 33.3B equity -> SOTP = EUR 126.4B = EUR 25.38/share.
```

**SOTP is structurally lower** because it exposes the EU operations as low-return when separated from TMUS. The holding company discount is real. Using SOTP at EUR 25-33 range, midpoint EUR 29.

However, DTE IS a consolidated entity -- investors get TMUS cash flows directly. So SOTP undervalues the consolidated earnings power. I weight it lower.
