## Business Understanding

### What does Konecranes do?

Konecranes is the world's largest supplier of industrial cranes and wire rope hoists, producing roughly 1 in 10 of the world's cranes. The company operates through three segments:

**1. Industrial Service (~1/3 of revenue, ~22% EBITA margin)**
- Maintenance, inspection, spare parts for industrial cranes of ALL manufacturers
- ~50% of service agreements are on competitors' equipment (brand-agnostic)
- Service agreement base value: EUR 339M (growing 4.4% in comparable currencies)
- ~16,500 technicians globally in 50+ countries
- **This is the crown jewel**: recurring, high-margin, installed-base-driven revenue with significant outsourcing TAM

**2. Industrial Equipment (~1/3 of revenue, ~12% EBITA margin)**
- Design, manufacturing, and sales of industrial cranes, hoists, components
- New X-series crane launched Aug 2024 (successor to CXT)
- Synergies with Service: every equipment sale creates decades of service revenue

**3. Port Solutions (~1/3 of revenue, ~9% EBITA margin)**
- Container handling equipment for ports and terminals: ship-to-shore cranes, RTGs, straddle carriers
- Order book: EUR 1.67B (provides revenue visibility)
- Electrification and automation as key differentiators
- Global #1-3 in all product categories

### What problem does it solve?
Industrial cranes are mission-critical equipment in manufacturing, logistics, and ports. Downtime is extremely expensive (production lines stop, ships wait). Konecranes solves the problem of uptime, safety, and productivity for heavy lifting operations.

### Revenue model
- **Equipment sales** (lumpy, project-based, ~2/3 of revenue): Large capex purchases with long lead times
- **Service revenue** (~1/3 of revenue, highly recurring): Maintenance contracts, inspections, spare parts. Multi-year agreements with high renewal rates
- **Aftermarket/parts**: High-margin, recurring

### Unit Economics
- Not a traditional CAC/LTV business, but the razor/blade model applies:
  - Equipment = razor (moderate margin)
  - Service = blade (high margin, decades of revenue per crane installed)
- A crane has 20-30 year useful life. Each crane generates service revenue for its entire lifespan
- **Capital intensity: MEDIUM** -- manufacturing requires factories and inventory, but the service business is asset-light

### Structure of Margins (EXPANDING -- a KEY positive signal)
```
Gross Margin:    55.0% (2022) -> 54.1% (2023) -> 55.6% (2024) -> 57.1% (2025)  [EXPANDING]
Op Margin:        6.7% (2022) -> 10.1% (2023) -> 12.1% (2024) -> 13.2% (2025)  [EXPANDING strongly]
FCF Margin:       0.7% (2022) -> 12.7% (2023) -> 10.0% (2024) -> 12.4% (2025)  [Strong post-2022]
```

The margin expansion from 6.7% to 13.2% operating margin in 3 years reflects:
1. Post-COVID supply chain normalization
2. Mix shift toward higher-margin Service
3. Pricing power in tight crane market
4. Operational efficiencies

Management target: 13-16% comparable EBITA margin by 2029.

- Largest global manufacturing footprint
- Procurement advantage on raw materials (steel)
- R&D amortized over larger volume
