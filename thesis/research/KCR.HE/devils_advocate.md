# Counter-Analysis: KCR.HE (Konecranes Plc)

## Fecha: 2026-02-18

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The bull thesis for Konecranes is built on five pillars: record margins, net cash, a defensible service moat, ZPMC de-Sinification tailwind, and 19.6% insider ownership. **Three of these five pillars have material weaknesses the thesis does not adequately address.** Record margins are almost certainly cyclical peak margins with only 3 years of history at elevated levels. The ZPMC tailwind is real but slower, more contested, and more priced-in than the thesis assumes. And the "19.6% insider ownership" is misleading -- the largest holder (11.1%) is Solidium Oy, the Finnish government's investment vehicle, which is a passive strategic holder, not an aligned operator-owner. The thesis survives scrutiny as a quality cyclical at a fair price, but the Fair Value of EUR 114 is aggressive. The DA-adjusted Fair Value is EUR 95-100, which at the current price of EUR 97.90 offers essentially zero margin of safety.

## Assumptions Challenged

### 1. "Record EBITA margin of 14.0% is sustainable and will expand toward 16%"

- **Evidence against:** This is the SINGLE MOST CRITICAL challenge. Konecranes' margin history reveals extreme cyclicality:
  - 2016: Comparable EBITA margin ~5.6%
  - 2017: ~6.9% (MHPS acquisition year)
  - 2018: ~9.4%
  - 2019: ~8.3% (pre-COVID already declining)
  - 2020: Severely depressed (COVID, H1 order intake -29.3%)
  - 2022: 8.5% (post-COVID recovery still incomplete)
  - 2023: 12.2%
  - 2024: 13.2%
  - 2025: 14.0% (ALL-TIME RECORD)

  The 10-year average EBITA margin is approximately 8-10%, NOT 14%. The current margin is 40-75% ABOVE the historical average. Management itself guides only "approximately same level" for 2026 -- they are NOT guiding for expansion. The 13-16% target for 2029 is aspirational and assumes continued favorable pricing, mix, and cycle.

  **Operating margin history confirms this**: 4.5% (2019) -> 6.7% (2022) -> 13.2% (2025). The operating margin TRIPLED in 3 years. This is not structural improvement alone -- it includes cyclical tailwinds (post-COVID demand surge, pricing power in tight supply, favorable steel costs).

  **EPS context**: Historical EPS (from available data in EUR):
  - 2016: ~EUR 0.64 (converted from CAD 0.94)
  - 2017: EUR 2.88 (MHPS acquisition boost)
  - 2018: EUR 1.29
  - 2019: EUR 1.03
  - 2022: EUR 1.77
  - 2023: EUR 3.48
  - 2024: EUR 4.65
  - 2025: EUR 5.05 (ALL-TIME RECORD)

  Mid-cycle EPS is approximately EUR 2.50-3.00 based on historical patterns. At mid-cycle EPS of EUR 2.75 and a fair P/E of 16x (generous for a cyclical industrial), the mid-cycle fair value is EUR 44. Even at optimistic mid-cycle EPS of EUR 3.50 (assuming structural improvement from service mix) and 18x P/E, mid-cycle FV is EUR 63. The thesis FV of EUR 114 implicitly assumes peak margins are permanent.

- **Severity:** **CRITICAL**
- **Resolution suggested:** Recalculate Fair Value using NORMALIZED earnings, not peak earnings. A mid-cycle EPS of EUR 3.00-3.50 (reflecting genuine structural improvement from service mix but not peak cycle) is more appropriate. This dramatically lowers FV from EUR 114 to EUR 55-75 range.

### 2. "19.6% insider ownership provides exceptional alignment"

- **Evidence against:** The "19.6% insider ownership" figure reported by yfinance is MISLEADING. Independent research reveals:
  - **Solidium Oy** (Finnish government investment vehicle) owns **11.1%** of shares (8.79M shares). This is the LARGEST single holder. Solidium is a PASSIVE strategic investor -- it holds stakes in "nationally important" Finnish companies. This is government policy, NOT operator alignment. Solidium does NOT run the company, does NOT have operational expertise, and its investment thesis is "keep Finnish companies Finnish," not "maximize shareholder value."
  - **Oras Invest Oy** owns ~3.4% (2.71M shares). This is the Paasikivi family's investment vehicle. More aligned than Solidium but still passive.
  - **Varma Mutual Pension Insurance** owns ~3.4% (2.70M shares). Pension fund -- purely financial.
  - **Ilmarinen Mutual Pension Insurance** owns ~3.1% (2.49M shares). Pension fund -- purely financial.
  - Together, these four holders account for ~21% of shares. The "insider" label is a classification artifact -- these are not founder-operators or management with their net worth on the line.
  - **Actual management ownership** is likely minimal. The yfinance "insider" classification bundles board members, pension funds, and strategic government stakes together.
  - No significant recent insider BUYING detected -- if insiders truly believed the stock was cheap, we would expect purchases.

  [Source Classification: PRIMARY DATA from Konecranes IR / SECONDARY from search results cross-referenced]

- **Severity:** **HIGH**
- **Resolution suggested:** Downgrade insider ownership assessment from "EXCEPTIONAL alignment" to "Government strategic stake + Finnish pension funds = passively supportive but NOT operator-aligned." This is a meaningful distinction. Compare to companies like BYIT.L where the director personally bought at 402p, or NVO where Novo Holdings has genuine research expertise. Solidium has no crane expertise.

### 3. "ZPMC de-Sinification creates $20B tailwind"

- **Evidence against:** The de-Sinification thesis is real but substantially overstated:

  **a) Timeline is decades, not years:**
  - ZPMC controls 70-80% of the global STS crane market. 80% of US port cranes are ZPMC-manufactured.
  - Replacing ALL ZPMC cranes in the US alone would take 15-25 years. A single STS crane costs $15-25M and takes 2-3 years to manufacture and install. The US has ~200 ZPMC STS cranes.
  - US domestic crane manufacturing capacity does NOT YET EXIST at scale. PACECO (Mitsui subsidiary) is being stood up, and Konecranes announced a US consortium (Ohio, Virginia, Wisconsin) -- but these are PLANS, not production lines. First cranes from domestic production are years away.

  **b) Ports are actively resisting:**
  - US port authorities have pushed back AGAINST tariffs on replacement cranes. Port operators face the cost burden and oppose rapid replacement mandates.
  - The May 2026 tariff exemption deadline means contracts signed before May 2024 and delivered before May 2026 are exempt from the 25% tariff on Chinese cranes. Ports rushed to order ZPMC cranes before the deadline.
  - The Port of Virginia ordered 8 MORE ZPMC cranes in 2024-2025, DESPITE the security concerns.

  **c) Konecranes may NOT capture the majority:**
  - Liebherr is an equally viable Western alternative and is ALSO positioning for the ZPMC replacement opportunity.
  - Kalmar (post-Cargotec demerger) is a third competitor.
  - A Konecranes STS crane costs $20M+ vs ZPMC at $10-14M -- this 40%+ price premium is a significant barrier, even with subsidies.
  - ZPMC itself has warned that tariffs could cause "port shutdowns" -- creating political pressure to soften enforcement.

  **d) Already partly priced in:**
  - The Maritime Supply Chain Security Act passed the Senate in June 2025. This is PUBLIC INFORMATION. The market has had 8+ months to price this in.
  - With only 4 analysts covering KCR.HE, the market may not have fully priced it, but institutional investors (43.2%) are aware.
  - My consensus analysis: mean analyst PT of EUR 104 likely INCLUDES some de-Sinification benefit. The thesis adds an additional 10% above consensus, partly for this tailwind -- but the tailwind is known.

  [Source Classification: PRIMARY DATA (US government legislation, Konecranes press releases, ZPMC tariff filings) + SECONDARY (market reports, port authority statements)]

- **Severity:** **HIGH**
- **Resolution suggested:** De-Sinification is a genuine structural tailwind but: (1) timeline is 15-25 years, not 3-5, (2) Konecranes shares this with Liebherr and Kalmar, (3) the legislative framework is already public. Do NOT assign material near-term FV uplift for this. At best, it supports the base case that Port Solutions maintains current revenue levels rather than declining.

### 4. "The market implies near-zero growth (0.8%/yr implied FCF growth) -- overly pessimistic"

- **Evidence against:** The reverse DCF shows 0.8% implied FCF growth. The thesis argues this is too pessimistic. But consider:
  - FCF of EUR 520M in 2025 is based on RECORD margins (14.0% EBITA). If margins revert to even 12% (still above historical average), FCF drops to ~EUR 400-430M.
  - If margins revert to mid-cycle 10%, FCF drops to ~EUR 300-350M.
  - The market may be implying that CURRENT FCF is above sustainable levels and will DECLINE, not grow at 0.8%. A 0.8% growth rate on peak FCF could be equivalent to 5-7% growth on normalized FCF -- which is actually reasonable for this business.
  - Revenue was FLAT in 2025 (-0.9%). Full-year revenue: EUR 4,188M vs EUR 4,227M in 2024. This is not a growth story.
  - Q4 2025 order intake declined 4.3% in comparable currencies. Management flagged "somewhat longer decision-making times within customers."

  The "historical FCF CAGR of 187%" cited in the reverse DCF is meaningless (recovery from EUR 22M trough in 2022). The thesis correctly identifies this but then still argues the market is too pessimistic. **The market may be correctly pricing in margin normalization.**

- **Severity:** **MODERATE**
- **Resolution suggested:** The market is not necessarily pessimistic -- it may be appropriately pricing in peak margin risk. The implied 0.8% growth on peak FCF is roughly equivalent to 4-5% growth on normalized FCF, which aligns with the thesis's own 4.5% base case. The market may be smarter than the thesis gives it credit for.

### 5. "QS 73, no adjustment warranted"

- **Evidence against:** The QS of 73 has several distortions:

  **Upward distortions (QS may be LOWER):**
  - **EPS CAGR 41.8% scores 10/10**: This is a base effect from EUR 1.77 (2022) to EUR 5.05 (2025). Sustainable EPS growth is 5-8%, which would score 5/10 instead of 10/10. This alone is a -5 point distortion.
  - **Gross margin 57.1% vs sector 28.0% = +29pp premium**: The moat-assessor correctly flagged this as a potential CLASSIFICATION ARTIFACT. Konecranes may report costs differently than pure equipment manufacturers. The true GM premium, while real (service business lifts margins), is likely overstated. If the real premium is +15pp instead of +29pp, scoring drops from 10/10 to 7/10 (-3 points).
  - **ROIC persistence 5/7**: ROIC > WACC only confirmed for 2023-2025 (3 years). Before that, ROIC was 6.4% in 2022 and likely below WACC for several prior years. A 3-year track record of adequate ROIC should score 3/7, not 5/7 (-2 points).

  **Downward distortions (QS may be HIGHER):**
  - **Market Position 0/8**: Konecranes is global #1 in industrial cranes. This should be 5-8 points.

  **Net assessment**: +5 to +8 (market position) MINUS -5 (EPS CAGR) -3 (GM artifact) -2 (ROIC persistence) = 0 to -2 adjustment. The thesis's conclusion of "no adjustment, 73 is right" may be approximately correct, but for the WRONG reasons. The tool overscores and underscores in different areas that happen to roughly cancel.

  **The key issue is not the QS number but what it means at this point in the cycle.** A QS of 73 at PEAK earnings is very different from a QS of 73 at MID-CYCLE earnings. At mid-cycle, financial quality sub-scores would be significantly lower (lower ROIC spread, lower FCF margin, potentially lower FCF consistency).

- **Severity:** **MODERATE**
- **Resolution suggested:** QS 73 at peak cycle is roughly equivalent to QS 60-65 on a normalized basis. Tier B classification is correct but the MoS requirement should reflect the cyclical risk -- closer to 25-30% than the standard 18-25%.

## Conflicts with Other Analyses

### Moat Assessment vs Valuation

The moat assessment rates the moat as NARROW (leaning WIDE) at 17/25. This is honest and well-reasoned. However, the fundamental analysis then uses a 14x EV/EBITA "quality premium" multiple in its bull case, which is more appropriate for a WIDE moat company. For a NARROW-leaning moat with 3 years of elevated ROIC history, 10-12x is more appropriate, yielding FV of EUR 84-100 -- significantly below the thesis FV of EUR 114.

### Risk Assessment vs Overall Thesis

The risk assessment correctly identifies cyclical downturn and peak margins as both HIGH risks and notes they are CORRELATED. It then rates overall risk as MEDIUM with the justification that "the risk is cyclical timing, not business quality." While this reasoning is valid, the INVESTMENT DECISION is made at a specific point in time. Cyclical timing IS the risk when buying at peak margins. The risk assessment's own calculation shows 40-55% potential downside in a downturn -- this should have elevated the entry price requirement, not been dismissed as "cyclical."

### Fundamental Analysis Internal Inconsistency

The fundamental analysis derives a conservative FV of EUR 108, then adjusts to EUR 110, then "gives slight credit for service moat underappreciation and 4 analysts" to arrive at EUR 114. This is a +6 adjustment based on OPINION ("underappreciation" is an unfalsifiable claim). The base analysis points to EUR 100-108, and the FV should have stayed there.

**STRONG COUNTER** -- The thesis has a fundamental structural problem: it values a cyclical industrial company at peak margins as if those margins were permanent.
