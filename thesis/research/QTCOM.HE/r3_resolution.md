## Conflicts Resolved

### 1. P/E Misleading (DA ACCEPTED)

R1 used FY2024 trailing P/E of 15.2x. DA correctly identified that FY2025E EPS is EUR 1.20 (-47%), making forward P/E 20.9x. FY2026E EPS EUR 1.28 gives 19.6x.

**Resolution:** ACCEPT. The 15.2x P/E was misleading. At EUR 25, the stock trades at ~20x on current-year earnings, which is fair-to-slightly-cheap for a high-quality software company in a trough — NOT screaming cheap as R1 implied.

**Impact on FV:** Downward revision. OEY and EV/EBIT recalculated on normalized FY2026-2027 earnings, not FY2024 peak.

### 2. Q3 Trough Assumption (DA ACCEPTED)

R1 implied Q3 2025 was the cyclical bottom. DA showed CEO explicitly guided for no Q4 improvement, and management issued TWO profit warnings in 2025.

**Resolution:** ACCEPT. Recovery timing unknowable. Revised scenario probabilities:
- Extended downturn (recovery H2 2026 only): 40% (was ~25%)
- Modest recovery Q1-Q2 2026: 35% (was ~50%)
- Strong recovery: 15% (was ~20%)
- Structural impairment: 10% (was ~5%)

### 3. ROIC Peak-Year (DA PARTIALLY ACCEPTED)

R1 highlighted 43% ROIC as exceptional quality. DA noted this was peak-year; annualized Q3 2025 ROIC is ~10-15%. 3-5 year average is ~35-40%.

**Resolution:** PARTIALLY ACCEPT. Use 35% normalized ROIC for OEY calculation, not 43%. Qt's ROIC has been consistently well above WACC even in downturns, which confirms business quality — but 43% overstates the sustainable level.

### 4. IAR Acquisition (DA PARTIALLY ACCEPTED)

DA raised valid concerns: 63.6% premium, terrible timing, balance sheet deterioration, SaaS transition plan unknown until Q1 2026.

**Resolution:** PARTIALLY ACCEPT. IAR is a profitable business (EUR ~15M EBITDA) in a strategic market, not a value-destructive deal. But the premium, timing, and management distraction during a revenue crisis are valid concerns. Model IAR contribution conservatively:
- 50% weight: IAR at acquisition cost (neutral)
- 30% weight: Modest synergies (positive ~EUR 1/share)
- 20% weight: Integration drag (negative ~EUR 2/share)
- Net impact: approximately -EUR 0.50/share vs R1 optimistic case

### 5. QS Staleness (DA ACCEPTED — CRITICAL)

DA's strongest point: QS 76-80 uses FY2024 data. On FY2025E data:
- Revenue growth: +5.6% (vs +15.7% → Growth Quality drops ~13 points)
- Operating margin: ~18% (vs 30.2% → Financial Quality drops ~5 points)
- FCF margin: <15% (vs 25.1% → drops further)

Estimated FY2025-based QS: ~60-65 (Tier B, not Tier A).

**Resolution:** ACCEPT. This is the most impactful finding. Reclassify QTCOM.HE as **Tier B for sizing/MoS purposes** until FY2025 data confirms otherwise. Feb 26 earnings will reveal whether trailing QS of 76 or forward ~62 is more representative.

**Implications:**
- MoS requirement: ~20-25% (Tier B) vs ~15% (Tier A)
- Sizing: 3% max (not 4%)
- Entry must be calibrated to Tier B standards

### 6. FV Convergence with Consensus (DA PARTIALLY ACCEPTED)

R1 FV EUR 37 converged with consensus PT EUR 35.50. Per Error #49, this means no edge.

**Resolution:** PARTIALLY ACCEPT. However, our REVISED FV of EUR 30 is now 15% BELOW consensus. Our independent analysis (using trough-year adjustments, conservative IAR, forward QS) produces a MORE CONSERVATIVE view than sell-side consensus. This IS an edge — we identify that even optimistic analysts may be anchoring to recovery that isn't evidenced.

The edge is not "Qt is undervalued vs consensus" but rather "we wait for confirmation that consensus is right before buying."
