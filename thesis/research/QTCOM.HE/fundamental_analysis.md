## Business Understanding

### What Qt Does

Qt Group develops and licenses the Qt framework -- the industry-standard cross-platform development framework used primarily for:

1. **Embedded Device UIs**: Automotive dashboards/infotainment (Mercedes, BMW, Porsche, Ducati), medical devices (GE HealthCare), industrial HMIs, consumer electronics
2. **Desktop Applications**: Cross-platform apps (Linux, Windows, macOS) -- e.g., VLC media player, Autodesk Maya, OBS Studio
3. **Design Tools**: Qt Design Studio for rapid UI prototyping
4. **Quality Assurance**: Testing tools (Squish, Coco)

With the IAR Systems acquisition (Oct 2025, EUR 204M), Qt expanded into embedded compilers and debuggers, covering the entire embedded development lifecycle from UI to metal.

### Revenue Model (CRITICAL -- Dual Revenue Streams)

Qt has a powerful dual revenue model:

| Stream | Description | Characteristics |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Developer Licenses** | Per-seat subscription (EUR ~5,800/yr/developer) | Recurring, SaaS-like |
| **Distribution Licenses** | Per-device royalty for shipped embedded devices | Scales with customer success, volume-based |

This is NOT a pure per-seat SaaS model. The distribution license component means revenue scales with DEVICE VOLUMES, not headcount. This is a critical distinction vs the SaaSpocalypse per-seat pricing risk.

**Revenue breakdown context:** ~60-65% developer licenses (recurring subscriptions), ~35-40% distribution licenses (device-linked royalties). The distribution component creates operating leverage tied to IoT/embedded device growth.

**Customer metrics:**
- 3,500+ commercial customers
- 1.5M+ developers using Qt (both open-source and commercial)
- 70+ industries
- 1B+ devices running Qt
- Customer churn stable (per Q3 2025 call)
- BUT: customers reducing license counts and shifting from 3-year to 1-year deals

### Unit Economics

- **Gross Margin:** 52.5% (expanding from 44.7% in 2021 -- excellent trajectory)
- **Operating Margin:** 30.2% (FY2024) -- but guided to 20-30% for FY2025

### IAR Systems Acquisition Context

- **Price:** EUR 204M cash (funded from balance sheet + small debt)

## Valuation (Tier A: OEY Primary + DCF Secondary)

### Method 1: Owner Earnings Yield (60% weight)

```
Owner Earnings = FCF - Maintenance Capex
FCF (FY2024) = EUR 52.4M
Depreciation = EUR 7.5M (estimated from capex/depreciation 0.1x ratio)
Maintenance Capex = Depreciation * 1.1 = EUR 8.3M
Owner Earnings = EUR 52.4M - EUR 8.3M + EUR 7.5M = EUR 51.6M

BUT: FY2024 was a strong year. Normalizing for cycle:
Normalized FCF (average FY2023-FY2024): (39.2 + 52.4) / 2 = EUR 45.8M
Normalized OE = EUR 45.8M - EUR 1.0M (minimal maintenance capex, asset-light) = EUR 44.8M

Market Cap = EUR 636M
OEY = EUR 44.8M / EUR 636M = 7.0%

Expected Growth = 10-12% (conservative organic + IAR synergies)
OEY + Growth = 7.0% + 11% = 18.0% vs WACC 10.6% = +7.4pp spread (ATTRACTIVE)
```

For fair value derivation:
```

Add: 5 years of above-terminal growth at 10%:

Total Equity Value = EUR 553M + EUR 150M + EUR 87M (net cash) = EUR 790M
Per share (25.4M shares) = EUR 31.10

Combined OE (FY2026E) = ~EUR 55-60M
Equity Value = EUR 60M / 0.081 + EUR 150M + EUR 50M (reduced net cash post-IAR) = EUR 940M
Per share = EUR 37.00
```
