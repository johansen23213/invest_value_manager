# Counter-Analysis: QTCOM.HE (Qt Group Oyj)

## Fecha: 2026-02-19

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The R1 thesis presents a compelling "quality compounder at distressed valuation" narrative, but my independent research reveals several material gaps. The thesis anchors to FY2024 financials (the GOOD year) while FY2025 has been catastrophic operationally. The P/E cited as 15.2x is misleading -- the forward P/E on consensus FY2025 EPS (EUR 1.20) is 20.9x, which is NOT cheap for a company whose EPS has collapsed 47% YoY. The moat is NARROW (correctly classified), but the thesis FV of EUR 37 assumes a recovery path that is far from guaranteed. The thesis survives scrutiny at a lower FV range (EUR 28-32) but the EUR 37 base case is aggressive.

---

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. "The stock is at 15.2x P/E -- extremely cheap for a 20% grower"

- **Evidencia en contra:** The 15.2x P/E uses FY2024 EPS of EUR 2.26, which is STALE. FY2025 consensus EPS is EUR 1.20 (-47% YoY). Forward P/E on FY2025E = 20.9x. Forward P/E on FY2026E (EUR 1.28) = 19.6x. This is NOT a cheap stock by any reasonable standard -- it trades at 20x depressed earnings for a company that SHRUNK its EPS by half. Nordic serial acquirers (Lagercrantz, Addtech) trade at 30-40x but are GROWING consistently. Qt is not growing; it is contracting.
- **Source classification:** FY2025 consensus from 6 analysts = LEVEL 2 (secondary analysis). But directionally confirmed by Q1-Q3 2025 actual data (LEVEL 1): 9-month EPS EUR 0.06, annualized ~EUR 0.08 (if you annualize Jan-Sep run rate). Even if Q4 is seasonally strong, full-year EPS recovery to EUR 1.20 requires Q4 EBIT of ~EUR 40M -- implying a MASSIVE seasonal spike that management gave no indication of expecting.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The thesis MUST recalculate all valuation multiples using FY2025E earnings, not FY2024. The "cheap" narrative collapses when using current-year numbers. The investment committee must see both the trailing P/E (15.2x on peak FY2024) and the forward P/E (20.9x on trough FY2025E).

### 2. "Q3 2025 was the trough -- cyclical recovery imminent"

- **Evidencia en contra:** The quarterly revenue trajectory in 2025 shows an ACCELERATING decline, not stabilization:
  - Q1 2025: EUR 47.2M (EBIT EUR 6.5M)
  - Q2 2025: EUR 51.2M (EBIT EUR 9.6M) -- seasonally strong
  - Q3 2025: EUR 40.7M (EBIT EUR 2.3M) -- collapse
  - Jan-Sep 2025 total: EUR 139.2M (-1.0% YoY)
  - Q3 EBITA margin collapsed to 10.5% vs 24.5% prior year

  CEO Juha Varelius explicitly stated: "Market softness in embedded, automotive, and consumer electronics segments expected to persist through Q4; no significant improvement expected." This is the CEO telling you NOT to expect recovery in Q4. The thesis assertion that "Q3 2025 was the bottom" has zero management backing and contradicts the CEO's own guidance.

  Furthermore, this is NOT a single-quarter problem. Revenue growth has DECELERATED for 5 consecutive quarters: 28.2% (FY2022) -> 16.4% (FY2023) -> 15.7% (FY2024) -> 4.8% (Q1 2025) -> ~8% (Q2 2025 implied) -> -3.4% (Q3 2025). This is a clear secular deceleration trend, not a cyclical blip.

  The company has issued TWO profit warnings in 2025 (April and October). Management was over-optimistic BOTH times. Why should we trust their "confidence in long-term growth prospects"?
- **Source classification:** CEO quote = LEVEL 1 (primary). Quarterly financials = LEVEL 1. Profit warnings = LEVEL 1.
- **Severidad:** **CRITICAL**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The investment committee MUST NOT assume cyclical recovery without earnings confirmation (the hard gate is correct). But the thesis should explicitly model a scenario where the downturn persists through FY2026 and assign it at least 35% probability, not 25%.

### 3. "ROIC of 43% proves business quality is intact"

- **Evidencia en contra:** The 43% ROIC is from FY2024. But Q3 2025 EBITA margin was 10.5% vs 30.2% operating margin in FY2024. If we annualize Q3 2025 economics (revenue ~EUR 163M, EBIT ~EUR 9.2M), ROIC drops to roughly 10-15% -- still above WACC but NOT the exceptional 43% the thesis highlights. The relevant question is: what is the SUSTAINABLE ROIC? Not the peak-year ROIC.

  The moat assessment correctly noted that ROIC has been declining: 136% (2020) -> 72% (2021) -> 43% (2022) -> 35% (2023) -> 45% (2024). The 2020-2021 figures were COVID software boom anomalies. The normalized ROIC may be 25-35%, which is still good but dramatically changes the OEY calculation.

  The thesis uses 43% ROIC as evidence of extreme mispricing. But if ROIC is normalizing to 25-35% (or lower during the current downturn), the mispricing narrative weakens significantly.
- **Source classification:** FY2024 ROIC = LEVEL 1. ROIC trend = LEVEL 1 (from quality_scorer). My ROIC annualization = LEVEL 3 (my estimate).
- **Severidad:** **MODERATE**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Use a 3-5 year average ROIC (35-45%) rather than peak-year ROIC for valuation inputs. Explicitly note that current-year ROIC is materially lower.

### 4. "IAR Systems transforms the TAM from EUR 400M to EUR 10B+"

- **Evidencia en contra:** The TAM expansion claim comes directly from Qt management -- a LEVEL 3 source (promotional). Management has strong incentive to justify the acquisition premium (63.6% over 3-month VWAP).

  Critical IAR acquisition concerns:
  - **Paid EUR 204M for a business contributing EUR 8-10M in partial-year revenue.** On a full-year basis, IAR does ~EUR 43M revenue with ~EUR 15M EBITDA. That's EV/Revenue of 4.7x and EV/EBITDA of 13.6x for a company in the process of transitioning from perpetual to subscription licensing (which means revenue may DECLINE temporarily during transition).
  - **Acquisition timing was terrible.** Qt completed this deal in Q3 2025 -- its worst quarter -- while its own core business was deteriorating. Management attention was split between a major acquisition and a revenue crisis.
  - **One-off transaction costs of EUR 6-7M** reduce FY2025 EBITA materially. Only EUR 1.7M was recorded in Q3, meaning EUR 4.3-5.3M still to come in Q4.
  - **IAR's SaaS transformation plan won't be presented until Q1 2026.** Investors are being asked to trust that the SaaS conversion will work without visibility into the plan.
  - **Balance sheet deterioration:** Qt went from net cash EUR 87M to roughly break-even or net debt post-acquisition. For a small-cap, this is a significant change in financial flexibility.
  - **The "EUR 10B+ TAM" is meaningless for a EUR 250M revenue company.** TAM expansion is relevant when you have a realistic path to capture it. Qt's pre-IAR TAM penetration was 52% -- if they can only capture 2.5% of the new TAM, the acquisition thesis needs decades to play out.

  Historical precedent for small-cap acquisitions of this relative size (~32% of acquirer's market cap): they frequently destroy value in the first 2-3 years due to integration costs, management distraction, and culture clash. Qt has no meaningful M&A track record at this scale.
- **Source classification:** TAM expansion claim = LEVEL 3 (management promotional). Acquisition price/terms = LEVEL 1. IAR revenue contribution = LEVEL 1.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The thesis should model IAR as a cost center for 2-3 years, not a value creator. The FV should be stress-tested with IAR value at zero (worst case) and at acquisition cost (neutral case), not at the optimistic synergy case.

### 5. "Distribution licenses (35-40% of revenue) make Qt immune to SaaSpocalypse"

- **Evidencia en contra:** The 35-40% distribution license split is cited from "qualitative sources" -- the thesis itself acknowledges this is not confirmed from primary filing data. Qt does not disclose segment revenue breakdown by license type in its public filings. This is a CRITICAL input that the thesis relies on WITHOUT primary data.

  Even if the 35-40% figure is correct:
  - The developer license portion (60-65%) IS exposed to per-seat pricing pressure. Q3 2025 management explicitly said: "Fewer developers due to downsizing" is driving license count reductions. This is the SaaSpocalypse in action at Qt.
  - Distribution licenses are volume-dependent. If Qt's automotive customers are deferring projects and reducing production, distribution license revenue also declines.
  - The shift from 3-year to 1-year license deals directly impacts revenue visibility and predictability -- a key quality marker.

  The thesis assertion that Qt is "much less exposed than pure SaaS" is directionally correct but quantitatively unsubstantiated.
- **Source classification:** Revenue split claim = LEVEL 3 (unverified qualitative). Management statement on downsizing = LEVEL 1.
- **Severidad:** **MODERATE**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The thesis should flag the revenue split as UNVERIFIED. If FY2025 annual report (Feb 26) provides segment data, this can be resolved. Until then, the SaaSpocalypse protection claim should carry a confidence discount.

## Conflictos con Otros Analisis

### Moat Assessment vs Thesis

The moat assessment correctly classified Qt as **NARROW**, not WIDE. The thesis implicitly treats Qt as having WIDE moat qualities (43% ROIC, dominant position, etc.) but the moat assessment highlights:
- Only 13/25 moat score
- ROIC data only covers 5 years (too short for WIDE classification)
- Declining ROIC trend (136% -> 35% -> 45%)
- Per-seat pricing under structural AI pressure
- Gross margin actually BELOW sector median (-2.5pp)

The thesis FV should be calibrated for a NARROW moat company, not a WIDE one.

### Risk Assessment vs Thesis

The risk assessment rated QTCOM.HE as **HIGH** risk with 5 HIGH/CRITICAL risks (revenue deceleration CRITICAL, margin compression CRITICAL, Feb 26 binary CRITICAL, IAR HIGH, value trap HIGH). This is materially more cautious than the thesis's tone, which emphasizes the opportunity.

The risk assessment explicitly warned: "The QS of 80 based on FY2024 data is STALE. The 2025 operational deterioration has not been captured."

### Key Discrepancy: QS Relevance

The thesis QS of 80 (tool) / 76 (adjusted) is based on FY2024 data. If we re-ran quality_scorer on FY2025E data:
- Revenue growth: +5.6% (vs +15.7% in 2024)
- Operating margin: ~18% (vs 30.2% in 2024)
- FCF margin: likely <15% (vs 25.1% in 2024)
- EPS growth: -47% (vs +61% in 2024)

The growth quality score would drop from 25/25 to roughly 8-12/25. The financial quality would also decline. A rough estimate puts QS at 55-65 on FY2025 data -- Tier B, not Tier A. This is a MATERIAL difference for sizing and MoS requirements.

### Interpretacion

**MODERATE COUNTER:** The thesis has genuine merit -- Qt IS a quality business with real switching costs, the price IS depressed, and the insider ownership IS reassuring. But the thesis has significant gaps:

1. **Valuation is anchored to peak-year data** (FY2024) while current-year (FY2025) fundamentals are materially worse.
2. **The "cyclical recovery" assumption** has no management backing and contradicts the CEO's own guidance.
3. **The IAR acquisition** is presented as a positive when it could easily be a drag for 2-3 years.
4. **The QS of 76-80 overstates current quality** because it uses backward-looking metrics from the best year.
5. **The FV of EUR 37** converges with consensus (no edge) and assumes recovery that is not yet evidenced.
6. **Forward P/E is 20.9x**, not 15.2x -- the "cheap" narrative is misleading.

The thesis is NOT wrong about the long-term quality of Qt's business. But the timing and valuation inputs are too optimistic. A more conservative FV of EUR 28-32 (using normalized/trough earnings) would be more defensible.
