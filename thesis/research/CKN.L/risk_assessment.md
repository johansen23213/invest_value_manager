# Risk Assessment: CKN.L (Clarkson PLC)

## Fecha: 2026-02-19

## Risk Score: HIGH

**Rationale for HIGH:** 3 risks classified as HIGH, 1 as CRITICAL. The combination of a confirmed profit warning (-18-26% vs FY2024), shipping cycle downturn (trade growth 0.5%), declining FCF margin (26.7% to 16.2%), and high operating leverage through staff costs creates a risk profile that demands significant margin of safety. The business quality is genuine (QS 83, Tier A, net cash, dominant market position), but the cyclical overlay is material and the timing of entry is the primary risk.

## Top 3 Riesgos Criticos

### 1. CRITICAL: Shipping Cycle Downturn — Confirmed Profit Warning

- **Categoria:** Fundamental
- **Descripcion:** Clarkson issued a profit warning for FY2025, guiding underlying PBT to GBP 85-95M vs GBP 115.3M in FY2024 — a decline of 18-26%. H1 2025 already showed revenue falling to GBP 297.8M (vs GBP 310.1M in H1 2024) and underlying PBT collapsing to GBP 39.4M (vs GBP 51.5M). Seaborne trade growth is projected at just 0.5% for 2025 (vs 2.4% in 2024). Dry bulk rates fell 31% YoY in H1. LNG spot rates dropped 56% YoY. This is NOT a theoretical risk — it is materializing now.
- **Evidencia:**
  - FY2025 guidance: GBP 85-95M PBT (down 18-26% from GBP 115.3M)
  - H1 2025: Revenue GBP 297.8M (-4.0% YoY), PBT GBP 39.4M (-23.5% YoY), EPS 98.6p (-23.6%)
  - Seaborne trade growth slowing to 0.5% (from 2.4% in 2024)
  - Dry bulk earnings down 31% YoY. LNG spot rates down 56% YoY
  - Company cited: tariffs, political tensions, weaker USD
  - Share price dropped 18-20% on results day (March 2025)
  - Revenue growth decelerating: 36.2% (2022), 5.9% (2023), 3.4% (2024), negative in H1 2025
- **Probabilidad:** Alta — already confirmed via profit warning. Question is whether trough is 2025 or if it extends to 2026
- **Impacto si materializa:** If FY2025 PBT comes in at GBP 85M (low end), EPS would be approximately 210-220p (vs 277p in FY2024). At current 4095p, the P/E on trough earnings would be ~19x. If shipping cycle worsens into 2026 (China further weakness, tariffs escalate), PBT could fall to GBP 70-80M, implying EPS 175-200p and P/E 20-23x on current price. That is expensive for a cyclical at trough.
- **Mitigante:** Clarkson has diversified beyond pure broking (Research and Support divisions are more stable). Forward Order Book of US$231M provides some visibility. H2 weighting is typical. Net cash means no balance sheet stress.
- **Kill condition?:** YES — if FY2025 PBT comes in below GBP 80M, or if FY2026 guidance suggests continued deterioration, the cyclical downturn is deeper than expected and the entry price needs significant downward revision.

### 2. HIGH: Staff Cost Rigidity Creates Negative Operating Leverage

- **Categoria:** Fundamental / Financiero
- **Descripcion:** Administrative expenses were GBP 526.0M in FY2024, representing approximately 79.5% of revenue. Staff costs (including broker bonuses) are the dominant component. In a people-intensive business where "brokers ARE the asset," there is a floor to cost reduction. When revenue declines (as in H1 2025: -4%), costs do not decline proportionally because base salaries are fixed and top brokers must be retained to prevent client departures. The bonus pool is variable but cannot go to zero without risking talent flight.
- **Evidencia:**
  - Admin expenses: GBP 526.0M = 79.5% of GBP 661.4M revenue (FY2024)
  - Operating margin declining: 16.7% (2022) to 15.3% (2024)
  - FCF margin declining sharply: 28.0% (2022) to 16.2% (2024)
  - OCF/NI ratio declining: 2.4x (2022) to 1.4x (2024) — cash conversion deteriorating
  - H1 2025 PBT fell 23.5% on only 4% revenue decline — classic negative operating leverage
  - UK employers' NI rises 1.2% in 2025, further pressuring costs
- **Probabilidad:** Alta — the cost structure is inherent to the business model
- **Impacto si materializa:** Every 5% revenue decline could produce 15-20% PBT decline due to operating leverage. In a severe shipping downturn (revenue -15-20% from peak), PBT could fall 40-50%, compressing EPS to 140-170p. At 4095p, that implies P/E of 24-29x — clearly overvalued.
- **Mitigante:** Bonus pool is partly variable (accrued bonuses are deducted from net cash, suggesting a meaningful variable component). Clarkson has demonstrated ability to manage costs through previous cycles. Research division (GBP 24.5M, 90%+ recurring) provides stable base.
- **Kill condition?:** NO — this is structural, not an event. But it amplifies kill condition #1 above.

### 3. HIGH: Tariff/Trade War Impact on Global Shipping Volumes

- **Categoria:** Geopolitico
- **Descripcion:** The April 2025 tariff announcements by the US government directly impacted Clarkson's business. The company explicitly cited tariffs in its profit warning. Additional risk from proposed fees on Chinese-built vessels entering US ports could structurally reduce trans-Pacific shipping volumes. Shipping rates are running 7% lower in dollar terms than anticipated.
- **Evidencia:**
  - Clarkson profit warning explicitly cited "US tariffs" and "changes in US government policy"
  - Shipping rates 7% lower in dollar terms than anticipated
  - USD weakness vs GBP reduces GBP-translated revenues (GBP 9.5M negative FX impact)
  - US-China tariffs at 10% with deal through Nov 2026, but could escalate
  - Proposed fees on Chinese-built vessels could disrupt trade patterns
  - 35% of brokers/carriers perceive digital platforms as threat (separate but compounding concern)
- **Probabilidad:** Media — tariffs are already in place but could moderate (deals proliferating) or escalate (China)
- **Impacto si materializa:** If tariffs escalate further (especially US-China above 10%), trans-Pacific volumes could decline 5-10%. Combined with already-weak seaborne trade growth (0.5%), this could push Clarkson's revenue into a deeper decline. GBP 9.5M FX impact alone = ~10% of estimated FY2025 PBT
- **Mitigante:** Tariff trend appears to be toward deals rather than escalation (Argentina, India, Bangladesh, EU Turnberry). Disruption can sometimes benefit brokers (rerouting creates more transactions). IMO regulations and sanctions create advisory fees.
- **Kill condition?:** YES — if proposed Chinese vessel fees are implemented broadly AND tariffs escalate above current levels, this changes the structural outlook for shipping volumes.

## Riesgos Adicionales Relevantes

### 4. MEDIUM: Key Person Risk (CEO + CFO Combined Tenure 38 Years)

- **Descripcion:** CEO Andi Case (since 2008, ~2% ownership) and CFO Jeff Woyda (since 2006, ~0.5% ownership) have run Clarkson for nearly two decades. The April 2025 insider data shows both selling shares (Case: 10K shares at 41.58p = GBP 400K; Woyda: 6K shares at 41.58p = GBP 255K) concurrent with option exercises. At the broker level, senior brokers who have built decades of relationships could leave and take clients. The annual report does NOT explicitly discuss key person risk or succession planning.
- **Probabilidad:** Media (departure within 3 years: ~30%)
- **Impacto:** Medio — disruption to operations but brand/platform persists
- **Mitigante:** 10.4% insider ownership overall suggests alignment. 53.7% institutional ownership provides governance. Clarkson's brand and data platform transcend any individual.

### 5. MEDIUM: Digital Disintermediation

- **Descripcion:** Digital freight platforms are growing (market projected to reach USD 8.6-66B by 2034-35). Signal Ocean specifically targets chartering decisions with SaaS tools. However, Signal Ocean's CEO has stated the platform is designed to "augment" not replace brokers.
- **Probabilidad:** Baja (within 5 years for deep sea shipbroking)
- **Impacto:** Alto (if materialized, could compress commissions 20-40%)
- **Mitigante:** Shipbroking is fundamentally different from road freight brokerage — vessels are unique assets, deals are complex multi-million dollar negotiations, relationships and trust are paramount. Clarkson ITSELF invests in digital (Research division with "millions of data points" is a competitive moat). The most likely outcome is digital tools enhancing incumbent brokers rather than disintermediating them. 1-1.25% commission rates have been "remarkably stable over time."

### 6. MEDIUM: Valuation Risk — Buying Mid-Cycle

- **Descripcion:** At 4095p with FY2024 EPS of 277p, P/E is 14.8x on peak-ish earnings. But FY2025 EPS is guided to approximately 210-240p, making the forward P/E 17-19.5x. For a cyclical business at the start of a downturn, this is not cheap. The 52wL of 2630p (based on the tariff shock) suggests the market sees significantly more downside risk. The scored entry of 3800p (from orchestrator input) implies only ~7% downside from current levels — insufficient for a business heading into a profit trough.
- **Probabilidad:** Media
- **Impacto:** Medio — dead money for 12-24 months if cycle extends
- **Mitigante:** QS 83 is legitimately high quality. Net cash of GBP 291M (22% of market cap). 22nd consecutive year of dividend increases. Through-cycle ROIC is exceptional (33-78%).

## Kill Conditions Sugeridas

1. **FY2025 PBT below GBP 80M** — Would indicate the downturn is deeper than management's own guidance and raises questions about whether the cycle trough is 2026, not 2025. At GBP 80M PBT, EPS would be approximately 200p, implying P/E of 20.5x at current price — too expensive for a cyclical trough.

2. **FY2026 guidance showing NO recovery** — If by H1 2025 results (August 2025) or FY2025 results (March 2026), management guides to flat or declining FY2026, this indicates a multi-year shipping downturn, not a single-year dip. The investment case requires earnings recovery.

3. **Senior broker departures at scale** — If 3+ senior brokers from top revenue-generating teams depart to competitors or digital platforms within 12 months, this signals talent drain that could impair the broking franchise.

4. **Chinese vessel fees implemented broadly** — Proposed US fees on Chinese-built vessels would structurally impair trans-Pacific trade volumes. If implemented without meaningful exemptions, revisit the structural volume outlook.

5. **Commission rate compression confirmed** — If industry data shows commission rates falling below 0.75% (vs current 1-1.25%), digital disintermediation is real. This would structurally reduce revenue per transaction.

## Riesgo Agregado

- **Numero de riesgos HIGH+CRITICAL:** 3 HIGH + 1 CRITICAL = 4
- **Riesgos correlacionados?** YES — risks #1 (shipping cycle), #3 (tariffs), and #2 (operating leverage) are highly correlated. A tariff-driven shipping downturn amplifies through operating leverage. If shipping volumes decline due to tariffs, ALL three risks compound simultaneously. China demand weakness (#9 in matrix) also correlates with #1.
- **Correlation amplification:** In a worst case (tariffs escalate + China weakens + USD weakens further), revenue could decline 10-15%, PBT could decline 30-45%, and the stock could trade to 2800-3200p (P/E 14-16x on trough EPS of 180-210p). This represents 22-32% downside from current 4095p.
- **Risk Score Final: HIGH**

## 🔄 META-REFLECTION

### Dudas/Incertidumbres
- **FCF margin decline — structural or cyclical?** The drop from 28% to 16% over 2 years is dramatic. If it is cyclical (lower bonus accruals in good years inflate FCF, then reverse), the risk is lower. If structural (growing headcount, office expansion, rising fixed costs), the risk is higher. I could not determine the precise split from available data.
- **Bonus pool mechanics:** I do not have the exact split between fixed salaries and variable bonuses. If bonuses are 40-50% of total staff costs, the operating leverage is partially mitigated in downturns (bonuses compress). If bonuses are only 20-30%, the fixed cost base is larger and the leverage is worse. The annual report mentions accrued bonuses as significant but does not quantify the percentage.
- **Where in the shipping cycle are we?** The data points to early-downturn (H1 2025 weaker, guidance lowered). But shipping cycles are notoriously hard to time. If Q4 2025 / Q1 2026 marks the trough, 4095p might be reasonable. If the trough is H2 2026, there is significant downside.
- **Insider selling context:** The CEO and CFO both sold shares in April 2025 concurrent with option exercises. This is likely exercise-and-sell (per our precedent with GL, Session 76), but the timing around a profit warning period deserves scrutiny. I could not determine if the sales occurred before or after the profit warning was communicated to the market.

### Riesgos que Podrian Estar Subestimados
- **Operating leverage severity.** The H1 2025 data (-4% revenue = -24% PBT) suggests operating leverage is more severe than typical quality businesses. A 10% revenue decline could produce 40-50% PBT decline. This risk may be underappreciated by anyone anchored to the QS 83 score.
- **Valuation on trough earnings.** At 4095p, the forward P/E on guided FY2025 earnings is 17-19.5x. For a cyclical business entering a downturn, this is NOT cheap. The QS 83 and ROIC 78% may create an illusion of safety that masks valuation risk on near-term earnings.
- **Inventory growth anomaly.** Narrative checker shows inventory growth of 30.3% vs revenue growth of 3.4% in FY2024. For a service business, inventory should be minimal. This could relate to Financial division (securities holdings) or Support division (port operations), but the divergence is anomalous and warrants investigation.

### Discrepancias con Thesis
- No thesis exists yet. However, the scored entry of 3800p (from orchestrator) implies an assumption that current levels offer reasonable value. My analysis suggests the appropriate entry may need to be significantly lower (2900-3200p range) to provide adequate margin of safety for a cyclical business entering a confirmed downturn.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema
1. **Cyclical business valuation protocol needed.** The current QS framework rewards ROIC, margins, and FCF — all of which can be peak-cycle metrics. For cyclical businesses like CKN.L, the system should flag when trailing metrics may be peak and require through-cycle normalization before entry decisions.
2. **Operating leverage stress test.** For any business with admin expenses >70% of revenue, the risk-identifier should automatically calculate the PBT impact of 5/10/15% revenue declines. This would have immediately flagged CKN.L's vulnerability.
3. **Profit warning database.** CKN.L issued a profit warning in 2025. The system should maintain a tracker of recent profit warnings for all universe/pipeline companies and flag them before entry decisions.

### Preguntas para Orchestrator
1. **Is the 3800p scored entry based on peak or trough earnings?** If peak, it needs significant downward revision given the FY2025 profit warning. If trough, what recovery assumption underpins it?
2. **Should the QS be adjusted downward for cyclicality?** QS 83 is based on trailing metrics that may be peak-cycle. Through-cycle ROIC is still exceptional (33-78% range), but FCF margin of 16% (and declining) vs the 28% that boosted the QS is worth discussing.
3. **UK concentration: CKN.L would be our 5th UK-listed position.** While Clarkson's revenue is global (shipbroking is inherently international), the listing currency (GBP) and regulatory jurisdiction (UK) add to our geographic concentration. Is this concern mitigated by the global revenue base?
4. **Timing: Should we wait for FY2025 results (March 2026) or H1 2025 results (August 2025) to confirm the trough?** Given the confirmed profit warning and shipping market weakness, patience seems warranted. Entering before earnings visibility improves risks catching a falling knife.
5. **Inventory anomaly: What is the 30.3% inventory growth?** This needs clarification from the annual report. If it relates to securities positions in the Financial division, it could represent mark-to-market risk.
