### 2. CCCA Legislative Risk (DA finding: 7/10 STRONG)

- **Calendar:** CCCA monitoring event added (June 2026).

### 3. Fair Value ($420 vs $385)
**SPLIT at $400.** DA's $385 uses more conservative growth (8-9%) and higher required OEY (4% vs 3.3%). R1's $420 uses 3 convergent methods. I weight R1 slightly more because:
- 3 methods converging at 2.7% divergence is strong
- DA's growth reduction is partially offset by VAS acceleration (25%+ YoY)
- But Wero and CCCA are genuine headwinds that R1 missed
- $400 = ~5% reduction from R1, reasonable given MODERATE counter.

### 4. Entry Price ($390 vs $340-360)
**RESOLVED at $315 (existing standing order).**
- $390 gives only 7.1% MoS — below ALL precedents (minimum was 18.4% for MMC)
- $340-360 gives 10-15% MoS — still below most Tier A precedents (29-38%)

- The existing standing order was set pre-pipeline but happens to be the right number

### 5. V vs MA Priority (DA recommendation)
