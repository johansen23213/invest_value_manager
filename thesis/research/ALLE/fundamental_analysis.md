## Business Understanding

### What Allegion Does

Allegion is a pure-play security company spun off from Ingersoll Rand in 2013. It designs, manufactures, and sells mechanical locks, electronic locks, smart locks, door closers, exit devices, access control systems, and related security products.

**Key brands:**
- **Schlage** (residential + commercial locks) -- #1 US residential lock brand
- **Von Duprin** (exit devices) -- de facto standard in commercial/institutional
- **LCN** (door closers) -- market-leading position
- **CISA** (European locks) -- leading Italian/European brand
- **Interflex** (workforce management) -- European focus
- **Stanley Security (access technologies)** -- acquired 2023 for ~$900M

## Competitive Position and Moat

### Moat Sources (Estimated: 20-22/25)

**1. Brand Intangible Assets (Strong)**
- Schlage: #1 most trusted lock brand in America for 7 consecutive years
- Von Duprin: de facto specification standard for exit devices in commercial buildings
- LCN: dominant door closer brand
- Brands are specified by architects and building code requirements -- once specified, competitors cannot easily substitute

**2. Switching Costs (Strong)**
- Commercial/institutional buildings designed around specific hardware standards
- Access control systems integrate with building management -- rip-and-replace is expensive and disruptive
- Code compliance: buildings must meet fire/safety codes that often name-specify products (Von Duprin in exit devices)
- Installed base creates aftermarket lock-in: ~50% of revenue from retrofit/replacement

**3. Distribution Network (Moderate-Strong)**
- Decades-long relationships with architects, contractors, security integrators, locksmiths
- Channel not replicable by new entrants without massive time and investment
- Allegion trains locksmiths and contractors on their products -- creates human capital lock-in

**4. Regulatory Moat (Moderate)**
- Fire codes, ADA requirements, and building safety standards mandate certified products
- Certification process is multi-year, creating entry barriers
- Post-COVID, institutional security standards have become stricter

### Moat Assessment: WIDE (20-22/25)
- GM Premium vs sector: +16.2pp (sector median 28%, ALLE 44.2%) = 10/10
- Market Position: #1-2 US in locks and exit devices = 8/8
- ROIC Persistence: 4/4 years above WACC, trajectory shows 20%+ consistently = 7/7
