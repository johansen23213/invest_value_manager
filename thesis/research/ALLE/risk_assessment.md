## Top 3 Riesgos Criticos

### 1. Mexico Tariff Exposure -- Material and Under-Discussed

- **Categoria:** Macro / Geopolitico
- **Descripcion:** Allegion operates 4 manufacturing plants in Mexico (Ensenada, Tijuana, Tecate, Queretaro) employing 2,300+ workers. These facilities produce core Schlage, Falcon, Von Duprin, and Dexter products -- many of which are Allegion's flagship brands for the U.S. market. A 25% tariff on Mexican imports outside USMCA compliance would materially impact COGS.
- **Evidencia:** Allegion's own locations page confirms Ensenada (opened 1995, 1,300 employees), Tecate (opened 1984, 500 employees), Tijuana, and Queretaro facilities. Management on Q4 call mentioned "list price increases" for 2026 but was notably vague on tariff dollar impact. The CFO did not disclose specific tariff headwind quantification for 2026.
- **Probabilidad:** Media (40-50%) -- USMCA compliance for locks is likely (high North American content), but the regulatory environment is volatile. Admin has shown willingness to apply tariffs broadly. USMCA review scheduled for 2026.
- **Impacto si materializa:** HIGH. If 25% tariff applies to even 30% of Mexico-sourced products, gross margin impact could be 200-400bp before pricing offsets. At current 44.2% gross margin, this takes margin to ~40-42%. Pricing pass-through is possible but creates volume risk in a soft residential market. Estimated stock impact: -15% to -25% if tariffs stick without full pass-through.
- **Mitigante:** USMCA compliance likely for lock products (high regional value content). Allegion has 15+ U.S. manufacturing facilities as potential production shift destinations. Management demonstrated pricing discipline historically (+price offset volume declines). But reshoring takes 12-24 months minimum.
- **Kill condition?:** YES -- If 25% Mexico tariff is confirmed applying to Allegion products AND management cannot demonstrate >80% pass-through within 2 quarters, reassess entire thesis.

### 2. Residential Demand Structural Decline Masking as Cyclical

- **Categoria:** Fundamental / Macro
- **Descripcion:** U.S. residential declined high-single-digits organically in Q4 2025 and management guided "down slightly" for 2026. The question is whether this is cyclical (housing affordability with rates at 3.5-3.75% Fed Funds) or structural (demographic shift, smaller households, fewer single-family homes, declining homeownership among millennials). If structural, a significant growth engine is permanently impaired.
- **Evidencia:** Q4 2025 residential down high-single-digits. Management offered NO upside catalyst visibility for residential recovery. Housing starts remain depressed vs. pre-COVID norms. Affordability crisis persists with mortgage rates still elevated. Management stated residential "ended the year softer than we had contemplated" -- suggesting they were surprised by the weakness.
- **Probabilidad:** Media (35-45%) for structural component. Pure cyclical recovery depends on Fed cuts (first expected June 2026). But even with cuts, housing affordability remains structurally challenged.
- **Impacto si materializa:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Residential is approximately 25-30% of Americas revenue. If residential is flat-to-declining for 3+ years instead of recovering, this removes ~2-3% from organic growth trajectory, reducing total company organic growth to 1-3% (from guided 2-4%). This fundamentally changes the growth profile from "steady compounder" to "low-growth industrial." Estimated stock impact: -10% to -15% as market re-rates growth multiple downward.
- **Mitigante:** Aftermarket/retrofit demand is less cyclical than new construction. Electronics penetration in residential creates upgrade cycle. Price increases have historically offset volume declines. Commercial/institutional non-residential (~70% of Americas) remains healthy.
- **Kill condition?:** YES -- If residential organic revenue declines exceed -10% for 2 consecutive quarters with no pricing offset, AND new construction starts show no recovery signal, reassess residential thesis entirely.

### 3. Overvaluation Risk: Market Pricing 15.3% FCF Growth vs 9.6% Historical

- **Categoria:** Valoracion
- **Descripcion:** The reverse DCF at $162.67 implies the market expects 15.3% annual FCF growth over 5 years. Allegion's historical FCF CAGR is 9.6%. The gap is 5.7 percentage points. At historical growth rates, fair value is approximately $125 -- meaning the stock is 23% OVERVALUED at current price relative to what history suggests it can deliver.
- **Evidencia:** Reverse DCF tool output: implied 15.3% vs historical 9.6%. At historical rate, FV = $125. 2026 EPS guidance midpoint $8.80 below consensus. Q4 EPS missed ($1.94 vs $1.98-2.01). Organic growth decelerating (3.3% Q4 vs 9.6% 3yr avg). Revenue growth 2024 was only 3.3% (vs 14.1% in 2022). PEG ratio 3.28 (expensive for a mid-single-digit grower).
- **Probabilidad:** Alta (60-70%) that the market is overpricing future growth. The stock trades at 22x trailing P/E and ~18.5x forward P/E for a company guiding 2-4% organic growth and ~8% EPS growth.
- **Impacto si materializa:** MEDIUM. If market re-rates to a more appropriate 16-18x forward P/E (consistent with 2-4% organic growth industrial), the stock trades at $140-$158. From entry at $155, downside is -3% to -10%. Not catastrophic, but means the "margin of safety" at $155 is thin or non-existent.
- **Mitigante:** Electronics growth (double-digit) could accelerate overall growth above historical. M&A adds 3-5% to reported growth. FCF conversion is excellent (85-95% of adj net income). Share repurchases reduce share count ~1% annually. Dividend growing 8%/year.
- **Kill condition?:** NO -- but suggests the entry target of $155 needs to be lowered to $130-140 to provide genuine margin of safety given the growth gap.

## Kill Conditions Sugeridas

Based on risk identification, the following kill conditions should be included in any ALLE thesis:

1. **KC#1: Mexico Tariff Confirmation** -- If 25% tariff confirmed applying to Allegion Mexico-produced products AND management cannot demonstrate >80% pricing pass-through within 2 quarters.

2. **KC#2: Residential Decline Acceleration** -- If Americas residential organic revenue declines exceed -10% for 2 consecutive quarters with no recovery signal in housing starts.

3. **KC#3: Organic Growth Below Inflation** -- If total organic revenue growth falls below 1% for 2 consecutive quarters (meaning real volume decline across ALL segments, not just residential).

4. **KC#4: Smart Lock Market Share Loss** -- If Allegion's share of the U.S. smart lock market drops below 20% (current ~30% estimated) as measured by unit shipments, indicating tech platforms are winning the channel.

5. **KC#5: M&A Leverage Escalation** -- If Net Debt/EBITDA exceeds 3.0x due to acquisition spending without commensurate ROIC contribution from acquired businesses within 24 months.

6. **KC#6: Electronics Growth Stalls** -- If electronics growth drops to low-single-digits for 2 consecutive quarters, removing the key secular growth driver that justifies premium valuation.

7. **KC#7: Gross Margin Compression** -- If gross margin drops below 40% (from current 44.2%), indicating loss of pricing power or tariff absorption without pass-through.

## Riesgo Agregado

- **Numero de riesgos HIGH+CRITICAL:** 4 (Mexico tariffs, residential decline, organic growth deceleration, overvaluation gap)
- **Riesgos correlacionados?** YES -- Risks #1 (tariffs), #2 (residential decline), and #3 (overvaluation) are POSITIVELY CORRELATED. If tariffs hit AND residential stays weak, organic growth decelerates further, which makes the overvaluation gap WIDER. All three would compound simultaneously in a scenario where tariffs stick and housing doesn't recover. This correlation pattern means the "reasonable bear case" is worse than the sum of individual risks.
- **Short interest rising** (+15.7% MoM to 3.7% of float) is a confirming signal that informed investors see similar risk clustering.
- **Risk Score Final: MEDIUM**

Rationale for MEDIUM (not HIGH): Despite 4 HIGH risks, important mitigants exist. The company has 1.6x leverage (conservative), 44.2% gross margin (substantial buffer), strong FCF generation ($583M, 15.5% FCF margin), and demonstrated pricing power. The commercial/institutional segment (~70% of Americas) provides stability. The MEDIUM rating acknowledges these are REAL risks that require vigilance, not reasons to avoid the company entirely, but the entry price of $155 does NOT provide sufficient margin of safety given the risk profile. An entry at $130-140 would move the risk-reward to FAVORABLE.

## 🔄 META-REFLECTION

### Dudas/Incertidumbres
- **Mexico tariff quantification**: I could not find the specific percentage of Allegion's COGS sourced from Mexico plants. The 4 plants and 2,300+ employees suggest it is material (perhaps 20-40% of U.S. volume for key brands), but without the exact manufacturing split, the tariff impact estimate (200-400bp gross margin) is a range, not precision. The Q4 earnings call was notably silent on tariff quantification, which itself is a red flag -- management may be avoiding the topic because the exposure is larger than investors realize.
- **Smart lock market share data**: I could not find precise market share data for Allegion vs Assa Abloy vs tech platforms. The "60% combined" figure for traditional players is from a market research report (Nivel 2 source), not verified primary data. Actual share dynamics may differ.
- **Residential mix accuracy**: I estimated residential at 25-30% of Americas based on the segment commentary (residential down high-single-digits, total Americas organic +4.8%). The exact split is not publicly broken out in granular detail.

### Riesgos que Podrian Estar Subestimados
- **Mexico tariff risk**: I rated this Media probability, but given the current administration's tariff escalation pattern AND the USMCA review in 2026, this could be ALTA probability. If Allegion has significant non-USMCA-compliant inputs sourced through Mexico, exposure could be worse than estimated.
- **Rising short interest**: The 15.7% MoM increase in short interest is a directional signal I rated as confirmatory. But rising short interest on a post-earnings drop of 8.4% could indicate informed sellers positioning for further downside from tariff announcements or continued residential weakness.

### Discrepancias con Thesis
- No thesis exists yet. However, the entry target of $155 warrants scrutiny. At $155, the stock is still 24% ABOVE the reverse-DCF historical-growth fair value of $125. This means the "margin of safety" at $155 is actually NEGATIVE if growth reverts to historical norms. The fundamental analyst should reconcile this gap.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema
- **Add tariff exposure to standard risk-identifier checklist**: For any company with significant manufacturing outside the U.S. (Mexico, China, EU), tariff exposure should be a mandatory Phase 3 check with plant-level detail.
- **Rising short interest threshold**: Consider adding a standard check: if short interest rises >10% MoM, flag as confirmatory risk signal.
- **Reverse DCF gap as standard gate**: If implied growth exceeds historical growth by >5pp, require explicit reconciliation in the thesis of WHY the market's optimism is justified.

### Preguntas para Orchestrator
1. Has the fundamental analyst accounted for Mexico tariff exposure in their revenue/margin projections? If yes, what assumptions did they use for USMCA compliance and pricing pass-through?
2. The entry target of $155 implies the analyst believes ALLE can grow FCF at ~13%+ annually. What is the specific driver model for this acceleration vs 9.6% historical? Is it electronics growth alone?
3. Should we lower the entry target to $130-140 given the reverse DCF gap and the 4 correlated HIGH risks?
