## Resolution Summary

R2 DA produced MODERATE COUNTER with 3 HIGH-severity challenges. After reviewing both sides, I resolve as follows:

### 1. ROIC: 23% (tool) vs 15.4% (company-reported)

**Resolution: Use 15.4% as primary figure.**

Both are methodologically valid:
- Tool's 23%: Strips goodwill from invested capital (standard for comps)
- Company's 15.4%: Includes goodwill (more honest for capital actually deployed via M&A)

For RELX with 54% of assets as goodwill, the company figure is the more conservative and honest measure. The ROIC spread narrows from +18pp to +8pp over WACC — still firmly positive, still Tier A territory, but the "exceptional compounder" narrative weakens to "high-quality business with good returns."

**Impact:** Narrative changes, but QS 78 maintained because ROIC persistence scored independently (7/7) and financial category (35/40) isn't driven solely by absolute ROIC level.

### 2. AI: Tailwind vs Dual-Edged

**Resolution: Net positive with meaningful transition risks.**

Points where R1 is right:
- RELX's proprietary data IS irreplaceable (138B legal docs, 90M scientific records)
- AI models need CLEAN, STRUCTURED, AUTHORITATIVE data — RELX provides this
- Legal segment grew 9% FY2025, accelerating from historical 5-6%
- Lexis+ AI driving double-digit growth in law firms subsegment

Points where DA is right:
- Harvey partnership is DEFENSIVE — LexisNexis conceded it cannot build AI layer independently
- RELX is a DATA PROVIDER to AI, not an AI company
- Long-term, value may accrue more to AI layer (Harvey, Claude) than data layer
- Per-seat pricing erosion IS a risk as AI agents reduce human researcher seats
- Each new AI tool announcement (Anthropic, OpenAI) creates fresh selloff pressure

**My view:** AI is 60/40 positive for RELX. The data moat is real and gets MORE valuable as AI adoption grows. But the margin on HOW RELX captures that value is uncertain. Best case: RELX becomes the "API for authoritative data" with usage-based pricing. Worst case: AI intermediaries (Harvey, Claude) commoditize access to RELX data. Most likely: RELX retains pricing power on data but shares economics with AI layer.

### 4. Valuation Resolution

**Resolved FV: EUR 30.00**

| Input | R1 Thesis | DA | Resolved |
|-------|-----------|-----|----------|
| P/E Multiple | 24x | 20.5x | 22x |
| WACC | 7.5% | 8.5% | 8.0% |
| FCF Growth | 10% | 6% | 7.5% |
| Terminal Growth | 2.5% | 2.0% | 2.5% |
| Method 1 Weight | 60% OEY | 60% earnings | 70% earnings |
| Method 2 Weight | 40% DCF | 40% DCF | 30% DCF |

Rationale:
- **22x P/E:** Current market regime has de-rated info services. RELX at 21x current. 22x assumes minimal re-rating, justified by 7% growth + expanding margins + WIDE moat in 2 of 4 segments.
- **8.0% WACC:** Split between thesis (7.5%) and DA (8.5%). Tool's 9% too high for beta ~0.7, but thesis's 7.5% too low for current risk environment.
- **7.5% FCF growth:** FY2025 delivered 9%, but H2 decelerated. 7.5% is sustainable mid-cycle.
- **70/30 method weighting:** DCF sensitivity too high (TV 80%+ of EV). Earnings approach more reliable for stable compounders.

Calculation:
- Method 1: 128.5p * 22x = GBP 28.27 * 1.146 = EUR 32.40
- Method 2: ~EUR 24-26 range at 8.0% WACC / 7.5% growth
- Weighted: 0.70 * 32 + 0.30 * 25 = EUR 29.90 → **EUR 30.00**
