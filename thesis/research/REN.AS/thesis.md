## Business Understanding

### What RELX Does

RELX provides information-based analytics and decision tools for professional and business customers across four segments:

**1. Risk (largest, ~35% of revenue, ~40% of profit)**
- LexisNexis Risk Solutions: insurance underwriting, fraud detection, financial crime compliance, identity verification
- Customers: insurance companies, banks, government agencies, healthcare providers
- Revenue type: ~85% recurring (subscriptions + data feeds)
- Growth driver: regulatory compliance mandates, digital fraud increasing

**2. Scientific, Technical & Medical (STM, ~33% of revenue)**
- Elsevier: world's largest scientific publisher (2,900 journals, ScienceDirect, Scopus)
- Customers: universities, research institutions, pharmaceutical companies, governments
- Revenue type: ~70% subscription (journal subscriptions, database licenses)
- Growth driver: global R&D spending, open access transition (higher margins), AI tools

**3. Legal (~22% of revenue)**
- LexisNexis Legal: legal research, analytics, and workflow tools
- Customers: law firms, corporate legal departments, government
- Revenue type: ~80% subscription
- Growth driver: AI-enabled legal analytics (Lexis+ AI), double-digit growth in law firms subsegment (9% segment growth FY2025)

**4. Exhibitions (~10% of revenue)**
- Reed Exhibitions: organizes ~500 events per year in 30+ countries
- Revenue type: transactional (booth sales, sponsorships)
- Growth driver: post-COVID recovery, geographic expansion, digital integration

### Why Is It Cheap (46% Below 52-Week High)?

**Primary narrative: SaaSpocalypse / AI disruption fears**

The selloff from EUR 49.88 (52wH) to EUR 26.80 coincides with the broader software/information services selloff that began in late 2025. Anthropic's Claude Cowork and similar AI tools triggered fears that:
1. AI could disrupt Elsevier's publishing model (why pay for journal access if AI can synthesize research?)
2. AI could replace LexisNexis legal research (why search case law manually?)
3. Per-seat pricing models across information services are threatened

**My contra-thesis:**

The market is WRONG about RELX and AI. Here is why:

1. **RELX's data IS the input to AI.** LexisNexis has 138B legal documents. Scopus has 90M scientific records. Risk Solutions has billions of consumer and commercial data points. AI models need CLEAN, STRUCTURED, AUTHORITATIVE data -- RELX provides exactly that. Competitors cannot replicate decades of curated data.

2. **AI is a TAILWIND for RELX, demonstrated in results.** Legal segment grew 9% in FY2025, ACCELERATING. Lexis+ AI is driving double-digit growth in law firms subsegment. Risk Solutions using AI for fraud detection and identity verification. STM using AI for research discovery tools.

4. **RELX is the publisher, not the subscriber.** The SaaSpocalypse hits per-seat software consumers. RELX is a content/data OWNER. When AI agents need legal or scientific data, they query RELX databases. RELX can charge MORE (per-query or usage-based pricing) not less.

5. **Historical precedent:** Every wave of technology disruption (internet, digitization, mobile) was initially feared as a threat to RELX. In each case, RELX used the new technology to expand margins and TAM. FY2025 format: 84% electronic (up from ~50% in 2010).

## Meta-Reflection

### Incertidumbres/Dudas

- **Beta distortion:** yfinance reports beta 0.21 which is unrealistically low for a large-cap info services company. I used 0.7 as more representative, but this significantly affects WACC and therefore DCF. The true beta is uncertain. If beta is genuinely ~0.3 (very defensive), WACC could be 5.5%, making DCF FV much higher (~EUR 40+). If beta is 0.9 (more cyclical than assumed), WACC 8.5% makes FV ~EUR 24.
- **GBP/EUR translation:** RELX reports in GBP but is listed in EUR on Euronext and GBP on LSE. The FV is sensitive to the GBP/EUR rate. I used current rate 1.1435 GBP/EUR. A GBP weakening to 1.10 would reduce EUR FV by ~4%.
- **FY2025 data quality:** The quality_scorer.py used FY2024 data. FY2025 results (published Feb 12) show continued improvement (margins +0.9pp, FCF +9%). The tool's QS might be slightly conservative.
- **Goodwill at 54% of assets:** This is high but typical for information services companies. The underlying assets (databases, journals, brand) are not on the balance sheet at fair value. Not a concern for a business generating 23% ROIC, but worth monitoring.
- **Capex/Depreciation ratio 5.8x:** This is very high, suggesting heavy investment. It could be growth capex (content acquisition, platform development) which is positive, or it could signal maintenance capex that is not flowing through depreciation (negative). The 99% FCF conversion suggests it's manageable.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema

- **Tool improvement:** quality_scorer.py should auto-detect market position for companies with known dominant positions (e.g., use yfinance or hardcoded data for top-50 AEX/FTSE companies). The 0/8 default for market position mechanically underscores all companies.
- **WACC sensitivity:** The DCF tool should allow custom WACC input. Using 9% default for a company with beta 0.21 produces misleading results. `dcf_calculator.py --wacc 7.5` would be useful.
- **Multi-currency FV:** For dual-listed companies (REN.AS in EUR, REL.L in GBP), the thesis should explicitly calculate FV in both currencies and note FX sensitivity.

### Anomalias Detectadas

- **Insider tracker shows only company buybacks, no individual insider transactions.** This is unusual. Either there are no reportable insider buys/sells (possible for a widely-held FTSE 100/AEX company where executives have small stakes), or yfinance doesn't capture Euronext insider filings well. The 0.5% insider ownership is accurately low.
- **Analyst coverage anomaly:** Only 2 analysts covering REN.AS on yfinance. This seems very low for a EUR 48B company. The London listing (REL.L) likely has 15-20+ analysts. The REN.AS listing may have limited sell-side coverage.
- **52-week high EUR 49.88 but WebSearch data shows ADR 52wH $56.33.** At EUR/USD 1.18, $56.33 = EUR 47.74. Close to EUR 49.88 but not identical -- suggests the EUR 49.88 high was driven by EUR/USD movement (EUR was weaker when price was at high).
