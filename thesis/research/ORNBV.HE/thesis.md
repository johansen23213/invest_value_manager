## Business Understanding

### What Does Orion Do?

Orion Corporation is Finland's largest pharmaceutical company (~EUR 9.5B market cap), founded in 1917, headquartered in Espoo, Finland. It develops, manufactures, and markets human and veterinary pharmaceuticals plus active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs) through its subsidiary Fermion.

**Five Business Divisions (FY2025 Revenue = EUR 1,889.5M):**

| Division | Revenue 2025 | % Total | YoY Growth | Description |
|----------|-------------|---------|------------|-------------|
| Innovative Medicines | EUR 812.7M | 43% | +54.8% | Nubeqa royalties/product sales + Easyhaler respiratory |
| Branded Products | EUR 314.6M | 17% | +9.4% | Easyhaler portfolio, Dexdor, hospital specialty |
| Generics & Consumer Health | EUR 552.8M | 29% | +4.6% | Generic drugs in Nordics/Europe, OTC |
| Animal Health | EUR 140.9M | 7% | +9.9% | Veterinary pharmaceuticals |
| Fermion (API) | EUR 68.7M | 4% | -5.0% | Active pharmaceutical ingredient manufacturing |

### Revenue Model

Orion generates revenue through three primary mechanisms:

1. **Nubeqa Royalties from Bayer (~23% of revenue excluding milestone):** Orion discovered darolutamide, Bayer commercializes it globally. Orion receives ~20% royalty on Bayer's sales plus product sales (manufacturing). FY2025: EUR 432.5M in royalties + EUR 177.3M in product sales = EUR 609.8M total. This also included a EUR 180M one-time milestone payment.

2. **Proprietary Branded Products (~17% of revenue):** Easyhaler dry powder inhaler portfolio (budesonide, salbutamol, formoterol, beclometasone) for asthma/COPD. Peak annual sales potential >EUR 300M. Strong in Nordics, expanding in EU and Japan.

3. **Generics & Consumer Health (~29% of revenue):** Generic pharmaceuticals in Nordics and Eastern Europe. Stable, low-growth, competitive. ~1/3 domestic market share in Finland.

4. **Animal Health + Fermion API (~11% of revenue):** Veterinary medicines and API manufacturing for internal use and external customers.

### Unit Economics

- **Gross Margin: 64.2%** (expanding, was 55.3% in 2023) -- driven by Nubeqa royalty revenue (essentially 100% GM)
- **Operating Margin: 33.4%** (FY2025 including EUR 180M milestone; underlying ~24% ex-milestone)
- **FCF Margin: 11.6%** (modest -- working capital drag from growing receivables/inventory)
- **R&D: 11.1% of sales** (EUR 210.4M) -- investing in biologics Cambridge center, ODM-212 pipeline
- **Capex: 6.0% of sales** (EUR 112.9M) -- moderate, includes Fermion manufacturing
- **Capital intensity: MEDIUM** -- API manufacturing requires physical assets but core pharma business is asset-light
- **ROIC: 39.1%** (exceptional but inflated by Nubeqa royalties which require zero incremental capital)

### The Nubeqa Story (Critical to Understand)

Darolutamide/Nubeqa is a next-generation androgen receptor inhibitor for prostate cancer:
- Discovered by Orion in 2010-2011
- Partnered with Bayer in 2014 (Bayer paid USD 68M upfront)
- Bayer handles all late-stage development, regulatory filings, and global commercialization
- Orion manufactures API (via Fermion) and co-promotes in Europe
- **Royalty rate: ~20%** escalating with sales volume
- **Milestone payments: EUR 280M total** (EUR 30M received Q3 2023, EUR 70M Q3 2024, EUR 180M Q4 2025 -- ALL milestones now RECEIVED)

**Bayer's Nubeqa sales:** EUR 1.52B (2024), projected EUR 2.5-3B by 2027-2030 (peak sales EUR 3B+)

**Competitive advantage of Nubeqa vs enzalutamide (Xtandi) and apalutamide (Erleada):**
- Superior safety profile -- no CNS side effects (key differentiator)
- FDA approved in three indications: nmCRPC (2019), mHSPC (2022), mCSPC (2025)
- Additional Phase III trials ongoing (ARASTEP for biochemical recurrence, DASL-HiCaP for localized)

**Patent protection:** Earliest generic entry 2038; estimates suggest 2042 for full generic availability.

### Pipeline Beyond Nubeqa

| Drug | Phase | Indication | Partner | Notes |
|------|-------|------------|---------|-------|
| Opevesostat/MK-5684 | Phase III | mCRPC (prostate cancer) | MSD/Merck | CYP11A1 inhibitor. Global rights licensed to MSD (July 2024). Two Phase III trials (OMAHA-003, OMAHA-004). MSD expanding to breast/endometrial/ovarian cancer. |
| ODM-212 | Phase I/II | Solid tumors (rare cancers) | None | TEAD inhibitor. Phase 2 initiated Dec 2025. Earliest readout 2027-2028 |
| TNX-103 (Levosimendan) | Phase III | Pulmonary hypertension (HFpEF) | Tenax Therapeutics | LEVEL study ongoing |
| Darolutamide (ARASTEP) | Phase III | Biochemical recurrence prostate cancer | Bayer | New indication expansion |
| Darolutamide (DASL-HiCaP) | Phase III | Localized prostate cancer + radiotherapy | Bayer | New indication expansion |

**Assessment:** The pipeline is MODERATE. Opevesostat/MK-5684 is the most advanced post-Nubeqa asset, but MSD holds global rights -- Orion receives royalties only. ODM-212 is very early stage. The company is heavily dependent on Nubeqa for growth.

1. **KC#1: Nubeqa Bayer sales peak below EUR 2B** -- would indicate competitive pressure from enzalutamide/apalutamide successors or new mechanism drugs. Monitor Bayer quarterly reports.

## Risk Factors

### High Probability, High Impact
- **Nubeqa concentration risk:** 32% of revenue from single drug partnership. Orion does not control pricing, marketing, or commercialization decisions
- **Growth deceleration post-2030:** When Nubeqa nears peak, Orion lacks a replacement growth driver
- **Working capital deterioration:** Trade receivables +38% YoY, inventory buildup -- cash conversion is poor

### Medium Probability, High Impact
- **Bayer relationship risk:** Bayer could restructure, spin off pharma, or face financial issues that affect the partnership
- **Prostate cancer treatment paradigm shift:** New mechanisms (e.g., PARP inhibitors, radioligand therapy) could erode androgen receptor inhibitor market share
- **EUR/USD currency risk:** Nubeqa sales largely in USD (Bayer global), Orion reports in EUR

### Low Probability, High Impact
- **Nubeqa safety signal:** Any unexpected safety issue could devastate the franchise
- **Patent invalidation:** Early generic entry would collapse the royalty stream
- **Finnish/EU regulatory changes:** Drug pricing regulation in EU markets

### Specific Concerns
- **Wide 2026 guidance range:** EUR 550-750M operating profit (EUR 200M spread = ~30% uncertainty). Suggests management itself is uncertain about near-term trajectory
- **Low liquidity:** Helsinki-listed small-mid cap. Could experience violent moves on limited volume
- **Dual share class:** A-shares (10 votes) and B-shares (1 vote). B-shares trade more liquidly but have less voting power
