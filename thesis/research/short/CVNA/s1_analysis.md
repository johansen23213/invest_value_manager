## Section 1: Business Understanding

### 1.1 Business Model

**What Carvana does:** Online used car retailer. Buy, recondition, and sell used cars via website/app. Also provides financing, GAP coverage, and extended warranties. Operates ~20 inspection/reconditioning centers (IRCs) and a proprietary logistics network.

**Revenue model:**
- Retail vehicle sales: ~85% of revenue (transactional, not recurring)
- Wholesale vehicle sales: ~8%
- Other revenue (financing, warranties, related-party commissions): ~7%

**Unit Economics (reported):**
- Retail GPU (GAAP): ~$6,938-$7,400 per unit (Q1-Q3 2025)
- Retail GPU (non-GAAP): ~$7,140 per unit
- Total GPU: ~$7,400 per unit
- **vs CarMax retail GPU: ~$2,269** -- Carvana reports 54% HIGHER GPU than CarMax

**CRITICAL FLAG (Hindenburg):** Hindenburg alleges GPU is inflated ~34.5% by dumping $390M/yr in selling costs into SG&A rather than cost of sales. If true, Carvana's real GPU is closer to $4,800/unit -- still above CarMax but not the "turnaround miracle" narrative.

### 1.2 Market Position

- US used car TAM: ~$1 trillion (~40M units/year)
- Carvana market share: ~1-1.5% (up from <0.5% in 2020)
- Online penetration: ~2% of used car transactions
- **Position:** #1 online used car dealer, but tiny vs total market
- **Competition:** CarMax (#1 overall, $31B revenue), AutoNation, Vroom (bankrupt), traditional dealers (28,000+)

### 1.3 The Turnaround Narrative

The bull case rests on Carvana's "turnaround" story:
- 2022: Near bankruptcy ($-15.74 EPS, stock fell from $370 to $3.55)
- 2023: Debt restructuring, cost cuts, returned to profitability ($4.12 EPS)
- 2024-2025: Record unit sales, margin expansion, S&P 500 inclusion
- Stock: $3.55 (Dec 2022) to $487 (Jan 2026) -- a 137x rally

**My assessment:** The turnaround in OPERATIONS is real (cost cuts, margin improvement, unit growth). But the VALUATION is decoupled from reality. A 137x stock rally for a business that generates $827M FCF on a $77.9B market cap is not fundamentally supported.

### Incertidumbres/Dudas
- Q4 2025 results are being announced TODAY (Feb 18) after market close. This S1 analysis does not include those results. The earnings reaction could materially change the entry timing and thesis strength.
- The company's actual response to Gotham/Hindenburg at earnings call will be critical. If they provide transparent, audited refutation, the accounting fraud thesis weakens significantly.

- The WACC of 23.1% from quality_scorer.py uses CVNA's actual beta of 3.57, which is extreme. A normalized WACC of 9-10% is more appropriate for DCF. However, the high beta DOES reflect genuine risk.
- The operational turnaround (margin expansion, unit growth, FCF generation) is REAL based on primary financial data. The question is whether accounting manipulation inflates these metrics.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema
- **Sector view needed:** There is no "online auto retail" or "used car dealerships" sector view. The existing automotive.md covers EU premium OEMs, which is irrelevant to CVNA. For S2/S3, the orchestrator should consider whether a sector view is needed for shorts (Gate 0 applies to longs, not explicitly to shorts in current framework).
- **Short-specific narrative_checker enhancements:** The narrative_checker correctly flagged inventory > revenue growth and low interest coverage. But it would benefit from flagging related-party revenue as % of total (not captured currently).
- **insider_tracker.py limitation:** The tool shows exercise-and-sell transactions but does not clearly separate them from open-market sales. Per Session 76 precedent (GL), exercise-and-sell is less alarming than open-market. However, in CVNA's case, the PATTERN of $1.6B in sales with minimal buys is concerning regardless of transaction type.

### Anomalias Detectadas
- **OCF/Net Income ratio of 4.4x is very unusual.** This suggests heavy non-cash charges or adjustments. Worth investigating whether this is D&A related (acceptable) or loan gain recognition (suspicious). In most businesses, OCF/NI around 1.0-1.5x is normal. 4.4x deserves scrutiny.

- **Deferred revenue growth of 110% (from $30M to $63M).** While small in absolute terms, this rapid growth in deferred revenue could indicate pulling forward warranty recognition.
- **Institutional ownership of 104.8%.** This exceeds 100%, which technically shouldn't happen -- it indicates heavy stock lending for short selling. The real institutional long ownership is lower than reported.
