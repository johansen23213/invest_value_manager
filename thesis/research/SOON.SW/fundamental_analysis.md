## Business Understanding

### What Sonova Does

Sonova is the world's largest hearing aid company, operating across three segments:

1. **Hearing Instruments (87% of revenue):** Designs and manufactures hearing aids under Phonak (#1 brand globally), Unitron, and Sennheiser brands. Products range from premium custom-molded (Virto) to behind-the-ear (BTE) and receiver-in-canal (RIC) devices. The latest Infinio platform integrates AI-powered sound processing.

2. **Audiological Care (39% of revenue, overlapping with HI):** Operates ~3,500 hearing clinics worldwide (AudioNova, Boots Hearingcare, Connect Hearing, Geers, etc.). This is a vertical integration play -- Sonova sells its own hearing aids through its own retail network, capturing both wholesale and retail margins.

3. **Cochlear Implants (7% of revenue):** Through Advanced Bionics, manufactures cochlear implants for severe-to-profound hearing loss. #2 globally behind Cochlear Ltd. (Australia). CI market growing ~8-9% CAGR.

### Revenue Model

- **Mix:** ~60% hearing instruments (wholesale to independent audiologists), ~33% audiological care (retail/service), ~7% cochlear implants
- **Recurring:** Hearing aids are replaced every 4-6 years. Average selling price ~$2,000-4,000 per pair. Once fitted, patients develop brand loyalty (switching costs from refitting, audiologist relationship)
- **Geographic:** EMEA ~45%, Americas ~38%, APAC ~17%

### Unit Economics

- **Gross Margin: 72.0%** (stable, world-class for medical devices)
- **ROIC: 15.2%** vs WACC 10.0% = +5.2pp spread (value creation)
- **FCF Margin: 17.0%** (CHF 656M on CHF 3.9B revenue)
- **Capital intensity: Medium** -- Capex/Depreciation 0.6x (asset-light for medtech)
- **SBC/Revenue: 0.5%** (negligible dilution -- excellent)
- **R&D/Revenue: 7.5%** (increasing investment, positive for innovation moat)

### The Hearing Aid Market

**TAM:** ~$12B global (2025), growing 5-7% CAGR driven by:
- Aging population (WHO estimates 900M people will have hearing loss by 2050, up from 430M today)
- Massive under-penetration: only ~20% of people who need hearing aids use them
- Technology improvement (AI, Bluetooth connectivity) reducing stigma
- OTC regulation opening US market to mild-moderate hearing loss

**Oligopoly structure:** Top 5 players control 92.4% of the market:
1. **Sonova:** ~27% (Phonak, Unitron, Sennheiser)
2. **Demant:** ~24% (Oticon, Bernafon)
3. **WS Audiology:** ~19% (Signia, Widex)
4. **Starkey:** ~12% (privately held, US-focused)
5. **GN Store Nord:** ~10% (ReSound, struggling)

This is one of the tightest oligopolies in healthcare. Barriers to entry are very high: R&D in miniaturized electronics/AI, regulatory approvals (FDA/CE), distribution through audiologists, and brand trust in a medical device that requires professional fitting.

### Competitive Position

**Strengths:**
- Global #1 with broadest brand portfolio (Phonak premium, Unitron mid-market, Sennheiser consumer)
- Only hearing aid company with cochlear implant capability (Advanced Bionics) -- unique full-spectrum offering
- Largest audiological care retail network (~3,500 clinics) -- vertical integration advantage
- 17.6% insider ownership (family-controlled, long-term oriented)
- Swiss engineering quality reputation
- Infinio AI platform driving innovation cycle

**Weaknesses:**
- Lost Costco Kirkland Signature contract (Nov 2022) -- was exclusive supplier, now re-entering as branded (Sennheiser Sonite) in limited locations
- CHF strength hurts reported numbers (6pp headwind on sales, 13-14pp on EBITDA)
- Consumer Hearing segment declining (-11.6% in H1 FY2025/26)
- Cochlear implants facing China volume-based procurement pressure

## Why Is It Cheap?

### Narrativa del mercado

The stock is down 36% from its 52-week high of CHF 301 for several reasons:

1. **Costco contract loss (2022-2023):** Sonova lost the exclusive Kirkland Signature hearing aid contract at Costco (~10% of US devices sold). This was a meaningful revenue/margin hit. Partially recovered via Sennheiser Sonite re-entry in 2024, but in only 107 locations vs previous full coverage.

2. **FX headwinds (CHF strength):** Swiss franc has appreciated significantly. Sonova earns ~90% of revenue outside Switzerland but reports in CHF. H1 FY2025/26: +4.9% local currency growth but -1.0% in CHF. EBITDA: +16% local currency but only +1.6% in CHF. EPS CAGR looks like -4.5% but this is almost entirely FX.

3. **OTC disruption fears:** US FDA authorized OTC hearing aids in 2022 for mild-to-moderate hearing loss. Market fears commoditization and pricing pressure from low-cost entrants (Jabra, Bose, Apple). Reality: OTC has had limited uptake so far and primarily targets first-time, mild-loss users that traditional hearing aids missed.

4. **European medtech selloff:** Sector has underperformed Stoxx 600 for 4th time in 5 years. General risk-off for EU medtech.

5. **Consumer Hearing weakness:** Sennheiser-branded consumer products declining (-11.6% H1 FY2025/26) due to weak consumer electronics market.
