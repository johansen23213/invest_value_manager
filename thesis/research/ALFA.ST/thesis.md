## Business Understanding

### What Alfa Laval Does

Alfa Laval is a 140-year-old Swedish industrial company that provides three core technologies globally:

1. **Heat Transfer** (~40% of revenue): Plate heat exchangers (gasketed, brazed, welded, spiral) used in HVAC, district heating, data centers, food processing, chemicals, and energy. Global #1 in plate heat exchangers with ~15% market share.

2. **Separation** (~30% of revenue): High-speed centrifuges and separators for marine fuel treatment, biopharma, food & beverage, and wastewater. Global #1 in high-speed separators alongside GEA Group.

3. **Fluid Handling** (~30% of revenue): Pumps, valves, and hygienic processing systems for food, pharma, biotech. Leading position in sanitary/hygienic applications.

These technologies serve three divisions:

| Division | FY2025 Revenue Split | Q4 2025 Growth | Service % of Orders |
|----------|---------------------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Energy** | ~38% | +12% organic | 21% |
| **Food & Water** | ~30% | +1% organic | 31% |
| **Marine (Ocean)** | ~32% | +3% organic | 39% |

### Revenue Model

- **Equipment Sales** (~68%): One-time capital equipment purchases. Lumpy, cyclical for large projects.
- **Service & Parts** (~32%): Recurring maintenance, spare parts, refurbishment. Higher margin, more predictable. Growing as installed base expands and company pivots to "solution partner" model with digital monitoring.

### Why This Business is Exceptional

1. **Mission-critical products**: Heat exchangers and separators are essential infrastructure. A failed heat exchanger in a data center or LNG plant = catastrophic downtime.
2. **Installed base leverage**: 40+ manufacturing sites, 2000+ patents, presence in 100+ countries. The installed base creates a recurring service annuity.
3. **Energy transition tailwinds**: Products are required for heat pumps, data center cooling, LNG liquefaction, hydrogen production, carbon capture -- all structural growth areas.
4. **Margin expansion trajectory**: GM expanding from 33.8% (2022) to 36.2% (2025). Operating margin from 12.4% to 16.9%. This reflects pricing power and mix shift toward higher-value solutions.
5. **Strong balance sheet**: Net debt/EBITDA 1.0x, interest coverage 28.7x, Tetra Laval family 29.6% ownership provides stability.

### Geographic Exposure

Alfa Laval has true global diversification: Europe ~35%, Asia ~30%, Americas ~25%, Other ~10%. The Q4 2025 report showed particularly strong demand in HVAC (Europe, data centers) and energy (global).

### Management Quality

CEO Tom Erixon (since 2016) has delivered consistent margin expansion and the strategic pivot toward energy transition. The Tetra Laval family (29.6% ownership) provides long-term strategic stability -- this is NOT a management team optimizing for quarterly earnings. Dividend growing consistently (SEK 8.50 -> SEK 9.00 proposed for 2025). Payout ratio 42.5% is sustainable.

### Unit Economics

- **Capex/Revenue:** ~4% (asset-medium, with recent increase for capacity expansion in energy transition areas)
- **R&D/Revenue:** 2.5% (stable, focused on energy efficiency and digital solutions)
- **Working Capital:** Moderate consumer of cash; inventory build in 2025 (+7.9% vs revenue +4.1%) is a watchpoint
- **Capex/Depreciation:** 1.1x (2025) -- declining from 1.6x in 2023, suggesting investment cycle peaking

## Por Que Esta Cara (Why It is NOT Cheap)

### Narrativa del Mercado

Alfa Laval is trading near its 52-week high (SEK 522 vs SEK 530 high) at P/E 26.1x. The market recognizes:

1. **Energy transition beneficiary** -- data center cooling demand "more than doubled" in Q4 2025
2. **Margin expansion story** -- EBITA margin reached record 17.7% in FY2025
3. **Structural growth** -- heat pump market CAGR ~10%, LNG market expanding, hydrogen emerging
4. **Quality premium** -- ROIC 15.8% and improving, 29.6% insider ownership, 140-year track record
5. **Fives Cryogenics acquisition** -- expanded presence in cryogenic heat exchangers for LNG/hydrogen ($884M acquisition completed July 2025)

### Why I Agree With the Market (Partially)

The market is CORRECT that this is a high-quality business with structural tailwinds. The improving margin profile is real, not financial engineering. The energy transition exposure is genuine and long-duration. The insider ownership alignment is excellent.

### Why the Price is Too High NOW

1. **Order intake declining**: FY2025 order intake declined 6% (organic -6%), and Q4 specifically -8% (-2% organic). The order book is strong (8.9 months of sales) but new orders are slowing.
2. **P/E 26x for a business growing revenue 4-8%**: Even with margin expansion, the growth rate does not justify mid-20s P/E for an industrial company. Historical P/E range is 18-25x.
3. **FCF conversion dropped**: FCF fell from SEK 9.4B (2024) to SEK 6.5B (2025) -- partly acquisition-driven, partly working capital. OCF/NI dropped from 1.7x to 1.1x.
4. **Analyst consensus is neutral**: Mean PT SEK 507, median SEK 511 vs current SEK 522. 5 holds, 3 sells/strong sells out of 16 analysts. The stock is ABOVE consensus.
5. **Cyclical risk in Food & Water**: The Food & Water division grew only 1% in Q4, and large project orders have been weak.
