## Business Understanding

### Model de Negocio

**Problem solved:** Designing modern semiconductors is impossibly complex without software tools. A single advanced chip can have billions of transistors. EDA software automates the design, simulation, verification, and manufacturing preparation of these chips. Without CDNS/SNPS tools, no advanced chip gets designed -- period.

**Revenue model (2024: $4.64B):**

| Segment | ~Revenue Share | Growth | Characteristics |
|---------|---------------|--------|-----------------|
| **Core EDA** | ~55% | High single-digit | Subscription/time-based licenses. Most recurring. Design, simulation, verification. |
| **IP Licensing** | ~20% | 25-28% | Semiconductor IP blocks (Tensilica, interface IP). Mix of upfront and royalty. |
| **System Design & Analysis** | ~25% | 35-40% | CFD, structural analysis, electromagnetic simulation (Clarity, Celsius). Fastest growing. |

**Revenue characteristics:**
- ~80-85% recurring (subscription + maintenance)
- Multi-year contracts typical (3-5 year EDA agreements)
- Record backlog: $7.0B (1.3x TTM revenue)
- cRPO (current remaining performance obligations): $3.5B
- Revenue visibility is exceptional -- among the best in all software

**Customers:** Every major semiconductor company (NVIDIA, AMD, Intel, Qualcomm, Apple, Samsung, TSMC, Broadcom, etc.) plus hyperscalers designing custom chips (Google TPU, Amazon Graviton, Microsoft Maia).

### Unit Economics

- **CAC/LTV:** Essentially infinite LTV. Customer lifetimes span 20+ years. "Customers stop using Cadence when they go out of business, not when they switch vendors."
- **Net retention:** Near 100%+ (customer expansion through more seats, more products, and more IP)
- **GAAP FCF Margin (TTM):** 28.4%
- **SBC-adjusted FCF Margin (TTM):** 19.8%
- **Capital intensity:** Very low. Capex ~2.7% of revenue (2024). Asset-light model.

This is a mix shift, not competitive degradation. Non-GAAP operating margin is the better profitability measure and is clearly expanding from 33% to 44% guided for FY2025.

### Moat Analysis

**Moat rating: WIDE -- among the widest in technology**

| Moat Source | Strength | Evidence |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| **Switching costs** | Extreme (10/10) | Chip design flows take 2-3 years to qualify. Retraining engineers = massive cost. Companies standardize on one EDA vendor for decades. |
| **Duopoly structure** | Extreme (9/10) | CDNS + SNPS control ~70% of market. Siemens EDA (Mentor Graphics) is distant #3 at ~13%. No new entrant in 30+ years. |
| **IP and know-how** | Very High (9/10) | Decades of accumulated process knowledge. Thousands of patents. Foundry-certified design flows (TSMC, Samsung, Intel). |
| **Network effects** | High (7/10) | Entire ecosystem (PDKs, IP libraries, foundry certifications) built around CDNS/SNPS tools. Self-reinforcing. |
| **Customer captivity** | Extreme (10/10) | Engineers train for years on CDNS tools. Tool proficiency is a career skill. Switching = productivity collapse + time-to-market risk. |

**Key evidence:** CDNS has maintained ROIC > WACC for at least 10 consecutive years, with gross margins persistently above 86%. This is textbook wide moat.
