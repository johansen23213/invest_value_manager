## Business Understanding

### Model de Negocio

HEICO operates through two synergistic segments:

**Flight Support Group (FSG) -- ~70% of revenue:**
- World's largest independent provider of FAA-approved PMA (Parts Manufacturer Approval) replacement parts
- ~19,500 FAA-approved parts (post-Wencor), next competitor has <2,000
- Reverse-engineers OEM parts and sells at 15-40% discount to airlines and MROs
- Also provides MRO services and specialty distribution
- FY2025: Net sales $3.14B (+20.6% YoY), organic growth 16%

**Electronic Technologies Group (ETG) -- ~30% of revenue:**
- Niche electronic/electro-optical products for aerospace, defense, medical, telecom
- Higher-margin, more defense-exposed
- FY2025: Net sales $1.35B (+14.4% YoY)

**How it solves a problem:** Airlines need aftermarket parts to maintain aging fleets. OEMs charge monopoly pricing. HEICO provides FAA-equivalent quality at 15-40% lower cost. The value proposition is immediate and quantifiable -- airlines literally save money on every part purchased from HEICO vs OEM.

**Revenue type:** Repeat/consumable (parts wear out and need replacement on predictable maintenance cycles). NOT subscription, but highly recurring due to installed base.

### Unit Economics

- LTV/CAC: Not applicable in traditional sense -- HEICO's "customer acquisition" is earning PMA certification for each new part number. Once approved, the part generates recurring revenue for 20-30 years (life of aircraft program)
- Each new PMA part number costs $50K-$200K to certify and generates revenue indefinitely
- 500+ new PMA parts certified annually = expanding installed base
- FCF Margin: 19.2% (FY2025, improving from 13.5% in FY2023)
- Capital intensity: Medium (Capex/Depreciation 1.3x, Capex ~$75M on ~$4.5B revenue = 1.7%)

## Valuation

### Method 1 (Primary, 60%): Owner Earnings Yield + Reverse DCF

**Owner Earnings Calculation:**
```
FCF (FY2025): $861M
Depreciation: ~$146M (Capex/D&A 1.3x, Capex ~$75M, implies D&A ~$190M... let me use actual)
Maintenance Capex ~ D&A * 1.1 = ~$209M
Growth Capex = Total Capex $75M - but this is below D&A, so likely all capex is maintenance
Owner Earnings = FCF = $861M (since total capex < depreciation, FCF approximates OE)
Market Cap: $48.2B
OE Yield = $861M / $48.2B = 1.79%
```

OE Yield of 1.79% is LOW. For context:
- Expected growth: 10-13% CAGR
- OEY + Growth = 1.79% + 11% = 12.8%
- vs WACC 9.5% = positive spread of 3.3pp
- This is positive but NARROW for a company trading at 70x P/E

**Reverse DCF interpretation:**
- Implied FCF growth: 13.1% for 5 years
- My projection: 10-13% (reasonable range matches implied)
- **The market is pricing HEICO roughly fairly IF growth sustains at 13% for 5 years**
- If growth decelerates to 8-10%, the stock is overvalued
- If growth sustains at 15%+ (historical organic was 16%), there is modest upside

**OEY Fair Value at 3% target yield:**
```
FV = OE / Target Yield = $861M / 3.0% = $28.7B market cap
```

At 2.5% target yield (more generous for Tier A quality):

But paying 49x for a company growing revenue at 11% (normalized) is PEG ~3.8x which is historically expensive
