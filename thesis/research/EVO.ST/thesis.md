## 2. Moat Assessment: WIDE

**Evidence:**

1. **Network Effects + Switching Costs (Strong)**: Operators integrate Evolution's API deeply into their platforms. Switching requires months of technical work, re-certification in each jurisdiction, and risks player disruption. Once integrated, operators rarely switch. Evolution serves 600+ operators globally.

2. **Scale Advantages (Dominant)**: 60-80% live casino market share in Europe, ~90% in regulated US states. 300+ live tables, 300+ patents, 110+ new game titles per year (vs Playtech 166, Pragmatic 140). No competitor can match this production volume.

3. **Regulatory Moat**: Licensed in 20+ jurisdictions. Regulatory compliance is expensive and time-consuming -- a significant barrier to entry. Each new market requires local licensing, creating cumulative advantage.

4. **Brand/Quality**: Evolution's live dealer product is considered the gold standard. Operators NEED Evolution content to compete -- it is essentially "must-have" content, giving Evolution pricing power.

5. **Asset-Light Scalability**: Studios are relatively low-cost. Incremental margin on each new operator/player is extremely high. EBITDA margin 66% demonstrates this.

**Moat Threats**: Pragmatic Play is gaining share in LatAm/Asia with lower-cost offerings. Playtech has ~30% share. However, neither threatens Evolution's core European/US dominance in the near term.

**Verdict: Wide Moat** -- dominant market position, high switching costs, regulatory barriers, unmatched scale.

## 9. Autocritique (Critical Thinking Protocol)

**Biases detected**:
1. **Popularity bias**: Evolution is well-known in my training data. MITIGATED by using quantitative screening data and yfinance financials.
2. **Anchoring to historical growth**: 29% FCF CAGR is backward-looking. I used 6% forward growth (conservative) in DCF base case.
3. **Confirmation bias risk**: The valuation looks very attractive. I actively searched for bear arguments (UKGC, Asia decline, competition).

**Key assumption**: Revenue stabilizes and returns to low-single-digit growth. If Asia decline accelerates and Europe/US growth slows, base case FCF growth of 6% is too high. Conservative DCF at 3% still gives 942 SEK = 63% upside.

**What I might be wrong about**: The UKGC investigation could be more serious than market perception. If it triggers a cascade of regulatory reviews across the EU, the stock could be a value trap at these levels. The 3% UK revenue is misleading -- the reputational/license risk extends far beyond UK revenue contribution.

**Alternative considered**: Waiting for UKGC resolution before buying. However, the 42.6% MoS provides significant buffer, and the Feb 5 earnings may provide clarity.
