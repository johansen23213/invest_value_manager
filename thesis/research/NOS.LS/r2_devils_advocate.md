## Resumen Ejecutivo

The R1 thesis for NOS.LS survives moderate scrutiny but has three material weaknesses that were not adequately explored: (1) the FCF inflection from EUR 29M to EUR 446M is OVERSTATED because it includes ~EUR 86M from a non-recurring tower portfolio sale to Cellnex -- underlying FCF was EUR 359.9M and even that figure benefits from capex phasing that may not persist; (2) the Digi competition risk is MORE SEVERE than the R1 acknowledges, with evidence from Spain showing Digi capturing 10% market share within 4-5 years and causing contract ARPU declines of up to 11.6% for incumbents; and (3) at the current price of EUR 4.80 (essentially unchanged from R1's EUR 4.79), the stock is already ABOVE the analyst consensus mean target of EUR 4.05 by 18.5%, meaning we would be buying what the market professionals consider overvalued. The R1 verdict of WATCHLIST LOW PRIORITY was correct, and I find insufficient grounds to upgrade this. The E[CAGR] of ~12% remains below our 15% Tier B threshold.

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. FCF Inflection is Structural (EUR 29M to EUR 446M)

- **R1 claim:** FCF grew from EUR 29M (2021) to EUR 446M (2024), driven by capex decline as 5G/FTTH rollouts complete. The R1 correctly flagged this as "too good" and noted normalized FCF of EUR 300-350M, but still used the headline EUR 446M figure as evidence of dramatic improvement.

- **Evidence in contra:**
  - FY2024 TOTAL FCF was EUR 359.9M (per NOS's own earnings release), NOT EUR 446M. The EUR 446M figure appears to include proceeds from a non-recurring tower portfolio sale to Cellnex. The R1 used the wrong number.
  - NOS's own earnings release distinguishes "underlying FCF" (which grew EUR 117.4M YoY) from total FCF which includes the tower sale. The extraordinary dividend of EUR 0.05/share was explicitly linked to this non-recurring event.
  - EBITDA AL minus CAPEX (the operational FCF proxy) was EUR 272.2M in FY2024, which is a much more conservative and realistic measure of cash generation capacity.
  - Q3 2025 data: EBITDA AL minus CAPEX was EUR 97.2M for the quarter. Annualized, that is ~EUR 389M -- but Q4 tends to have higher capex due to investment timing. A more realistic FY2025 figure for this metric is EUR 340-380M.
  - The capex decline thesis is correct directionally (capex fell 3.9% in FY2024, Telco capex/revenue down 2.6pp to 21.4%), but the PACE of improvement may decelerate as the easy comparisons (peak 5G/FTTH spend) are behind us. Capex intensity at 21.4% is still HIGHER than European telecom average of ~16%.

- **Severidad:** **MODERATE** -- The R1 acknowledged the issue but used an incorrect headline number. The underlying story of FCF improvement is real, but the magnitude is overstated by ~25%. This matters because the DDM valuation and dividend sustainability arguments depend on FCF trajectory.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** Committee should use EUR 300-330M as normalized sustainable FCF, not EUR 446M. The capex intensity still needs to decline from 21.4% to sector-average 16% before the full FCF thesis materializes. This is a 2-3 year timeline, not imminent.

---

### 2. Digi Entry Risk is Manageable (R1: "SINGLE BIGGEST RISK")

- **R1 claim:** Digi entered Portugal Nov 2024, has 3%+ mobile market share and 813K subscribers. Kill condition set at >5% share AND ARPU decline >5% for 2 consecutive quarters.

- **Evidence in contra -- STRONGER THAN R1 ACKNOWLEDGED:**

  **Spain precedent (Digi entered 2008, became MVNO then MNO):**
  - Digi Spain captured ~10% market share in both mobile telephony AND broadband by 2025
  - Contract ARPU for Masmovil, Telefonica, and Vodafone all declined YoY in 2023 despite PRICE INCREASES. Masmovil saw -11.6% ARPU decline
  - MNOs lost combined contract market share of 1.9pp YoY to 2023
  - Digi grabbed 75% of all new fixed broadband subscriptions in November (one month)
  - Digi was accused of "predatory pricing" by local operators
  - Even Digi's own ARPU fell from EUR 8.7 to EUR 7.8 (-10%) in 2025 as it competed more aggressively

  **Portugal early signals (12 months into Digi entry):**
  - Digi already at 3.5% of mobile internet subscribers by Q2 2025 (ANACOM data)
  - Digi pricing: bundles starting at EUR 4 for 50GB -- this is RADICALLY below NOS pricing
  - Vodafone Portugal EXPLICITLY stated: "service revenue impacted by lower mobile ARPU following the launch of a fourth player"
  - ANACOM (Portuguese regulator) ACTIVELY ENCOURAGES Digi as fourth entrant
  - NOS's own B2C segment declined 1.2% in Q3 2025 "due to increased competition impacting RGU"
  - NOS launched "WOO" brand as low-cost fighter brand -- this is a DEFENSIVE move that typically cannibalize ARPU
  - NOS admitted "blended price in retail has declined" in Q3 2025

  **Romania precedent:**
  - In Romania where Digi IS the incumbent, prices are "significantly lower than in other countries in the EU while quality was very high"
  - EC study: countries with 4 MNOs have structurally lower ARPU than countries with 3 MNOs
  - Digi's playbook is consistent: underprice aggressively, build subscriber base, then gradually monetize

  **Timeline analysis:**
  - In Spain, Digi went from new entrant to ~10% market share over approximately 10-15 years
  - But with NOWO acquisition (130K fixed + 270K mobile pre-existing customers), Digi's Portugal ramp may be FASTER than Spain
  - Digi already has 2,130 5G sites in Portugal and its own spectrum licenses -- this is NOT an MVNO, this is a full MNO
  - The kill condition of >5% share + >5% ARPU decline for 2 consecutive quarters may already be on track: Digi is at 3.5% after 8 months and ARPU pressure is confirmed

- **Severidad:** **HIGH** -- The Digi risk is MORE advanced than the R1 acknowledges. The Spain precedent shows this is not a "maybe" but a "when and how much" question. The kill condition at >5% share could trigger within 12-18 months based on current trajectory. The ARPU impact on Portuguese incumbents is ALREADY confirmed by Vodafone and by NOS's own B2C decline.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** Committee should treat the bear case probability as HIGHER than 25%. I would argue 35-40% based on the Spain precedent and early evidence from Portugal. This significantly lowers the expected value calculation.

### 3. Fair Value EUR 5.99 is Defensible

- **R1 claim:** Weighted FV of EUR 5.99 (60% EV/EBITDA at EUR 5.74, 40% DDM at EUR 6.38).

- **Evidence in contra:**

  **EV/EBITDA (EUR 5.74 at 5.75x):**
  - The 5.75x multiple is REASONABLE for a European telecom. I do not challenge this materially.
  - However, the 2025E EBITDA of EUR 810M may be slightly optimistic. Q3 2025 annualized is ~EUR 890M, but this includes Claranet (which has much lower margins -- 7.5% EBITDA margin vs NOS core 45-49%). The consolidated EBITDA margin for Q3 2025 was 48.7%, but this was a single quarter. FY2024 was 45.3%. Using EUR 790-810M is not unreasonable, but using the lower end is more conservative.
  - The NET DEBT figure of EUR 1,720M used by R1 appears to be from Q3 2025 (post-Claranet, which added EUR 152M of debt-funded acquisition). FY2024 net debt was EUR 912.5M. I suspect the R1 is using a different net debt definition or including lease liabilities. This matters significantly -- if net debt is EUR 912M rather than EUR 1,720M, the equity value is EUR 800M HIGHER, which would mean FV around EUR 7.30. But if EUR 1,720M includes lease liabilities (which it should for telecom EV/EBITDA), then the R1 is correct. This needs clarification.

  **DDM (EUR 6.38 at Ke 8.1%, g1 4%, g2 2%):**
  - The 4% dividend growth rate for 5 years is QUESTIONABLE given:
    - Payout ratio already 70% on current dividends
    - FCF per share is currently ~EUR 0.70 (EUR 360M / 515M shares), so EUR 0.35 dividend is 50% of FCF -- looks sustainable
    - But if Digi compresses EBITDA by 5-10% and capex stays elevated for competitive response, FCF could decline
    - The "sustainable dividend growth" requires BOTH earnings growth AND stable payout. If EPS consensus is already declining (forward P/E 12.26 vs trailing 9.6x suggests EPS dropping from EUR 0.50 to ~EUR 0.39), then 4% dividend growth is ASPIRATIONAL not realistic
    - More realistic: 2% dividend growth (inflation indexation only) for 5 years, then 1.5% terminal
  - With 2% growth instead of 4%, DDM FV drops to:
    - D1 = EUR 0.357, PV Stage 1 = EUR 1.494
    - Terminal = D6/(Ke-g) = 0.394/(0.081-0.015) = EUR 5.97
    - PV Terminal = 5.97/1.081^5 = EUR 4.04
    - DDM FV = EUR 5.53 (vs R1's EUR 6.38)

  **Revised weighted FV:**
  - EV/EBITDA: EUR 5.74 (60%) = EUR 3.44
  - DDM revised: EUR 5.53 (40%) = EUR 2.21
  - Revised weighted FV: EUR 5.65

  **Analyst consensus as reality check:**
  - Mean target EUR 4.05, median EUR 4.00
  - HIGHEST analyst target: EUR 5.00 -- even the most bullish analyst is BELOW our R1 weighted FV of EUR 5.99
  - 4 out of 13 analysts have Sell/Strong Sell
  - The stock has already rallied 36% from 52-week low of EUR 3.51 -- much of the FCF improvement is PRICED IN

- **Severidad:** **MODERATE** -- The EV/EBITDA valuation is reasonable. The DDM is modestly aggressive on growth assumptions. The net debt figure needs verification. My revised FV of EUR 5.65 is 5.7% below R1's EUR 5.99, which is not a dramatic difference but narrows the MoS further.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** Committee should use EUR 5.50-5.75 as FV range rather than EUR 5.99 point estimate. At current price EUR 4.80, this gives MoS of 14.5-16.5% -- below the 20-25% Tier B precedent range.

### 4. Dividend Sustainability (7.2% Yield)

- **R1 claim:** EUR 0.35/share dividend is sustainable with 70% payout and improving FCF. S&P upgraded outlook to Positive.

- **Evidence in contra:**
  - The 7.2% yield at EUR 4.80 is calculated on the current price, but the ANALYST CONSENSUS price target is EUR 4.05, at which the yield would be 8.6%. High yields often signal that the market expects a cut.
  - FY2024 included EUR 0.05 extraordinary dividend (from tower sale). If the market is pricing in any expectation of continued extraordinary dividends, the base 7.2% yield overstates the sustainable yield.
  - Portugal GDP growth is modest (~1.5-2%), population stable at 10.4M. Revenue growth at 2% is essentially inflation pass-through, not real growth.
  - If Digi pressure forces NOS to invest more in network quality, marketing, or price cuts, the payout ratio could rise ABOVE 70%, making the dividend less sustainable.
  - NOS has NO BUYBACK program. Free float is 13-15%. The company cannot return capital via buybacks even if it wanted to (would reduce float further).

- **Severidad:** **LOW-MODERATE** -- The dividend is currently sustainable based on FY2024 numbers. But the thesis of "7.2% yield as primary investment attraction" is fragile because it depends on FCF NOT declining. If even one kill condition is triggered (Digi >5% share, ARPU decline), the dividend becomes the first casualty.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** Committee should note that the dividend yield, while attractive, is NOT a growth story. It is a bond-like return dependent on stable competitive conditions. The Digi entry disrupts that stability.

### 5. Claranet Acquisition is Accretive

- **R1 claim:** EUR 152M for EUR 205M revenue and EUR 15.4M EBITDA. Diversifies into IT/Cloud.

- **Evidence in contra:**
  - EBITDA margin of 7.5% (EUR 15.4M / EUR 205M) is dramatically below NOS core telecom EBITDA margin of 45-49%. This is MARGIN DILUTIVE.
  - At EUR 152M acquisition price, the implied EV/EBITDA is 9.9x -- a PREMIUM to NOS's own trading multiple of 5.5x. NOS paid MORE per unit of EBITDA for Claranet than the market values NOS itself.
  - IT integration: NOS stated "no expected integration costs" and Claranet will "continue to operate autonomously." This suggests NO SYNERGIES are expected -- it is a bolt-on with separate operations.
  - Q3 2025 IT business EBITDA grew 10.4% -- positive, but the IT segment is ~10% of revenue. Even 10% growth in IT adds less absolute EBITDA than 1% growth in the 77% telco segment.
  - Management narrative is "IT market (EUR 4.6B) is 4x larger than telco market (EUR 1.1B)." But NOS has NO competitive advantage in IT services against pure-play IT companies. This is diversification for growth's sake, not strategic moat extension.

- **Severidad:** **LOW** -- Claranet is not a BAD acquisition (reasonable price for a growing segment), but it is not the "transformation" the thesis implies. It is a small bolt-on with lower margins that will take years to become material.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** Committee should discount the Claranet contribution to FV. It adds revenue but dilutes margins. The IT market "opportunity" narrative is common among telecoms trying to diversify -- few succeed at it.
