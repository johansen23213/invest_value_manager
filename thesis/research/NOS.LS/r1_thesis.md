## Business Understanding

### Business Model

NOS is Portugal's #2 integrated telecom and entertainment operator, created from the 2013 merger of ZON (cable TV/broadband) and Optimus (mobile). It operates three segments:

1. **Telecommunications (~77% of revenue):** Fixed/mobile telephony, broadband internet, pay TV, and converged bundles (quad-play). 6.2M mobile voice customers, 1.6M broadband users, 1.7M pay-TV customers (~11.4M RGUs total).

2. **IT & Cloud (~10% of revenue):** ICT, cloud, data center, cybersecurity solutions for businesses. Strengthened by Claranet Portugal acquisition (completed March 2025 for EUR 152M, adding EUR 205M revenue and EUR 15.4M EBITDA).

3. **Audiovisual & Cinema (~5% of revenue):** Cinema exhibition (NOS Cinemas -- market leader in Portugal), content distribution, pay-TV rights. Volatile segment depending on blockbuster calendar.

**Revenue model:** ~80%+ recurring (subscription-based telco + IT contracts). The business is a classic European telecom with high capex requirements but improving FCF as 5G/FTTH deployment peaks.

### Competitive Position

Portugal is a small telecom market (population ~10.4M) dominated by three operators:
- **MEO (Altice Portugal):** #1 with ~36.9% mobile market share
- **NOS:** #2 with ~30.6% mobile market share
- **Vodafone Portugal:** #3 with ~28% mobile market share

These three control ~95.5% of the market. NOS leads in 5G deployment (4,786 sites) and claims the fastest speeds in Portugal with 93% 5G population coverage.

**NEW ENTRANT RISK -- DIGI:** Romanian low-cost operator Digi entered Portugal in November 2024 through the acquisition of NOWO (~130K fixed + 270K mobile customers). Digi is known for aggressive pricing in Romania, Spain, and Italy. As of Q1 2025, Digi had captured >3% of mobile internet subscriptions with 813K subscribers and 2,385 5G sites. ANACOM (Portuguese regulator) actively encourages this fourth entrant. This is the SINGLE BIGGEST RISK to the thesis.

### Method 2: Dividend Discount Model (DDM) -- 40% weight

Appropriate for NOS given:
- Stable, mature telco with predictable cashflows
- 7.2% yield, sustainable payout ratio (~70%)
- Dividend policy: EUR 0.35/share maintained

**Gordon Growth Model:**

```
D0 = EUR 0.35
g = sustainable dividend growth rate
Ke = cost of equity = 6.6% (from tool: Rf ~2.5% + Beta 0.38 * ERP 5.5% + illiquidity premium 2%)
```

Wait -- the tool calculates Ke = 6.6% with beta 0.38. This seems LOW for a small-market telecom with 13% free float. I add 1.5% illiquidity premium = **Ke 8.1%**.

**Sustainable dividend growth:** NOS is growing EBITDA at ~4-5% and reducing capex. But the payout ratio is already 70%. Sustainable dividend growth = (1 - payout) * ROE + organic EBITDA growth through payout = approximately 3-4% per year for the next 5 years, then stabilize at 2% terminal.

Two-stage DDM:

Stage 1 (Years 1-5): Dividend growth 4% per year
- D1 = EUR 0.364, D2 = 0.379, D3 = 0.394, D4 = 0.410, D5 = 0.426

Stage 2 (Terminal): Growth 2.0%

```
PV Stage 1 = 0.364/1.081 + 0.379/1.081^2 + 0.394/1.081^3 + 0.410/1.081^4 + 0.426/1.081^5
           = 0.337 + 0.324 + 0.311 + 0.299 + 0.288 = EUR 1.559

Terminal Value at end of Year 5 = D6 / (Ke - g) = (0.426 * 1.02) / (0.081 - 0.02) = 0.435 / 0.061 = EUR 7.12
PV of Terminal = 7.12 / 1.081^5 = 7.12 / 1.477 = EUR 4.82

DDM Fair Value = 1.559 + 4.82 = EUR 6.38
```

**DDM Fair Value: EUR 6.38 per share**
