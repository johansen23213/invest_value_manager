## Business Understanding

### What Does ASM Do?

ASM International designs and manufactures semiconductor process equipment, specifically **deposition equipment** used in chip fabrication. Their core technology is **Atomic Layer Deposition (ALD)** -- a technique that deposits materials one atomic layer at a time with extreme precision and conformality.

**Problem Solved:** Modern chips (sub-5nm nodes) require manufacturing steps with atomic-level precision. As transistor architectures evolve from FinFET to gate-all-around (GAA) and eventually to CFET, the number of ALD steps per chip INCREASES dramatically. GAA nanosheet transistors require the gate material to wrap completely around horizontal silicon channels -- only ALD can achieve the required conformality.

**Revenue Breakdown (FY2024):**
- Equipment sales: ~85% (ALD dominant, followed by Epitaxy/CVD)
- Spares & Services: ~15%

**End Markets:**
- Logic/Foundry (TSMC, Samsung, Intel): ~55-60% (primary growth driver)
- Memory (DRAM/NAND): ~25-30%
- Power/Analog/Other: ~10-15%

**Geographic Revenue (FY2024):**
- China: ~40% of equipment revenue (declining to low-to-high 20s% in 2025 due to export controls)
- Rest of Asia (Korea, Taiwan, Japan): ~40%
- US/Europe: ~20%

### Revenue Model

- **Equipment sales**: Large upfront purchases by semiconductor fabs ($1-10M per tool)
- **Spares & Services**: Recurring, growing, higher-margin
- **Cycle dependency**: Revenue is cyclical (tied to fab capex cycles) but with a strong secular growth overlay from advanced node transitions

### Unit Economics / Capital Intensity

## Competitive Moat Assessment

### Moat Sources

**1. Technology / IP Leadership (STRONG)**
- 2,800+ active patents covering ALD chemical delivery, plasma processing, deposition innovations
- First-mover advantage in single-wafer ALD with 55%+ market share
- The geometric complexity of GAA transistors (wrapping gates around nanosheets) requires conformality that ONLY ALD provides at scale
- Each new node generation INCREASES the number of ALD steps required

**2. Switching Costs (VERY STRONG)**
- 12-24 month qualification cycles at semiconductor fabs
- Once a tool is qualified for a process step, fabs almost NEVER switch mid-node
- The "recipe" (process parameters) is developed jointly with the fab -- deeply embedded
- Risk of switching: yield loss during re-qualification could cost fabs $100M+ per production line

**3. Installed Base / Recurring Revenue (MODERATE)**
- Spares & services growing, but still only ~15% of revenue
- Not as strong as ASML's installed base moat (ASML has 4,000+ active tools globally)
- Growing as installed base expands with GAA ramp

**4. R&D Scale (STRONG)**
- EUR 370M/year R&D budget creates barrier for new entrants
- Cumulative know-how in ALD process chemistry built over decades
- Collaboration with leading-edge fabs (TSMC, Samsung) on next-generation processes
- Chinese competitors (NAURA) are 2-3 generations behind in ALD

### Moat Width Assessment: WIDE (but narrower than ASML)

- ASML has a true monopoly (100% EUV). ASM has near-monopoly in single-wafer ALD (55%+) but AMAT and TEL can compete in broader deposition
- The moat WIDENS with each new node generation (more ALD steps, more switching cost accumulation)
- Potential moat risk: If deposition technology shifts away from ALD toward a fundamentally different approach (low probability in 10-year horizon)

### Method 3: Owner Earnings Yield (Tier A method) -- Weight 20%

Owner Earnings Yield = EUR 533M / EUR 34.7B market cap = **1.54%**

OEY + Expected Growth = 1.54% + 12% sustainable growth = **13.5%**
vs WACC 10.8%

The spread of 2.7pp (13.5% - 10.8%) is POSITIVE but THIN. For a Tier A compounder, I would want OEY + Growth > WACC by 5pp+ (i.e., >15.8%) to feel comfortable. At current price, the return above cost of capital is marginal.

- If fabs defer spending, ASM revenue could decline 20-30% in a single year

- The bull thesis depends heavily on GAA driving ALD content per wafer
- If GAA adoption is slower than expected (Intel delays, yield issues), the revenue ramp could disappoint
- This is timing risk more than structural risk -- GAA will happen eventually
- **Impact**: Revenue growth 8% instead of 12% CAGR

- Any miss on growth, margins, or bookings will be punished severely
- If the market re-rates from 44.5x to 30x (still premium), stock drops to EUR 480

- China revenue dependency (40% in FY2024) is a structural risk that the market may be underpricing given the current geopolitical trajectory of increasing decoupling.

- The quality_scorer.py "Technology" sector median for gross margin (55%) is inappropriate for semiconductor equipment companies (where 47-52% is excellent). A sub-sector median would be more accurate. However, this is a systemic issue -- fixing it for one sub-sector means the tool needs sub-sector intelligence.
- The DCF calculator using a default 5-year projection period is too short for a company like ASM where the growth story extends to 2030. A 10-year option would be more appropriate. The tool does have --years but the default should perhaps be configurable.
