# Moat Assessment: ITRK.L (Intertek Group plc)

## Fecha: 2026-02-18

## Clasificacion: NARROW (strong, approaching WIDE)

Intertek possesses a durable competitive advantage rooted in regulatory-driven switching costs, intangible assets (accreditations and brand trust), and moderate cost advantage from global scale. The moat is real, well-evidenced, and strengthening under the AAA strategy. However, the classification falls short of WIDE because: (a) only 4 years of ROIC data are available from tools (though all years show strong spreads), (b) organic growth of ~4.5% LFL trails the sector leader BVI.PA (6-10%), and (c) the industry's fragmented structure below the top tier constrains pricing power. Intertek is the strongest NARROW moat I have assessed in the TIC sector -- stronger than BVI.PA on margins and ROIC, but weaker on organic growth trajectory.

### Key Quantitative Observations

1. **Intertek has the HIGHEST operating margin** among the Big 3 at 17.4% (FY2024), with a clear trajectory toward 18.5%+ under the AAA strategy. This is the strongest quantitative evidence of cost advantage/operational excellence.

2. **ROIC trajectory is accelerating:** 15.3% (2022) to 22.5% (H1 2025), a +720bps improvement in 3 years. This suggests the AAA strategy is generating real economic value above cost of capital.

3. **Organic growth (~4.5% LFL) is the weakest among the Big 3.** BVI delivers 6-10% organic, SGS ~5-6%. Intertek's LFL growth is solid in absolute terms but trails peers. The Consumer Products division (7.9% H1 2025 LFL) and Corporate Assurance (8.2%) are strong, but Industry and World of Energy are lagging.

4. **FCF is consistent and improving:** GBP 418M-462M range over 4 years, with 118% cash conversion in H1 2025. FCF margin of 13.6% is comparable to peers.

5. **Low leverage (1.5x ND/EBITDA, down to 1.0x in H1 2025)** gives significant M&A and capital return firepower.

### Detailed Threat Analysis

**1. AI/Automation (Probability: Low-Medium, Impact: Medium, 5-10yr)**
AI is more enabler than disruptor for TIC incumbents. Intertek introduced AI-enabled inspection software in 2023, cutting test turnaround by 22%. Regulation requires human expert oversight for most certifications. The Big 3 are deploying AI, not being disrupted by it. However, if regulatory bodies eventually accept fully automated inspections, barriers to entry collapse. Monitoring: changes to ISO/IEC standards regarding automation, new tech entrants gaining accreditations.

**2. US Deregulation (Probability: Low-Medium, Impact: Medium, 2-5yr)**
The current US administration has deregulatory instincts. If mandatory testing requirements are relaxed in the US, ~38.5% of Intertek's revenue (Americas) could face headwinds as some testing becomes voluntary. Mitigant: most TIC demand is driven by customer/liability requirements (product liability law), not just government mandates. Even without regulation, companies need certification to sell globally (EU CSRD is ADDING requirements).

**3. Organic Growth Stagnation (Probability: Medium, Impact: Medium, 1-3yr)**
Intertek's LFL organic growth of ~4.5% is the weakest among the Big 3. If Consumer Products (28% of revenue) or Corporate Assurance slow, overall growth could dip below 4%, which would weaken the investment case. The World of Energy division was flat in H1 2025, and Industry/Infrastructure grew only 3%. Monitoring: divisional LFL growth trends at each reporting period.

**4. M&A Execution Risk (Probability: Medium, Impact: Medium, 2-5yr)**
The AAA strategy includes "margin accretive investments" which implies M&A. Goodwill at 37.9% of total assets suggests significant historical M&A. If Intertek overpays for acquisitions (Eurofins-style dilution) or integrations fail, ROIC on deployed capital could decline. Mitigant: net debt reduced to 1.0x EBITDA gives conservative firepower, and recent M&A appears disciplined.

## Escenarios de Erosion

### 1. Most Probable: Organic Growth Underperformance vs Peers (Probability ~25-30%)
If Intertek's organic growth remains at ~4.5% while BVI achieves 6-7% and SGS 5-6%, the market will increasingly question whether Intertek's premium margin profile compensates for lower growth. Over a 5-year period, the compounding difference between 4.5% and 6.5% organic growth is ~10pp of cumulative revenue. This would not destroy the moat, but could erode the valuation multiple relative to peers.

**Monitoring signal:** LFL organic growth dropping below 4% for two consecutive reporting periods. Consumer Products LFL growth slowing below 5%.

### 2. Moderate Risk: Margin Compression from Competition/Wage Inflation (Probability ~15-20%)
Intertek's operating margin improvement (14.2% to 17.4% over 3 years) is impressive but partially driven by restructuring savings (GBP 11M/year). Once restructuring benefits are exhausted, maintaining margin progression requires organic leverage. If wage inflation (Intertek's primary cost) accelerates or competitors undercut pricing, margins could stall at ~17-18% rather than reaching the 18.5%+ target.

**Monitoring signal:** Operating margin progression stalling for 2+ reporting periods. SBC/revenue increasing above 1% (talent competition).

### 3. Tail Risk: Regulatory Derecognition or Certification Scandal (Probability ~3-5%)
A systemic certification fraud or catastrophic safety incident traced to an Intertek certification could trigger accreditation revocations. The ETL Listed mark and Notified Body status are existentially important. Loss of these would be near-fatal to the business.

**Monitoring signal:** Regulatory actions against Intertek, withdrawal of accreditations, major litigation tied to certification failure.

### Dudas/Incertidumbres
- **ROIC history pre-2021:** The quality_scorer.py only provides 4 years of ROIC data (2021-2024). Intertek has been public since 2002. A 10-year ROIC history would be critical to determine if the moat qualifies as WIDE. Company reports cite "strong ROIC progress over the last 10 years" which implies ROIC has been above WACC for at least a decade, but I cannot verify this from the tool alone. If confirmed, the moat could be upgraded to WIDE.
- **Gross margin data gap:** The quality_scorer.py flagged "Data gaps: Gross margins" and reported 56.9%, which seems low for a services business. BVI/SGS report ~72%. This is likely a reporting classification difference (Intertek may include more items in COGS). This prevents reliable gross margin peer comparison.
- **Client retention rate precision:** "Above 90%" is cited by multiple sources but I cannot verify the exact figure from primary data. The ~4.5% organic growth and 400,000+ client base are strong indirect evidence of high retention, but direct retention disclosure would strengthen the assessment.
- **Company-reported ROIC (22.4%) vs tool ROIC (18.0%):** The 4.4pp gap is significant. Both confirm a strong moat, but the absolute level matters for WIDE vs NARROW classification. I have documented this discrepancy but cannot resolve it without understanding the exact methodology difference.
