## Business Understanding

### What Intertek Does

Intertek is a Total Quality Assurance provider -- the company tests, inspects, and certifies products, commodities, processes, and infrastructure to ensure they meet regulatory and quality standards. Founded in 1885, operating in 100+ countries with ~44,000 employees and 1,000+ laboratories.

**Problem solved:** Companies MUST prove their products comply with safety, environmental, and quality regulations before they can sell them. This is not optional -- it is legally required in most markets. Intertek provides the independent third-party verification that regulators demand.

**Revenue model:** Mixed recurring/project-based. Regulatory testing is repeat/recurring (products need annual re-certification, ongoing compliance monitoring). R&D testing and new product certification is more project-based but repeats with each new product generation.

## Moat Assessment

### Moat Type: WIDE

**Moat Sources:**

1. **Regulatory Barriers / Accreditation (9/10):** TIC companies need government accreditations (ISO 17025, ISO 17020, etc.) that take years and significant investment to obtain. Intertek has 1,000+ accreditations globally. A new entrant cannot replicate this overnight. Regulators in most industries REQUIRE independent third-party testing from accredited bodies -- this is not optional.

2. **Switching Costs (8/10):** Changing TIC provider means re-auditing everything, re-certifying products, re-validating test methods. For a multinational with 10,000 SKUs across 50 countries, switching is enormously costly and disruptive. Intertek's historical data on product testing results is irreplaceable for existing clients.

3. **Global Scale (7/10):** Operating in 100+ countries with 1,000+ labs creates a network advantage -- multinational clients prefer a single TIC provider that can certify their products globally. Only SGS and Bureau Veritas have comparable global reach.

4. **Brand/Trust (7/10):** 140 years of testing history. A certification mark from Intertek (the "S" mark, "ETL" mark for North America) is recognized by regulators globally. Trust in TIC is existential -- one failure (certifying an unsafe product) can be catastrophic. This creates enormous reputational barriers.

**Morningstar upgraded Intertek to WIDE moat** (Source: Morningstar analyst report -- Level 2 Secondary Analysis. I concur based on my own analysis of the regulatory barriers and switching costs.)

**Moat Evidence from Financials:**
- ROIC consistently >15% (last 4 years: 19%, 15.3%, 15.6%, 18%, trending to 22.5%)
- Gross margin ~57% (premium vs sector median ~45%)
- ROIC persistence: 4/4 years > WACC, improving trajectory

**Moat Risks:**
- AI in visual inspection could reduce SOME manual testing (3-5 year timeline, LOW probability of major impact since regulation requires human oversight)
- US deregulation under Trump could reduce mandatory testing requirements in US market (~25% of revenue). MEDIUM probability, LOW-MEDIUM impact
- Bureau Veritas growing faster organically could indicate relative competitive weakness in some segments
