## Business Description

MSCI Inc. is a global provider of decision-support tools and services for the investment community, organized into four segments: **Index** (57% of revenue), **Analytics** (23%), **Sustainability & Climate** (11%), and **Private Assets** (9%). The company serves institutional investors, asset managers, hedge funds, pension funds, and banks worldwide.

The core business model is a **toll on global passive investing**. MSCI licenses its proprietary indexes (240,000+) to fund managers who create ETFs, mutual funds, and institutional mandates benchmarked against them. $2.34 trillion in ETF AUM was linked to MSCI equity indexes as of December 31, 2025 -- up 35.7% YoY. This creates a revenue stream that grows with both AUM (market appreciation + inflows) and pricing. The subscription business (73% of revenue) has a 94.4% retention rate, making revenue highly predictable.

MSCI is effectively a **financial data infrastructure monopoly** in international equity indexing. While S&P Dow Jones dominates US equity benchmarks and FTSE Russell covers UK/small-cap, MSCI is the undisputed standard for international, emerging market, and ESG-integrated equity benchmarks. This oligopoly structure creates extraordinarily high barriers to entry: decades of historical data, regulatory entrenchment, investment mandate inertia, and brand recognition embedded in fund names and institutional workflows.

## Valuation

### Method Selection Rationale

As a Tier A Quality Compounder, the primary method is **Owner Earnings Yield** and secondary is **Reverse DCF / Multi-scenario DCF**. Per valuation-methods skill.

### Method 1: Owner Earnings Yield (Primary, 60% weight)

```
FCF (FY2025): $1,459M
Depreciation: ~$180M (estimated from D&A $185-195M guide minus amort)
Maintenance Capex estimate: Depreciation x 1.1 = ~$198M

Owner Earnings = FCF - Maintenance Capex + Total Capex (since FCF already deducts total capex)
Actually: Owner Earnings = Operating Cash Flow - Maintenance Capex
= $1,588M - $198M = $1,390M

Owner Earnings Yield = $1,390M / $39,200M (Market Cap) = 3.55%

Expected Growth: 9-11% (organic growth + pricing + AUM growth)

OEY + Growth = 3.55% + 10% = 13.55%
WACC = 9.5% (derived: Rf 4.5% + 1.28*3.9% ERP = 9.5%)
Spread vs WACC = +4.05pp

Implied Fair Value at OEY = WACC - Growth:
At 10% growth, WACC 9.5%: OE/(WACC-g) is undefined (growth > WACC-like return).
Use more practical framework:

At a 3.5% OEY target (appropriate for a quality compounder with 10% growth):
FV = $1,390M / 0.035 = $39.7B / 73.4M shares diluted = ~$541

At a 4.0% OEY target (more conservative):
FV = $1,390M / 0.040 = $34.8B / 73.4M shares diluted = ~$474

At 3.0% OEY target (aggressive, pricing in full growth):
FV = $1,390M / 0.030 = $46.3B / 73.4M shares diluted = ~$631

Weighting: 3.5% OEY target as base (growth 10% > Tier A compounder avg)
OEY Fair Value: ~$540
```

**Calibration check:** With OEY of 3.55%, current price already implies roughly fair value at the 3.5% OEY target. The market is pricing MSCI at approximately the right level for a 10% growth compounder. This is a rational pricing.

**Precedent context:** Consensus analyst target is $680 (median $700). My OEY FV of $540 is significantly BELOW consensus, which is correct per Principle #49 (never anchor to consensus). The consensus assumes more aggressive growth than I do.

- **DATA:** Revenue +9.7%, organic +9.3%, retention 94.4%, FCF $1.46B growing, CEO buying shares. All positive.
- **NARRATIVE:** "Index monopoly deserves premium" -- partly opinion. The monopoly is real but the premium is already priced.
- **COUNTER-NARRATIVE:** ESG new sales -27.5%, receivables growing 2x revenue, negative shareholders' equity, 74.5% terminal value dependency in DCF.

### Conclusion

Consensus is that MSCI is a buy at current levels. Data supports the quality thesis but the PRICE already reflects it. My FV of $530 is significantly below consensus $680. If consensus is right and I'm wrong, I underestimate growth. If I'm right and consensus overestimates, there's meaningful downside risk (reverse DCF at historical growth = $398). I believe the correct approach is PATIENCE -- wait for a correction to provide margin of safety in a business where the quality is not in question, only the price.

## META-REFLECTION

### Incertidumbres/Dudas

- **Receivables divergence (+20.2% vs revenue +9.7%)** is the most concerning data point I could not fully resolve. It could indicate: (a) timing of large contract invoicing at year-end (benign), (b) slower collections from international clients (moderate concern), or (c) aggressive revenue recognition (serious concern). Need to monitor Q1 2026 to see if this normalizes.
- **ESG trajectory**: The -27.5% decline in Sustainability & Climate new sales is ambiguous. Is it cyclical (US political backlash that reverses with next administration) or structural (ESG ratings becoming commoditized)? My base case assumes cyclical, but structural erosion would reduce growth by ~1-2pp.
- **The OEY method requires choosing a "target OEY" which introduces subjectivity.** I used 3.5% as appropriate for a 10% growth compounder, but reasonable people could argue 3.0% (bullish, FV $631) or 4.0% (bearish, FV $474). The range is wide.
- **DCF sensitivity is HIGH** (TV = 74.5% of EV, FV spread 100%). This means the DCF fair value is unreliable as a point estimate -- it's better used as a range ($225-$750 across scenarios). This is why I weight OEY at 60%.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema

- **quality_scorer.py market_position default**: MSCI gets 0/8 for market position (manual default). This is clearly wrong for the #1 international index provider. The default should be 0/8 but flagged for manual override in thesis. This is a known issue from Session 52 audit (market_position default was fixed to 0 from 5, but still requires manual input).
- **OEY valuation methodology**: The framework says "OEY = OE / Market Cap" but doesn't provide clear guidance on what OEY level implies fair value. I had to reason from first principles (compare OEY + Growth vs WACC). A more structured OEY-to-FV conversion framework would help consistency across analyses.

### Anomalias Detectadas

- **Receivables growing 2x revenue growth** -- needs explanation from earnings call transcript or 10-K. This is the one financial anomaly that warrants investigation before R2.
- **Negative shareholders' equity** -- not anomalous (by design via buybacks) but unusual for a Tier A compounder. All valuation frameworks reference equity value which is meaningless when equity is negative. Enterprise value and FCF-based methods are the only valid approaches.
- **Beta of 1.28 seems high** for a quasi-monopoly data business. S&P Global (SPGI) has beta ~1.15, Moody's (MCO) ~1.25. The 1.28 may reflect the leverage (amplifies equity volatility) rather than business risk. Consider using unlevered beta for WACC calculation in R2/R3.
