## Business Understanding

### What Rational Does

**Problem solved:** Commercial kitchens need versatile, reliable, efficient cooking equipment that reduces labor dependency and ensures consistent food quality across locations. Labor scarcity in hospitality is structural and worsening globally.

**Revenue model:** Capital equipment sales (iCombi Pro, iVario Pro, iHexagon) + recurring aftermarket (accessories, cleaners, service parts, training, ConnectedCooking digital platform). Combi ovens = ~90% of appliance sales, iVario ~10%. Equipment prices range $10K-$50K per unit.

**Customer segments:** Restaurants, hotels, caterers, hospitals, corporate canteens, schools, cruise ships -- anyone with a commercial kitchen. Geographic mix: Europe (Germany 5% growth 9M, rest of Europe 10% growth), North America (6% growth 9M), Asia expanding.

### Why Is It "Expensive"? (The Market Narrative)

The market narrative on Rational is consistent and decades-old: "Wonderful company, always too expensive." This is reflected in the stock trading at P/E ~33x, well above industrial sector averages.

**The market is correct that the quality is exceptional.** The question is whether the price adequately compensates for the quality or whether it bakes in too much optimism.

**My assessment: The current price (P/E 33x, EV/EBIT 24.3x) implies ~19.5% FCF growth for 5 years.** This is achievable only if:
- Revenue accelerates from 6% (FY2025) back to 15%+ (unlikely without M&A or major new product cycle)
- Margins continue expanding from already-high 27% EBIT (possible but tapering)
- Some combination of both

The FY2025 growth rate of 6% (8% CC) is the first year materially below the 5-year CAGR of 15.2% (which was inflated by the post-COVID rebound from 2021-2022). The underlying growth is normalizing to high-single-digit, which is excellent but not 19.5%.

## Valuation -- Multi-Method (Tier A Protocol)

### Method 1: Owner Earnings Yield (Primary, 40% weight)

```
FCF (FY2024): EUR 251M
Depreciation: ~EUR 52M (estimated from capex/depreciation data)
Maintenance Capex: EUR 52M * 1.1 = ~EUR 57M
Owner Earnings = EUR 251M - EUR 57M + EUR 52M = EUR 246M

Market Cap: EUR 8.4B
Owner Earnings Yield = EUR 246M / EUR 8.4B = 2.9%

Expected Growth: 8% (my base case)
OEY + Growth = 2.9% + 8.0% = 10.9%
vs WACC: 9.5%
Spread: +1.4pp
```

**Interpretation:** The OEY + Growth spread is positive but THIN. For context, when we bought ADBE (also Tier A), the OEY + Growth was well above WACC with a 31% MoS. Here, at current prices, the spread is barely positive, suggesting the stock is fairly valued to slightly overvalued.

**OEY-implied Fair Value:** For a Tier A compounder, I would want OEY + Growth to provide at least a 3-4pp spread over WACC to justify entry. That means OEY + 8% >= 12.5-13.5%, so OEY >= 4.5-5.5%.

At OEY 4.5%: FV = EUR 246M / 4.5% = EUR 5.47B market cap = EUR 5.47B / 11.37M shares = **EUR 481/share**
At OEY 3.5%: FV = EUR 246M / 3.5% = EUR 7.03B market cap = **EUR 618/share**

**OEY method range: EUR 481-618, midpoint EUR 550**
