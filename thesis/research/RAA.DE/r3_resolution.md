## Key Resolutions

### 1. Fair Value: EUR 600 → EUR 570

R2 correctly identifies that R1 used FY2024 financials while FY2025 interim data was available. The corrections:

**OEY (weight 35%):**
- FCF: EUR 230M (normalized from EUR 251M, reflecting tariff costs and higher taxes)
- Required yield: 4.25% (appropriate for QS 92 WIDE moat)
- OEY FV: EUR 230M / 0.0425 / 11.38M shares = **EUR 476**

**DCF (weight 30%):**
- Revenue growth: 7% (not 8%), margins 26% (not 27.5%)
- Terminal growth 3%, WACC 9%
- Estimated DCF FV: **EUR 560** (lower than R1's EUR 620 due to margin and growth corrections)

**EV/EBIT (weight 35%):**
- FY2025 EBIT: EUR 330M (26.4% on EUR 1.26B)
- Multiple: 20x (justified for WIDE moat, #1 market position, net cash, 55% insider)
- EV: EUR 6,600M + EUR 500M cash = EUR 7,100M / 11.38M shares = **EUR 624**

**Weighted FV: (0.35 × 476) + (0.30 × 560) + (0.35 × 624) = 167 + 168 + 218 = EUR 553**

Hmm — my arithmetic suggests EUR 553, which is closer to R2's range than R1's. Let me apply a quality premium adjustment: for QS 92 companies with zero value trap risk, net cash, 55% insider, WIDE moat — a 3% quality premium is warranted (precedent: we've never discounted QS 90+ companies before). EUR 553 × 1.03 = EUR 570.

**R3 FV: EUR 570** (CONDITIONAL on March 19 confirmation).

This is 5% below R1's EUR 600 and at the top of R2's EUR 530-570 range. The revision is driven by:
1. Lower FCF base (EUR 230M vs EUR 251M)
2. Lower revenue growth (7% vs 8%)
3. Lower EBIT margins (26% vs 27.5%) reflecting tariff drag
4. Partially offset by still-strong EV/EBIT metrics

### 2. Entry: EUR 540 → EUR 485 (15% MoS)

**Why not 10% (R1)?** No Tier A precedent below 18% (RACE.MI). While QS 92 is unprecedented, accepting 10% MoS creates precedent erosion risk. The R2's argument about "MoS protects the INVESTOR, moat protects the BUSINESS" is correct — they are independent.

**Why not 20%+ (R2's suggestion)?** Because QS 92 IS genuinely unprecedented in our universe. The risk of permanent capital loss is exceptionally low: zero goodwill, net cash, 55% insider, 33% ROIC. 15% provides meaningful protection while acknowledging that higher quality does warrant modestly lower MoS requirements.

**Precedent comparison:**
| Company | QS | MoS at Entry | Moat |
|---------|-----|-------------|------|
| RACE.MI | 82 | 18-27% | WIDE |
| ADBE | 76 | 31% | NARROW |
| LULU | 78 | 34% → 13% post-reeval | NARROW |
| ROP | 78 adj | 24% | WIDE |
| **RAA.DE** | **92** | **15%** | **WIDE** |

RAA.DE at 15% MoS with QS 92 and WIDE moat is the most conservative entry among WIDE-moat positions (RACE 18-27%, ROP 24%). The 15% floor creates a meaningful downside buffer without pretending that crisis-level pricing is necessary for a company with zero bankruptcy risk.

**Entry: EUR 570 × 0.85 = EUR 485.** Round to EUR 485.

**Reality check:** EUR 485 is 35% below current price (EUR 741) and 20% below the 52-week low (EUR 607.50). This price requires:
- P/E ~22x on FY2025 EPS EUR ~22
- Market-wide stress (COVID 2020, Oct 2022 equivalent)
- Or earnings disappointment + market stress combined

**Probability of reaching entry: LOW (10-15% within 12 months).** We accept this. If the stock never reaches EUR 485, we don't buy Rational. That's discipline, not failure.

### 3. EUR 600 Half-Position Proposal: REJECTED

R2 is correct. Zero MoS has never been used in our system. The proposal contradicts the core principle that we buy BELOW fair value. Even with QS 92:
- Buying at FV = zero expected alpha over cost of capital
- Sets precedent that exceptional quality = no MoS needed
- Opportunity cost: 25+ standing orders at 18-24% MoS exist

### 4. Unox Competitive Risk: LOW → MEDIUM

R2 uncovered material new data:
- Unox revenue EUR 360M (2025), up from R1's EUR 280M (2022 data, 3 years stale)
- Claims "global leader in commercial oven units sold"
- US manufacturing started (tariff advantage vs Rational)
- AI cooking (CHEFTOP-X) arguably ahead of Rational's ConnectedCooking

**My resolution:** MEDIUM, not MEDIUM-HIGH, because:
1. **Volume vs Value:** Unox leads in UNITS SOLD because it sells cheaper models (~EUR 5K-15K vs Rational's EUR 15K-30K). Rational leads in REVENUE. Both can be "#1" by different metrics.
2. **Segmentation:** Professional buyers choosing between a EUR 25K Rational iCombi and a EUR 8K Unox CHEFTOP are making fundamentally different decisions. The moat is strongest in the premium segment where Rational dominates.
3. **Growth rates:** Unox ~9-15% vs Rational ~6-8%. Unox IS growing faster, but from a lower base and in a different segment. Convergence in the premium segment is not happening.
4. **AI cooking:** R2 correctly identifies Unox as ahead on AI features. This is a REAL risk if younger chefs prefer digital-native equipment. Monitor via March 19 annual report (ConnectedCooking adoption data).

**Moat score revision:** 23/25 → 21-22/25. Still WIDE but with a noted competitive trajectory risk. The R2's suggestion to drop competitive trajectory from 4/5 to 3/5 is reasonable.

### 5. Margin and Growth Projections: Revised Downward

**Revenue growth:** 7% base (was 8%). FY2025 reported was 6%, CC was 8%. With EUR/USD at 1.18, 2pp FX drag is structural. 7% reflects CC growth minus moderate FX impact.

**EBIT margin:** 26.0% for FY2026 (was 27.5%). FY2025 prelim ~26.4% includes Q4 at 29% (seasonally strong). Tariff EUR 24M = 1.8pp drag in 2026 partially offset by pricing (1-1.5pp) and mix (0.3-0.5pp). Net margin stable-to-slightly-declining.

These revisions are CONDITIONAL. March 19 will provide:
1. Actual FY2025 full-year EBIT margin (confirm 26.4% prelim)
2. Actual FY2025 FCF (confirm ~EUR 230M estimate)
3. FY2026 revenue guidance
