# Counter-Analysis: UTG.L (Unite Group PLC)

## Fecha: 2026-02-08

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The thesis survives partial scrutiny but has MATERIAL weaknesses that significantly erode the margin of safety. The 745 GBp fair value is overstated by an estimated 20-30%. The core assumption -- that FY2026 is a trough with recovery from FY2027 -- is challenged by three structural headwinds that did not exist when the thesis was written: (1) the UK Immigration White Paper introducing an international student levy of GBP 925/student from 2028, (2) Empiric's actual occupancy of 89% (far worse than Unite's 93-96% guidance), and (3) sector-wide PBSA vacancy rates tripling to 11.2% in some cities. The "near-monopoly" claim is misleading as Unite controls only ~10% of total UK PBSA beds, and unlisted institutional competitors (Greystar/GIC with 35,000+ beds, CRM/Dot Group with 80,000+ beds) dwarf it. The 42% NAV discount may be rational, not "historically extreme."

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. "Structural housing deficit of 620,000 beds will underpin demand"

- **Thesis claim:** Deficit of 620k beds means demand is structurally guaranteed. Only 15k beds/year delivered.
- **Evidence in contra:**
  - The deficit is a THEORETICAL number comparing total full-time students to total PBSA beds. Most students do NOT seek PBSA -- many live at home, in HMOs (houses of multiple occupation), or with family.
  - PBSA vacancy rates have TRIPLED in some cities. Nottingham went from 3.5% to 11.2% vacancy in one year (2023/24 to 2024/25), despite the supposed "deficit."
  - A record 23% of PBSA beds in England are priced ABOVE the maximum Student Maintenance Loan, creating an affordability ceiling that caps effective demand regardless of theoretical bed shortage.
  - PBSA providers in Sheffield are cutting rents by -5.5% to fill rooms. This is incompatible with a genuine supply shortage.
  - Fresh PBSA supply has slowed (88,000 beds in 5 years vs 158,000 in prior 5 years) but demand has also weakened simultaneously, especially from international postgraduates.
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolution sugerida:** The "620k deficit" should not be used as a blanket demand floor. The EFFECTIVE deficit (students who CAN and WANT to afford PBSA) is much smaller. Committee should discount this assumption by at least 50%.

### 2. "UK domestic 18yo cohort growing offsets international decline"

- **Thesis claim:** Domestic 18-year-old cohort growing 4-5%/year compensates for any international student decline.
- **Evidence in contra:**
  - UK 18yo applications for 2025/26 grew only 2.2%, not 4-5%.
  - 79.6% of UK universities MISSED their international recruitment forecasts for September 2024.
  - Indian student enrollment in UK fell 11.5% year-on-year; Chinese students fell 4.3%. These are the TWO largest international cohorts.
  - International students fell 36% from the 2022 peak by 2024 (Indian nationals).
  - Russell Group universities -- where Unite claims 93% portfolio concentration -- saw a 5% decline in international students, less than the 14% sector-wide but still declining.
  - The Graduate Route visa reduction from 24 to 18 months (effective January 2027 for students starting from January 2026) DIRECTLY impacts the attractiveness of UK education for international students.
  - NEW RISK NOT IN THESIS: International student levy of GBP 925/student/year starting August 2028. Government's own impact analysis estimates loss of 14,000-16,500 international student enrollments per year. This is a PERMANENT structural headwind that did not exist when the thesis was written.
  - Dependant restrictions (only PhD students can bring dependants) already in effect since 2025 -- a major deterrent for postgraduate students who were a key PBSA demographic.
  - English language requirements tightened to B2 level -- narrows the eligible pool.
- **Severidad:** CRITICAL
- **Resolution sugerida:** The thesis fundamentally underestimates the cumulative impact of 5+ policy changes that ALL point in the same direction: fewer international students. The domestic cohort growth of 2.2% CANNOT offset a 5-14% decline in international students who pay PREMIUM rents. This is the single biggest flaw in the thesis.

### 3. "42% discount to NAV is historically extreme and will close"

- **Thesis claim:** 42% discount is a decade low, reflecting irrational fear that will revert.
- **Evidence in contra:**
  - UK REITs have traded at persistent NAV discounts for YEARS. Academic research shows discounts result from agency costs, tax liabilities, and the interaction of noise traders. They do NOT always close.
  - VNA.DE (Vonovia) traded at 45-60% discount to NAV for years and the discount NEVER fully closed -- we sold it in our portfolio for exactly this reason.
  - The EPRA NTA of 986p is based on independent valuations at a 5.1% net initial yield. If cap rates expand by just 50bps (to 5.6%), the NAV drops by approximately 9-10%. If they expand by 100bps, NAV could fall by 18-20%.
  - Q4 valuations already showed "modest dips reflecting market-wide yield expansion." The direction of travel is AGAINST the thesis.
  - UK interest rates at 4.0% make a 5.1% property yield less attractive than historically (spread only 110bps vs historical 200-300bps).
  - Stifel just DOWNGRADED Unite from Buy to Hold, slashing target from 975p to 600p. This is an analyst house that was bullish and is now turning cautious.
  - The market may be CORRECTLY pricing the risk that NAV is overstated. The 42% discount may represent fair value, not a bargain.
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolution sugerida:** NAV-based valuation should carry less weight (reduce from 40% to 20% in the weighted method). The thesis should primarily rely on FFO-based and DDM methods, which are more conservative. At 14x FY2026 FFO of ~43p = 602p FV. At DDM with r=8.5%, g=2% = ~590p FV. Both of these suggest FV is 590-640p, NOT 745p.

### 4. "FY2026 is earnings trough, recovery from FY2027"

- **Thesis claim:** Earnings decline of 7-10% in FY2026 is the bottom, with growth from FY2027 driven by Empiric synergies and above-inflation rental growth.
- **Evidence in contra:**
  - FY2027 bookings at 64% vs 67% same time last year. The thesis assumes recovery, but early indicators show FURTHER DECELERATION, not stabilization.
  - Empiric's actual occupancy was 89% for 2025/26 -- SIX percentage points below Unite's own 95.2%. This means Unite is integrating a WEAKER portfolio that will drag down group-level occupancy.
  - Supply and demand imbalances in Nottingham, Sheffield and Glasgow accounted for over 5 percentage points of Empiric's lost occupancy. These are PERMANENT locational issues, not cyclical.
  - Cost of debt rising from 3.6% (2024) to 4.1-4.5% (2026), and hedge roll-off means further increases possible beyond 2026.
  - Building Safety Levy effective October 2026 will increase development costs for new PBSA projects.
  - Technology integration costs of ~GBP 10 million additional in 2026.
  - The "pathway to earnings growth from 2027" depends on MULTIPLE things going right simultaneously: occupancy stabilizing, Empiric synergies materializing, rental growth continuing, cost of debt stabilizing. If ANY of these fails, FY2027 is NOT a recovery year.
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolution sugerida:** The trough assumption is NOT validated by early FY2027 booking data. The committee should model a scenario where FY2027 is FLAT to slightly down (not recovering). This materially changes the investment timeline from 6-18 months to 24-36+ months.

### 5. "Near-monopoly in listed UK PBSA market (75k beds post-Empiric)"

- **Thesis claim:** Unite has near-monopoly status in listed PBSA, creating an efficient scale moat.
- **Evidence in contra:**
  - The "near-monopoly in LISTED PBSA" is semantically correct but STRATEGICALLY irrelevant. Unite's 75k beds represent roughly 10-15% of the estimated 500,000+ total UK PBSA beds.
  - CRM Students / Dot Group operate 80,000+ beds -- MORE than Unite -- and are unlisted.
  - Greystar/GIC operate 35,000+ beds (Student Roost + Chapter + Canvas brands) and are backed by sovereign wealth fund capital with lower cost of capital.
  - The top 5 operators control only 41% of beds, meaning the market remains fragmented.
  - Institutional capital (GIC, APG, CPPIB, Goldman Sachs) has invested GBP 1.5B+ into London student housing alone, bypassing Unite entirely.
  - Being "listed" is actually a DISADVANTAGE in this market: listed means higher cost of capital (equity investors demand returns), public earnings pressure, and inability to accept lower returns during downturns. Unlisted SWF-backed competitors can afford to wait and accept lower yields.
- **Severidad:** MODERATE
- **Resolution sugerida:** The moat assessment should be downgraded from "Narrow trending Wide" to "Narrow at best." The efficient scale moat is against OTHER LISTED operators (trivially true), not against the ACTUAL competitive landscape of institutional capital.
