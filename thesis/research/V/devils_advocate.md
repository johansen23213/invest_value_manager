# Counter-Analysis: V (Visa Inc.)

## Fecha: 2026-02-14

---

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The V thesis identifies the highest-quality toll-booth business in the world (QS 82, WIDE moat 23/25) and correctly concludes it is overvalued at current prices ($314 vs FV $345). The WATCHLIST verdict is sound. However, the R1 analysis has a **CRITICAL blind spot**: the original thesis (Feb 4) completely omitted the DOJ debit antitrust lawsuit -- a Section 2 Sherman Act monopolization case filed in September 2024 that is V-specific and does NOT affect MA. While the R1 specialist reports (Feb 14) correctly identified and analyzed this risk, the valuation report's FV of $345 is inflated by 13% above its own method-weighted average ($305) through subjective upward adjustments. More importantly, V faces a **historically unprecedented regulatory correlation cluster** -- 6 simultaneous regulatory actions across US, UK, and EU -- that no prior Tier A position in our portfolio has faced. The central question is unanswerable by the thesis: **if both V and MA face CCCA risk, but V ALSO faces DOJ debit lawsuit + disproportionate CCCA exposure (52% vs 35% share), why not just buy MA instead?**

My independent FV estimate: **$280-300**. Entry should be **$240-260**, not the R1's $270.

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. FV of $345 Is Justified Despite DOJ CRITICAL Risk

- **Thesis assumption:** Valuation specialist resolved FV at $345, 13% above the method-weighted average of $305, citing DCF tool FCF CAGR understatement and Q1 FY2026 acceleration.
- **Evidence against this assumption:**
  - The valuation specialist's OWN method-weighted average is $305. The $345 is an upward "judgment call" that is explicitly questioned in the specialist's own meta-reflection: "If the tool's FCF data is more accurate than revenue/EPS growth, then $305 is the truer FV and $345 is generous."
  - The three methods clustered tightly: OEY $320, DCF $290, Peer $292. The average is $305. The specialist then OVERRIDE this to $345 (+13%) based on: (a) Q1 FY2026 revenue acceleration (+15%), and (b) VAS growth (+28%). But one quarter of acceleration does not warrant a permanent FV increase -- especially when regulatory headwinds (DOJ, CCCA) could reverse the trend.
  - The DOJ risk-weighted FV (from the specialist's own DOJ scenario analysis) was $291. The combined DOJ+CCCA risk-weighted FV was $295. Both are BELOW $305 and FAR below $345.
  - At $345, implied P/E on TTM is 33.8x -- at the UPPER END of the historical 28-32x range. This is NOT a conservative valuation for a company facing CRITICAL litigation.
  - **Cross-check with MA:** MA's R3-resolved FV was $400 with a 24.2x P/E. V at $345 implies 29.4x FY2026E P/E -- a 22% PREMIUM to MA on forward P/E. V should trade at a DISCOUNT to MA (lower growth, DOJ-specific risk), not at a premium.
  - Wall Street consensus price target is $402 (mean), suggesting $345 is not aggressive by sell-side standards. But sell-side analysts do NOT incorporate DOJ structural remedy risk or CCCA passage into base cases -- they assume regulatory status quo.
  - Independent valuation services (Alpha Spread, Simply Wall St) estimate intrinsic values ranging from $217-$390, with most models concluding V is overvalued at current prices.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH** (8/10)
- **Resolution sugerida:** Use the method-weighted average of $305 as the CEILING for FV, then apply the DOJ+CCCA risk discount of ~5-8%, yielding FV $280-290. The $345 is a bull-case number masquerading as a base case.

### 2. DOJ Debit Antitrust Is Manageable / Will Settle

- **Thesis assumption:** R1 risk assessment assigns 40% probability to settlement, 25% to Visa winning, and treats even a trial loss as non-fatal (revenue impact 5-10% permanently). The moat assessment treats it as "genuine risk but NOT moat-destroying."
- **Evidence against this assumption:**
  - **This is a Section 2 Sherman Act monopolization case** -- the most serious type of antitrust action in US law. The DOJ does not bring these lightly. The only recent comparable is US v. Google, which resulted in liability finding (August 2024) and behavioral remedies (2025-2026).
  - **Motion to dismiss DENIED (June 2025).** Judge Koeltl ruled the allegations are "plausible." This is a significant negative signal -- frivolous cases get dismissed at this stage.
  - **The DOJ's specific allegations are damaging:** (a) Visa entered illegal agreements with potential competitors to NOT compete; (b) Visa used "cliff pricing" to penalize merchants who route away; (c) Visa paid issuers billions in incentives specifically to lock them into Visa debit. These are NOT marginal competitive practices -- they are textbook exclusionary conduct.
  - **Google precedent is LESS favorable than the thesis suggests.** The court found Google liable for monopolization and imposed behavioral remedies including prohibition of exclusive distribution contracts. The DOJ appealed for STRONGER remedies including Chrome divestiture. If applied to Visa's debit business, analogous behavioral remedies would: mandate multi-network routing on all Visa debit cards, ban exclusive debit routing agreements, cap debit incentive payments, and mandate transparent scheme fee disclosures.
  - **Visa's debit revenue is ~25-30% of total payment volume, ~15-20% of global revenue.** A structural remedy forcing routing competition on debit could reduce debit scheme fees by 15-30%, translating to 4-6% permanent revenue reduction. This is NOT a one-time fine -- it is STRUCTURAL.
  - **The Trump administration's stance is ambiguous.** While the case was filed under Biden, career DOJ staff are driving it. Trump's endorsement of CCCA (credit routing competition) suggests appetite for payment network regulation, not protection.
  - **Timeline is long but uncertainty is priced NOW.** Discovery ongoing 2026, trial likely 2027-2028. The stock trades at a P/E premium to its own history despite having a CRITICAL overhang. Each negative development (discovery revelations, pre-trial motions) creates step-down risk.
  - **V-specific vs MA:** Mastercard is NOT a defendant. MA has ~25% debit share -- below monopoly threshold. This means V carries a risk MA does not, yet the thesis recommends V over MA. This is backwards.
- **Severidad:** **CRITICAL** (10/10)
- **Resolution sugerida:** The DOJ suit should be treated as a HARD GATE for any V investment. The thesis should explicitly model the probability-weighted debit revenue loss (~4-6% permanent under settlement, ~8-12% under trial loss) and reduce FV accordingly. At minimum, FV should be discounted by $25-35 from the base case for DOJ risk alone. Furthermore, the committee should ask: why accept this CRITICAL V-specific risk when MA offers comparable quality without it?

### 3. CCCA Will Probably Fail Like Previous Years

- **Thesis assumption:** R1 risk assessment assigns 40-50% probability of passage. Thesis treats it as manageable.
- **Evidence against this assumption:**
  - **Trump endorsed CCCA on January 13, 2026** -- a FIRST. No prior president endorsed this bill. V dropped 4.5-4.7% intraday on the news.
  - **Senate Majority Leader Thune committed to a floor vote before mid-2026 summer recess** -- also unprecedented for this bill.
  - **Bipartisan sponsors:** Durbin (D) + Marshall (R) in Senate, Lofgren (D) + Gooden (R) in House. This is genuine bipartisan populism, not partisan posturing.
  - **Senator Marshall already attempted to attach CCCA to the CLARITY Act** on January 29, 2026. Failed this time, but demonstrates legislative momentum and willingness to use rider strategy.
  - **CCCA DISPROPORTIONATELY affects V over MA:** The bill requires large-bank credit cards to enable at least two unaffiliated networks, and "neither can be Visa or Mastercard simultaneously." Since Visa is the primary network on 52% of cards (vs MA's 35%), Visa is the network most likely to be DISPLACED from the secondary routing slot. Banks routing through Star, NYCE, or Discover/CapOne instead of Visa would directly reduce Visa's credit transaction revenue.
  - **The Durbin Amendment precedent is instructive AND alarming:** After Durbin passed in 2010, debit interchange fell 52% ($0.44 to $0.21 per transaction). Banks' interchange revenue fell 29%. However, the thesis correctly notes Visa adapted by raising scheme fees ~2x since 2018. But CCCA specifically targets scheme fees and routing, not just interchange. The "adaptation playbook" may not work twice.
  - **If CCCA passes:** 15-25% reduction in US credit scheme fee revenue over 3-5 years. US credit is ~30-35% of V's total revenue. Impact: -5-9% permanent revenue reduction. Combined with DOJ debit impact (4-6%), the total regulatory revenue hit could be 9-15%.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH** (8/10)
- **Resolution sugerida:** Given Trump endorsement and Thune floor vote commitment, CCCA probability should be 45-55%, not 40-50%. Any standing order for V should include a HARD GATE: "CCCA floor vote NOT scheduled or defeated." If CCCA passes before V reaches entry, FV drops by $40-60 and the entry becomes immediately invalid. This is a binary catalyst that GATES the investment.

### 4. Revenue Growth of 10-11% Will Continue Despite Regulatory Headwinds

- **Thesis assumption:** Valuation report uses 10% base growth (revenue) and 11-12% FCF/share growth.
- **Evidence against this assumption:**
  - **Client incentives are growing FASTER than revenue.** From ~24% of gross revenue in 2020 to ~42% in 2025. Q4 FY2025: incentives +17% vs revenue +12%. Q1 FY2026: incentives +12% vs net revenue +15% (better ratio, but one quarter). Management guides ~20% of payments volume impacted by renewals in 2026, implying continued incentive pressure.
  - **This is the quiet margin compressor the thesis ignores.** The original thesis (Feb 4) does not mention client incentives at ALL. The valuation report notes it but does not reduce growth for it. If incentives continue growing 2-3pp faster than gross revenue, NET revenue growth compresses to 7-8% from the headline 10-11%.
  - **Permanently excluded markets reduce the TAM:** India (1.4B people, 18B+ UPI monthly transactions), China (UnionPay monopoly), Russia (MIR) -- combined ~3B people and ~$3T+ in addressable payment volume permanently excluded. The thesis's "only 15-20% of global transactions digital" growth argument assumes this TAM is accessible. It is not.
  - **India's zero-MDR model is being EXPORTED.** NPCI International plans to expand UPI to 20 countries by FY2029. MOUs signed with Kenya, Rwanda, Mozambique, Namibia, Peru. If 10+ emerging markets adopt zero-MDR A2A, the "secular digitalization runway" for card networks shrinks from >15 years to ~10 years, and the EM growth component drops from 3-4pp to 1-2pp of Visa's growth.
  - **Wero (European Payments Initiative) is LIVE and growing.** 46+ million registered users, 100+ million P2P transactions, EUR 7.5B processed. E-commerce launch: Germany end-2025, Belgium and France 2026. Netherlands (iDEAL migration to Wero) 2026-2027. NFC POS 2026. First merchants signed: Air France, E. Leclerc, Orange, Veepee. This is ECB-backed sovereign infrastructure specifically designed to reduce dependence on V/MA. The R1 moat assessment mentions Wero but the thesis does not.
  - **Net growth projection after regulatory drags:** TAM growth 6-7% (not 8%, after India/EM exclusion), share delta +0.2%, pricing +1-1.5% (constrained by CCCA/regulation) = 7.5-9% gross, minus incentive drag -1pp = 6.5-8% net revenue growth. This is BELOW the thesis's 10%.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH** (7/10)
- **Resolution sugerida:** Base case revenue growth should be 7-8%, not 10%. FCF/share growth should be 9-10% (including buybacks), not 11-12%. This alone reduces DCF FV by ~$25-35 and OEY FV by ~$15-20.

### 5. V Is Preferred Over MA For Capital Deployment

- **Thesis assumption:** Valuation report recommends "Prioritize V for near-term deployment" because V ($270 entry) is closer to current price than MA ($315 entry), with V's entry requiring only -14% drop vs MA's -39%.
- **Evidence against this assumption:**
  - **V has 1 CRITICAL risk (DOJ debit) that MA does not have.** The DOJ sued Visa specifically because it has 60%+ debit monopoly. MA at ~25% debit is NOT a monopoly target. This is not a marginal difference -- it is the difference between facing structural remedies and not.
  - **CCCA disproportionately affects V.** V is the primary network on 52% of cards. Under CCCA, the "displaced" secondary network will more often be Visa. MA at 35% is less likely to be the displaced network on most cards.
  - **MA has HIGHER growth (13.8% vs 10.9% CAGR)** and higher QS (86 vs 82). MA's faster growth provides more buffer to absorb regulatory hits.
  - **V's "closer to entry" advantage is misleading.** V at $314 is closer to its entry ($270 = -14%) than MA ($518 vs $315 = -39%). But V's entry zone SHOULD be LOWER given its worse risk profile. If V's FV is $280-300 (my estimate) and it needs 20-25% MoS (given DOJ CRITICAL), then entry should be $210-240 -- which is -24% to -33% from current, comparable to MA's distance.
  - **MA's Greensill risk (A$7B trial Aug 2026) is bounded and time-limited.** It is a KNOWN quantity with a trial date. V's DOJ risk is UNBOUNDED (trial 2027-2028, remedies unknown) and could escalate.
  - **V's higher FCF margin (54% vs 50%) does not compensate** for DOJ CRITICAL + CCCA disproportionate + lower growth. The 4pp margin advantage is worth ~$10-15 in FV; the DOJ risk discount is worth -$25-35.
  - **If forced to choose one payment network:** MA is the cleaner, higher-quality, faster-growing business without a monopoly lawsuit. V is the bigger, higher-margin, wider-moat business WITH a monopoly lawsuit. Risk-adjusted, MA is superior.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH** (7/10)
- **Resolution sugerida:** The investment committee should explicitly address: "Given that V faces DOJ CRITICAL risk that MA does not, and CCCA disproportionately affects V, what is the specific argument for preferring V?" The thesis's answer ("closer to entry") is a convenience argument, not a quality argument. If both drop to entry, buy MA first.
