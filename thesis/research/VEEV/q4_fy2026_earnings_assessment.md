### CRM Competitive Dynamic -- UPDATED

**This was the key uncertainty going into earnings.** The results CLARIFY the picture:

- **10 of top-20 biopharmas committed** to Vault CRM globally (was "9" at Q3; now 10)
- **Expects ~14 of top-20** to commit (unchanged from prior guidance)
- **125+ customers live** on Vault CRM (was 100+ at Q2, ~115 at thesis)
- **2 top-20 biopharmas live** in major markets (new milestone)

**Assessment:** The CRM situation is STABILIZING, not deteriorating. 10 committed (up from 9) and 125+ live (up from 100+) shows continued momentum. The fear that Salesforce would rapidly erode Veeva's position has NOT materialized in the data. However, the delta from 18 (historical) to ~14 (target) remains a real loss of ~4 top-20 customers. The question is whether those 4 represent the FINAL loss or the BEGINNING of more losses. The stabilization at 10 committed (heading toward 14) suggests the former.

- Veeva AI Agents for CRM and commercial content launched December 2025
- Several customers already live on AI agents
- CEO Gassner emphasized "industry-specific AI deeply integrated into applications" leveraging Veeva's systems of record position
- This positions VEEV as an AI BENEFICIARY, not victim -- they can embed AI into their vertical platform where generic AI tools lack regulatory compliance

### Quality of Results -- EXCEPTIONAL

- Revenue growth maintained at 16% (no deceleration from FY2025's 16.3%)

- Operating cash flow grew +29.8% to $1,415M

## 10. META-REFLECTION

### Incertidumbres/Dudas

1. **SBC remains the elephant in the room.** FY2026 SBC was likely $490-520M (15-16% of $3.2B revenue). The $2B buyback helps offset dilution but the GAAP vs non-GAAP gap remains material. The market CHOOSES to value SaaS companies on non-GAAP, and so do we, but real economic dilution is ongoing.

2. **The stock surged 11% post-earnings.** At $195.49, the easy money has been made. The stock is now trading within 6% of my updated FV ($207). For a deep value investor, this is NOT the time to buy. The thesis is intact but the margin of safety has evaporated with the post-earnings rally.

3. **Billings growth of ~16% FY2026 decelerating to ~10.4% guided FY2027.** This is the first sign that forward revenue growth will decelerate. The market is currently pricing ~12.1% FCF growth (per reverse DCF), which is close to the guided 12.5% revenue growth. If billings decelerate further, the 12.5% revenue growth target could be at risk in FY2028.

4. **OCF of $1,415M is extraordinary but includes working capital benefit.** Deferred revenue grew to $1,489M -- this represents advance customer payments that boost OCF. The sustainable FCF run rate may be closer to $1.2-$1.3B once working capital normalizes.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema

1. **Update quality_universe.yaml with post-earnings FV $207 and entry $165.** The current universe shows FV $190.

2. **Consider creating a "pharma-healthcare-IT" sector view** that covers VEEV specifically. It does not fit cleanly in "technology" or "pharma-healthcare" -- it is vertical SaaS for life sciences.

3. **The R3-to-post-earnings update process should become a standard workflow.** When pipeline candidates report earnings, a structured assessment (like this one) should be the standard output.

### Anomalias Detectadas

1. **The DCF tool's default scenarios significantly undervalue VEEV.** Base FV of $155.75 vs guided 12.5% growth is a major disconnect. The tool's conservative defaults (FCF growth ~5%) are inappropriate for a company growing 16% with expanding margins. This is a known tool limitation but particularly acute for VEEV.

2. **The quality_scorer.py ROIC calculation remains distorted.** ROIC-WACC spread is now +0.5pp (was +0.7pp pre-earnings, the update reflects slightly higher WACC from beta increase 1.08 to 1.12). The actual economic ROIC excluding excess cash is likely 16-20%. The tool should be enhanced with an excess-cash adjustment.

3. **Forward P/E of 22.1x (on guided $8.85 EPS) is historically cheap for VEEV.** The 5-year average forward P/E is ~35-40x. The stock is at a ~40% discount to its own historical valuation, which reflects the CRM fears and SaaSpocalypse. If CRM stabilizes as guided, a re-rating toward 28-30x forward P/E ($248-$265) is plausible but would require sustained execution through FY2027.
