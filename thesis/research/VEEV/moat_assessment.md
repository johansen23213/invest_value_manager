# Moat Assessment: VEEV (Veeva Systems Inc.)

## Fecha: 2026-02-13

## Clasificacion: WIDE

Veeva Systems possesses a Wide moat built on three reinforcing sources: exceptionally high switching costs, efficient scale in a regulatory-constrained vertical, and significant intangible assets. The moat is EXPANDING as Veeva migrates off Salesforce onto its proprietary Vault platform, wins share in R&D/clinical from Medidata, and embeds AI agents that deepen product stickiness.

## Detailed Source Analysis

### 1. Switching Costs (Score: 7/8 -- DOMINANT)

This is Veeva's primary moat. The switching costs are among the highest in enterprise SaaS because they are **regulatory in nature**, not just operational.

**Quantitative Evidence:**
- **Gross retention rate: 95%** (churn ~4-5% annually, mostly from small biotech failures, not competitive losses)
- **Net dollar-based retention: ~120%** (existing customers spend 20% more each year through cross-sell/upsell of additional Vault modules)
- **Contract duration: multi-year** (typically 3-5 year contracts with auto-renewal)
- **Customer tenure: extremely long** -- once a top-20 pharma validates and deploys Veeva across clinical, regulatory, quality, and commercial, switching would require:
  - Re-validating the entire system under 21 CFR Part 11 (6-18 months)
  - Migrating years of regulated documents, audit trails, electronic signatures
  - Retraining thousands of users across multiple departments
  - Risk of regulatory inspection gaps during transition
  - Estimated switching cost: $10M-$50M+ for a top-20 pharma, plus 12-24 months of organizational disruption

**Why switching costs are EXPANDING:**
- The Vault CRM migration (off Salesforce, onto Veeva's proprietary platform by 2030) makes customers MORE dependent on Veeva's stack. Previously, Veeva CRM ran on Salesforce infrastructure -- customers could theoretically port data via Salesforce ecosystem. Now, Vault CRM is fully proprietary, and 9 of top-20 pharma have committed to it (vs. 3 for Salesforce Life Sciences Cloud).
- Cross-module adoption deepens embedding: a customer using Vault CRM + Vault EDC + Vault QualityDocs + Vault RegulatoryOne has data flowing across all modules on one platform. Replacing ANY single module means breaking the data flow.
- Veeva AI Agents (launched Dec 2025) are trained on Veeva's domain-specific data structures. The more customers use AI within Vault, the harder it becomes to leave.

**Score justification: 7/8 rather than 8/8** because switching costs are slightly lower for smaller biotech companies (fewer users, simpler implementations), and the per-seat pricing model means customer VALUE can erode even if customers don't leave (if they reduce headcount).

### 2. Network Effects (Score: 3/5 -- MODERATE)

Veeva has indirect and data network effects, but they are weaker than classic platform network effects (e.g., Visa/Mastercard).

**Quantitative Evidence:**
- **Industry standardization:** When 47 of the top 50 pharma companies use Veeva, the platform becomes a de facto standard. CROs (Contract Research Organizations), clinical sites, and regulatory consultants train on Veeva. This creates an ecosystem where Veeva expertise is the market default.
- **SiteVault:** Veeva's free tool for clinical trial sites (5,000+ sites) creates a cross-company network for site/sponsor interactions. More sponsors using Vault CTMS = more sites adopting SiteVault = more value for both sides.
- **Data standardization:** Regulatory submission formats, document templates, and compliance workflows standardized across the industry through Veeva usage.
- **Veeva OpenData:** Commercial data (HCP/HCO reference data) used by 800+ customers. More participants = better data quality = more valuable.

**Why not higher:**
- These are NOT winner-take-all network effects. A competitor could build a parallel network (IQVIA has its own data assets). The network effects are more about standardization inertia than exponential value creation.

### 3. Intangible Assets (Score: 4/5 -- STRONG)

**Quantitative Evidence:**
- **Brand = gold standard in life sciences cloud.** Being the first to prove that GxP-regulated workflows could run in the cloud (starting 2007) created a first-mover brand advantage that persists.
- **Domain expertise:** 15+ years of building for one specific vertical. Every feature, every workflow, every validation package is designed for pharma/biotech. This is NOT replicable by a horizontal SaaS player without years of investment and customer trust-building.
- **Validated cloud platform:** Vault is pre-validated for 21 CFR Part 11 and EU GMP Annex 11. Customers inherit compliance controls. This is a massive regulatory intangible -- competitors must build equivalent validation infrastructure.
- **Patent portfolio:** While Veeva's moat is not patent-dependent (no patent cliff risk like pharma), its proprietary Vault platform architecture and domain-specific AI training data constitute significant trade secrets.
- **No pricing power erosion observed:** Gross margins EXPANDING from 71.3% (2024) to 74.5% (2025), suggesting pricing power is intact or strengthening.

---

### 4. Cost Advantage (Score: 2/4 -- MODERATE)

**Quantitative Evidence:**
- **R&D amortization:** With $2.75B in revenue and 1,500+ customers, Veeva spreads R&D costs across a larger base than any competitor in life sciences vertical SaaS. This allows ~14% revenue CAGR while maintaining ~40% FCF margins.
- **Multi-tenant SaaS:** Single platform serves all customers, reducing infrastructure costs vs. competitors with on-premise or fragmented architectures (Oracle).
- **However:** Salesforce, Oracle, and Dassault have LARGER absolute R&D budgets. Veeva's cost advantage is relative within the vertical, not absolute. A well-funded competitor could outspend Veeva.

---

### 5. Efficient Scale (Score: 3/3 -- STRONG)

**Quantitative Evidence:**
- **Market concentration:** Veeva holds ~80% of life sciences CRM. In R&D (Vault EDC, CTMS), it is the #2 behind Medidata but gaining rapidly (8 of top-20 pharma standardized on Vault EDC by mid-2025, up from 6 in 2023).
- **Market size limits competition:** The total life sciences software TAM is ~$30-50B. This is large enough for 2-3 major players but NOT large enough to attract a dozen well-funded entrants. Compare this to horizontal CRM ($80B+) where Salesforce, Microsoft, Oracle, SAP, and HubSpot all compete intensely.
- **Regulatory complexity creates natural barriers:** Building GxP-validated software requires specialized knowledge that horizontal tech giants lack. Salesforce's Life Sciences Cloud (launched Oct 2025) is a simplified product compared to Veeva's full suite -- it will take years to match Vault's depth.
- **IQVIA partnership (2025):** The fact that IQVIA chose to PARTNER with Veeva (interoperability) rather than compete head-on in software validates that the market is too small for both to build parallel platforms.

### Detailed Threat Analysis:

**1. Salesforce Life Sciences Cloud (Probability: Medium-Low)**
Salesforce launched Life Sciences Cloud in Oct 2025 and has won 3 top-20 pharma commitments vs. Veeva's 9. However, Salesforce's product is nascent and lacks the depth of Veeva's 15-year-old platform. The bigger risk was BEFORE the split -- now that Veeva is on its own platform (Vault), the Salesforce threat is diminishing. Salesforce's main advantage is its existing enterprise relationships and Agentforce AI, but pharma companies prioritize domain expertise and regulatory compliance over general AI capabilities.

**2. Per-Seat Pricing / SaaSpocalypse (Probability: Medium -- MOST WATCHED RISK)**
This is the most relevant structural threat. Veeva's CRM is priced per-user-per-month ($120-$150/user/month). If AI reduces the number of pharma sales reps (the primary CRM users), revenue-per-customer could decline even without churn. The Feb 3, 2026 SaaSpocalypse event wiped ~$285B from global SaaS valuations on this thesis.

**Mitigating factors:**
- Veeva CRM users are FIELD sales reps who visit doctors in person. AI cannot replace in-person physician engagement in the near term (regulatory and relationship reasons).
- Veeva is transitioning to PLATFORM pricing (Vault = per-module, not just per-seat). R&D Solutions (clinical, regulatory, quality) are priced per-module and per-study, which is usage-based, not headcount-based.
- R&D Solutions subscription revenue is now EXCEEDING Commercial Solutions (~$1.42B vs ~$1.25B projected FY2026). This means the per-seat CRM exposure is becoming a minority of revenue.
- Veeva AI Agents (launched Dec 2025) are priced on usage/outcome basis, not per-seat. This is Veeva proactively adapting its pricing model.

**3. Medidata/Dassault competition in R&D (Probability: Low-Medium)**
Medidata was the incumbent in EDC (electronic data capture) for clinical trials. Veeva has been winning share aggressively (8 of top-20 pharma now standardized on Vault EDC). Medidata's parent DSY.PA is dealing with its own challenges (-21% crash recently). The competitive direction favors Veeva in clinical.

**4. Regulatory change (Probability: Very Low)**
Life sciences regulation (FDA 21 CFR, EU GxP, ICH guidelines) is INCREASING, not decreasing. The EU AI Act classifies medical AI as "high-risk," requiring more compliance infrastructure. Every new regulation strengthens Veeva's moat because it makes the validated platform more valuable and the switching cost higher.

## Escenarios de Erosion

### Scenario 1: AI Eliminates Pharma Sales Reps (Most Probable Erosion Path)
**Probability: 15-25% over 10 years**
If AI enables pharma companies to engage physicians digitally (AI-driven virtual detailing, chatbots, personalized content delivery), the number of field sales reps could decline 30-50%. Since Veeva CRM is priced per-seat, this would directly compress CRM revenue. However, Veeva's R&D Solutions (now the majority of subscription revenue) are NOT per-seat, so total company impact would be moderate.

**Kill condition equivalent:** If Veeva's Commercial Solutions subscription revenue declines >10% year-over-year for 2 consecutive quarters without offsetting growth elsewhere.

### Scenario 2: Horizontal Cloud Giant Makes Serious Vertical Play (Tail Risk)
**Probability: 5-10% over 10 years**
If Microsoft or Google decided to build a dedicated life sciences cloud platform with GxP validation, they could theoretically leverage their AI and cloud scale. However, this would require 5-7 years of investment and regulatory trust-building. No horizontal player has shown willingness to make this commitment. The Salesforce attempt (Life Sciences Cloud) is the closest precedent, and it has achieved limited traction so far.
