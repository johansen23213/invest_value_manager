# Counter-Analysis: BME.L (B&M European Value Retail)

## Fecha: 2026-02-08

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The thesis "temporary trough in structurally advantaged discount retailer" is built on several assumptions that do not withstand scrutiny. The most damaging findings are: (1) the thesis claims ROIC >30% as evidence of model quality, but current ROIC is 12-14%, meaning the business is destroying far less value than a compounder and may barely exceed its WACC; (2) Home Bargains -- the closest peer -- grew profits 13% in the same period B&M's EBITDA dropped 30%, proving the problem is B&M-specific, not environmental; (3) the thesis underestimates the permanence of cost headwinds (NI, wages) and overestimates margin recovery; (4) there was a material accounting error and CFO departure in October 2025 that the thesis does not mention; (5) the fair value of 321p uses an 8x EV/EBIT multiple that is generous for a leveraged, turnaround situation with impaired governance. **This thesis does NOT survive adversarial scrutiny in its current form.**

---

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. "ROIC >30% proves the model works"

- **Thesis claim:** ROIC >30% historically demonstrates the business model is structurally sound.
- **Evidencia en contra:** Current ROIC is 12.07% (StockAnalysis.com, TTM), less than HALF the claimed historical figure. The thesis cites historical ROIC as if it is still operative. The 30%+ figure excluded goodwill and was measured during peak profitability. With EBITDA down 30%, current ROIC including goodwill is ~12%. B&M's WACC is 6.75-8% per the thesis's own calculation. At 12% ROIC vs 8% WACC, the spread is only 4pp -- this is NOT a compounder, it is a marginal value creator at current profitability levels. If margins do not recover as hoped, ROIC could approach WACC, making this a value-neutral business at best.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Recalculate ROIC with current earnings, not historical. If current ROIC spread vs WACC is <5pp, the margin of safety must compensate for this being a borderline business, not a proven compounder.

### 2. "Margin recovers to 9% by FY28"

- **Thesis claim:** EBITDA margin recovers from 7.7% (H1 FY26) to 9% by FY28, supported by shipping normalization and NI absorption.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - The UK employer NI increase from 13.8% to 15% is **permanent** -- the threshold stays at GBP 5,000 until at least April 2028, then only rises with CPI. This is NOT a one-time cost that gets "absorbed." It is a structural +1.2pp increase in the cost base for a labor-intensive retailer.
  - The minimum wage increased 6.73% in 2025 and has been on a political escalation path. No UK government will reverse this.
  - Two-thirds of retailers say they will raise prices -- but B&M's entire model is "everyday low prices." Raising prices erodes the value proposition vs Tesco/Asda. B&M has limited pricing power.
  - The thesis assumes shipping normalizes, but provides no evidence that container rates have actually declined. Even if they normalize, NI + wages are permanent structural headwinds worth GBP 30-40M/year.
  - Home Bargains achieved 7.9% sales growth AND profit growth of 13% in the SAME UK environment. This proves a well-run discount retailer CAN absorb these costs. B&M cannot because of execution failures, not macro.
  - No evidence of margin recovery START. H1 FY26 showed 7.7%. The thesis projects 8.5% for FY27 with zero evidence of inflection. Q1/Q2 FY26 LFL sales were flat.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The margin recovery assumption must be pressure-tested. A more realistic normalized margin is 8-8.5% (not 9%), which would reduce fair value by approximately 15-20%.

### 3. "Discount retail is defensive / gains share in downturns"

- **Thesis claim:** B&M is a "structurally advantaged discount retailer" that performs well in economic downturns.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - B&M's own numbers CONTRADICT this claim: EBITDA down 30%, LFL sales flat to slightly negative, guidance cut TWICE. If discount retail is defensive, why is B&M's EBITDA collapsing?
  - Home Bargains: Revenue +7.9%, profits +13%. This is what "defensive discount retail" looks like. B&M is NOT performing like a defensive business.
  - The thesis itself acknowledges this contradiction in the Autocritique section ("Narrative bias: 'Discount retail wins in downturns' but B&M's numbers show the OPPOSITE") yet proceeds to buy anyway. This is a red flag.
  - B&M's discretionary mix (garden, home decor, GM) is higher than Home Bargains (food-focused). Discretionary items are NOT defensive. B&M is partially a general merchandise retailer, not purely a discount essentials retailer.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Reclassify B&M as "partially cyclical general merchandise retailer" rather than "defensive discount retailer." The defensive narrative is WRONG for B&M specifically, even if true for the discount sector broadly.

### 4. "Fair value 321p is conservative"

- **Thesis claim:** Conservative fair value of 321p based on bear-weighted EV/EBIT and DCF.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - **Normalized EBIT is overstated.** The thesis uses GBP 535M (mid-range of 520-550M). But this includes FY21 (GBP 611M -- COVID-boosted year). COVID boost should be fully excluded. Without COVID years, average EBIT for FY22-FY25 is (535+614+571+481)/4 = 550M. But FY25 at 481M is the trend direction. If normalized EBIT is 480-520M (reflecting permanent cost step-up), the valuation changes materially.
  - **8x EV/EBIT is too generous.** The thesis awards an 8x multiple after adjustments net to zero. But B&M has: (a) elevated leverage (Debt/Equity 3.49, Altman Z-Score 2.5 -- below cautionary threshold), (b) a CFO who RESIGNED over an accounting error, (c) a "Back to Basics" turnaround that is unproven, (d) two profit warnings in 6 months, (e) multiple analyst downgrades (JPMorgan Underweight at 205p). For a leveraged turnaround with governance issues, 6-7x is more appropriate. Peers like Poundland/Pepco trade at 5-6x.
  - **Recalculated fair value at 6.5x EV/EBIT with EBIT 490M:**
    - EV = 490M x 6.5 = GBP 3,185M
    - Less net debt GBP 860M
    - Equity = GBP 2,325M
    - Per share = 233p
    - This gives MoS of (233-169)/233 = 27%, which is BELOW the 35% threshold for Tier C.
  - **Net debt is understated.** The thesis uses GBP 859M (ex-IFRS16). But total debt is GBP 2.48B and total liabilities due within 12 months are GBP 997M. The IFRS16-adjusted picture is far worse. Debt/equity ratio is 3.49.
- **Severidad:** **CRITICAL**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Revalue using 6-7x EV/EBIT with normalized EBIT of 480-510M. This gives a fair value range of 195-250p, with a midpoint of approximately 220-230p. At 169p entry, MoS would be 23-27% -- insufficient for Tier C.

### 5. "Action threat is manageable (<20 UK stores)"

- **Thesis claim:** Action has <20 UK stores and UK rollout will take 5+ years to scale.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - **Action is NOT in the UK at all.** Based on my research, Action operates in 14 European countries and is entering Croatia and Slovenia in 2026, but the UK is NOT one of their markets. The thesis claims "<20 UK stores" which appears to be FACTUALLY INCORRECT. If Action has zero UK stores, the threat is not "manageable" -- it simply does not exist yet.
  - HOWEVER, this cuts both ways. If Action IS planning UK entry (which some reports suggest), the REAL threat is that Action has 3,302 stores across Europe, opens 384+ per year, is backed by 3i Group (deep pockets), and has a proven model. When they DO enter the UK, they could scale rapidly. The thesis's dismissal of "5+ years to scale" ignores that Action opened 384 stores across Europe in a single year.
  - The more immediate competitive threat is Home Bargains, which is actively outperforming B&M with plans to grow from 632 to 800-1,000 stores.
- **Severidad:** **MODERATE** (factual error in thesis, but competitive threat is real regardless of Action timing)
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Correct the Action factual claim. Reframe competitive analysis around Home Bargains as the primary threat (already demonstrably outperforming B&M).

### Interpretacion:

**STRONG COUNTER.** The thesis has multiple serious problems:

1. **The central narrative is contradicted by peer evidence.** Home Bargains thrived in the same environment where B&M collapsed. This is a B&M-specific execution problem, not a "temporary trough."

2. **The valuation is inflated.** Using a more appropriate 6.5x multiple on lower normalized EBIT gives a fair value of ~230p, making the MoS at 169p only ~27% -- insufficient for a Tier C turnaround with governance issues.

3. **Material information omitted.** The CFO departure over an accounting error (October 2025) is not mentioned in the thesis (dated February 2026). This is a governance red flag that should increase the required MoS, not be ignored.

4. **The "ROIC >30%" anchor is misleading.** Current ROIC is 12%. Citing historical ROIC while current profitability has halved is intellectually dishonest.

5. **Cost headwinds are permanent, not cyclical.** The UK NI increase and minimum wage trajectory are structural. The thesis treats them as temporary.
