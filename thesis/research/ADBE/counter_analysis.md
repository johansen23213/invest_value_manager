## Resumen Ejecutivo

The thesis partially survives scrutiny but requires significant downward revision. Adobe's core financial engine (89% gross margin, 41% FCF margin, net cash) is genuinely strong and not under imminent threat. However, the thesis: (a) inflates fair value by 14% through an overly generous OEY method at excessive weight, (b) dismisses the ARR deceleration trend as "stable growth" when it is a persistent 5-quarter downtrend, (c) completely ignores two material legal proceedings, (d) underestimates the competitive threat from Canva/Affinity going free and Figma's IPO, and (e) overstates QS by 8 points (76 vs independent assessment of 68). The position (2 shares) is small enough to HOLD but should NOT be increased at current prices. The ADD trigger should be lowered from $250 to $220-230.

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. "AI strengthens Adobe, not disrupts it" -- Firefly generates $400M, 11% of new ARR

- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - Firefly's $400M direct revenue over 2024-2025 is ~1.7% of FY2025 total revenue ($23.77B). This is meaningful but NOT transformative.
  - AI-first ARR of $250M contributed 11% of Creative Cloud's NEW ARR -- but total ARR deceleration continued throughout all of FY2025 (12.3% -> 11.5%). Firefly adoption is not offsetting the broader growth slowdown.
  - Midjourney has an estimated $500M+ ARR and 15M users, growing faster than Firefly in pure AI image generation.
  - AI inferencing costs are structurally higher: cost of subscription revenue grew 13% vs 11% revenue growth, compressing margins. Adobe's non-GAAP operating margin guided down 120bps YoY to ~45% for FY2026 specifically because of AI scaling costs.
  - The core risk is not that Adobe fails at AI, but that AI commoditizes creative work, making "good enough" cheaper and reducing willingness to pay premium prices. Canva + free Affinity + ChatGPT image generation can produce 80% quality at 10% of the price.
  - Firefly's "commercially safe" training data advantage is now directly under legal challenge (SlimLM copyright lawsuit, Dec 2025).
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee should acknowledge that AI is a double-edged sword. The thesis narrative of "AI strengthens Adobe" is plausible but NOT proven by the data. ARR deceleration is the decisive metric; if Firefly were truly strengthening the business, ARR growth would be stabilizing or accelerating. It is not.

### 2. "Canva/Figma attack different markets -- pros vs amateurs"

- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - Canva acquired Affinity (professional-grade Photoshop/Illustrator/InDesign alternative) and relaunched it as FREE in early 2026. 1 million signups in the first 4 days. This is NOT attacking "amateurs" -- Affinity Designer/Photo are professional tools that compete directly with Adobe's core products.
  - Figma IPO'd at $20B valuation (July 2025) on $1.1B ARR growing 38% YoY. Figma dominates UI/UX design (80-90% market share) and is expanding into broader creative tools with AI capabilities.
  - Adobe abandoned Adobe XD in 2023, conceding the UI/UX design category entirely to Figma.
  - Adobe's Creative Cloud All Apps costs $720/year. Canva/Affinity is now $0 for core features. For budget-conscious professionals, freelancers, and small studios, this is a seismic shift.
  - The thesis claims "Adobe = professionals ($60/month); Canva = amateurs ($13/month)" but Affinity targets the SAME professional segment at $0/month.
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The thesis must update its competitive analysis. The "different markets" argument was plausible before Canva launched free Affinity. Now there is a legitimate free professional alternative. The committee should evaluate how many of Adobe's 41M subscribers are price-sensitive enough to consider switching. Even 5% churn would mean ~$1.2B revenue impact.

### 3. "Growth stable at 7-8%" from TAM 5% + pricing 2%

- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - ARR growth trend: Q1 12.3% -> Q2 11.8% -> Q3 11.7% -> Q4 11.5% -> FY2026 guidance 10.2%. This is a persistent, sequential deceleration over 5 data points. Calling this "stable at 7-8%" is misleading -- current growth is 10% but FALLING.
  - The thesis assumes TAM growth 5% + pricing 2% + market share 0% = 7%. But:
    - Pricing power is being challenged: Adobe raised prices up to 50% in 2025 (Creative Cloud Pro rebrand), generating massive user backlash. Bluesky posts drew 1,600+ hate comments. High-profile defections to Affinity (free) and alternatives.
    - Market share may be declining: Adobe's consensus analyst rating fell to 3.91/5 -- lowest since 2013. Five major analyst downgrades in 2025-2026.
    - CIO survey shows 16-point net decrease in sales/marketing budgets, directly hitting Digital Experience (24% of revenue).
  - KeyBanc downgraded to Underweight citing "flat net-new ARR" -- the most bearish metric for a SaaS business.
  - Consensus FY2026 and FY2027 growth: 9.5% and 9.0%. Even Wall Street's base case shows continued deceleration, not stability.
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Replace "Growth stable at 7-8%" with "Growth decelerating from 12% to ~9%, trajectory unclear." The projection framework should model growth deceleration, not stability. If growth stabilizes at 8-9%, the thesis works. If it continues to 6-7%, fair value drops materially.

### 4. Fair Value $394 (OEY $430 at 60%, DCF $340 at 40%)

- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - Valuation-specialist independently calculated FV $340 using 3 methods (DCF 40%, EV/FCF comps 30%, OEY 30%). This is -13.7% vs thesis.
  - Risk-identifier independently estimated FV $310-340 with central estimate $325. This is -17% vs thesis.
  - The OEY method at $430 uses 5.5% required yield. For a decelerating business with 5 analyst downgrades, legal overhang, and margin compression, 6.5-7.0% is more appropriate. At 7%: FV = $339.
  - The thesis weighted OEY at 60% and Reverse DCF at 40%. Reverse DCF is a diagnostic tool, not an independent valuation method. This methodology inflates the weighted average by giving excessive weight to the highest estimate.
  - No peer comparable analysis was performed in the thesis. Adobe trades at 16x P/E vs peer median of 28x -- a 42% discount. Even applying a 35% justified discount, fair EV/FCF multiple is ~13x, giving FV of $312.
  - Wall Street post-downgrade targets cluster $310-400. KeyBanc: $310 (Underweight). The thesis FV of $394 is at the upper end of even analyst estimates.
  - This is consistent with the adversarial pattern: 12 of 13 prior positions had inflated thesis FV (average -15%). ADBE at -14% to -17% fits the pattern exactly.
- **Severidad:** CRITICAL
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Revise thesis FV from $394 to $340. Adjust ADD trigger from $250 to $220-230. The current MoS at $268 is ~21% (vs thesis claim of 31%). This is still positive but thin for a contested Tier A/B business.

### 5. Bear case $260 provides floor

- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - Thesis bear uses 4% growth, 13% WACC, 2% terminal = $260. But a true bear scenario includes:
    - Margin compression from AI investment costs (non-GAAP OpMar guided down 120bps)
    - FTC settlement + forced one-click cancellation increasing churn
    - AI copyright liability ($500M-2B potential)
    - Growth deceleration below 5% if enterprise budgets shift structurally
  - Severe bear at 4% growth, 13% WACC: DCF = $241. At 10x EV/FCF: $240.
  - If ARR growth drops to 5-6% and P/E compresses to 12-14x on FY2025 GAAP EPS of $16.70: stock price = $200-234.
  - The thesis bear case is "base-in-disguise" -- it assumes only growth deceleration without any structural damage from legal, competitive, or margin risks.
- **Severidad:** MODERATE
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Revise bear case from $260 to $220-240. At current $268, the stock is NOT meaningfully above the true bear floor. This reduces the asymmetry of the trade.
