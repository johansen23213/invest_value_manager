## Top 3 Riesgos Criticos

### 1. ARR Deceleration and Flat Net-New ARR Guidance

- **Categoria:** Fundamental
- **Descripcion:** Adobe's ARR growth has decelerated sequentially every quarter of FY2025: Q1 12.3% -> Q2 11.8% -> Q3 11.7% -> Q4 11.5%. FY2026 guidance of 10.2% ARR growth continues this downtrend. KeyBanc cited "flat net-new ARR" as the primary reason for their downgrade to Underweight with a $310 target. This is the single most important metric for a SaaS business, and the trend is unmistakably negative.
- **Evidencia:**
  - FY2026 ARR guidance: 10.2% growth ($2.6B net new) vs FY2025 11.5%
  - KeyBanc downgrade to Underweight ($310 PT) citing flat net-new ARR
  - Piper Sandler downgrade to Neutral ($330 PT)
  - Oppenheimer downgrade citing "decelerating top-line growth and down YoY operating margin guidance"
  - 5 major downgrades in 2025-2026: Morgan Stanley, KeyBanc, Piper Sandler, Jefferies, Oppenheimer
- **Probabilidad:** Alta -- the deceleration is already happening, it is a fact, not a prediction
- **Impacto si materializa:** If ARR growth drops below 8%, the market will price Adobe at 12-14x earnings permanently. At $16.6 EPS (FY2025) x 12x = $199 per share. That is a further -26% downside from current $268. If growth stabilizes at 10%, fair value is more defensible.
- **Mitigante:** Firefly AI features, generative credit consumption tripling QoQ, freemium users growing 35% YoY to 70M. These are leading indicators that COULD re-accelerate growth, but have not yet translated into net-new ARR acceleration.
- **Kill condition?:** YES -- If ARR growth drops below 7% for 2 consecutive quarters, the "growth compounder" thesis is invalid. This should be added to the thesis.

### 2. Thesis Fair Value Likely Inflated (Adversarial Valuation Check)

- **Categoria:** Valoracion
- **Descripcion:** The thesis claims FV of $394 weighted ($430 OEY method at 60% weight, $340 DCF at 40% weight). My independent analysis finds this is inflated by 15-25%, consistent with the adversarial review pattern found in 12 of 13 prior positions (average inflation ~15%).
- **Evidencia for inflation:**
  - **OEY method ($430):** Uses 5.5% target OEY. But for a business with decelerating growth, 5.5% is too generous. A 6.5-7% OEY is more appropriate for a company where 5 analysts have downgraded in 6 months. At 6.5% OEY: FV = $9.73B / 0.065 / 410.5M shares = $365. At 7% OEY: FV = $339.
  - **DCF tool output ($421 base):** Uses 9% default WACC. The thesis correctly derives 12% WACC, giving $340. However, the thesis then weights the $430 OEY at 60%, pulling the weighted average UP. The DCF at 12% WACC is the more rigorous number.
  - **Reverse DCF reality check:** At $268, the market prices in ~4% perpetual growth. Adobe's actual growth is 10% but DECELERATING. The question is where it stabilizes. If 7-8%, fair value is $300-323. If it decelerates further to 5-6%, fair value is $275-299.
  - **Wall Street consensus:** Mean PT $419-432, but this includes stale targets from pre-downgrade analysts. Post-downgrade targets cluster around $310-400.
  - **Bear case too optimistic:** Thesis bear is $260 at 4% growth, 13% WACC, 2% terminal. But a true bear case would include margin compression from AI investment AND growth below 5%. A realistic bear: $220-240.
- **My adjusted fair value:** $310-340 (range), with $325 as central estimate. This represents a -17% haircut from thesis $394.
- **MoS at $268 vs my adjusted FV ($325):** 17.5% -- still positive but much thinner than thesis claimed 31%.
- **Probabilidad:** Alta -- every prior adversarial review found thesis FV inflated
- **Impacto si materializa:** The position was purchased at $270.60 avg. If true FV is $325, upside is 20% (acceptable but not exceptional). If true FV is $310, upside is only 15%. If bear case materializes ($220-240), downside is -11% to -18%.
- **Mitigante:** The stock is at $268, near 52w low of $264. Much of the bad news may be priced in. Even at my lower FV estimate, there is positive MoS.
- **Kill condition?:** NO -- the position is small (2 shares, ~4.7%) and even at my lower FV there is positive upside. But the thesis FV should be revised downward.

### 3. Multi-Front Legal Exposure (FTC + AI Copyright)

- **Categoria:** Legal/Regulatorio
- **Descripcion:** Adobe faces two distinct legal threats that the thesis barely mentions:
  - **FTC/DOJ lawsuit** over deceptive subscription practices (hidden early termination fees, difficult cancellation). A federal judge denied Adobe's motion to dismiss in May 2025. The FTC seeks permanent injunction and civil penalties. A class action for damages is also pending.
  - **AI copyright class-action** (December 2025) alleging Adobe used pirated books (via SlimPajama/Books3 datasets) to train its SlimLM model. This is Adobe's first major AI copyright challenge and could undermine its "commercially safe" AI narrative -- the very differentiator that gives Firefly its enterprise advantage.
- **Evidencia:**
  - FTC case: Judge Noel Wise ruled in May 2025 that cancellation process is "far from simple." Case proceeding to discovery/trial.
  - AI copyright: Proposed class action filed Dec 2025 by author Elizabeth Lyon. Anthropic settled similar suit for $1.5B.
  - One Adobe executive allegedly described the early termination fee as "like heroin" for retention.
  - If FTC wins, "one-click cancellation" mandate could spike churn among casual users.
- **Probabilidad:** Media-Alta (FTC case has survived dismissal; AI copyright is early stage but industry trend is plaintiff-favorable)
- **Impacto si materializa:**
  - FTC: Settlement of $500M-1B + mandatory changes to subscription practices. If forced "one-click cancellation" increases churn by 3-5pp, revenue impact = $700M-1.2B annually (3-5% of revenue). Stock impact: -10-15%.
  - AI copyright: If courts rule that training weights "encode infringing copies," Adobe may need to retrain models or pay licensing fees. Could cost $500M-2B. More importantly, it DESTROYS the "commercially safe" narrative that differentiates Firefly from Midjourney.
  - Combined worst case: -20-25% stock impact.
- **Mitigante:** FTC case may settle for modest amount. AI copyright law is unsettled; Adobe can argue fair use. Firefly was primarily trained on Adobe Stock (licensed content), not SlimLM.
- **Kill condition?:** YES (conditional) -- If AI copyright ruling mandates that Firefly itself (not just SlimLM) used infringing training data, this kills the "commercially safe AI" moat. Monitor closely.

### DCF Sensitivity

The DCF tool with default 9% WACC outputs $421 base FV. This is misleading for ADBE because:
- Adobe's beta is 1.51, well above market
- WACC derived from first principles = 12.1%
- Using 9% WACC overstates FV by ~24%

The thesis correctly uses 12% WACC for its own DCF ($340), but then weights the OEY method at 60% to arrive at $394. The OEY method itself uses a generous 5.5% target yield that is inappropriate for a decelerating business facing 5 analyst downgrades.

## Kill Conditions Sugeridas

In addition to the 5 kill conditions already in the thesis, I suggest adding:

1. **ARR growth drops below 7% for 2 consecutive quarters** -- this would mean the "growth compounder" thesis is invalid and Adobe is transitioning to a mature, low-growth business. At that point, a 16x P/E is not cheap; it is fair or expensive.

2. **FTC ruling mandates "one-click cancellation" AND churn increases above 12%** -- the current sub-10% churn is critical to the valuation. If the FTC forces easier cancellation and churn jumps, the revenue base deteriorates.

3. **AI copyright ruling finds Firefly training data includes infringing material** -- this would destroy the "commercially safe AI" moat that differentiates Adobe from Midjourney/Stable Diffusion. Enterprise customers would lose confidence.

4. **Operating margin contracts below 33% for 2 consecutive quarters** -- Oppenheimer flagged "down YoY operating margin guidance" for FY26. If margin compression becomes a trend (not a one-quarter investment), it signals the business model is under pressure.

5. **Figma captures >15% of Creative Cloud's professional user base** -- this would signal that the "different market" thesis is wrong and Figma is directly taking Adobe's core customers.

### Dudas/Incertidumbres
- **ARR re-acceleration vs continued deceleration:** The 70M freemium users and 3x credit consumption growth are genuinely strong leading indicators. - **FTC lawsuit outcome:** I cannot reliably estimate the financial impact. Settlement could be $100M (immaterial) or $1B+ with structural changes (material). The range is too wide to price precisely.
- **AI copyright legal landscape:** This is genuinely unprecedented. Courts could go either way. If the Anthropic $1.5B settlement sets a precedent, Adobe could face similar exposure. But Adobe's situation (SlimLM, not Firefly) may be distinguishable.
- **Is this a falling knife or a contrarian opportunity?** The stock is down 42% from highs with 5 analyst downgrades. Maximum pessimism often creates value (our thesis). But sometimes stocks are cheap for good reason. The ARR deceleration trend is the deciding factor.
