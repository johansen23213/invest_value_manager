## Business Understanding

### What Graco Does

Graco designs, manufactures, and markets systems and equipment used to manage fluids. Founded 1926 in Minneapolis, the company operates globally across three segments:

1. **Contractor ($1.07B, 48% of FY2025 revenue):** Spray equipment for applying paints, coatings, and textures. Sold to professional painting contractors and home centers. Market leader in airless spray technology.

2. **Industrial ($997M, 44%):** Equipment for automobile, wood, and general manufacturing. Includes sealant/adhesive application systems, finishing equipment, and fluid transfer systems. Broad-based global demand.

3. **Expansion Markets ($168M, 8%):** Newer applications including environmental systems, high-pressure valves, and electric motor applications. Growth segment with improving margins (+6pp in FY2025).

### Why Graco is Special

1. **Mission-critical, low-cost equipment:** Graco's equipment costs a fraction of the total project cost but failure means shutting down a paint line, manufacturing line, or lubrication system. Customers are EXTREMELY price-insensitive. This is the "toll booth" model.

2. **Aftermarket lock-in:** Once a contractor buys a Graco spray system, they buy Graco tips, filters, hoses, and parts for years. Repeat/replacement revenue is substantial.

3. **Regulatory-driven demand:** Environmental regulations increasingly require precision application (reduce overspray, VOC emissions). Graco's technology is the compliance solution.

4. **Asset-light, high-margin:** 53% gross margin, 27% operating margin, 24% FCF margin. Capex only $46M on $2.24B revenue (2% of sales). The business throws off enormous cash relative to capital employed.

5. **Decentralized R&D culture:** Graco spends 4% of revenue on R&D (vs 1-2% for peers) and operates with decentralized product divisions that act like mini-businesses. This produces a steady stream of new products and applications.

6. **Pricing power:** Gross margin expanded from 49.3% (2022 trough) to 53.1% (FY2024), with price realization exceeding raw material inflation. Management consistently passes through costs.

### Unit Economics
- **CAC:** Low -- Graco sells through distribution (specialty paint stores, home centers, OEM relationships). No direct sales force for most products.
- **LTV:** Very high -- a contractor using Graco spray equipment will buy replacement parts, accessories, and new units for 10-20+ years. The installed base creates perpetual aftermarket demand.
- **FCF Margin:** 24.4% (FY2024), 28.5% (FY2025 = $638M / $2.24B)
- **Capital intensity:** Asset-light. Capex/Revenue = 2% (FY2025). Maintenance capex ~ depreciation ~ $50M.

### Revenue Quality
- Repeat/replacement: ~40-50% of Contractor revenue, higher in Industrial
- New equipment: cyclical component, but offset by ongoing replacement cycle
- Aftermarket parts: high-margin, recurring
- M&A pipeline adds $100M+/year (Corob added ~$100M in 2025)
