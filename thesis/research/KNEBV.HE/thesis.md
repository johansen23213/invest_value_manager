## Business Understanding

### The Elevator Business Model: Razor/Blade

KONE operates in what is arguably one of the best business models in industrials: the elevator/escalator "razor and blade" model.

**How it works:**
1. **Install new equipment** (the "razor"): Win a bid for a new building's elevators/escalators. Margins are low (Otis shows 6.1% operating margin on New Equipment). This is the entry point.
2. **Lock in service contracts** (the "blade"): Once installed, the building owner needs 20-30+ years of maintenance. Switching elevator maintenance providers is extremely rare because:
   - Regulatory compliance requires certified technicians with proprietary knowledge
   - Safety liability -- a single failure means lawsuits and press
   - The OEM knows its own equipment best (proprietary parts, diagnostics, firmware)
   - Switching costs are real: new provider must learn the specific installation
3. **Modernization** (the "upgrade"): After 15-25 years, the equipment needs modernization (not just maintenance). The original installer has massive advantage.
4. **Repeat**: Every unit installed expands the installed base, which expands recurring service revenue, which expands cash flow.

**KONE's revenue mix (FY2025, estimated from data):**
- New Building Solutions: ~35-38% of revenue (declining share, flat/declining absolute)
- Service: ~42-44% of revenue (now the LARGEST segment, growing ~7-10% annually)
- Modernization: ~18-20% of revenue (double-digit growth, +15.5% at comparable FX)
- Service + Modernization = >60% of revenue and growing

**Unit economics:**
- Maintenance base: >1.8 million units under contract (2025)
- Connected units: >40% connectivity rate (IoT-enabled predictive maintenance)
- Average contract duration: 20-30 years
- Retention rate: Extremely high (industry-wide, estimated >90%)
- Each new unit installed = decades of recurring high-margin revenue

### China: The Risk and the De-Risking

**China was KONE's historical growth engine but is now a material headwind:**
- China property market "continued to face significant pressure with no signs of recovery"
- Greater China's share of order book declined from ~40% (Q1 2024) to ~30% (Q1 2025)
- China's share of sales dropped from ~30% to ~19% over the same period
- New Building Solutions in China declining (new housing starts collapsed)

**BUT the de-risking is already well advanced:**
- KONE has pivoted: China orders as % of total fell from ~40% to ~30% in one year
- Service doesn't depend on new construction -- the installed base is there and growing
- "Efforts to strengthen competitiveness in the residential segment paid off" (management)
- Asia-Pacific excluding China showed "excellent customer traction"

**My reading:** China risk is REAL but DECLINING in materiality as service mix increases. The installed base in China still needs maintenance regardless of new construction. The worst of the China headwind is likely behind us (30% of orders vs 40% a year ago). But recovery is NOT in my base case.

### Moat Assessment

**Moat: WIDE (21/25 estimated)**

Sources of moat:
1. **Switching costs (9/10):** Once an elevator is installed, switching maintenance provider is extremely difficult. Regulatory, safety, proprietary knowledge, and inertia all favor the incumbent. Retention rates >90%.
2. **Oligopoly structure (8/10):** 4 players control 65% of global market. Barriers to entry are massive: safety certifications, global service network, installed base advantage, R&D costs. No new entrant has successfully challenged the Big 4 in decades.
3. **Installed base flywheel (7/10):** Every unit sold expands the recurring revenue base. The installed base grows monotonically (buildings rarely demolish elevators). Modernization keeps units in the system for another 20-30 years.
4. **Network/data advantage (5/10):** 1.8M+ connected units generate data for predictive maintenance. More data = better algorithms = better service = justification for premium pricing. But this advantage is shared among Big 4.
5. **Brand trust (moderate):** In safety-critical infrastructure, building owners/managers strongly prefer established brands. KONE's Finnish engineering reputation is strong in Europe and Asia.

**Moat risk:** Near zero. No technological disruption threatens the business model. AI/IoT ENHANCES the moat (more connected units = more data = better predictive maintenance = higher retention). China-made elevators do not compete credibly in premium/commercial segments outside China.
