## Adjudication by Conflict

### 1. WACC: R1's 8.5% vs R2's 9.0-9.5%

**Resolution: 9.0%**

R1's beta = 1.0 is too low. Straumann IS a premium medtech company but it has:
- Significant emerging market exposure (China, LATAM) adding volatility
- CHF reporting currency amplifying translation effects
- Elective procedure sensitivity (not emergency healthcare)

R2's upper range of 9.5% is also excessive — Straumann has near-zero debt, #1 market position, and 30-year track record. The WACC for a company with 0.2x net debt/EBITDA and 71% gross margins should not be nearly as high as the tool's 12.6%.

**WACC 9.0%** = beta ~1.15, Swiss RF 1.5%, ERP 6.0%, plus minimal debt cost contribution. This respects R2's critique (not as low as 8.5%) while not penalizing a genuinely defensive business.

Impact: Modest — affects DCF cross-check but not the primary EV/EBIT method.

### 2. FCF Margin Normalization: R1's 15% vs R2's 12-13%

**Resolution: 13% (R2 wins this clearly)**

R2's argument is the strongest in the entire counter-analysis. The facts:
- 5-year declining trajectory: 21.7% → 16% → 14% → 12.6% → 11.1%
- No management guidance for FCF margin targets (only EBIT margin guided)
- Ongoing capex: Shanghai manufacturing, AXS digital platform, ClearCorrect B2B build-out
- "Heavy investment year" has been the narrative for 3 consecutive years

The R1 thesis assumed capex cycle normalization without management evidence. This is the definition of speculative. R2 correctly identified it as the load-bearing pillar.

**Normalized FCF margin: 13%** as base case (reflecting partial capex normalization, not full recovery). Upside scenario: 15% (requires demonstrated capex decline in FY2026-2027).

Impact: HIGH — reduces OEY from 3.0% to ~2.5% at current price, weakening the return case.

### 3. EV/EBIT Multiple: R1's 29x vs R2's 26-27x

**Resolution: 27x**

R1's 29x reflected quality premium. R2 correctly notes:
- Current market valuation is 21.4x EV/EBITDA vs 5-year average 33.4x
- European medtech is in a de-rating phase ("Great Rationalization")
- Multiple expansion back to historical levels is NOT the base case

However, R2's 26x is too punitive for a company that is:
- Global #1 with 35% market share
- 71% gross margins (well above sector median)
- Near-zero debt
- 29% insider ownership (founding family)

**27x EV/EBIT** is the right base case — below historical average, reflecting current sector sentiment, but above trough (22-24x) because Straumann's quality characteristics warrant a premium within the de-rated sector.

Impact: Moderate — reduces EV/EBIT FV by ~CHF 8 vs R1.

### 4. Gross Margin Trajectory

**Resolution: STRUCTURAL decline, not temporary. R2 substantially correct.**

600bps compression in 4 years (76.2% → 70.1%) with three additive and persistent drivers:
1. **China VBP**: Permanently lower ASPs in China (not reversible)
2. **Value segment growth**: Neodent/Anthogyr growing faster = deliberate strategic mix shift
3. **ClearCorrect**: Lower-margin orthodontics growing as % of revenue

R1's characterization of "mix, not pricing power loss" is partially correct — core implant GMs remain ~75%. But the MIX SHIFT IS THE STRATEGY, which means blended GM will NOT recover.

**Base case GM: 69.5-70.5%** (stabilizing at current levels, not recovering). Management guides EBIT margin expansion through operating leverage and SG&A efficiency, implicitly conceding GM is structurally lower.

**AMBER flag on kill condition**: Blended core GM at 70.1% is within 10bps of the 70% threshold. NOT triggered, but monitor closely with H1 2026 results (July).

### 5. China VBP Assessment

**Resolution: Somewhere between R1 and R2. NET NEUTRAL to slightly negative at 3-year horizon.**

R1 (temporary → volume recovery): Too optimistic. The math doesn't work yet — 40-45% ASP decline with only 25% volume increase is NET NEGATIVE for revenue per unit. Q4 2025 APAC -12.8% confirms ongoing weakness.

R2 (permanent structural impairment): Too bearish at the longer horizon. VBP does expand the addressable patient pool (220M potential vs 16M treated). The question is TIME — recovery needs 3-5 years to play out, not 1-2.

**My view**: China VBP is a NET NEUTRAL contributor over our 3-year investment horizon. Not the positive catalyst R1 expects, not the permanent impairment R2 fears. Model China contribution as FLAT (volume offset by ASP decline) for the base case. Bull case: net positive contribution starts 2028+.

### 6. Bear Case: R1's CHF 102.5 vs R2's CHF 85-90

**Resolution: CHF 92**

R1's bear case was unrealistically benign — too high a floor, using 24x on still-growing EBIT. R2's bear case at CHF 85-90 requires severe conditions (22x multiple + margin compression + tariff escalation) that represent a deep bear, not a moderate one.

**My bear case construction:**
- Revenue growth stalls: 1-2% CHF-reported (organic 5%, FX drag 3-4%)
- EBIT stagnates at FY2025 level: CHF 655M
- Multiple compresses to 23x (de-rating for stalled growth, but not trough for this quality)
- EV = 23x × CHF 655M = CHF 15,065M
- Equity = CHF 15,065M - CHF 165M = CHF 14,900M
- FV = CHF 14,900M / 159.4M = **CHF 93.5 → rounded to CHF 92**

This means at current CHF 94.16, the bear case is essentially AT the current price. Downside protection at current price is ZERO.

**Deep bear (tail risk, 10% probability):** 22x on CHF 640M = CHF 86. This aligns with the 52wL of CHF 83.

**FV = CHF 17,979M / 159.4M = CHF 112.8**

### Method 2: OEY Return Check (35% weight)

Using revised FCF margin 13%:
- Revenue 2027E: CHF 2,605M × 1.05^2 = CHF 2,872M (5% annual CHF-reported growth)
- Normalized FCF: CHF 2,872M × 13% = CHF 373M
- At FV CHF 110: Market cap = CHF 17.5B
- OEY = 373/17,500 = 2.13%
- Growth (CHF-reported sustainable): 5%
- Dividend yield: 1.1%
- **Total expected return at CHF 110 = 8.2%** (below 10% hurdle — tells me CHF 110 is fair value ceiling, not undervalued)

At CHF 95 (current): OEY = 373/15,100 = 2.47%. Total = 8.6%. Still below 10%.
At CHF 80 (proposed SO): OEY = 373/12,750 = 2.93%. Total = 9.0%.

**Note**: OEY method produces lower FV because it uses CHF-reported growth (5%) not organic growth (9%). The business compounds intrinsically at organic rates, but CHF reporting dampens measured returns. The truth is between — investors earn the organic economics but the stock is priced off CHF-reported numbers.
