## Business Understanding

### Model de Negocio

Deckers Outdoor is a premium footwear company operating a dual-brand platform:

**HOKA (~45% of revenue, ~$2.2B FY25):** Performance running and fitness footwear. The brand was founded in 2009 for ultramarathon runners, acquired by Deckers in 2013, and has grown from $23M to $2.2B in revenue. The core innovation is maximalist cushioning (thick soles, meta-rocker geometry) that has crossed over from performance running into lifestyle/athleisure. HOKA competes directly with Nike, On Holdings, Brooks, New Balance, and ASICS.

**UGG (~53% of revenue, ~$2.5B FY25):** Comfort/lifestyle footwear. UGG is the #1 premium comfort brand globally, built on sheepskin boots that have evolved into a broader lifestyle platform (slippers, sneakers, sandals). UGG is a CASH COW -- mature, high-margin, seasonal (Q3 holiday peak). Growth is low-to-mid single digits but highly profitable.

**Other brands (~2%):** Teva (outdoor sandals) and Ahnu being phased out. Koolaburra already wound down.

### Why is it cheap? The HOKA Deceleration Narrative

**The market's thesis (negative):**
1. HOKA growth is decelerating rapidly: 24% --> 18.5% --> 11% over recent quarters
2. "HOKA is the next Under Armour" -- hot brand fading to commodity
3. Tariffs will compress margins ($110M impact estimated FY26)
4. Consumer spending weakening, late-cycle risk
5. CEO Caroti is new (since Aug 2024), selling stock
6. On Holdings growing 43% vs HOKA's 11% -- competitive loss

**My counter-thesis:**
1. **Growth deceleration from 24% to mid-teens is NORMAL for a brand scaling from $2B to $3B.** Under Armour comparison is wrong -- UA had GM 47% and was losing market share. HOKA has GM 57% and is GAINING share (+2pp US road running in trailing 12m). The deceleration is base-effect math, not competitive failure.
2. **HOKA is NOT a fashion brand -- it has performance credibility.** Winners of major marathons wear HOKA. The Mach 7, Clifton 10, Bondi 9 are best-sellers because they PERFORM, not because they are trendy. This gives HOKA more durability than pure-fashion athletic brands.
3. **On Holdings' 43% growth is from a much smaller base (~$2B vs HOKA's $2.2B).** On's growth rate will decelerate the same way when it scales further. The comparison is misleading.
4. **Tariff impact has been REDUCED from $75M to $25M net impact.** Management has demonstrated ability to pass costs through pricing power. Gross margin guided at ~57% for FY26 (vs 57.9% FY25) -- only 90bps compression despite tariffs. Consumers are accepting price increases.
5. **UGG is underappreciated as a cash cow.** Low growth but massive margins and cash generation. UGG alone could support the current valuation.
6. **$1B+ annual buybacks at current depressed valuation = accretive capital allocation.** Management repurchased 8M shares for $813M in first 9 months at ~$100 average -- buying back stock at a discount to intrinsic value.

## Cyclicality Assessment

DECK is consumer discretionary -- inherently cyclical. However, several factors moderate the cyclicality:

1. **Affluent consumer focus.** HOKA ($150-200 shoes) and UGG ($100-250) target upper-middle to affluent consumers who are more recession-resistant.
2. **Fitness/wellness is defensible.** Running shoes are a replacement cycle product for active consumers. Unlike discretionary fashion, runners NEED new shoes every 300-500 miles.
3. **UGG provides stability.** UGG's comfort positioning makes it less cyclical than pure fashion brands. People buy comfort products in all economic environments.
4. **International diversification.** 35% international reduces dependence on US consumer cycle.

**BUT:** In a severe recession, ALL consumer discretionary suffers. Beta 1.15 confirms DECK moves with the market. Late-cycle positioning (Feb 2026, recession probability 20-40%) means timing is not ideal.

## META-REFLECTION

### Incertidumbres/Dudas
- **HOKA brand cycle duration is unknowable.** The biggest risk is that HOKA follows Under Armour's trajectory (peak 2016, then decline). I have reasoned that HOKA is more durable due to performance credibility, but this is a qualitative judgment, not a quantitative fact. No model can predict brand longevity.

While this is standard practice, it means there is no concentrated insider with conviction capital at risk. The institutional ownership at 98.6% provides some governance discipline but is not the same as a founder-operator with 10%+ ownership.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema
- **Brand momentum indicator:** The system lacks a systematic way to track brand health metrics (Google Trends, social media mentions, sell-through data). For brand-dependent companies like DECK, LULU, NKE, these leading indicators are critical. Consider adding a brand-health module to the screening tools.
- **Consumer cyclicality overlay:** The QS tool does not penalize cyclical businesses for their position in the business cycle. A Tier A consumer cyclical at late-cycle should be treated differently than a Tier A defensive at any point in cycle. Consider adding a cycle-adjustment modifier.

2. The $100-105 entry target is 16-20% below current price. Is it worth creating a standing order now, or should we wait for R4 approval and then set the order? Given the FY26 earnings in May could move the price significantly in either direction, timing matters.

### Anomalias Detectadas
- **ROIC of 106.3% is extraordinarily high.** This reflects the asset-light nature of the business (minimal goodwill, low fixed assets). While genuine, such high ROIC tends to mean-revert over time as competition increases and brand heat fades. The tool gives max points for ROIC spread, which is correct factually but may overstate forward durability.
- **FCF CAGR of 99.1% is distorted** by the base year (FY22 FCF was only $121M due to inventory build-up, not representative). True underlying FCF growth is closer to 25-30% which is still excellent but not 99%.
- **The reverse DCF shows the market implying only 2.5% FCF growth** which is extremely pessimistic. Either the market is very wrong (opportunity) or the market sees HOKA deceleration risk more clearly than historical data shows (risk). The 9.5pp gap between implied and my estimate is large enough to be interesting but not extreme enough to be a screaming buy.
