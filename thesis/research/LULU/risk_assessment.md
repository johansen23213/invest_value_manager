## QS Reassessment (Adversarial -- UPDATED)

The QS tool scores LULU at **80/100 Tier A** (legacy). The thesis claims 82/100. Here is what the tool misses or overstates:

### Financial Quality (Tool: 35/40 -- Adversarial: 27-30/40)

1. **ROIC spread +37.1pp is BACKWARD-LOOKING.** The tool's trailing 4-year ROIC trajectory (44.1% -> 37.4% -> 48.9% -> 46.7%) reflects the peak period when US comps were positive. With Americas revenue declining 2% and comps -5%, and $320M+ in tariff costs, forward ROIC will compress materially. Adjusted forward ROIC spread: 20-25pp (still excellent, but the trajectory is DOWN).
   - Score adjustment: 12-13/15 (not 15/15)

2. **FCF Margin 15% under threat.** FY2026 will see $320M minimum in tariff-driven margin compression (on ~$10.5-11B revenue = ~300bp). With operating margin already compressed to 18.5% in Q1 FY2026 (from 23.7% trailing), FCF margin could drop to 10-12%.
   - Score adjustment: 3-4/10 (not 5/10)

3. **Leverage 0.2x is misleading.** Net debt $727M looks benign, but operating lease obligations total $1.575B. As a retailer with 700+ stores, the real leverage picture is more like 1.3-1.5x including leases.
   - Score adjustment: 8/10 (not 10/10)

4. **Net adjustment: -5 to -8 points** (27-30/40 vs 35/40)

### Growth Quality (Tool: 25/25 -- Adversarial: 17-20/25)

1. **Revenue CAGR 19.2% is historical peak.** Forward reality: Americas (70% of revenue) declining. FY2025 guidance was CUT. "Power of Three x2" $12.5B target will be missed. Forward revenue growth is 4-7%, not 19%.
   - Score adjustment: 4-5/10 (not 10/10)

2. **EPS CAGR 25% is historical peak.** With $320M tariff headwind and operating margin compression, FY2026 EPS is expected FLAT to slightly down. Needham expects mid-single-digit EPS decline.
   - Score adjustment: 4-6/10 (not 10/10)

3. **GM Trend "Expanding" will reverse.** GM was 59.2% trailing, but tariffs (Vietnam 46%, Cambodia 49%) will compress this. Management REFUSED to give FY2026 margin guidance.
   - Score adjustment: 3-4/5 (not 5/5)

4. **Net adjustment: -5 to -8 points** (17-20/25 vs 25/25)

### Moat Evidence (Tool: 17/25 -- Adversarial: 14-17/25)

1. **GM Premium +24.2pp is the strongest signal.** Real and significant. However, DTC market share dropped from 30% to 24% in 10 months (Jan-Nov 2025). Alo went 8% to 14% in 2 months. GM CAN lag share loss by 2-3 years.

   - Score: 3-4/8

3. **ROIC Persistence fair at 7/7** given historical consistency.

4. **Net adjustment: -0 to -3 points** (14-17/25 vs 17/25)

### Capital Allocation (Tool: 3/10 -- Adversarial: 2-3/10)

## Top 5 Riesgos Criticos

### 1. Three-Way Governance Crisis: Wilson vs Elliott vs Board

- **Categoria:** Governance / Fundamental
- **Descripcion:** Lululemon faces an unprecedented governance situation with THREE competing power centers:
  - **The Board** (led by Executive Chair Marti Morfitt + Advent International representatives): Running the CEO search, interim co-CEOs Meghan Frank (CFO) and Andre Maestrini (CCO)
  - **Chip Wilson** (founder, ~9% stake): Launched proxy fight Dec 29, nominating Marc Maurer (ex-On Running co-CEO), Laura Gentile (ex-ESPN CMO), Eric Hirshberg (ex-Activision CEO). Demands Advent directors leave.
  - **Elliott Management** ($1B+ stake, ~5%): Advocating for Jane Nielsen (ex-Ralph Lauren CFO/COO) as CEO. Preparing to force "radical restructuring of board and product strategy."

  These three factions have DIFFERENT visions for the company's direction. The annual meeting (expected June 2026) will be the battleground.

- **Evidencia:**
  - CEO departed Jan 31, 2026 -- 11 days without permanent CEO and counting
  - Wilson proxy nominations filed Dec 29, 2025
  - Wilson demanded Advent board members leave (Jan 29, 2026)
  - Elliott disclosed $1B+ stake Dec 18, 2025
  - Elliott pushing Jane Nielsen as CEO candidate
  - "Get Low" product pulled Jan 20 under interim leadership -- quality control during vacuum
  - Bernstein downgraded citing "growing uncertainty"

- **Probabilidad:** **Alta** -- this is HAPPENING NOW, not hypothetical
- **Impacto si materializa (continued paralysis):** 20-35% additional downside
  - Strategic decisions frozen (who approves 2027 product strategy?)
  - Spring 2026 launch execution compromised by leadership distraction
  - Board instability if Wilson/Elliott win seats creates split governance
  - New CEO hire delayed or compromised by competing factions
  - Institutional investors exit due to governance uncertainty
  - ~15-25% of fair value at risk from governance discount alone

- **Mitigante:** Wilson's and Elliott's nominees are CREDIBLE retail operators. If either faction's CEO candidate is hired, it could be a positive catalyst. The proxy fight may ACCELERATE needed changes.
- **Kill condition?:** YES -- if no permanent CEO by July 2026 (6 months post-McDonald), OR if proxy fight results in fragmented board with no clear strategic authority.

### 2. Tariff Margin Destruction: FAR Worse Than Thesis Claims

- **Categoria:** Financial / Geopolitical
- **Descripcion:** The thesis characterizes tariff impact as "marginal (~100bp GM)" and "Low" impact. This is **SEVERELY understated by at least 3x.** The actual situation:
  - **Supply chain exposure:** 40% Vietnam (46% tariff), 17% Cambodia (49% tariff), 11% Indonesia (32% tariff), ~30% China/Sri Lanka (various rates)
  - **FY2025 tariff impact:** $240 million gross profit reduction
  - **FY2026 tariff impact:** $320 million minimum operating margin reduction
  - **De minimis removal impact:** Additional ~$170M (170bp of 220bp tariff-related decline)
  - **Total annualized impact:** $320M on ~$11B revenue = ~300bp margin compression
  - **Key risk:** These tariff rates are CURRENT. Further escalation is a live risk (world view: "Incertidumbre comercial global ALTA, probabilidad 60-70%")

- **Evidencia:**
  - Lululemon's own FY2026 guidance: $320M net tariff impact
  - Management REFUSED to give FY2026 operating margin guidance -- will update March 31
  - Vietnam tariffs jumped to 46% -- Lululemon's LARGEST manufacturing country
  - Operating margin already compressed to 18.5% in Q1 FY2026 (from 23.7% trailing)
  - Supply chain diversification to alternatives takes 12-24 months
  - "Cannot raise prices aggressively during a fragile spending environment" -- management
  - De minimis removal specifically strains LULU's Canada-to-US e-commerce fulfillment (2/3 of US e-commerce orders)

- **Probabilidad:** **Alta** (tariffs already enacted) + **Media** for further escalation
- **Impacto cuantificado:**
  - Base case: 300bp margin compression ($320M)
  - If Vietnam tariffs stay at 46% (vs prior 10%): additional $150-200M impact on top of guidance
  - Total worst case: 450-500bp operating margin compression
  - Operating margin could fall from ~23.7% to 18-19% (Q1 FY2026 already at 18.5%)
  - FCF margin drops to 10-11% from 15%
  - DCF impact: reduces fair value by 20-30%

- **Mitigante:** Pricing power (GM 59.2% suggests room), vendor negotiations, network optimization. But management said they cannot price aggressively.
- **Kill condition?:** SHOULD ADD -- "Operating margin falls below 18% for 2 consecutive quarters."

### 3. US Business: Structural Decline Masked as Cyclical

- **Categoria:** Fundamental
- **Descripcion:** The thesis asserts US weakness is CYCLICAL (product staleness) not STRUCTURAL (moat erosion). The evidence is more ambiguous than the thesis admits:
  - Americas comps: -5% in Q3 2025 (accelerating decline)
  - DTC market share: dropped 30% to 24% in 10 months
  - Alo Yoga: grew 8% to 14% DTC share in just 2 months
  - Vuori: raised $825M at $5.5B valuation, targeting 100 stores by 2026
  - TWO product launches failed on basic quality (Breezethrough 2024, Get Low 2026)
  - SG&A deleveraging: 39.8% in Q1 FY2026 vs 35.5% FY2025

  **The critical question:** GM can lag market share loss by 2-3 years. Nike maintained its GM even as it was losing share before eventually compressing. If DTC share erosion continues, GM compression follows with a lag. The thesis uses current GM as proof the moat is intact -- but it may be a lagging indicator.

- **Evidencia:**
  - Among high-income consumers ($200k+), Vuori's share rose from 11.87% to 15.31% (2023-2025). This is LULU's core demographic being poached.
  - Alo Yoga seeking $10B valuation (was $4B in 2023) -- competitor is scaling fast
  - Inventory up 11% vs revenue growth 6.5% -- supply outpacing demand
  - UBS warns US sales "may not turn sustainably positive in 2026"
  - Bernstein downgrade: "delayed product strategy" carries "increased risk"
  - Lululemon pulled its own "Get Low" launch due to see-through fabric -- basic quality failure

- **Probabilidad:** **Media-Alta** (40-50%)
- **Impacto si materializa:**
  - Americas revenue declines 3-5% annually for 3+ years
  - GM compresses 200-400bp as pricing power erodes
  - P/E re-rates to 8-10x (closer to Under Armour territory)
  - Fair value: $110-140 (35-45% below current price)

- **Mitigante:** Spring 2026 product refresh (35% newness) is the real test. If it succeeds, structural narrative weakens. But note: this is a HOPE, not evidence. And the last two product launches FAILED.
- **Kill condition?:** Should ADD: "DTC market share falls below 20%"

### 4. Serial Product Quality Failures Signal Execution Breakdown

- **Categoria:** Fundamental
- **Descripcion:** Two premium product launches failed on BASIC quality in 12 months:
  - **Breezethrough (2024):** Pulled from stores due to sizing/fit issues. Lululemon's attempt to compete with Alo's "Airlift" line failed.
  - **Get Low (January 2026):** Pulled from online sales within days of launch due to see-through fabric. Customers complained about sheerness. Founder Chip Wilson publicly expressed outrage.

  The thesis frames the product refresh as a CATALYST. But the last two times LULU tried to innovate, they shipped defective product. This is not "staleness" -- it is an EXECUTION problem. And it is happening while there is no permanent CEO and interim leadership is distracted by proxy fights.

- **Evidencia:**
  - Bloomberg reported Get Low pulled Jan 20, 2026
  - Lululemon "blames customers" narrative re-emerged (echoing the 2013 see-through pants debacle)
  - Wilson specifically cited product quality as reason for proxy fight
  - Spring 2026's 35% newness comes from "untested designer in higher-risk lifestyle categories" (Bernstein)
  - The creative team was recently refreshed -- new team has not proven itself

- **Probabilidad:** **Media** (30-40%) that Spring 2026 launch has quality issues
- **Impacto si materializa:**
  - Another product recall destroys the "product refresh as catalyst" narrative
  - Brand credibility with core customers erodes
  - Stock could drop 10-15% on news of a third quality failure
  - Validates the "structural, not cyclical" bear thesis

- **Mitigante:** Management is aware and investing in quality. New creative team may have learned from failures.
- **Kill condition?:** Should monitor: if Spring 2026 launch has material quality issues, re-evaluate entire thesis.

### 5. Elliott Management's "Radical Restructuring" Push

- **Categoria:** Governance / Valoracion
- **Descripcion:** Elliott Management ($1B+ stake, ~5% of company) is not a passive investor. Reports indicate Elliott is:
  - Pushing Jane Nielsen (ex-Ralph Lauren) as CEO
  - Preparing to force "radical restructuring" of board and product strategy
  - Planning to demand cost reductions and "disciplined approach to inventory management"
  - Expected to force "strategic update calls" in February and March

  Elliott's activist playbook typically involves: (a) aggressive cost-cutting, (b) margin improvement programs, (c) leadership changes, (d) potential divestitures or strategic alternatives. While this CAN create value, it can also:
  - Cause short-term disruption
  - Conflict with brand-building investments
  - Create tension with other factions (Wilson, existing board)

- **Evidencia:**
  - CNBC: Elliott "preparing to force radical restructuring"
  - Elliott controlling ~5% of shares
  - Elliott's track record: aggressive, short-term focused
  - Competing with Wilson's vision (both want different CEOs)

- **Probabilidad:** **Alta** (Elliott is already engaged)
- **Impacto si materializa:**
  - Positive: cost discipline, margin improvement, better CEO
  - Negative: brand damage from cost-cutting, internal conflict, leadership instability
  - Net impact: uncertain, could be positive or negative
  - Risk: if Elliott and Wilson are BOTH fighting the board, paralysis deepens

- **Mitigante:** Elliott has resources and expertise. Their CEO candidate (Nielsen) is experienced.
- **Kill condition?:** Monitor: if Elliott pushes for actions that damage brand equity (excessive cost-cutting in product quality, marketing reduction), reassess.

- **China risk.** The thesis treats China as the growth savior. But world/current_view.md says "Evitar China." Chinese TikTok factory knockoffs specifically targeting LULU are going viral -- this could undermine brand exclusivity in LULU's fastest-growing market.
- **NikeSkims launch.** Nike partnering with Kim Kardashian's Skims brand for premium women's athleisure in Spring 2026 is the first time Nike has DIRECTLY targeted LULU's core customer with a comparable product. Nike has $95B market cap and unlimited marketing budget.

### Discrepancias con Thesis
1. **QS overstated by 10-20 points (Tier A vs Tier B).** Largest QS inflation we have seen.
2. **Tariff impact understated by 3x+.** Thesis says ~100bp, actual $320M+ = ~300bp minimum.
3. **Fair value inflated by 27-36%.** Thesis $261 vs adversarial $167-190.
4. **Governance risk upgraded from "manageable" to CRITICAL.** Three-way fight, not just "CEO transition."
5. **15 material risks not in thesis.** Most of any adversarial review.
6. **Bear case too optimistic by $60-90.** Thesis $190 vs adversarial $100-130.
7. **NVO comparison is flawed.** NVO has pharmaceutical moat (patents, FDA, manufacturing complexity). LULU has brand + community -- BOTH of which CAN be eroded, as Alo demonstrates. These are not equivalent moats.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema
1. **Thesis FV should be updated IMMEDIATELY** to reflect tariff escalation and governance developments since Feb 5.
2. **Consider PROBATION status.** The adversarial FV ($167-190) is essentially at current price ($179), offering no meaningful margin of safety for a HIGH-risk position.
3. **Set monitoring triggers for Q4 earnings (March 31):** Operating margin, Americas comps, CEO search update, FY2026 margin guidance.
4. **Create governance risk sub-score.** QS tool does not capture CEO vacuums, proxy fights, or board instability. LULU proves this is material.
5. **DCF tool should accept manual FCF overrides.** For companies with known structural cost increases (tariffs), using historical FCF misleads.

### Preguntas para Orchestrator
1. **Given adversarial FV of $167-190 and current price $179, the MoS is 0-6%.** For a Tier B company (adversarial QS 60-70) with 11 HIGH/CRITICAL risks, is this sufficient? Precedents suggest Tier B needs 25-30%+ MoS. Should we EXIT?
2. **Should the position be moved to PROBATION?** The adversarial review found materially more risk than any prior review (15 missed risks, 27-36% FV inflation, worst of 13 reviews).
3. **Should we set a tight stop-loss before Q4 earnings (March 31)?** If earnings reveal margin guidance worse than $320M, or CEO search has no progress, or Spring 2026 sell-through disappointing, the stock could drop 15-25%.
4. **How much weight to give the activist optionality?** Wilson and Elliott both bring strong CEO candidates. The proxy fight is messy but could result in BETTER leadership. Is there a way to quantify this upside option against the governance risk downside?
