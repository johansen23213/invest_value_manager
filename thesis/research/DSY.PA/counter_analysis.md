# Counter-Analysis: DSY.PA (Dassault Systemes SE)

## Fecha: 2026-02-11

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The thesis for DSY.PA presents a compelling narrative -- a wide-moat quality compounder temporarily punished by a cloud transition and cyclical weakness. However, independent investigation reveals **significant weaknesses** in the thesis that go beyond what the analyst acknowledges. The QS adjustment from 70 to 78 is partially justified but inflates the tier classification at a borderline where it matters most. The Adobe parallel is superficially attractive but materially flawed in key dimensions. The "compounder" narrative is strained at 3.2% revenue CAGR with flat EPS guidance. The EUR 21.00 FV, while already conservative vs consensus, may still be too generous given that growth deceleration is a multi-year TREND, not a one-quarter anomaly. Medidata appears increasingly like a value-destroying acquisition. Most critically, the thesis lacks a near-term catalyst -- this could be dead money for 12-24 months even if the moat is wide.

**Overall Assessment: MODERATE-STRONG COUNTER.** The thesis survives on moat quality but is weakened materially on growth, valuation, and timing.

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. "The cloud transition is the Adobe parallel" (Severity: STRONG)

- **The thesis claims:** DSY.PA is going through the same cloud transition pain that Adobe did in 2012-2014, and the stock will eventually recover as Adobe did (from $20 to $700).

- **Evidence against:**

  The Adobe parallel has critical structural differences that the thesis glosses over:

  **a) Speed of transition.** Adobe completed its cloud transition in approximately 3 years (2012-2015). By 2015, growth had accelerated to 19% CAGR. Dassault has been talking about 3DEXPERIENCE cloud since 2012 -- that is **14 years**. The platform is NOT new. 3DEXPERIENCE Cloud grew only +32% in FY2025 on a small base. More damningly, 3DEXPERIENCE platform revenue was DOWN 3% in Q4 2025 -- the flagship next-gen product is shrinking.

  **b) Nature of customers.** Adobe's customers are creative professionals -- individuals and small teams who can switch tools in weeks. Dassault's customers are aerospace/automotive enterprises with 18-36 month migration cycles. This means Dassault's transition will be MUCH slower than Adobe's. But the thesis uses Adobe as the upside precedent without discounting for this fundamental difference.

  **c) Adobe's revenue trajectory.** Adobe went from 13% average revenue growth (FY06-FY12) to a brief dip, then 19% average (FY15-FY23). Dassault has gone from 10% organic (2021-2022) to 7% (2023) to 5% (2024) to 4% (2025) to guidance of 3-5% (2026). This is a DECELERATING trajectory, not a transition trough. Adobe's revenue accelerated within 3 years. Dassault's has decelerated for 4 consecutive years.

  **d) Total addressable market.** Adobe's TAM expanded massively with cloud (from professional designers to "everyone with a subscription"). Dassault's TAM does not expand the same way -- PLM software is inherently a B2B enterprise product used by engineers. Cloud deployment does not create new user categories for CATIA the way Creative Cloud created new users for Photoshop.

  **e) ARR growth is underwhelming.** If the cloud transition were working as the thesis suggests, ARR growth should be accelerating. Instead, ARR growth has been stuck at 6% since late 2023 (Jefferies). For context, Adobe's subscription revenue grew at 44% CAGR during its transition. 6% ARR growth for a "cloud transition" company is a red flag, not a transition indicator.

- **Severidad:** **STRONG** -- The Adobe parallel is the emotional heart of the thesis ("it went from $20 to $700"). Demonstrating that the parallel is materially flawed weakens the most compelling bull narrative.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** The investment committee should strip out the Adobe parallel entirely from the thesis and evaluate DSY.PA on its own merits: a wide-moat, low-growth enterprise software company. The question becomes whether 3-5% growth justifies the current multiple, not whether "this is the next Adobe."

### 2. "Revenue growth deceleration is cyclical, not structural" (Severity: STRONG)

- **The thesis claims:** Q4 miss is cyclical. Core business grew +6%. The weakness is in Medidata (-2%) and SOLIDWORKS (+2%), both cyclically depressed.

- **Evidence against:**

  The deceleration is a 4-year TREND across ALL segments:
  - FY2023: +7% cc
  - FY2024: +5% cc
  - FY2025: +4% cc
  - FY2026 guide: +3-5% cc (midpoint 4%)
  - Q4 2025: +1% cc (worst quarter in recent memory)

  This is not a one-quarter miss. This is a multi-year pattern of slowing growth. The thesis attributes it to "cloud transition cannibalization" and "European auto weakness," but:

  **a) 3DEXPERIENCE platform was DOWN 3% in Q4.** This is the growth engine. If the growth engine is shrinking, the transition is not working as claimed.

  **b) Recurring software revenue grew only 3% in Q4, below 5-8% guidance.** This is the subscription/cloud component -- the part that should be ACCELERATING during a transition. It is not.

  **c) License revenue fell 7% in Q4.** The thesis says this is expected (transitioning to subscription). But if subscription is growing 3% and license is falling 7%, the net effect is deceleration, not transition.

  **d) Management itself guided flat EPS for FY2026 (EUR 1.30-1.34 vs EUR 1.31 actual).** Zero EPS growth for a "quality compounder" Tier A is a contradiction in terms.

  **e) JPMorgan called it "worse than even the most negative had feared."** When the bearish buyside consensus of 2% growth is exceeded to the downside, the market is not overreacting -- it is recalibrating.

- **Severidad:** **STRONG** -- If growth is structurally stuck at 3-4%, the entire "compounder" thesis falls apart. A 3-4% grower with 84% gross margins is a high-quality mature business, not a compounder. It deserves a mature-business multiple (12-15x non-IFRS EPS = EUR 16-20), not a compounder multiple.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee should require evidence of growth re-acceleration (at least one quarter of >5% revenue growth) before classifying DSY.PA as a "compounder." Until then, it should be treated as a mature Tier B business.

### 3. "QS adjustment from 70 to 78 is justified" (Severity: MODERATE)

- **The thesis claims:** +8 points for market position (from 0/8 to 8/8) is a straightforward data gap correction, not subjective inflation.

- **Evidence against:**

  The +8 adjustment is partially justified but has problems:

  **a) The adjustment moves DSY from Tier B to Tier A.** This is not a minor correction -- it changes the entire investment framework. At Tier B, DSY requires 20-25% MoS (per precedents). At Tier A, it requires only 15-30% MoS. The current 18% MoS at EUR 17.77 fails Tier A precedents (all prior Tier A entries were 29-38%) but passes a liberal Tier B threshold. The tier classification drives the decision.

  **b) Market position score inflation pattern.** The adversarial program (Sessions 48-52) found that 12/13 positions had inflated QS. The pattern was always the same: adjust QS upward to reach a higher tier. The analyst explicitly acknowledges this pattern but claims "the tool gives 0/8 here, the opposite problem." However, 0/8 is the correct tool output when the tool cannot verify market position. The ADJUSTMENT should be conservative (5-6/8 rather than 8/8) given the adversarial learnings.

  **c) At 78, DSY is the weakest Tier A entry ever for this portfolio.** Every other Tier A position has QS 79-82. DSY at 78 with 3.2% revenue CAGR and flat EPS guidance has a qualitatively different growth profile than ADBE (76, double-digit growth), NVO (82, GLP-1 market leader), or LULU (82, international expansion). The QS framework captures historical quality but not forward momentum. A forward-adjusted QS would be lower.

  **d) Growth quality is 10/25.** This is the single weakest category, reflecting the core problem: DSY has wide moat quality but lacks compounder growth. A company with 10/25 growth quality does not belong in the same Tier A category as ADBE (which would score higher on growth).

- **Severidad:** **MODERATE** -- The market position adjustment is factually defensible (DSY IS #1 in PLM). But the QS Tool-First protocol (Error #43) exists precisely because analysts inflate QS at tier boundaries. A more conservative adjustment of +5 (market position 5/8 reflecting dominance but with honest uncertainty about Medidata's competitive position dragging "company-wide" market leadership) would give QS 75 -- still borderline Tier A but without the appearance of inflation.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee should evaluate DSY at QS 70-78 range and decide whether to treat it as Tier A (borderline, requiring upper-end MoS) or Tier B (requiring 20-25% MoS, which the current price barely provides).

### 4. "Medidata is transitioning, not failing" (Severity: STRONG)

- **The thesis claims:** Medidata is being pivoted from clinical-trial-dependent to enterprise PLM for life sciences. Enterprise life sciences grew +3% within the segment.

- **Evidence against:**

  **a) Medidata was acquired for EUR 5.8B in 2019. Adjusted for inflation, segment growth has been "very modest" over 5+ years** (MarketScreener). Life Sciences revenue declined 6% at current FX in FY2025. Medidata specifically declined 7% in Q4 recurring revenue.

  **b) Veeva is eating Medidata's lunch.** Veeva Vault EDC has been gaining ground and now Veeva's R&D Solutions subscription revenue is projected to exceed Commercial Solutions revenue in FY2026. Veeva has ~80% global market share in life sciences CRM and growing clinical trial capabilities. Medidata's historical dominance (70% of FDA-approved novel drugs developed on Medidata) may be a legacy position, not a growing one.

  **c) Siemens acquired Dotmatics for $5.1B in 2025.** This is a direct attack on Dassault's life sciences position. Siemens is cross-pollinating its industrial AI and digital twin solutions into life sciences -- exactly what Dassault claimed Medidata would do. Now Dassault faces a competitor doing the same thing with more resources.

  **d) Pharma CRO spending cuts are not just cyclical.** Biotech funding has been challenged since 2022. Clinical trial starts have declined. While this is partially cyclical, there is also a structural shift toward decentralized trials and AI-assisted drug design that may permanently reduce the volume of traditional clinical trials Medidata was built to serve.

  **e) Goodwill risk is material.** Medidata accounts for the vast majority of DSY's EUR 11.6B goodwill (~70% of equity). If Medidata revenue continues declining 5-7% annually and Veeva/Siemens take share, an impairment charge is increasingly likely. This would not destroy cash flow but would be a confidence-shattering event.

  **f) The "enterprise PLM for life sciences" pivot is unproven.** Management has been pitching this pivot for 2+ years. Enterprise life sciences growing +3% within a segment that is declining -6% overall means the core Medidata clinical trials business is declining even faster (perhaps -8 to -10%). The pivot is not offsetting the decline.

- **Severidad:** **STRONG** -- Medidata represents ~15-17% of DSY revenue and was acquired for EUR 5.8B. If it continues declining, it is a value-destroying acquisition that permanently impairs ROIC on invested capital. The thesis rates Medidata decline probability at 35% -- I rate it 50-60% based on competitive evidence.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee should model a scenario where Medidata is written down to zero growth (value = current revenue stream at 0% growth discounted). If the thesis still works with Medidata as "dead weight" rather than "growth contributor," it becomes more defensible.

### 5. "Fair value of EUR 21.00 is deliberately conservative" (Severity: MODERATE)

- **The thesis claims:** FV of EUR 21.00 is anchored to the DCF conservative case, well below Morningstar (EUR 32.50) and consensus (EUR 31.10).

- **Evidence against:**

  **a) DCF sensitivity is 69-78% with TV >72%.** The thesis acknowledges this makes DCF "UNRELIABLE as a point estimate." Yet DCF carries 50% weight in the reconciliation. Why give 50% weight to a method you acknowledge is unreliable?

  **b) The 5% growth base case may be too optimistic.** The DCF base case uses 5% growth, but the company just guided 3-5% for FY2026 with flat EPS. Q4 delivered 1%. Using 5% as "base" means the base case is the TOP of guidance. A truly conservative base case should use 3-4% growth, which yields EUR 18.50-19.29 per the thesis's own DCF runs.

  **c) EV/EBIT of 14x is debatable.** The thesis uses 14x EV/EBIT as "conservative vs 15x midpoint." But for a company growing 3-4% with flat EPS, the appropriate multiple is closer to 11-13x. The thesis argues Dassault deserves 15-17x because of quality -- but quality premium with no growth premium should be more like 12-14x. PTC at 21x is growing 8-10%. Dassault at 14x growing 3-4% gives Dassault a higher "multiple per point of growth" than PTC.

  **d) The WACC of 7.0-8.5% is generous.** The thesis uses a base WACC of 6.5% (from tool) and adds 0.5-2.0pp for execution risk, yielding 7.0-8.5%. But with 7 HIGH/CRITICAL risks, 2 CRITICAL risks, and a data breach, the risk premium should be higher. Using 8.5-9.5% WACC (adding 2-3pp for the elevated risk profile), the DCF FV drops to EUR 17-19.

  **e) If we use the risk-identifier's probability-weighted FV.** The independent risk assessment produced a probability-weighted FV of EUR 22.51 with bull (20%), base (50%), bear (30%). But the risk-identifier assigned 30% to bear vs the thesis's 25%. Using the risk-identifier's probabilities: (0.20 x 28) + (0.50 x 21) + (0.30 x 16) = EUR 20.90. The gap between EUR 20.90 and EUR 21.00 is negligible, but the key issue is that the bear case at EUR 16.00 is only 10% below current price. The asymmetry is poor for a Tier A entry.

  **f) IFRS P/E of 20.4x is not cheap for a 0-4% grower.** The thesis focuses on non-IFRS P/E of 13.6x, but the EUR 429M in non-IFRS adjustments (SBC + Medidata amortization) are REAL costs. SBC dilutes shareholders. Amortization reflects the ongoing cost of an underperforming acquisition. At IFRS P/E 20.4x with flat growth, DSY is fairly valued, not cheap.

- **Severidad:** **MODERATE** -- The EUR 21.00 FV is not wildly wrong -- it is within a reasonable range. But a truly conservative FV, given the growth headwinds, should be EUR 18.50-20.00. At EUR 17.77, this means MoS is 4-12% vs a genuinely conservative FV, not the 18% the thesis claims.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee should recalculate FV using (a) 3-4% growth base case, (b) 8.5-9.0% WACC, (c) 12-13x EV/EBIT. This would yield FV approximately EUR 18.50-19.50, with MoS of 4-10% at current price -- clearly insufficient for a Tier A entry.
