## STEP 8: SaaSpocalypse IMPACT ON DSY.PA SPECIFICALLY

### Is DSY.PA a SaaSpocalypse victim or a beneficiary?

**Key distinction:** DSY.PA is NOT per-seat commodity SaaS.

| Factor | Commodity SaaS (CRM, HR) | DSY.PA (PLM) |
|--------|--------------------------|--------------|
| Pricing model | Per seat/user | Per design workspace + subscription |
| AI impact | AI agents REPLACE seats | AI ENHANCES simulation (net positive) |
| Switching cost | Low-Medium | EXTREME (18-36 month migration) |
| Customer base | Broad enterprise | Aerospace/Auto engineering |
| Revenue at risk from AI | 20-40% (seat reduction) | <5% (pricing power intact) |

**My assessment:** DSY.PA was caught in the SaaSpocalypse NARRATIVE but is fundamentally DIFFERENT from the companies being disrupted:

1. **CATIA is not seats-based.** You do not license CATIA "per sales rep" the way you license Salesforce. You license it per engineering workstation, and aircraft design requires MORE engineering, not less, as products become complex.

2. **AI is net positive for PLM.** The NVIDIA partnership validates this. AI-accelerated simulation INCREASES the value of Dassault's software (faster iteration, more accurate physics models). It does not replace it.

3. **KC#7 (per-seat erosion >10%) does NOT apply to DSY.PA.** This kill condition was designed for true SaaS companies (ROP's verticals, ADBE Creative Cloud seats, PAYC per-employee). DSY's licensing model is different.

4. **The narrative contagion IS real for the stock.** Investors selling "European software" indiscriminately have hit DSY alongside SAP, Sage, Temenos. This creates the opportunity -- the baby thrown out with the bathwater.

**Bottom line:** The SaaSpocalypse is a SHORT-TERM sentiment headwind and a MEDIUM-TERM opportunity for DSY.PA. The stock is being priced as if it were commodity SaaS when it is actually mission-critical industrial software with the widest moat in the sector.
