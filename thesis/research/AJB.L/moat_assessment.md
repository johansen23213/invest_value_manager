## Detailed Analysis by Moat Source

### 1. Switching Costs (Score: 5.5/7)

**This is AJ Bell's PRIMARY moat source.**

Quantitative evidence:
- **Customer retention rate:** 94.1% (FY2025), stable from 94.2% (FY2024)
- **Pension (SIPP) stickiness:** Pension assets are inherently sticky -- transferring a SIPP requires paperwork, 4-8 weeks of processing, potential exit fees from old provider, and the customer is out of the market during in-specie transfers
- **ISA transfer friction:** ISA transfers take 15-30 business days on average, during which the customer may be out of the market if transferred as cash
- **Tax wrapper complexity:** Moving investments between platforms can trigger CGT events outside tax wrappers. Inside wrappers (ISA/SIPP), no tax event but significant administrative burden
- **Accumulated history:** Customers build years of transaction history, tax records, watchlists, and familiarity with the platform
- **D2C customers grew 25% to 462,000:** Rapid customer acquisition suggests low barriers to ENTRY but evidence confirms low propensity to LEAVE once on platform

**Assessment:** Switching costs are REAL but not INSURMOUNTABLE. The 94% retention rate is strong but not exceptional vs financial services broadly (banking ~95%+). Crucially, the switching cost is primarily procedural/administrative, not contractual or deeply technical. A determined customer CAN switch in 4-8 weeks. The stickiness is strongest in pensions and weakest in dealing accounts.

**Durability: 15-20 years** -- Pension transfer friction is structural (regulatory complexity). However, Open Banking and potential future regulatory changes to simplify transfers could erode this over time.

### 2. Cost Advantage (Score: 3.5/4)

Quantitative evidence:
- **Platform fee:** AJ Bell 0.25% vs Hargreaves Lansdown 0.35% (from March 2026, was 0.45%) vs Interactive Investor flat fee model
- **Operating margin trajectory:** 36.0% (2022) -> 39.5% (2023) -> 42.1% (2024) -> 41.9% (2025) -- near-best-in-class
- **PBT margin:** 43.4% (FY2025) -- exceptional for a financial services platform
- **Revenue per AUA:** 32.3 bps -- efficient extraction
- **Scale leverage:** GBP103.3bn AUA spreading fixed technology/compliance costs over a large base
- **Asset-light model:** Capex/depreciation 0.7x (FY2025) -- capex declining as a share, incremental margin very high
- **SBC/Revenue:** Only 1.3% -- minimal dilution

**Assessment:** AJ Bell has genuine cost advantage vs the largest competitor (HL) and competitive positioning vs flat-fee alternatives (Interactive Investor is cheaper for portfolios >GBP50k). The scale advantage is REAL -- GBP103bn AUA provides operating leverage that smaller platforms cannot match. However, free-trading platforms (Trading 212, Revolut) are attacking from below with zero-commission models, which could compress ad valorem fees over time.

**Durability: 10-15 years** -- The cost advantage relative to HL is sustainable given HL's higher cost base under private equity (debt service). But fee compression is a secular trend in financial services globally (see US: Schwab, Fidelity went zero-commission). The question is timing, not direction.

### 3. Intangible Assets (Score: 3/5)

Quantitative evidence:
- **FCA regulatory licenses:** 4 regulated entities. FCA authorization is a meaningful barrier to entry -- compliance costs, capital requirements, and scrutiny deter casual entrants
- **Brand:** 4.9 stars Trustpilot. AJ Bell is now the largest listed UK investment platform (after HL delisting). Significant brand recognition among UK retail investors
- **Consumer Duty compliance:** FCA's Consumer Duty (effective 2023-2024) raises compliance bar, advantaging established players
- **Media presence:** AJ Bell invests heavily in brand (podcasts, media commentary, "AJ Bell Money & Markets" brand), CEO Andy Bell is a known figure

**Assessment:** The regulatory license is a genuine but LIMITED moat -- it keeps out casual entrants but not well-capitalized fintechs (Revolut has an FCA license, Trading 212 is FCA-authorized). The brand is building but AJ Bell does not have the decades of brand equity that HL accumulated. No proprietary technology that cannot be replicated.

**Durability: 10-15 years** -- Brand is strengthening (customer growth accelerating). Regulatory moat is stable. But neither creates pricing power -- AJ Bell competes on LOWER prices, not premium pricing.

### 4. Network Effects (Score: 0/5)

**NOT PRESENT.** AJ Bell is a platform, but NOT a network-effect business. One customer's presence does not make the platform more valuable for another customer. There are no cross-side dynamics (unlike a marketplace). The "platform" terminology is misleading from a moat perspective -- this is an infrastructure/utility business, not a network business.

### 5. Efficient Scale (Score: 2/4)

Quantitative evidence:
- **Market concentration:** Top 5 platforms hold 72% of GBP572bn DIY market. HHI moderately concentrated
- **HL market share declining:** From 39% (2020) to 33% (2025) -- market is fragmenting, not consolidating
- **New entrants in last 5 years:** Revolut ISA (2025), Webull UK (2025), Investing.one (2025), multiple neobanks. Entry is HAPPENING
- **UK market growing:** 22% in 2025 to GBP572bn, 2M new accounts opened. Growing pie attracts new competitors

**Assessment:** The UK DIY investment market is large enough to support multiple competitors profitably. This is NOT a natural monopoly or efficient scale situation. The market is growing rapidly and attracting new entrants. While the top 5 are dominant, the presence of viable alternatives prevents oligopolistic pricing power.

**Durability: 10 years** -- Market growth may slow as UK retail investor penetration matures, which could favor incumbents. But right now, the growing market is ATTRACTING competition, not deterring it.

## Escenarios de Erosion

### 1. Most Probable: Secular Fee Compression (5-10 year horizon)
The US market went from fee-based to zero-commission in 5 years (2014-2019). UK is following with a lag. Free platforms (Trading 212, Revolut) are gaining share among younger investors. AJ Bell's 0.25% ad valorem fee will face downward pressure. The moat narrows as the fee delta vs competitors shrinks. AJ Bell may need to shift toward flat-fee or freemium models, compressing margins from current 42% level. This does NOT destroy the business (Schwab thrived post-fee-compression by growing scale) but it NARROWS the moat by reducing the barrier between AJ Bell's cost structure and competitors'.

**Probability:** 70-80% over 10 years. **Impact:** Moderate -- AJ Bell has time to adapt and is already lower-cost. But margin compression from 42% to 30-35% is plausible.

### 2. Tail Scenario: Regulatory Disruption (Open Finance)
If FCA mandates seamless, instant platform switching (analogous to current account switching service), the procedural switching costs that drive 94% retention could collapse overnight. Customers could switch platforms in days, not weeks. This would commoditize the platform layer and make customer acquisition cost the key competitive factor.

**Probability:** 20-30% over 10 years. **Impact:** High -- would fundamentally reduce the primary moat source (switching costs).

## Moat Classification Reasoning

**NARROW MOAT -- not Wide, not None.**

**Why not Wide:**
1. Only ONE strong moat source (switching costs). Cost advantage is real but under secular pressure from fee compression. Intangibles are moderate. No network effects.
2. The moat has not been tested through a full market cycle as a public company (IPO 2018, no severe recession since). ROE persistence is only 4 years of data.
3. New entrants ARE entering the market. The UK DIY investment market is not an efficient scale situation.
4. Fee compression is a real secular trend that will narrow the cost advantage over time.

**Why not None:**
1. 94.1% customer retention is genuinely strong, driven by structural pension transfer friction
2. ROE of 48.3% and expanding -- well above cost of capital for 4+ consecutive years
3. Operating margin of 42% indicates real pricing power and scale advantage
4. #2 market position with the strongest growth trajectory of any major UK platform
5. 18.8% insider ownership = strong founder alignment

**The moat is NARROW but the TRAJECTORY is positive.** AJ Bell is gaining share, growing customers 19%/year, and expanding margins. If it reaches GBP200bn+ AUA, the cost advantage becomes more durable. The risk is that fee compression catches up before scale solidifies.

**Durability estimate: 10-15 years** for the composite moat. Switching costs are the longest-lasting component. Cost advantage is the most vulnerable.
