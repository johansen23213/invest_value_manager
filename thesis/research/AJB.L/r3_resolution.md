## Conflicts Resolved

### 1. Buyback Accretion (+36p) — R2 WINS
R1 FV of 600p includes 36p buyback add-on on top of 564p weighted methods. This double-counts: buybacks already boost per-share FCF in OEY calculation. **Removed.** FV adjusted from 600p to 564p baseline.

### 2. Interest Income — UNRESOLVED (HARD GATE)
R2 correctly identifies that interest income is buried in "recurring ad valorem" revenue without separation. Citi models 15% EPS headwind from 2pp BoE rate cut. Exact magnitude unknown. Estimated GBP 25-35M of GBP 232M ad valorem is interest-derived.
**HARD GATE for R4:** AJ Bell annual report decomposition of interest income required before committee approval.

### 3. FCF Decline — PARTIALLY RESOLVED
R2 identifies GBP 98M→93M→84M decline as unexplained. R1 dismisses as "timing."
**My assessment:** Likely working capital related — regulatory capital scales with AUA growth (GBP 92B→108B = 17% growth requiring more cash buffers). Ultra-light capex model (0.8%) means FCF can rebound when WC normalizes. **Concerning but NOT thesis-killing.** Flag for H1 FY2026 data.

### 4. Insider Selling — R2 WINS
R1 classifies insider activity as POSITIVE based on 150 BAYE plan purchases (GBP 201 each = automatic). R2 reveals Andy Bell sold GBP 46M via deliberate placings in 2024 (stake 21%→17.8%).
**Reclassified: POSITIVE → NEUTRAL-NEGATIVE.**
**New KC:** Founder stake drops below 15% → SELL.

### 5. Fee Compression — PARTIAL R2 WIN
HL fee cuts (Mar 2026), JP Morgan DIY launch (2026), Trading 212 (zero fees, GBP 25B AUA). Individually manageable, collectively they create 12-month competitive pressure window.
**Discount:** -15-20p from FV for structural fee compression over 3-5 years.
**Monitor:** Q2 FY2026 customer acquisition data post-HL fee cut.

### 6. UK Concentration — UNRESOLVED (HARD GATE)
Adding AJB.L would be 5th UK position (~40% of positions). Principio 2 requires explicit geographic diversification reasoning.
**HARD GATE for R4:** Why AJB.L over non-UK alternatives (FBK.MI, FTK.DE)?

### 7. Bear Case — R2 WINS
R1 bear of 450p (5.5% downside from 425p) is inadequate. R2's correlated bear (rate cuts + HL fee war + UK weakness + pension IHT) = 330-360p.
**Adopt R2 bear: 350-370p** as realistic worst case.

### 8. WACC — PARTIAL R2 WIN
R1 uses 9.1% (beta 1.02). R2 argues 10-11% for pure UK-domestic, rate-sensitive business.
**R3 WACC: 9.5%** — split the difference. UK-only exposure warrants small premium over beta-derived.
