## Business Understanding

### What AJ Bell Does (2-Minute Test)

AJ Bell operates the UK's leading listed investment platform. It enables individuals and financial advisers to buy, hold, and manage investments (ISAs, SIPPs, general accounts) via a digital platform. Revenue comes primarily from ad valorem fees on assets under administration (AUA), plus transaction fees for trading. The company does NOT take investment risk — it is a pure platform/intermediary that clips a fee on the stock of assets.

**Problem solved:** Making investing accessible, low-cost, and simple for UK retail investors and financial advisers. Replaces legacy paper-based wealth management and high-cost active management.

**Revenue model:**
| Revenue Type | FY2025 | % of Total | Characteristics |
|-------------|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Recurring Ad Valorem | GBP 232.4M | 73% | Fee on AUA (~32bp avg). Grows with assets. Highly recurring. |
| Recurring Fixed | GBP 32.5M | 10% | Fixed per-account fees. Stable. |
| Transactional | GBP 53.0M | 17% | Dealing commissions + FX. Lumpy, elevated in FY25. |
| **Total** | **GBP 317.8M** | **100%** | **83% recurring** |

**Two channels:**
- **Advised (Investcentre):** 182K customers, GBP 62.4B AUA. Serves financial advisers. Stickier, lower growth.
- **D2C (AJ Bell / Dodl):** 462K customers, GBP 40.9B AUA. Direct-to-consumer. Higher growth (+25% customers YoY), lower AUA per customer, higher margin.

### Moat Assessment: NARROW-to-WIDE

**Sources of moat:**

1. **Switching costs (7/10):** Once assets are on a platform, moving them is painful (re-register ISAs, SIPPs, etc.), takes weeks, and creates tax events. 94.1% retention rate demonstrates this. BUT: new customers can choose ANY platform, so switching costs protect the stock of assets, not new flows.

2. **Scale economics (6/10):** Platform costs are largely fixed (technology, compliance). Each incremental GBP of AUA generates ~32bp revenue with near-zero marginal cost. AJ Bell at GBP 108B AUA has significant scale. BUT: HL at GBP 173B is larger; Fidelity and II have global parent scale.

3. **Brand (6/10):** AJ Bell has strong brand recognition in UK (Which? Recommended, Trustpilot 4.9, 644K customers). BUT: not yet at HL-level household name status. Multi-year brand investment ongoing.

4. **Network effects (3/10):** Limited. Platform value doesn't increase with each user (unlike a marketplace). Adviser network has some network characteristics (advisers attract clients who are on the platform).

5. **Regulatory barriers (5/10):** FCA-regulated platform with complex compliance requirements. Not trivial to replicate. FSCS protection. BUT: many firms already have licenses.

**Moat Score: 20/25 (NARROW-to-WIDE)**
The combination of switching costs + scale + brand creates a durable competitive position. Not a pure monopoly (unlike AUTO.L's 75% share), but in a market with only 5 significant platforms, AJ Bell is well-positioned. The HL privatization makes AJ Bell the UK's listed platform of choice for institutional investors seeking exposure.
