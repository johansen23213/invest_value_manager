## Business Understanding

### What Foresight Does

Foresight Group Holdings Limited is a sustainability-led alternative investment manager specializing in real assets (infrastructure, renewable energy, battery storage, forestry) and growth capital for UK/Irish SMEs. Founded in 1984, it IPO'd on the LSE in February 2021. Headquartered in London with operations across UK, Europe, and Australia.

### Revenue Model

Revenue comes from three main streams:

1. **Management fees (~87% of revenue, recurring):** Annual fees charged on FUM (Funds Under Management, GBP 9.6B of GBP 13.7B total AUM). Typical fee rates for infrastructure funds: 0.75-1.25% on committed/invested capital. This provides a highly predictable, recurring revenue base.

2. **Performance fees (~5-10% variable):** Carried interest and performance fees earned when funds exceed hurdle rates. Currently limited (only ~2 funds in realisation phase generating performance fees) but expected to grow significantly -- management targets 10+ funds in realisation by FY29. Zenith Energy sale (Australia) generated GBP 3.4M in H1 FY26.

3. **Other income (~3-5%):** Advisory fees, transaction fees, fund administration.

## Valuation

### CRITICAL NOTE ON ASSET MANAGER VALUATION

Traditional DCF and ROIC-based methods are distorted for asset managers because:
1. AUM is the "asset" but it belongs to clients, not shareholders
2. ROIC is mechanically very high due to low invested capital
3. The key value driver is AUM * Fee Rate * Margin -- not FCF from operations per se

**For Tier B asset managers, the appropriate methods are:**
1. **P/E on Adjusted Earnings (primary)** -- captures the earnings power from fees
2. **AUM-based valuation (secondary)** -- values the franchise based on AUM quality
3. **DCF as cross-check**

### Method 1: P/E on Adjusted Earnings (60% weight)

Forward Adjusted EPS (FY26E): ~44-46p (assuming 8-10% growth in line with AUM/revenue trajectory)

- 3i Group: ~12x
- ICG: ~14x
- Impax: ~16x
- Bridgepoint: ~10x

Foresight deserves a PREMIUM to sector median because:
+ 87% recurring revenue (higher than most peers)

## META-REFLECTION

### Incertidumbres/Dudas

- **Receivables +49.4% vs Revenue +9.0% is the biggest unresolved flag.** This could be innocent (performance fee accruals, timing) or concerning (revenue quality, collection issues). I could not determine the cause from available data -- the FY25 annual report detail is needed.
- **FCF margin declining from 42.6% to 27.1% over 3 years** while revenue grows. This could reflect: (a) increased SBC (now normalizing), (b) working capital consumption (receivables), (c) acquisition integration costs, or (d) structural FCF conversion deterioration. The tool's FCF data suggests the absolute FCF has been relatively stable (GBP 42-51M) while revenue grew significantly -- so the denominator is driving the margin decline, not the numerator collapsing.
- **Beta of 0.46 seems too low** for a GBP 471M market cap UK-listed alternative asset manager. This is likely an artifact of the limited listed history (Feb 2021 IPO) and low liquidity. Using this beta in WACC gives 6.7% which is unrealistically low. I used 9.0% as a judgment call, but this introduces subjectivity.
- **The "double EBITDA by FY29" target** is ambitious. Core EBITDA was GBP 62.2M in FY25; doubling to ~GBP 125M by FY29 requires ~19% CAGR for 4 years. With AUM growth of 8-10% and margin expansion from 40% to 45%, this would require significant performance fee contribution. Achievable but requires execution.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema

- **quality_scorer.py needs an `--asset-manager` flag** (similar to `--insurer` and `--serial-acquirer`). Asset managers systematically score inflated on ROIC, FCF margin, and leverage because the business model is inherently asset-light and the "capital" is client AUM. The tool should flag this and potentially use ROE instead of ROIC, and adjust financial sub-scores accordingly. This would affect FSG.L, IPX.L, EMG.L, ICP.L, and any future AM analysis.
- **The insider_tracker.py buyback data** shows company buybacks but doesn't clearly distinguish between company-level buybacks and insider purchases. In FSG.L's case, all "insider" transactions shown are company buybacks. A "company buyback" tag would be useful.
- **Receivables/Revenue divergence in narrative_checker.py** should trigger a more prominent flag -- this is a critical quality-of-earnings indicator.

### Anomalias Detectadas

- **Revenue CAGR 21.4% but EPS CAGR only 7.9%:** This is a massive dilution gap. The Infrastructure Capital acquisition was paid 50% in shares (diluting EPS) and the SBC has been high (8.9-9.7% of revenue in FY23-24). This compression of revenue-to-EPS translation is a structural concern that needs monitoring. If adjusted EPS (40.8p) grows at ~10% going forward while basic EPS (28.9p) catches up as SBC normalizes, this is acceptable. If the gap persists, it signals that management is extracting value through SBC rather than creating it for shareholders.
- **Payout ratio 82% (basic EPS) vs 60% target (adjusted EPS basis):** There is a significant gap between GAAP EPS and adjusted EPS. The dividend looks sustainable on adjusted earnings but stretched on GAAP. The adjustments include SBC add-backs and acquisition-related items -- standard for the industry but worth monitoring.
