## Resumen Ejecutivo

Moncler's FY2025 results (published Feb 19) were genuinely better than feared: Q4 revenue +7% cFX broke the deceleration trend, EBIT margin held at 29.2%, Stone Island Q4 surged +16-17% cFX, and net cash strengthened to EUR 1,458M. Management guided for 3% pricing in 2026 with store openings in US and Korea. The market responded by rallying the stock ~8% from EUR 50 to EUR 54, pushing it ABOVE the R3 fair value of EUR 52.

This rally VALIDATES the adversarial thesis, not the bull thesis. The argument was never that Moncler is a bad business -- it is exceptional. The argument was that there was no edge at the proposed price, and the market would quickly close any gap. That is exactly what happened: the consensus target of EUR 59.18 pulled the stock upward. We would have been buying at consensus, not at a discount. The fundamental "no edge" problem identified in the original R2 has been proven correct.

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas (Updated Post-Results + Rally)

### 1. "Growth will normalize at 6% CAGR" -- NOW PARTIALLY SUPPORTED

- **R1 Claim:** 6% revenue growth CAGR (TAM 3% + share 1% + pricing 2%)
- **FY2025 Actual:** Revenue EUR 3,132.1M, +3% cFX. Q4 +7% cFX acceleration. Stone Island Q4 +16-17% cFX.
- **Updated evidence:**
  - Q4 was genuinely strong. Moncler brand Q4 revenue EUR 1,167.7M (+6% cFX), Stone Island EUR 123.1M (+16-17% cFX). All regions contributed.
  - Asia Q4 +11% cFX with China showing "continued double-digit growth" -- impressive given the structural aspirational consumer decline.
  - Americas showed positive momentum; management plans US store openings in 2026.
  - EMEA was the weak link: -3% cFX Q4, -4% FY, dragged by subdued tourism (American, Korean, Japanese visitors down).
  - Management guidance: 3% pricing for 2026, "positive current trading." But notably NO explicit revenue growth guidance. This omission is telling -- if management had confidence in 6%+ growth, they would guide for it.
  - Goldman Sachs EBIT CAGR forecast: +2% for 2024-2027. This remains the most bearish credible estimate.
  - Deutsche Bank kept HOLD, naming Moncler "one of the least preferred stocks" in their Luxury 2026 Year Ahead report amid "high growth and earnings risk expectations."
- **Severidad:** **MODERATE** (downgraded from HIGH). Q4 +7% demonstrates resilience, but FY was +3% -- not 6%. The R3 compromise of 4.5% growth appears reasonable.
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Maintain R3 growth assumption of 4.5%. Q1 2026 data (April) will be decisive -- if Q1 confirms momentum, upgrade to 5%.

### 2. "EBIT margins are stable at 29-30%" -- VALIDATED

- **R1 Claim:** 29-30% operating margin is sustainable.
- **FY2025 Actual:** EBIT EUR 913.4M, margin 29.2% (vs 29.5% FY2024). H2 recovered fully from H1's 18.3%.
- **Updated evidence:**
  - Management's "phasing" explanation was correct. Full-year margin landed squarely in the 29-30% target range.
  - Gross margin held at 78.1%, confirming pricing power intact.
  - Net income EUR 626.7M, margin 20.0% (vs 20.6% prior year). The 2% net income decline on +1% revenue suggests slight operational headwind but nothing alarming.
  - Higher A&P spend (10% vs 8% of revenue) appears structural -- Moncler is investing to maintain brand heat in a competitive environment. This is the right decision but caps operating leverage.
  - The trend: 30.0% (FY2023) -> 29.5% (FY2024) -> 29.2% (FY2025). A subtle 80bps compression over 3 years. Statistically insignificant per year, but the direction is clear.
- **Severidad:** **LOW** (confirmed downgrade from original R2). Margin thesis is validated.
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Accept 29% EBIT margin assumption.

### 3. "China luxury slowdown is cyclical, not structural" -- PARTIALLY SUPPORTED BUT RISK REMAINS

- **R1 Claim:** China is cyclical; Moncler serves UHNW clientele less sensitive to cycles. 30% prob of being wrong.
- **FY2025 Actual:** Asia Q4 +11% cFX, China showed "continued double-digit growth."
- **Updated evidence:**
  - Moncler's China performance has been genuinely resilient -- outperforming broader luxury peers.
  - However, the STRUCTURAL backdrop has NOT improved:
    - Bain: Global luxury customer base shrunk from 400M (2022) to 340M (2025). Another 20-30M loss expected.
    - The "aspirational luxury buyer has virtually disappeared" -- CNBC.
    - China Briefing: "visible contraction of aspirational consumer base."
    - K-shaped luxury market is real: resilience at the top, fragility in aspirational tiers.
  - Moncler's resilience suggests it serves the UHNW segment, not the aspirational segment. This is GOOD for thesis survival but LIMITS TAM growth. If Moncler has effectively "graduated" from aspirational to true luxury, the growth ceiling is lower but more sustainable.
  - EMEA was DOWN 3-4% FY on subdued tourism. Chinese, Korean, Japanese tourist flows to Europe remain weak. This means China strength is LOCAL, not traveling. The tourism-driven EMEA revenue (historically significant) remains impaired.
  - JP Morgan: China luxury expected "broadly flat" in 2026. Not recovery, not collapse -- flat.
- **Severidad:** **MODERATE** (downgraded from CRITICAL). China is not collapsing for Moncler, but EMEA tourism weakness is the flipside of local China consumption that analysts may underappreciate.
- **Resolucion sugerida:** China risk downgraded but EMEA tourism risk upgraded. The revenue MIX is shifting (more Asia local, less EMEA tourist) which is structurally different from historical patterns.

### 4. "CEO transition is orderly and low-risk" -- UNRESOLVED, TIMING IMMINENT

- **R1 Claim:** Ruffini retains creative direction. Rongone is experienced. Planned transition.
- **Updated evidence:**
  - Rongone starts April 1, 2026 -- six weeks from now. Roberto Eggs (Chief Business & Global Market Officer) departed March 1.
  - No strategic communication from Rongone has been made public. The market has NO visibility into his vision.
  - Luxury CEO transition precedents remain overwhelmingly negative:
    - Burberry: -53% during leadership transition
    - Hugo Boss: -11% on strategy reset under new CEO
    - Ferragamo: multi-year decline after family-to-professional transition
  - The FY2025 earnings call was Ruffini's last as CEO. His tone was described as "upbeat" -- which could be interpreted as valedictory optimism.
  - The market appears to have FULLY PRICED the Q4 beat while NOT pricing in transition risk. The P/E at EUR 54 is ~23.5x (using EUR 2.31 EPS based on EUR 626.7M / 271.6M shares). This is a RECOVERY multiple being applied before we know what the new leadership will actually do.
  - Ruffini's statement: "A forward-looking decision... also with a view to a possible future generational transition." The word "generational" implies Ruffini may step back further over time.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH** (maintained). The timing is critical -- 6 weeks to CEO change with zero visibility on Rongone's strategy.
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The +50bps WACC premium from R3 (WACC 9.0%) is the minimum. Buying before Rongone's first strategic communication is speculative. Wait for Q1 2026 results and Rongone's first strategic message.

### 5. "FV EUR 58 represents independent value, not consensus anchoring" -- PROVEN WRONG

- **R1 FV:** EUR 58 | **Consensus mean:** EUR 59.18 | **R3 FV:** EUR 52 | **Current price:** EUR 54
- **Updated evidence:**

  - Per Error #49: "Consensus IS the price. If my thesis = consensus, I have no edge." At EUR 54, the stock is approaching consensus fair value. The "discount" that justified the thesis has evaporated.
  - The R3 resolution acknowledged this: "This thesis requires PATIENCE, not insight." At EUR 54, there is nothing left to be patient for -- the price is above our fair value.
- **Severidad:** **CRITICAL** -- This is now the single most important finding. The thesis was "quality at discount." At EUR 54, there is no discount.
- **Resolucion sugerida:** **DO NOT SET ANY STANDING ORDER ABOVE EUR 42.** The stock must come to us, not the other way around.

## Alternative Fair Value Calculation (Updated at EUR 54)

### What the market is pricing at EUR 54

```
Market Cap at EUR 54: EUR 54 * 271.6M shares = EUR 14.7B
Trailing P/E: EUR 14.7B / EUR 626.7M net income = 23.4x
Forward P/E (est FY2026 EPS EUR 2.50): 21.6x
EV/EBIT: (EUR 14.7B - EUR 1.46B cash) / EUR 913M = 14.5x
OEY: EUR 617M OE / EUR 14.7B = 4.2%
OEY + Growth (4.5%): 4.2% + 4.5% = 8.7%
WACC (9.0%): Spread = -0.3pp (NEGATIVE at EUR 54)
```

At EUR 54, even with R3-generous growth of 4.5%, the OEY + Growth spread is NEGATIVE. The stock does not create value for a buyer at this price.

### What price would create value?

For OEY + Growth to equal WACC + 1pp spread (minimum for Tier A):
- Need OEY of 5.5% (with 4.5% growth = 10% total = 9.0% WACC + 1pp)
- EUR 617M / 5.5% = EUR 11.2B market cap / 271.6M shares = EUR 41.3

This independently confirms the R3 entry of EUR 42 as the minimum value-creating price.

## META-REFLECTION

### Dudas/Incertidumbres
- **The reverse DCF historical FCF CAGR debate remains unresolved.** R1 argued 2.7% is distorted by abnormally high 2021 base; R2 argued it is the tool's best estimate. The truth matters: at 4-5% sustainable FCF growth, the stock is roughly fairly valued at EUR 52-54. At 2.7%, it is overvalued. At 6%+, it is undervalued. FY2025 FCF was EUR 529M (DOWN from EUR 587M prior year), which does NOT support the 6%+ growth thesis.
- **China customer segmentation is unknown.** Moncler's China resilience could mean UHNW loyalty (sustainable) or lagging aspirational decline (temporary). Without customer demographics data, this remains ambiguous. The Q4 "double-digit growth" is encouraging but not conclusive.
- **IFRS 16 gross margin comparability.** The moat-assessor flagged that Moncler's 78.1% GM potentially exceeds Hermes (~70%) due to accounting treatment differences (lease classification under IFRS 16). This has not been verified and slightly weakens the moat evidence if true.
- **Italian insider data remains a blind spot.** Cannot verify whether Ruffini or other insiders have been trading around the CEO transition announcement. CONSOB filings not accessible through our tools.
