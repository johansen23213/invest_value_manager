## Business Understanding

### What Moncler Does

Moncler S.p.A. is an Italian luxury group operating two brands:

1. **Moncler Brand (87% of revenue):** Ultra-premium outerwear and lifestyle apparel. Founded 1952 in Monestier-de-Clermont, France. Known for iconic down jackets elevated from utilitarian Alpine gear to luxury status symbols. Average jacket price EUR 1,000-3,000. The Moncler Genius collaborative platform drives cultural relevance and attracts younger demographics.

2. **Stone Island Brand (13% of revenue):** Premium Italian sportswear/streetwear, acquired in 2020 for EUR 1.15B. Known for fabric innovation and compass logo. Lower price point than Moncler but higher than mass-market. DTC transition underway (+12% DTC growth H1 2025).

### Revenue Model

| Channel | % Revenue | Characteristics |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) | ~87% (Moncler brand) | 287 mono-brand boutiques + e-commerce. High margin, brand control |
| Wholesale | ~13% (declining by design) | Selective distribution. Being pruned strategically |

DTC dominance (87%) is critical: it means Moncler controls pricing, customer data, brand presentation, and inventory. This is among the highest DTC ratios in luxury -- comparable to Hermes (90%+) and superior to LVMH (~75%).

### Geographic Revenue Mix (FY2024)

| Region | Revenue | % Total | Growth (cFX) |
|--------|---------|---------|--------------|
| Asia (APAC + Japan + Korea) | EUR 1,379M | 44% | +11% |
| EMEA | EUR 949M | 31% | +5% |
| Americas | EUR 379M | 12% | +4% |
| Stone Island (all regions) | EUR 402M | 13% | -2% |
| **Total Group** | **EUR 3,109M** | **100%** | **+7% (cFX)** |

**Asia exposure (44%) is the key risk/opportunity.** China within Asia is outperforming -- positive in 9M 2025 even against high comparables. Japan and Korea weaker. Americas is smallest and steadiest.

## Why It Is Cheap (26% Below 52wH)

### Market Narrative

The market believes:
1. **China luxury spending has peaked** -- 44% of revenue from Asia, China luxury spending decelerating post-COVID revenge spending
2. **Luxury sector de-rating is justified** -- from 50x to 22x P/E for Moncler; sector-wide phenomenon affecting LVMH, Kering, Richemont
3. **Growth is decelerating** -- from +27% (2022) to +15% (2023) to +4% (2024 current FX). 9M 2025 flat
4. **Stone Island underperforming** -- -2% in 2024, -1% in 9M 2025, wholesale channel declining
5. **FY2025 results uncertainty** -- reporting TOMORROW (Feb 19). Market pricing in flat/negative growth year

### My Counter-Thesis

For each narrative:

1. **China peak:** My evidence suggests China is NOT peaking for Moncler specifically. 9M 2025 China outperformed the rest of Asia with positive growth against high comps. Moncler's positioning in the "absolute luxury" tier (EUR 1,000-3,000 jackets) targets UHNW/HNW clientele less sensitive to economic cycles. Canada Goose (lower tier) is suffering far more. UHNW population growing +5% CAGR globally (Knight Frank). **Probability I'm wrong: 30%**

2. **Sector de-rating justified for Moncler:** P/E compressed from 40-50x to 22x. This de-rating was appropriate for aspirational luxury (Kering, Burberry) but potentially excessive for Moncler given 78% GM, 20%+ ROIC, net cash. Hermes still trades at 48x because markets recognize quality differences. Moncler is closer to Hermes quality than to Kering. **Probability I'm wrong: 20%**

3. **Growth decelerating:** True at the headline level. But: the deceleration is from abnormally high post-COVID growth (+27% in 2022). Underlying mid-single-digit organic growth on a EUR 3.1B base with 87% DTC, in an environment where the entire luxury sector is stagnant, is actually resilient. The real question is: what is normalized growth? I estimate 6-8% long-term (TAM + share + pricing). **Probability I'm wrong: 25%**

4. **Stone Island underperforming:** Stone Island is 13% of revenue. Its DTC is growing (+12% H1 2025) while wholesale is deliberately being pruned (-9%). The brand transition from wholesale-heavy to DTC is a conscious strategic choice that depresses headline growth but improves quality of revenue. This is POSITIVE, not negative. **Probability I'm wrong: 15%**

5. **FY2025 uncertainty:** 9M 2025 revenue was flat cFX. Management said Q4 started positive in all regions. Consensus expects roughly flat to low-single-digit growth for FY2025. Results come TOMORROW (Feb 19). This is a binary event that could move the stock +/-10%. **Not a reason for structural cheapness; this is a timing question.**

## Valuation (Tier A: OEY Primary + Reverse DCF Secondary)

### Method 1: Owner Earnings Yield (60% weight)

```
FCF (2024):                 EUR 794M
Depreciation:               EUR 234M (estimated from capex/depreciation ratio)
Maintenance Capex:          EUR 234M * 1.1 = EUR 257M
Growth Capex:               EUR 794M - EUR 257M = EUR 537M (implied)

Owner Earnings = FCF - Maintenance Capex + Depreciation
               = EUR 794M - EUR 257M + EUR 234M
               = EUR 771M

Wait -- more precisely:
Owner Earnings = FCF = EUR 794M (FCF already subtracts all capex)
Actually, OE = Net Income + D&A - Maintenance Capex
Net Income = EUR 640M
D&A = ~EUR 234M (estimated)
Maintenance Capex = D&A * 1.1 = EUR 257M

Owner Earnings = EUR 640M + EUR 234M - EUR 257M = EUR 617M

Alternatively, using FCF directly:
Owner Earnings = FCF = EUR 794M (this includes growth capex reduction)

For conservatism, use the lower figure: EUR 617M

Market Cap: EUR 13.6B
Owner Earnings Yield = EUR 617M / EUR 13.6B = 4.5%

Expected Growth: 6%
OEY + Growth = 4.5% + 6.0% = 10.5%
WACC: 8.5%
Spread: +2.0pp (positive but not exceptional)
```

**Interpretation:** OEY + Growth of 10.5% exceeds WACC of 8.5% by 2.0pp. This is positive but modest compared to our best compounders. The spread suggests Moncler is reasonably valued but not deeply discounted at current price.

**OEY-implied Fair Value:**
