## 2. Critical Assumptions Identified (Attack Vectors)

### Assumption 1: China luxury slowdown is CYCLICAL, not STRUCTURAL
**R1 claim:** "9M 2025 China outperformed the rest of Asia with positive growth against high comps." Probability of being wrong: 30%.

**Counter-evidence found:**
- **Bain & Company (Level 1 data):** China personal luxury goods market contracted 3-5% in 2025. This is the SECOND consecutive year of contraction.
- **China Briefing (Level 2):** "Visible contraction of the aspirational consumer base" -- the segment that powered luxury growth for a decade. Next generation faces "limited economic opportunities and weaker upward mobility."
- **JP Morgan Luxury Outlook (Level 2):** China luxury sales expected "broadly flat" in 2026. Not recovery -- flat.
- **Structural factors (macro data):**
  - China property market collapse destroying wealth effect
  - "Luxury shame" social phenomenon emerging
  - Government "common prosperity" campaign
  - Youth unemployment 15%+ constraining aspirational spending pipeline
  - Demographic decline (population peaked 2022) permanently shrinks future consumer base

**Adversarial assessment:** The R1 thesis treats China as a cyclical dip ("40% prob base"), but the evidence suggests structural shift is at minimum 50/50. Moncler has 44-48% Asia exposure. If the aspirational consumer base -- which historically drove 70% of China luxury consumption (McKinsey) -- is permanently smaller, Moncler's growth ceiling is materially lower than the 6% CAGR assumed.

**Key question for post-earnings R2:** What was Moncler brand China growth in Q4 2025? If negative or flat despite being Q4 (strongest seasonal quarter), the structural thesis gains significant weight.

**Severity: CRITICAL**

### Assumption 2: Growth will normalize at 6% CAGR (TAM 3% + Share 1% + Pricing 2%)
**R1 claim:** Revenue growth of 6% is conservative below the 15% historical CAGR.

**Counter-evidence found:**
- **Actual trajectory:** 27.2% (2022) -> 14.7% (2023) -> 4.2% (2024) -> ~0% flat (9M 2025 cFX). This is NOT deceleration toward 6% -- it is deceleration toward ZERO.
- **Reverse DCF (tool output):** Market implies 4.5% FCF growth. Historical FCF CAGR is only 2.7%. The market is ALREADY pricing in ABOVE-historical FCF performance. R1 thesis claims market underprices Moncler -- reverse DCF says market prices ABOVE historical.
- **Asymmetry analysis (tool):** Bull FV EUR 67.75 (+34.6%) vs Bear FV EUR 33.25 (-33.9%). Asymmetry ratio 1.02x -- essentially NO asymmetric upside.
- **Goldman Sachs (Level 2):** Forecasts EBIT CAGR of just +2% for 2024-2027 (not 6% revenue growth).
- **DTC comp sales FELL 4% in H1 2025** -- the supposedly superior channel is already declining same-store.

**Adversarial assessment:** The 6% growth assumption is anchored to a historical period (2021-2024) that included abnormal COVID-recovery demand. The FORWARD trajectory (flat 9M 2025, DTC comps -4%) suggests normalized growth may be 2-3%, not 6%. At 3% growth, OEY + Growth = 4.5% + 3% = 7.5%, which is BELOW WACC of 8.5%. This would mean Moncler at current price is DESTROYING value, not creating it.

**Key question for post-earnings R2:** What is the actual FY2025 revenue growth and FY2026 guidance? If guidance is below 5% revenue growth, the 6% assumption is invalidated.

**Severity: HIGH**

### Assumption 3: EBIT margins are stable at 29-30% (management target)
**R1 claim:** Operating margin 29.2% in FY2024, management targets 29-30%.

**Counter-evidence found:**
- **H1 2025 EBIT margin: 18.3%** (vs 21.0% H1 2024) -- a 270bps decline
- **H1 2025 operating profit: EUR 224.8M** (vs EUR 258.7M H1 2024) -- 13% decline in absolute profit
- **Cause:** Higher A&P spending (10% vs 8% of revenue), lost insurance one-offs, different allocation of marketing between halves
- **Management explanation:** "Different allocation of marketing activities between H1 and H2" -- implying H2 should be better. But if revenue stays flat, even a H2 recovery may not restore 29% for the full year.
- **Net result:** H1 2025 net income EUR 153.5M, down 15% YoY

**Adversarial assessment:** Management claims the H1 margin compression is timing-related (A&P phasing). This is plausible BUT requires H2 to compensate significantly. If FY2025 EBIT margin comes in at 26-28% (vs 29.2% FY2024), the "stable margins" assumption breaks. At 27% EBIT margin on flat revenue, EBIT drops from EUR 916M to ~EUR 840M, and at 22x P/E the stock is overvalued.

**Key question for post-earnings R2:** What is FY2025 EBIT margin? Is the "phasing" explanation validated by H2 margin recovery?

**Severity: HIGH**

**Counter-evidence found:**

- **Market reaction:** Shares "edged lower" after CEO and senior exec announcements

  - Gucci/Kering: Underperformed significantly after creative director departure
  - Ferragamo: Multi-year decline after family-to-professional management transition
- **Rongone comes from Kering** -- a conglomerate whose luxury brands (Gucci, Balenciaga, YSL) have ALL underperformed in 2024-2025. His operational track record at Bottega Veneta was during a strong cycle; untested in a downturn

**Adversarial assessment:** The R1 thesis treats CEO transition as a neutral-to-slight-negative risk. But the timing is terrible: new CEO arrives (April 2026) at a moment of flat growth, compressing margins, and China structural uncertainty. This is when brand judgment matters MOST. Rongone inherits a challenging environment with the brand visionary moving to a "chairman" role. The probability of execution missteps is underweighted.

**Key question for post-earnings R2:** Any commentary on Rongone's strategic vision? Does earnings call provide continuity signals?

## 4. Pre-Built Attack Frameworks (Ready for Post-Earnings R2)

### Attack 1: "The Growth Illusion"
**Thesis:** R1 projects 6% normalized growth. Reality: revenue trajectory is 27% -> 15% -> 4% -> 0%.

**Framework:**
1. Calculate actual FY2025 cFX growth
2. Compare to R1 projection of 6%
3. If actual < 3%: The "deceleration to normalization" narrative is wrong -- this is deceleration to stagnation
4. Recalculate FV with 2-3% growth instead of 6%:
   - OEY + Growth = 4.5% + 2.5% = 7.0% vs WACC 8.5% = NEGATIVE spread
   - Bear FV: EUR 33-38

### Attack 2: "The Margin Mirage"
**Thesis:** R1 assumes 29-30% EBIT margin. H1 2025 showed 18.3%.

**Framework:**
1. Calculate actual FY2025 EBIT margin
2. If <28%: The "phasing" explanation is partial at best
3. Model forward: if EBIT margin settles at 27-28% (not 29-30%), what happens to FV?
4. At 27.5% EBIT margin and 3% growth:
   - EBIT ~EUR 855M (vs EUR 916M)
   - At 18x EV/EBIT (de-rated for slow growth) = EUR 15.4B EV
   - Equity = EUR 16.7B (adding net cash)
   - FV = EUR 61/share (looks okay)
   - BUT at 15x (bear multiple for flat growth luxury) = EUR 12.8B EV -> EUR 52/share

### Attack 3: "The CEO Timing Trap"
**Thesis:** R1 treats CEO transition as low risk. Luxury precedents disagree.

**Framework:**
1. Document earnings call commentary on transition
2. Map Rongone's stated priorities vs Ruffini's legacy strategy
3. Any divergence = execution risk premium needed
4. Calculate: if we add 100bps to WACC for transition risk -> FV impact?
   - At WACC 9.5% (vs 8.5%): DCF base FV drops from EUR 51 to ~EUR 45
5. Historical comp: average luxury stock performance in Year 1 post-CEO change

### Attack 4: "Consensus = No Edge"
**Thesis:** Our FV (EUR 58) = consensus (EUR 59.18). No alpha.

**Framework:**
1. After earnings, check if consensus revises up/down
2. Recalculate our FV with actual data
3. If FV and consensus re-converge: REJECT on grounds of no informational edge
4. The ONLY edge case: if we are willing to hold through volatility when others are selling -- but that is not an analytical edge, it is a behavioral bet

### Attack 5: "Stone Island = Value Destroyer"
**Thesis:** EUR 1.15B acquisition generating sub-WACC returns.

**Framework:**
1. Calculate Stone Island standalone ROIC: EBIT / (EUR 1.15B + accumulated investment)
2. If ROIC < WACC: acquisition is destroying value
3. Quantify drag: group ROIC ex-Stone Island vs with-Stone Island
4. Model: what would Moncler be worth WITHOUT Stone Island?
