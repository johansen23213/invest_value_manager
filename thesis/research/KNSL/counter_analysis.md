# Counter-Analysis: KNSL (Kinsale Capital Group, Inc.)

## Fecha: 2026-02-18

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The bull thesis for KNSL is built on a genuine technology-driven cost advantage and an exceptional historical track record. However, the thesis suffers from three meaningful weaknesses: (1) the QS adjustment of +15 is the largest in system history and pushes a Tier B company to Tier A without sufficient justification relative to precedent; (2) the valuation methods anchor heavily on forward BVPS projections that assume growth rates inconsistent with the current deceleration trajectory; and (3) the bear case MoS of just 2.1% provides no downside protection if ANY of the identified risks materialize. The thesis survives scrutiny on business quality but is vulnerable on valuation and timing. This is a HIGH-quality company at a FAIR price, not a quality company at a CHEAP price.

---

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. QS Adjustment +15 (61 -> 76, Tier B -> Tier A)

- **Thesis assumption:** KNSL deserves QS 76 (Tier A) after adjustments for insurer structural distortion (ROIC->ROE +8), GM data gap (+3), and market position default fix (+4).
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - The ACGL precedent set the insurer adjustment at +11 (ROE +8, GM gap +3). KNSL gets +15, the highest adjustment in system history. The extra +4 for "market position default fix" is legitimate as a tool correction, but it effectively means KNSL's ACTUAL company-specific quality (ex-tool-bugs) is only earning +11, identical to ACGL.
  - ACGL has a 24-year track record through multiple cycles (founded 2001). KNSL has operated through exactly ONE full cycle (founded 2009, IPO 2016). Assigning the SAME insurer adjustment (+8 for ROE) to a company with half the track record is generous.
  - At QS 76, KNSL barely clears Tier A (threshold 75). The MoS precedent for Tier A (10-15% minimum) is then applied to a company that is BARELY Tier A. If the adjustment were +13 (still generous), KNSL would be QS 74 -- Tier B -- requiring 18-25% MoS and significantly changing the entry calculus.
  - The risk-identifier independently estimated the adjustment at "+10-15 points" and the moat-assessor at "+10-15 points." Taking the LOW end (+10) yields QS 71, firmly Tier B.
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee should consider KNSL as Tier B (QS ~71-73) for sizing and MoS purposes, regardless of the adjusted score. At minimum, acknowledge that Tier A classification is borderline and should be treated with Tier B discipline on entry requirements.

### 2. BVPS Growth of 19-20% CAGR for 5 Years

- **Thesis assumption:** BVPS will compound at 19-20% annually from $84.66 to ~$203 by 2030, driven by 24% ROE and 97% retention.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - BVPS growth is a FUNCTION of ROE x retention. The thesis assumes 24% sustainable ROE. But ROE already declined from 28.3% (2023) to 28.0% (2024) to 26.4% (2025). This is a DECLINING trend.
  - The 26.4% ROE in 2025 includes 3.9 points of favorable reserve development. Normalize for this: the loss ratio WITHOUT favorable development is ~59% instead of 55.1%, pushing combined ratio to ~80%. At 80% combined ratio, operating ROE drops to approximately 20-22%.
  - The E&S market is entering a soft cycle (AM Best revised outlook to Stable from Positive in Nov 2025). Historical precedent: KNSL's ROE was 21-22% during the softer 2021-2022 period. Assuming 24% through a full soft market is aggressive.
  - GWP growth of 5.7% (and 1.8% in Q4) constrains the premium base. Float grows more slowly when premiums grow slowly. Float growth drives investment income which supports ROE. Slower float growth = lower ROE.
  - The $250M buyback was just announced -- KNSL has never done a large buyback before. If they deploy it aggressively, it could support EPS but it REDUCES BVPS (buying back shares above book value destroys BVPS when P/B > 1).
  - **My estimate:** BVPS CAGR of 14-17% is more realistic, yielding 2030E BVPS of ~$165-185, not $203.
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Sensitivity analysis on FV with 15% BVPS CAGR (BVPS 2027E of ~$112 instead of $121). At P/B 4.5x, this gives FV $504, reducing MoS to 25.3%. Still acceptable but less exceptional than thesis claims.

### 3. Fair Value of $539 With Bear Case at $385 (MoS 2.1%)

- **Thesis assumption:** Weighted FV of $539 provides 30% MoS. Bear case of $385 at 25% probability.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - **The bear case MoS of 2.1% is the tightest of ANY entry in system history.** For comparison: ADBE bear MoS was ~15%, NVO bear MoS was ~20%, MONY.L bear MoS was ~18%. A 2.1% bear MoS means if ANYTHING goes wrong, the position is underwater from day one.
  - Cantor Fitzgerald just cut PT to $360 (below current price of $377) on Feb 18 -- the SAME day as this analysis. The analyst LOW PT is $385, matching the thesis bear case exactly. This suggests the bear case is actually closer to the BASE case for cautious analysts.
  - The thesis uses 4.5x P/B on 2027E BVPS for the primary method. But P/B has trended from 12x to 4.5x over 2 years. Multiple compression has been relentless. There is no guarantee it stops at 4.5x. ACGL trades at 1.5x P/B with 17-21% ROE. If KNSL's ROE normalizes toward 20%, a P/B of 3.0-3.5x would be more consistent with peers.
  - The normalized P/E method uses 20x on 2027E EPS. KNSL's current P/E is 17.4x with growth decelerating. The P&C industry average is 12-13x. Assigning 20x to a company whose growth just fell from 30% to 6% is anchoring to historical multiples that reflected much higher growth.
  - **My counter-valuation (conservative):**
    - P/B method: 3.5x on $112 BVPS (2027E, 15% CAGR) = $392
    - Normalized P/E: 16x on $24 EPS (2027E) = $384
    - BVPS compounding: 4.0x terminal on $175 (2030E BVPS at 15% CAGR) / (1.098)^5 = $439
    - **Devil's Advocate FV: $400 (weighted)** -- implying MoS of only 5.8% at $377.
- **Severidad:** CRITICAL
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee MUST stress-test the FV with lower growth and lower multiples. If DA FV of $400 is closer to reality, current price offers insufficient margin of safety for ANY tier. A standing order at $300-320 would be more appropriate.

### 4. Growth Deceleration Is "Deliberate Discipline" Not Structural Weakness

- **Thesis assumption:** The GWP growth decline from 30%+ to 5.7% is primarily a DELIBERATE pullback from underpriced property. Non-property growth of 13.3% proves the engine is strong.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - Non-property growth of 13.3% is ALSO decelerating. In 2024, non-property growth was ~22%. It dropped 9pp in one year. If the same trajectory continues, non-property growth could hit 5-8% by 2027.
  - Q4 non-property growth was only 10.2% (vs 13.3% full-year), suggesting the deceleration is continuing into the most recent quarter.
  - The "law of large numbers" argument is real: KNSL's premium base grew from $290M at IPO to $2B today. Growing a $2B book at 15%+ in a $100B market requires taking significant share from entrenched competitors. The easy growth is over.
  - Management said the competitive environment will "continue well into 2026" and property may take "several more quarters" to stabilize. This is vague guidance that could mean H2 2026 or 2027.
  - D&O and professional lines are ALSO experiencing soft pricing -- this is not just a property problem. Multiple casualty lines are seeing competitive pressure.
  - The market's reaction (stock dropped 10.3% after earnings BEAT) suggests institutional investors are not buying the "deliberate discipline" narrative. They see structural growth deceleration.
- **Severidad:** MODERATE
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The thesis needs to model the REALISTIC growth trajectory: if non-property growth decelerates from 13% to 8-10% over 2 years and property remains flat/negative, total GWP growth could be 5-8% -- mid-single-digits, not the 12-15% the thesis assumes.

### 5. Technology Moat Is Unassailable

- **Thesis assumption:** The proprietary tech platform (7 years to build, 15+ years of data) cannot be replicated. The moat is WIDENING with AI.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - 52% of insurers are now using AI tools for underwriting (per industry surveys). This was <10% three years ago. The technology gap is narrowing at the INDUSTRY level.
  - Kinsale's specific expense ratio advantage (~15pp vs peers) has been stable, not widening. If the moat were widening, we would expect the gap to increase over time.
  - The thesis says competitors would need "multi-billion dollars and years" to replicate. But modern cloud/AI platforms can accelerate development cycles. A competitor does not need to match KNSL's exact system -- they just need to get their expense ratio from 35% to 25%, which halves KNSL's advantage.
  - The "hundreds of MGAs" entering E&S (per CEO Kehoe) suggests the barriers to entry are LOWER than the thesis implies, even if individual MGAs are not direct competitors.
  - KNSL's advantage is most pronounced in SMALL accounts (<$10K premium). This is a large but finite addressable market. As KNSL grows, it must move into larger accounts where the technology advantage is less decisive.
- **Severidad:** LOW
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The moat is real and durable for 5+ years, but the thesis overstates the impossibility of replication. A reasonable scenario is that the expense ratio gap narrows from 15pp to 8-10pp over 5-10 years. This is already partially reflected in the ROE normalization.

### Interpretacion

**MODERATE COUNTER:** The thesis identifies a genuinely high-quality business with a real and durable competitive advantage. The moat is wide and the management is excellent. However, the valuation work is optimistic, the QS adjustment pushes an upper-Tier-B company to Tier A which changes the entry calculus, and the bear case provides almost no downside protection. The market is not wrong that growth is decelerating -- it is pricing that in. The question is whether the market is pricing in TOO MUCH deceleration or just enough.

The thesis does NOT fail on business quality. It fails on PRICE. At $377, KNSL is a fair-to-slightly-expensive stock for what it is today. The thesis requires 19-20% BVPS growth and sustained premium multiples to reach $539 -- assumptions that are optimistic given the current trajectory.

## Recomendacion al Investment Committee

1. **Treat KNSL as Tier B (QS ~73) for entry purposes**, not Tier A. This means requiring 18-25% MoS, not 10-15%.

2. **Lower the standing order significantly.** At $340-355 (thesis proposal), the DA FV of $400 provides only 11-15% MoS -- inadequate for Tier B. A standing order at **$300-310** would provide 23-25% MoS vs DA FV, consistent with Tier B precedents (ROP at $300 = 22% MoS, ACGL at $88 = 20% MoS).

3. **Resolve the ACGL vs KNSL capital allocation question.** Both are E&S/specialty insurers. ACGL at P/B 1.5x with 17-21% ROE vs KNSL at P/B 4.5x with 26% ROE. The incremental 5-9pp of ROE is real but costs 3x the book value. The committee must explicitly decide: does the ROE premium justify the P/B premium? My assessment: no, not at a 3x ratio.

4. **Require the thesis to model REALISTIC growth.** Non-property growth of 8-10% (not 13.3%) and total GWP growth of 5-8% (not 12-15%) are more defensible base cases given the trajectory.

5. **Monitor two specific data points before setting any standing order:**
   - Q1 2026 GWP growth (expected April 2026): if total GWP goes negative or non-property drops below 8%, the growth thesis weakens further
   - Reserve development: if favorable development shrinks in Q1/Q2 2026, it may signal the property development tailwind is exhausting

6. **Do NOT buy at current price ($377).** Even under the bull thesis, the analyst recommended waiting ($340-355). Under the DA counter-valuation, current price is close to fair value with inadequate margin of safety.

## META-REFLECTION

### Dudas/Incertidumbres
- My counter-valuation assigns 3.5x P/B to a company with 26% ROE. Mathematically, 26% ROE on 10% CoE with 3% terminal growth justifies P/B of (26%-3%)/(10%-3%) = 3.3x in the Gordon model. So my 3.5x is actually slightly above theoretical justified. But the Gordon model undervalues high-growth compounders during their growth phase. If KNSL sustains >20% ROE for 5+ more years, the 2-stage model DOES justify 4.0-4.5x on current BVPS. The question is conviction on ROE sustainability -- and there I give the thesis partial credit but not full credit.
- The reserve risk is fundamentally unverifiable from outside the company. Management says reserves are "never more conservatively stated" but I cannot independently assess this. My HIGH severity rating is based on the industry-wide social inflation trend and the mathematical reality that 2020-2024 rapid growth created a large untested casualty book. I may be overweighting this risk.
- I was unable to find specific insider buying data beyond the aggregate figures (12.2K bought vs 5K sold). The CEO's "gifting" of 250 shares could be charitable/tax-planning or could be a subtle form of disposition. Without details, I classify this as NEUTRAL.
