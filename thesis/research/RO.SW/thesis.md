## Business Understanding

### The 2-Minute Explanation

Roche solves the problem of diagnosing and treating serious diseases at scale. It operates two synergistic divisions:

1. **Pharmaceuticals (77% of sales, CHF 47.7B)**: Develops and markets biologics and small molecules across oncology, immunology, neuroscience, ophthalmology, and infectious diseases. Top franchises: Ocrevus (MS, CHF 7.0B), Hemlibra (hemophilia A, CHF 4.8B), Vabysmo (eye diseases, CHF 4.1B), Tecentriq (cancer, CHF 3.6B), Xolair (allergies, CHF 3.1B).

2. **Diagnostics (23% of sales, CHF 13.8B)**: World's #1 in-vitro diagnostics (IVD) company with 19.6% global market share. Provides the testing infrastructure that identifies patients for pharma's targeted therapies. This creates a unique pharma-diagnostics flywheel that no competitor replicates at scale.

**Revenue model**: Product sales (drugs + diagnostic tests/instruments), primarily recurring (chronic disease treatments + diagnostic consumables). Over 70% of pharma revenue comes from drugs treating chronic conditions requiring ongoing therapy.

**What makes Roche unique**: The pharma-diagnostics integration is a structural competitive advantage. Diagnostics identifies patients who will respond to pharma's drugs (companion diagnostics). This reduces clinical trial costs, accelerates approvals, and creates a pull-through demand loop. No other company has this at Roche's scale.

### Unit Economics

- **R&D cost per NME**: ~CHF 3-4B (industry avg ~$2.6B), but offset by higher success rates through diagnostics-guided patient selection
- **FCF Margin**: 20.6% (CHF 13.1B on CHF 63.4B revenue)
- **Capital intensity**: Medium -- Capex/Depreciation 2.1x (investing in manufacturing expansion, including $50B US investment over 5 years for gene therapy, obesity, CGM)
- **LTV/CAC**: Not directly applicable (physician-mediated sales), but switching costs are very high for biologics (physician familiarity, patient titration, insurance formulary)
