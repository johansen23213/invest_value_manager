## CRITICAL FINDING (Immediate Attention Required)

**The thesis headline Fair Value of $255 is materially misleading.** The thesis itself calculates a weighted average FV of $212, then abandons it in favor of a forward estimate ($255) that requires (1) 16x EV/EBITDA multiple sustained AND (2) continued margin expansion on EBITDA that the Q4 2025 guidance has now revealed will grow only 3.6% in 2026 -- far below the thesis's 5.5% revenue growth assumption. The Q4 guidance showed revenue of $17.05-17.15B (3.1% growth), not the thesis's assumed $17.8B. **The $255 FV is based on assumptions that the actual guidance has already invalidated.**

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The RSG thesis describes a genuine quality business with durable moat characteristics. However, the investment case is severely weakened by three problems: (1) the Fair Value is inflated by ~$40-55 per share due to selective weighting of the most optimistic valuation method and now-stale growth assumptions; (2) at $221, RSG is approximately fairly valued or slightly overvalued by nearly every independent method; and (3) even the thesis's own entry target of $210 provides inadequate MoS when the FV is honestly recalculated using actual Q4 2025 guidance data.

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. Fair Value of $255 -- Forward 16x on $5.5B EBITDA

- **Thesis assumes:** 2026E EBITDA of $5,500M at 16x EV/EBITDA = FV $255. Additionally assumes 5.5% revenue growth for 2026.
- **Evidencia en contra:** Q4 2025 actual results and 2026 guidance directly contradict these assumptions.
  - **Actual 2026 revenue guidance:** $17.05-17.15B (midpoint $17.1B) vs thesis assumption of $17.8B. That is a **$700M shortfall** (3.1% growth vs 5.5% assumed).
  - **Actual 2026 EBITDA guidance:** $5.475-5.525B (midpoint $5.5B). While the EBITDA number is close to thesis ($5.5B), the EBITDA growth rate is only 3.6%, not the ~7% implied by the thesis's revenue growth + margin expansion.
  - **Actual 2026 EPS guidance:** $7.20-7.28 (midpoint $7.24) vs consensus $7.30. **Below consensus.** This is not the "confirmation of growth trajectory" the thesis expected.
  - Using 16x on $5.5B gives $255, but 16x is the CURRENT trading multiple. Using a forward FV at the current multiple is circular logic -- it says "the stock is worth what it trades at plus a year of growth." A 15x multiple (sector average, as used in the thesis's own Method 2) on $5.5B gives $232. A 14x multiple (historical low-end) gives $209.
  - The thesis itself calculated a WEIGHTED average FV of $212 using trailing metrics but then **chose to override this** in favor of the forward number. This is selective bias.
- **Severidad:** **CRITICAL**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Fair Value should be recalculated using the actual 2026 guidance. A defensible FV is $213-232 (15x-16x on actual guided EBITDA of $5.5B). The $255 headline should be revised downward.

### 2. Revenue Growth of 5.5% Annually

- **Thesis assumes:** Organic growth 4.5% + M&A 1.5% = ~6.5% for 2026-2030, modeled at 5.5% for conservatism.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - **Actual FY2025 revenue growth: 3.5%** (not 5.5%).
  - **2026 guided revenue growth: 3.1%** at midpoint ($17.1B on $16.59B base).
  - **Volume is NEGATIVE:** Volume declined total revenue by 1.0% in both 2025 and guided 2026. The thesis assumed +0.5% volume growth.
  - Revenue growth is decelerating: 19.6% (2022) -> 10.8% (2023) -> 7.1% (2024) -> 3.5% (2025) -> 3.1% guided (2026). This is a clear downtrend.
  - The thesis's "mid-single-digit revenue growth" long-term algorithm is not visible in recent results. What is visible is pricing offsetting volume declines, resulting in low-single-digit growth.
  - Average yield guidance for 2026 is 3.2-3.7% on total revenue (down from 4.1% in 2025), suggesting pricing power may be moderating.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Revenue growth assumptions should be revised to 3-4% for near-term (2026-2027) based on actual guidance, with potential improvement to 5% only if volumes recover.

### 3. ROIC Spread of +2.0pp Is "Modest But Improving"

- **Thesis assumes:** ROIC 8.9% vs WACC 6.9% = +2.0pp spread, with trajectory improving.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - A +2.0pp ROIC-WACC spread is the narrowest of any company in our pipeline or portfolio. For comparison: AUTO.L has +38pp, MONY.L has +22pp, BYIT.L has +21pp, ADBE has +15pp.
  - The thesis uses WACC of 6.9% (calculated at 6.6%, rounded to 7.0%). If we use a more realistic WACC of 8-9% (which our DCF tool defaults to, and which is more typical for leveraged industrials), the ROIC spread DISAPPEARS or turns negative. ROIC 8.9% vs WACC 9.0% = -0.1pp.
  - Historical precedent in this portfolio: **every position where ROIC < WACC was confirmed has been exited** (8/8 pattern, per decisions_log.yaml). If the WACC is even slightly higher than 6.9%, this precedent would trigger concern.
  - The low beta of 0.55 that drives the low WACC is a backward-looking metric. If RSG's beta normalizes to 0.7-0.8 (still defensive), WACC rises to 8.0-8.5% and the spread vanishes.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee should stress-test the ROIC-WACC spread at WACC of 8.0% and 9.0%. If ROIC does not clearly exceed WACC by >1pp at reasonable WACC assumptions, this undermines the quality thesis.

### 4. "Duopoly Creates Durable Local Monopolies"

- **Thesis assumes:** RSG's position as #2 in a duopoly with WM is an unassailable moat. Landfill permits are irreplaceable.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - The duopoly narrative is correct at the national level but incomplete. RSG does NOT control all markets. Waste Connections (#3) is growing faster and trades at a premium (18x EV/EBITDA) precisely because it operates in exclusive markets where RSG/WM do not compete.
  - RSG is SHEDDING residential contracts -- residential volumes were -3% in Q4 2025 and are expected to be negative for all of 2026. The thesis portrays this as "disciplined" but it could also be interpreted as losing competitive positions in lower-margin markets.
  - The Environmental Solutions segment (from US Ecology acquisition at $2.2B) has been weak: revenue down 7% YoY in Q3 2025, EBITDA down 25%. This is NOT a durable local monopoly business -- it is cyclical and competitive.
  - Recycled commodity prices declined 29% YoY (from $164/ton to $135/ton in 2025, guided $115/ton for 2026). This is a headwind the thesis barely mentions.
  - The moat is real for LANDFILL disposal but NOT for collection (which is 65-70% of revenue). Collection is competitive, subject to municipal bidding, and margins are thinner.
- **Severidad:** **MODERATE**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The moat assessment should distinguish between disposal (strong moat) and collection (weaker moat). The ES segment weakness should be given more weight.

### 5. "Defensive Compounder Worth Owning on Pullbacks"

- **Thesis assumes:** RSG is a quality defensive compounder that should be accumulated on pullbacks.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - **The reverse DCF tells a damning story.** At current price of $221, the market implies 25.3% FCF growth per year for 5 years. Historical FCF CAGR is 12.3%. The gap is -13pp. To justify current price, RSG needs to DOUBLE its historical FCF growth rate. This is not what a "fairly valued" stock looks like -- it is what an EXPENSIVE stock looks like.
  - Owner Earnings Yield of 3.72% + 7% growth = 10.72% expected total return. This is BELOW our Tier B precedent of 12%+ (per valuation report). The thesis acknowledges this.
  - The stock is up only 2% in the last 12 months despite strong operational execution. This suggests the premium is already fully priced.
  - P/E of 32.9x is at the high end of its historical range. It is above the US Commercial Services industry median of 25.7x.
  - The thesis analyst himself writes "RSG is approximately FAIRLY VALUED at current prices" and "the market has already priced in the quality." These are his own words from the thesis body -- yet the headline FV is $255 (implying 15% upside).
- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** If the thesis's own analyst acknowledges the stock is fairly valued, the FV should reflect that assessment, not contradict it.
