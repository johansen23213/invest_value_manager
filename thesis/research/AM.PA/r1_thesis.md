# AM.PA - Dassault Aviation

## TL;DR

Dassault Aviation is a rare Tier A defense/aerospace company: 77.5% family-owned, net cash EUR 9.4B (~34% of market cap), 220-aircraft Rafale backlog providing 8+ years visibility, and a EUR ~14B hidden asset (26.6% Thales stake). ## Business Understanding

### What Dassault Aviation Does

Dassault Aviation designs, manufactures, and supports two product lines:

1. **Rafale Fighter Aircraft (~65% of revenue)**: France's primary combat aircraft. Multi-role fighter with air-to-air, air-to-ground, and nuclear strike capability. Sold to 8+ countries. Production ramping from 26/yr (2025) to 35/yr by 2029-2030. Current backlog: 220 aircraft (175 export + 45 France), worth EUR ~50B+ at ~EUR 230M/unit average.

2. **Falcon Business Jets (~35% of revenue)**: Ultra-long-range executive jets. Portfolio includes Falcon 6X (entry into service 2023), Falcon 8X, and upcoming Falcon 10X (largest cabin in class, service entry late 2027). 37 deliveries in 2025 (vs 31 in 2024). Backlog: 73 aircraft (declining from 79).

### Revenue Model
- **Delivery-based recognition**: Revenue is lumpy, recognized on aircraft delivery. Defense deliveries drive significant quarterly/annual swings.
- **Aftermarket/support**: Growing but smaller than OEM sales.
- **Thales stake**: 26.6% of Thales (defense electronics) contributes EUR ~460-500M/yr to net income via equity method. At EUR 263/share, the stake is worth EUR ~14.4B.

### Unit Economics
- Rafale unit price: ~EUR 200-250M (varies by configuration and customer)
- Falcon 10X list price: ~EUR 75M
- Gross margin expanding: 32.9% (2021) to 37.8% (2024)
- EBIT margin: 8.7% (2024), lower than peers due to fixed-price defense contracts and delivery mix
- FCF highly variable: EUR 1.5B (2021), EUR 4.9B (2022, advance payments), EUR -1.0B (2023, production ramp), EUR 1.5B (2024)

### H1 2025 Results (Most Recent)
- Revenue: EUR 2.8B (+12% YoY)
- EBIT: EUR 180M (+6% YoY)
- Net income: EUR 386M (includes EUR 67M French tax surcharge)
- Order intake: EUR 8.1B (up from EUR 5.1B H1 2024 -- massive)
- FY2025 guidance raised to >EUR 7B revenue (from previous guidance)

### Structure of Ownership
- **Groupe Industriel Marcel Dassault (GIMD)**: 77.5% -- Dassault family holding
- **Public float**: ~12.5%
- **Airbus**: 9.94%
- **Total shares**: 83.5M (very low float = low liquidity, high insider alignment)

### The Thales Hidden Asset

Dassault's 26.6% stake in Thales (HO.PA) at current price EUR 263 = ~EUR 14.4B.
- Dassault's entire market cap: EUR 27.2B
- Thales stake: EUR 14.4B = **53% of market cap**
- Implies the CORE aviation business is valued at EUR 12.8B
- Core business generates ~EUR 6.2B revenue with 8.7% EBIT margin = EUR 540M EBIT
- Core EV/EBIT on aviation alone: ~24x (after subtracting Thales and cash)

This is a structural discount but NOT new -- the market has always valued Dassault with the Thales overhang because the family will not sell it.

## Why It Could Be Expensive at EUR 351

### Narrative
The market is pricing in:
1. **European defense rearmament**: NATO 3%+ GDP, multi-year contracts, structural spending upcycle
2. **Rafale export success**: 220-aircraft backlog, ramp to 35/yr, 8+ customer nations
3. **Falcon 10X catalyst**: New flagship jet entering service 2027
4. **Family ownership premium**: Aligned interests, no short-term pressure

### Counter-Thesis (Why Near ATH Might Be Fair Value)
1. **Revenue CAGR is negative (-4.9%)**: yfinance data captures 2021-2024, which includes a massive revenue drop in 2023 (delivery timing). However, the REAL trajectory is UP: FY2025 guided >EUR 7B (vs 6.2B in 2024 = +13%), and 2026 production ramp continues. The negative CAGR is a data artifact of lumpy defense deliveries.
2. **Rafale backlog provides 8+ years visibility**: At 26-35 deliveries/yr, 220 aircraft = 6-8 years of production. New orders from France (52+ post-2027), potential new export customers (Saudi Arabia, India follow-on) extend this further.
3. **EPS CAGR +17.4%** reflects improving mix and Thales contribution
4. **EUR 9.4B net cash** protects downside massively

### But -- Why Near ATH Is Concerning
1. **Analyst consensus PT EUR 328 is BELOW current price (EUR 351)**: 17 analysts, avg PT EUR 328, high EUR 390, low EUR 270. The stock is ABOVE consensus. This means the market may be ahead of fundamentals.
2. **EU defense stocks have rallied ~260% since Feb 2022**: Dassault has participated. Much of the rearmament thesis is priced in.
3. **P/E 35x is historically elevated** for a defense company. 5-year average P/E for Dassault is ~25-28x. Current premium reflects narrative, not earnings acceleration (yet).
4. **Falcon backlog declining**: 73 aircraft (from 79). Falcon 10X delayed to 2027. Business jet cycle uncertain.
5. **FCF highly volatile**: EUR 4.9B to EUR -1.0B to EUR 1.5B. DCF is unreliable for companies with this pattern.
6. **Low liquidity**: 12.5% free float means large orders move price significantly. Getting in (and out) at target prices is harder.

## Preliminary Valuation

### Method 1: Sum-of-Parts (SOTP) -- PRIMARY (60% weight)

This is the most appropriate method given the Thales stake represents 53% of market cap.

| Component | Value | Basis |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Thales stake (26.6% of EUR 54.1B MCap) | EUR 14.4B | Market value today |
| Aviation business (EV/EBIT 18x on normalized EUR 600M) | EUR 10.8B | 18x = defense peer discount for low-margin OEM |
| Net cash | EUR 9.4B | Balance sheet |
| **Total equity value** | **EUR 34.6B** | |
| **Per share (83.5M shares)** | **EUR 414** | |

Notes:
- EBIT normalized at EUR 600M (FY2025 guided >EUR 7B revenue x 8.5% margin)
- 18x EBIT is conservative vs Thales 23x, BAE Systems 21x, but appropriate for OEM with fixed-price risk
- No conglomerate discount applied (family alignment actually warrants PREMIUM, but Thales illiquidity = cannot monetize)
- If apply 10% conglomerate discount on Thales: SOTP = EUR 391/share

### Method 2: Owner Earnings Yield (OEY) -- Tier A Secondary (40% weight)

```
Normalized FCF: EUR 1.5B (FY2024, conservative -- excludes EUR 4.9B FY2022 advance)
Maintenance capex: ~EUR 300M (Dep EUR 200M x 1.5 for defense ramp)
Owner Earnings: EUR 1.2B

Owner Earnings Yield: EUR 1.2B / EUR 27.2B MCap = 4.4%
Expected sustainable growth: ~8% (revenue ramp 13% FY2025, normalizing to 5-8% on delivery schedule)
Dividend yield: 1.4%

Total expected return: 4.4% + 8% + 1.4% = 13.8%
vs WACC 7.0%: Spread +6.8pp -- POSITIVE but not exceptional
```

Implied FV from OEY + Growth:
- If WACC = 7%: Can justify paying up to OEY 3-4% (given 8% growth)
- OEY at 3.5% implies MCap = EUR 1.2B / 0.035 = EUR 34.3B = EUR 411/share
- OEY at 4.0% implies MCap = EUR 30.0B = EUR 359/share

## META-REFLECTION

### Confidence Level: HIGH on quality assessment, MEDIUM-HIGH on valuation

The quality case is robust and data-driven. The valuation is more uncertain due to:
- FCF lumpiness making DCF unreliable (acknowledged, used SOTP instead)
- Thales stake valuation tied to defense sentiment (could swing 10-20%)
- Low float means price can gap significantly on limited volume

### Key Uncertainties
- **FY2025 annual results (Mar 4)**: Will confirm or deny the >EUR 7B guidance and margin trajectory
- **Ukraine peace talks impact**: If ceasefire occurs, defense stocks could correct 10-20% on sentiment alone (budgets won't change but multiples will compress)
- **Falcon 10X execution**: Any further delay would hurt Falcon franchise value
- **How much of Thales valuation is defense euphoria?** Thales at P/E 51.8x is historically very expensive. If Thales corrects, Dassault's SOTP drops materially

### Anomalies Detected
- **Revenue CAGR -4.9% in quality_scorer but actual trajectory is strongly positive**: The tool uses yfinance data that captures 2021-2023 (which included a massive delivery dip in 2023). FY2025 guided >EUR 7B vs EUR 6.2B (2024) = +13% growth. This creates a distortion where the QS penalizes revenue growth heavily (0/10) when the real trajectory is positive. This is a known limitation for defense companies with lumpy delivery schedules.
- **FCF margin of 71% in 2022**: This was driven by large advance payments from Rafale export customers, not sustainable operations. The tool captures it but it distorts historical averages.

### Suggestions for the System
- Consider a "delivery-adjusted revenue growth" metric for defense/aerospace companies where revenue is driven by lumpy aircraft deliveries rather than steady-state demand
- The SOTP approach should be formalized as a skill for companies with major equity stakes (Dassault/Thales, Berkshire, Samsung/Samsung Electronics, etc.)

2. FY2025 results on Mar 4 -- should this be added to the calendar for a post-results re-evaluation if price corrects to EUR 310-320?
