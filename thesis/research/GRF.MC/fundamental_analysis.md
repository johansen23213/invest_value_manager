## Business Understanding

### Model de Negocio

**What problem does Grifols solve?**
Grifols manufactures life-saving plasma-derived medicines from donated human blood plasma. These products are NOT substitutable -- they are biological products that cannot be synthetically manufactured at scale. Key products:

1. **Immunoglobulins (IVIg/SCIg)** -- ~55% of revenue. Treats immune deficiencies, neurological disorders (CIDP, Guillain-Barre), and autoimmune conditions. Growing 15-18% annually.
2. **Albumin** -- ~20% of revenue. Used in critical care, liver disease, plasma exchange. China is the key growth market.
3. **Clotting Factors (Factor VIII/IX)** -- ~10% of revenue. For hemophilia patients. Declining due to recombinant competition.
4. **Alpha-1 Antitrypsin** -- ~10% of revenue. Grifols has dominant position in A1AT for genetic emphysema.
5. **Diagnostic Solutions** -- ~5% of revenue. Blood typing, NAT screening.

**Revenue model:** Recurring consumable -- patients need regular infusions (weekly/monthly). Once prescribed, switching is rare. Revenue is tied to plasma collection capacity (the bottleneck).

**Revenue breakdown by segment (FY2024):**
- Biopharma: EUR 5,849M (81%)
- Diagnostic: EUR 781M (11%)
- Bio Supplies: EUR 583M (8%)

### Industry Structure -- The Plasma Oligopoly

The plasma-derived medicines industry is one of the most structurally defensible oligopolies in healthcare:

| Company | Approx Market Share | Key Strengths |
|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| CSL Behring | ~35% | Largest, best margins, most efficient |
| Takeda (Shire legacy) | ~20% | Strong in IG, broad portfolio |
| **Grifols** | **~18-20%** | **#3, strong in albumin/A1AT, largest EU player** |
| Octapharma | ~10% | Private, family-owned |
| Kedrion/BPL | ~5% | Niche |

**Barriers to entry are EXTREME:**
- Regulatory: FDA/EMA approval takes 5-10 years for new plasma facilities
- Capital: A single fractionation plant costs EUR 500M-1B
- Supply: You need 400+ plasma collection centers (Grifols has ~400)
- Biology: Human plasma cannot be synthetically replicated at scale
- Safety: Blood-borne pathogen risk requires decades of safety track record

**This is a natural oligopoly.** New entrants cannot realistically challenge the Top 3. The competitive dynamics are more like utilities than pharma.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema
- **quality_scorer.py enhancement:** For turnaround situations, the tool should have a --turnaround flag that weights forward trajectory (improving metrics) alongside backward-looking data. Currently, a company improving from terrible to decent gets the same score as one that has always been terrible.
- **Sector view:** We need a dedicated "blood-plasma" or "plasma-derived-medicines" sector view. The current pharma-healthcare.md does not cover this subsector. Grifols is fundamentally different from pharma companies (no patent cliff, no drug discovery, biological commodity with oligopoly dynamics).
- **Leverage-adjusted ROIC:** For highly leveraged turnarounds, ROIC is distorted by the massive debt on the balance sheet (goodwill from acquisitions). An unlevered ROIC or ROIC-excluding-goodwill metric would be more informative. This is similar to the serial acquirer adjustment pattern.

3. Should I create a separate "blood-plasma" sector view?
