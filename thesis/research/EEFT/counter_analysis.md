# Counter-Analysis: EEFT (Euronet Worldwide, Inc.)

## Fecha: 2026-02-18

## **!! CRITICAL FINDING: Organic Growth Is Stalling -- EPS Growth Is Buyback-Engineered !!**

The thesis rests on 10-15% EPS growth guidance at P/E 5.8x as proof of extreme undervaluation. But my independent decomposition reveals that **the majority of recent EPS growth comes from aggressive share buybacks, NOT organic business growth**. On a constant-currency basis, FY2025 revenue grew only 4% and Q4 2025 revenue grew only 1%. This is a fundamentally different investment case than the thesis presents.

---

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The bull thesis PARTIALLY survives scrutiny but requires material downward revision. The core narrative of "market pricing -19.7%/yr FCF decline but company guiding 10-15% EPS growth" is MISLEADING because: (1) EPS growth is substantially buyback-driven, not organic; (2) constant-currency revenue growth has decelerated to 1% in Q4; (3) a NEW federal 1% remittance tax (law since July 2025) directly impacts Money Transfer's largest segment; (4) 14% short interest with NO insider buying at 52-week lows signals informed skepticism. The FV of $133 is significantly overstated. My bear-case fair value is $65-75, suggesting the stock is fairly valued to slightly overvalued at current prices.

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. "Market Is Pricing -19.7%/yr FCF Decline, But Company Growing"

- **Evidencia en contra:** The thesis frames this as an obvious mispricing. But the market may be correctly pricing STRUCTURAL DECLINE, not a cyclical dip. Here is my EPS decomposition:

**EPS Growth Decomposition (FY2022-FY2025):**

|------|---------|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| 2022 | $6.51 | -- | -- | -- | ~52.4M | -- |

In FY2025, net income grew by only $3.5M (from $306M to $309.5M = +1.1%). Yet EPS grew 11.6%. The gap is entirely attributable to share count reduction from 47.4M to 45.2M (~4.6% fewer shares). On a PER SHARE basis, ~43% of the EPS growth in FY2025 came from buybacks, not business improvement.

In Q4 2025 specifically, constant-currency revenue grew ONLY 1%, and adjusted operating income DECLINED 6% in constant currency. The 15% Q4 EPS growth is almost entirely financial engineering via buybacks.

- **Severidad:** **CRITICAL**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The investment committee must understand that the "10-15% EPS growth" guidance for FY2026 will likely also be substantially buyback-dependent. If revenue growth continues at 4% CC and margins stabilize, organic EPS growth is ~5-6% at best. The remaining 4-9% comes from buyback accretion. This is a FINANCIAL ENGINEERING story, not a growth story. Valuation should reflect this reality.

### 3. "FCF $616M on $3B Market Cap = 20.5% FCF Yield"

- **Evidencia en contra:** This FCF yield is real but may not be sustainable. Several factors:

(a) **FCF includes ATM cash float benefits.** Euronet holds ~$650M in ATM cash at any given time. Changes in ATM cash deployment affect working capital and inflate/deflate reported FCF in any given year. The "normalized" FCF may be lower.

(b) **Capex is rising.** The pivot to merchant acquiring in EFT, digital investment in Ria, and CoreCard integration all require capital. If capex rises from $170M to $220M+ (management has signaled increased investment), FCF drops to ~$560M.

(c) **FCF is being returned to shareholders via buybacks at DECLINING stock prices.** In FY2025, EEFT repurchased $388M of shares at an average price of ~$80-90 (higher than current $70.55). This means the company bought back shares at HIGHER prices than where the stock sits today. Value destruction, not creation.

(d) **The convertible notes ($850M due 2030) are a contingent call on FCF.** If the stock stays below $127 at maturity, Euronet must repay $850M in cash. That is 1.4x current FCF. Not a crisis (they have 4+ years), but it reduces the true "free" cash flow available for shareholders.

- **Severidad:** **MODERATE**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Use normalized FCF of $520-560M (adjusting for ATM cash and rising capex), not peak $616M. FCF yield of 17-19% is still attractive but less extraordinary than claimed.

### 4. "Immigration Headwind Is Temporary"

- **Evidencia en contra:** This is NOT temporary. Multiple structural policy changes compound:

(a) **The 1% Remittance Tax is NOW LAW.** The "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" (signed July 4, 2025) imposes a 1% excise tax on remittances sent via cash, money orders, or cashier's checks. This went into effect January 1, 2026. Ria's core business model is CASH-BASED remittance transfers. This tax directly hits Ria's cost proposition vs digital competitors (Wise, Remitly) whose transfers via bank accounts/debit cards are EXEMPT from the tax.

This is a NEWLY DISCOVERED RISK not mentioned in the R1 analysis. The remittance tax:
- Adds 1% friction to Ria's cash-based transfers (its primary channel)
- Does NOT apply to Wise/Remitly bank-to-bank digital transfers
- Creates compliance burden and operational cost for Ria's 639K agent locations
- Effectively subsidizes digital competitors at Ria's expense
- Mexico alone could see $1.5B+ in reduced formal remittances annually

(b) **ICE enforcement is INTENSIFYING, not moderating.** DHS reports 1,200+ arrests per day in 2026. Over 3 million undocumented immigrants have left the US (deportation + self-deportation). This directly reduces the remittance-sending population.

(c) **Central Bank of Mexico data:** Remittances to Mexico fell ~5% in FY2025. While some argue immigrants are sending more pre-emptively, the NET effect is negative for sustained volumes.

(d) **Even the "positive" counter-narrative is concerning:** Newsweek reports remittances to Latin America are UP 8% in 2026 -- but this is panic-sending before expected deportation, not sustainable growth. When the sending population physically leaves the US, remittances will fall sharply.

- **Severidad:** **CRITICAL** (upgraded from HIGH due to remittance tax discovery)
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The 1% remittance tax MUST be added as a kill condition. This structurally disadvantages Ria's cash-based model vs digital competitors. Combined with immigration enforcement, Money Transfer (42% of revenue) faces a dual headwind that is structural, not cyclical.

### 5. "CEO Owns 6.2% -- Aligned Incentives"

- **Evidencia en contra:** CEO Michael Brown has made 6 transactions in the past 5 years: **0 buys, 6 sells**. Most recently, he sold 95,000 shares on March 13, 2025 at $100.69 (netting ~$10M). Total insider activity over 18 months: 194,350 shares SOLD vs only 3,206 shares BOUGHT (net sale of 191,144 shares).

If the CEO genuinely believed in 10-15% EPS growth at P/E 5.8x (implying ~100% upside to thesis FV of $133), he would be buying aggressively at 52-week lows, not selling. His behavior is inconsistent with the bull thesis. He sold at $100 and has NOT bought at $66-70.

Per the critical-thinking skill, insider behavior is Level 1 (primary data) -- it is what people DO with their own money, not what they SAY in earnings calls. The CEO's actions contradict his words.

- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee should heavily weight this signal. "Skin in the game" analysis (per our skill) says: the CEO has skin in the game (6.2% ownership) but is REDUCING it, not increasing it. This is the OPPOSITE of what a confident insider does at supposed deep value prices.
