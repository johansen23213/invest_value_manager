## Business Understanding

### Model de Negocio

Goodwin PLC is a 140-year-old family-controlled specialty engineering group based in Stoke-on-Trent, UK. The company operates through two segments:

**Mechanical Engineering Division (63% of segment profit, ~71% of revenue):**
- **Goodwin Steel Castings**: Large-scale steel castings for nuclear waste containers (SSBs), naval propulsion components (AUKUS submarines, Type 26 frigates), and industrial applications
- **Goodwin International**: Precision-machined valves for oil/gas, LNG, nuclear, and water industries
- **Noreva GmbH** (Germany): Axial valves, particularly strong in LNG projects (US, Qatar)
- **Easat Radar Systems**: Turnkey radar systems for air traffic control, defense, and border security
- **Duvelco International**: Greenfield polyimide resin plant (patent pending, competitor to DuPont Vespel). GBP 14M invested. Startup phase -- expected to contribute starting FY2027
- **Slurry pumps**: Mining pumps (India, South Africa, global). New SlurryChamp excavator attachment

**Refractory Engineering Division (37% of segment profit, ~29% of revenue):**
- **Investment casting materials**: ~80% global market share after Kerr's 2017 exit. Powders for jewelry industry
- **Hoben mineral processing**: Cristobalite, perlite, vermiculite products
- **Dupre Minerals**: Core markets normalizing post-COVID
- **AVD fire suppression**: Lithium-ion battery fire extinguishing agents (UL certified, US expansion)

### Revenue Model

Revenue is project/order-based with significant lumping in the mechanical division. Order book provides visibility: GBP 365M as of October 2025, vs GBP 296M a year earlier (+23%). Revenue is NOT recurring in the SaaS sense, but defense/nuclear contracts are multi-year and provide extended visibility. The refractory division has more recurring characteristics (consumable materials for jewelry industry).

### Unit Economics

- **Capital intensity**: Medium. Capex/Revenue ~6-7% (GBP 13M capex on GBP 220M revenue). Capex/Depreciation ~2.0x (investing for growth). The company sources used equipment from liquidation sales, keeping capex low
- **FCF conversion**: Volatile. FY2025 was exceptional (OCF/NI = 2.4x). Historical average is lower. Working capital swings driven by large project timing
- **Gross Margin**: 41.7% FY2025, expanding to 49.3% in H1 FY2026 (extraordinary improvement from higher-margin defense/nuclear work displacing lower-margin general engineering)
- **Apprentice training moat**: 300+ graduates from 13-year program, primarily retained. Addresses regional skills shortage competitors face

### Why It Appears Attractive (at first glance)

1. **Exceptional recent growth**: Revenue +15% FY2025, H1 FY2026 +27%. Trading profit +47% FY2025, H1 FY2026 +118%
2. **Defense/nuclear tailwinds**: AUKUS submarine program, nuclear waste containment, NATO spending
3. **Family control with skin in the game**: 64% insider ownership, multi-generational commitment
4. **Near-zero debt**: Net debt GBP 5.8M at H1 FY2026 (before special dividend)
5. **High QS**: 80-86 Tier A
6. **Multiple growth optionalities**: Duvelco (polyimide), Easat (radar), AVD (fire suppression), SlurryChamp

## Valuation

### Approach

For Tier A companies, the primary method is Owner Earnings Yield + Reverse DCF. However, Goodwin presents a special challenge: the company is in the middle of an unprecedented earnings inflection. FY2025 pre-tax profit was GBP 35.5M; FY2026E guidance is GBP 71M+ (doubling). This makes trailing metrics meaningless and forward estimates uncertain beyond FY2026.

### Method 1: EV/EBIT on Normalized Earnings (60% weight)

I use EV/EBIT rather than OEY because FCF is too volatile (CV 2.8) and the business is mid-inflection.

**Current EV:** Market Cap GBP 2.0B + Net Debt GBP 53M (post-special dividend) = GBP 2.05B

**Normalized EBIT scenarios:**

| Scenario | EBIT | Basis | EV/EBIT Multiple | Fair Value |
|----------|------|-------|-------------------|------------|
| FY2026 Guidance | GBP 71M | Management guidance (high confidence) | 15x | GBP 1.065B = 13,850p |
| FY2026 Guidance | GBP 71M | Management guidance | 18x | GBP 1.278B = 16,600p |
| FY2027 Blue Sky | GBP 90M | Analyst est. (Sweet Stocks) + Duvelco | 15x | GBP 1.35B = 17,550p |
| FY2027 Blue Sky | GBP 90M | Blue sky | 18x | GBP 1.62B = 21,050p |

**Multiple justification:** Specialty engineering companies with defense exposure typically trade at 12-18x EBIT. Comparable UK specialty engineers: Spirax-Sarco (22x but larger/more diversified), Halma (25x but serial acquirer), Rotork (18x). Given Goodwin's smaller size, illiquidity, family governance, and lumpier earnings, a 15-18x range is appropriate. I weight toward 15x for conservatism given the cyclical nature of defense orders.

**Method 1 Fair Value: 15,000p** (using GBP 71M EBIT at ~16x average multiple, net of debt)

### Method 2: Owner Earnings Yield (40% weight)

**FY2025 actuals (normalizing FCF):**
- FCF reported: GBP 42M (inflated by working capital releases)
- Normalized FCF: Use 3-year average FCF margin (9.6%) on FY2026E revenue (~GBP 280M est.) = GBP 27M
- Depreciation: ~GBP 6M
- Maintenance Capex: GBP 6M x 1.1 = GBP 6.6M
- Owner Earnings: GBP 27M - GBP 6.6M + GBP 6M = GBP 26.4M (approximately)

BUT: H1 FY2026 shows FCF margin of 27.4% trading margin -- if sustained, FCF could be materially higher. Let me use the H1 data more directly:

**FY2026E estimates:**
- Revenue: ~GBP 280M (H1 GBP 135.6M x 2, with H2 historically similar or stronger)
- Post-tax profit: ~GBP 54M (GBP 71M pre-tax at 24% effective rate)
- Capex: ~GBP 18M (H1 GBP 9.3M x 2)
- D&A: ~GBP 12M (estimated)
- FCF estimate: GBP 54M + GBP 12M - GBP 18M +/- WC = ~GBP 48M (base), GBP 35-60M range

**Owner Earnings (FY2026E base):**
- FCF GBP 48M - Maintenance Capex GBP 7M + D&A GBP 12M - D&A GBP 12M = ~GBP 41M
- OEY = GBP 41M / GBP 2.0B market cap = 2.05%
- OEY + Expected Growth (~10%) = 12.05% vs WACC ~9% = spread of +3.05%

This is a THIN spread for a Tier A compounder. Compare to precedents:
- ADBE at purchase: OEY + Growth = 15.1% vs WACC 9% = +6.1pp spread
- BYIT.L at purchase: OEY + Growth = 14.4% vs WACC 9% = +5.4pp spread
- NVO at purchase: OEY + Growth = 14.8% vs WACC 9.5% = +5.3pp spread

**Method 2 Fair Value:** Using a 4% target OEY (given growth profile): GBP 41M / 4% = GBP 1.025B = 13,300p
