## Business Understanding

### Model de Negocio

**Problem solved:** Businesses need clean, compliant workwear, facility supplies (first aid, fire protection, restroom supplies), and safety services. Managing this in-house is expensive and distracting. Cintas provides it as a recurring service.

**Revenue model:** Route-based rental/service model. ~80% recurring revenue. Customers sign 3-5 year contracts. Cintas delivers, picks up, launders, and returns uniforms on weekly routes. Cross-sells first aid, safety, restroom, and fire protection services on the same route stops.

**Revenue breakdown (H1 FY2026):**
- Uniform Rental & Facility Services: $4.28B (77% of total, +8.3% YoY)
- First Aid & Safety + All Other: $1.28B (23%, +12.8% YoY)

**Unit Economics:**
- Customer retention: 97% (extremely high)
- Contract duration: 3-5 years, 92% renewal rate
- Route density economics: Adding a customer to an existing route costs nearly zero incremental delivery cost. More customers per route = higher margins
- Cross-sell opportunity: 15M+ businesses in US that do not yet rent uniforms ("no-programmers")
- CAC/LTV: Not publicly disclosed, but 97% retention with multi-year contracts implies very high LTV/CAC

**Why this business is excellent:**
1. Recurring, subscription-like revenue (laundry routes are weekly)
2. Extremely high switching costs (operational disruption to change uniform provider)
3. Route density creates natural local monopoly economics
4. Cross-selling on same routes drives margin expansion with minimal incremental cost
5. Capital-light at the margin (heavy upfront, but incremental customers are very profitable)

### Moat Assessment

**WIDE moat (22/25 adjusted):**

1. **Route density / scale economies (10/10):** Cintas operates thousands of delivery routes across North America. Each route has a fixed cost (truck, driver, fuel). Adding customers to an existing route has near-zero marginal cost. This creates a powerful local-market moat where competitors cannot match economics without equivalent density. Similar to waste management route economics.

2. **Switching costs (8/10):** Changing uniform provider requires new sizing, new contracts, often disrupts employees' daily routines (different pickup/delivery schedule). For businesses with hundreds of workers, this is operationally painful. 97% retention confirms switching costs are real and high.

3. **Cross-sell platform (4/5):** Once Cintas has the route relationship, cross-selling first aid, safety, fire protection, and restroom services on the same truck stop is almost free incremental revenue. Competitors offering only one category cannot match this integrated value proposition.

4. **Brand / reputation (moderate):** Cintas has strong brand in B2B space but this is not a consumer brand moat.

**Competitive landscape:**
- #1 Cintas: ~31-40% US market share, $10.3B revenue
- #2 Aramark (uniform division): ~7% share
- #3 UniFirst: ~5% share
- Fragmented remainder: local/regional operators

The 4-6x size advantage over #2 is significant and growing.

## Valuation

### Method 1 (Primary, 60%): Owner Earnings Yield

```
FCF FY2025: $1.8B
Depreciation: ~$380M (estimated)
Maintenance Capex: ~$420M (D&A * 1.1)
Owner Earnings = $1.8B - $420M + $380M = $1.76B

Owner Earnings Yield = $1.76B / $79.6B market cap = 2.2%
Expected sustainable growth: 8-10%
OEY + Growth = 2.2% + 9% = 11.2%

vs WACC 9.2% = spread of +2.0pp
```

**Interpretation:** The spread is POSITIVE but THIN. For a Tier A compounder, an OEY+Growth vs WACC spread of only +2.0pp means the market is pricing in most of the compounding already. Compare with our precedents:
- ADBE at purchase: OEY+Growth ~14.4% vs WACC ~9% = +5.4pp spread
- BYIT.L at purchase: OEY+Growth ~14.4% vs WACC ~9% = +5.4pp spread
- LULU at purchase: much wider spread

At $198, CTAS offers a very slim margin of safety via the OEY method.

**What price would give an acceptable OEY + Growth spread?**
For a Tier A compounder, we want OEY+Growth - WACC >= 4pp (based on precedents):
- Need OEY >= 4% (to get 4% + 9% growth = 13% vs 9.2% WACC)
- OEY 4% = Owner Earnings $1.76B / X market cap = $44B market cap
- $44B / 399.5M shares diluted = ~$110 per share

### Method 2 (Secondary, 40%): EV/EBIT Normalized

```
EBIT FY2025: $2.35B (operating income)
EBIT FY2026E: ~$2.6B (at 23.5% margin on $11.2B revenue)

Sector median EV/EBIT: ~20x for industrials
Quality premium for CTAS:
  + Superior ROIC (23.9% vs sector ~12%): +3x
  + Wide moat (route density, #1 share): +2x
  + Above-avg growth (9% vs 5%): +2x
  + Expanding margins: +1x
  = Base 20x + 8x = 28x

Fair Value EV = $2.6B * 28x = $72.8B
Net Debt: $3.0B
Equity Value: $69.8B
Shares: 399.5M
FV per share = $174.8

Conservative (25x): EV $65B → Equity $62B → FV $155
Aggressive (30x): EV $78B → Equity $75B → FV $188
```

### Reconciliation

| Method | Fair Value | Weight | Weighted |
|--------|-----------|--------|----------|
| OEY (implied FV at 4% spread) | $110 | 60% | $66 |
| EV/EBIT (28x normalized) | $175 | 40% | $70 |
| **Weighted Average** | | **100%** | **$136** |

**Note on FV rounding:** The OEY method gives a much lower FV because it strips out the premium the market pays for "quality compounding" beyond what the cash flows justify at today's yield. The EV/EBIT method gives a higher figure because it assigns a quality premium multiple. The weighted average of $136 represents a reasonable mid-point.

**Sensitivity assessment:**
- DCF tool: FV Spread 85%, TV 74.5% of EV -- **HIGH SENSITIVITY**. The DCF is unreliable as a point estimate.
- DCF scenarios: Bear $54, Base $70, Bull $92 -- all significantly below current price.
- Reverse DCF: Market implies 30% FCF growth for 5 years vs historical 10.6% -- a 19.3pp gap.
- The DCF scenarios are VERY conservative because the tool uses 5-year projection with standard terminal value, which undervalues long-duration compounders. However, even EV/EBIT at a generous 28x gives only $175.

**Fair Value Range: $110 - $175**
**Central Estimate: $136**

## Verdict: OVERVALUED / FANTASY

**Classification: FANTASY entry.** The stock needs to fall 37-40% to reach an actionable entry price. Without a catalyst for such a decline (recession, sector-specific shock, company-specific earnings miss), this is not a realistic near-term entry.

**Quality: Exceptional.** CTAS is one of the best businesses in the business services universe. Wide moat, expanding margins, 15% insider ownership, 97% retention, zero AI disruption risk, 51 years of dividend growth. QS 83 (adjusted) firmly Tier A.

**Price: Dramatically overvalued.** The market has fully priced in the quality premium and then some. Reverse DCF implies 30% FCF growth vs 10.6% historical. Even a generous 28x EV/EBIT gives FV of $175, still below the current $198. The stock is trading at a level that requires near-perfect execution AND multiple expansion to justify.

**Comparison with sector view:** The business-services sector view correctly flagged CTAS as "Quality but P/E 42x, way too expensive." This analysis confirms that assessment.

## 🔄 META-REFLECTION

### Incertidumbres/Dudas
- The DCF tool dramatically undervalues CTAS ($54-92 range) because it uses 5-year projections that don't capture the long-duration compounding of route economics. I compensated by using EV/EBIT as the higher-weight secondary method, but the "true" DCF FV for a compounder with 20+ year runway may be higher than the tool suggests.
- Receivables growth (13.9%) significantly exceeds revenue growth (7.7%) in FY2025. This could be benign (timing, mix shift to larger enterprise customers with longer payment terms) or could indicate early revenue quality deterioration. Worth monitoring in Q3 FY2026 results.
- The 15% insider ownership is high, but recent transactions are all stock gifts, no open-market purchases. At this valuation, insiders are clearly not adding exposure.
- Dividend yield shows as 91% in price_checker -- this appears to be a data error (likely 0.91% or similar). The actual dividend yield is approximately 0.9% based on annual dividend ~$1.56/share vs $198 price.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema

- CTAS should be added to quality_universe as SCORED with QS 83 adj, FV $136, entry $120, status FANTASY/OVERVALUED. It is worth tracking because any 30%+ correction in a Tier A compounder of this quality would be a significant opportunity.
- The DCF tool systematically undervalues long-duration compounders because the 5-year projection period captures only a fraction of the value. Consider adding a "compounder mode" with 10-year projections as an optional parameter.

### Anomalias Detectadas

- DCF scenarios produce Bear $54, Base $70, Bull $92 -- ALL below current price by 50%+. This is unusual and indicates either the tool is miscalibrated for this type of company (long-duration compounder) or the stock is genuinely 50%+ overvalued. I believe it is a combination: the stock IS overvalued (30-40% by my EV/EBIT estimate), but not by 50%+ as the DCF suggests.
