## Business Understanding

### What Intuit Does

Intuit solves the problem of financial complexity for consumers and small businesses. Its four platforms form an integrated ecosystem:

**1. Small Business & Self-Employed (QuickBooks) -- ~60% of revenue**
- QuickBooks Online: Cloud accounting, invoicing, payroll, time tracking
- QuickBooks Online Accounting grew 25% in Q1 FY26
- Online Ecosystem revenue (excl. Mailchimp) grew 25%
- Mid-market/Enterprise Suite (QBO Advanced) grew ~40%
- Mailchimp (email marketing) -- acquired for $12B in 2021, underperforming (revenue flat/down YoY)
- TAM: $41B US bookkeeping + $78B connected services + $30B international = $149B

**2. Consumer (TurboTax) -- ~25% of revenue**
- DIY tax filing: #1 with ~60% market share in DIY
- TurboTax Live (expert-assisted): growing 51% in Q1 FY26
- 600 Expert Office locations + 20 TurboTax Stores launching (physical expansion)
- TAM: $20B assisted market + $4B DIY = $24B
- Seasonal: ~85% of Consumer revenue in Q3 (Jan-Apr)

**3. Credit Karma -- ~10% of revenue**
- Personal finance platform: credit scores, monitoring, recommendations
- 45M monthly active users, 5x/month engagement
- Revenue model: referral fees from financial product recommendations
- Revenue grew 27% in Q4 FY25 to $651M annually
- TAM: ~$10B+ (financial product distribution)

**4. ProTax -- ~5% of revenue**
- Tax preparation software for professional accountants
- Stable, low-growth, high-retention

### Revenue Model

| Revenue Type | % of Total | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| Subscription (SaaS) | ~65% | QuickBooks Online, recurring, predictable |
| Transaction-based | ~15% | Payments processing, payroll |
| Consumer (seasonal) | ~15% | TurboTax, concentrated Q3 |
| Referral fees | ~5% | Credit Karma recommendations |

**Recurring revenue:** ~80%+ (QBO + ProTax + Credit Karma = highly recurring)
**Seasonal concentration:** Tax season (Jan-Apr) drives ~40-45% of annual revenue

### Unit Economics

- **QuickBooks ARPU growing:** Mix-shift to higher tiers (Advanced, Enterprise Suite) and connected services (payments, payroll) increasing wallet share per customer
- **TurboTax Live ARPU:** $100-200+ per return vs $0-80 for DIY -- massive ARPU uplift
- **Credit Karma LTV/CAC:** Exceptionally high -- free product, users self-serve, monetized via referral fees with near-zero marginal cost
- **Payback period:** QBO customer acquisition pays back in <12 months given low churn and growing ARPU
- **LTV/CAC ratio:** Estimated >5x across the platform (strong evidence: 80%+ GM, growing ARPU, low churn)

### Margin Structure

```
Gross Margin:     79.6% (FY25) -- vs 55% sector median -- +24.6pp premium
Operating Margin: 26.2% (FY25) -- expanding from 20.1% (FY22) -- +6.1pp in 3 years
FCF Margin:       32.3% (FY25) -- $6.1B FCF on $18.8B revenue
Net Margin:       17.2% (FY25)
```

**Trend:** Expanding. Operating margin expanded 6pp in 3 years despite heavy AI investment ($100M+ OpenAI deal, GenOS platform). This demonstrates operating leverage in the SaaS model.

### Capital Intensity: Asset-Light

- Capex ~3% of revenue
- Working capital is a SOURCE of cash (negative working capital -- customers pre-pay)
- Maintenance capex << depreciation (software business)
- R&D is the major cost center (~17% of revenue) but expensed, not capitalized

## Valuation

### Method Selection: Tier A = OEY + Reverse DCF

Since INTU is borderline Tier A (QS 75), I will use:
- **Primary (60%):** Owner Earnings Yield analysis
- **Secondary (40%):** Reverse DCF + DCF scenarios

### Method 1: Owner Earnings Yield (OEY)

```
FCF (TTM):                  $6.1B
Depreciation (estimate):    $1.0B
Maintenance Capex:          $1.1B (= Dep x 1.1)
Owner Earnings:             $6.1B - $1.1B + $1.0B = $6.0B

Market Cap:                 $111.3B
Owner Earnings Yield:       5.4%

Expected Growth:            11% (base case)
OEY + Growth:               16.4%
WACC:                       9.7%
Spread:                     +6.7pp (attractive)
```

**Interpretation:** OEY + Growth of 16.4% is significantly above WACC of 9.7%. This spread of +6.7pp suggests the stock is undervalued at current price. For context:
- ADBE at purchase: OEY + Growth = 16.5% -- similar profile
- NVO at purchase: OEY + Growth = 14.8%
- LULU at purchase: OEY + Growth = 14.4%

INTU at 16.4% is among the most attractive OEY + Growth profiles in our pipeline.

**OEY-implied Fair Value:**
If OEY + Growth should equal WACC + reasonable spread (~3-4pp for Tier A), then target OEY = WACC + 3% - Growth = 9.7% + 3% - 11% = 1.7%. At OEY 1.7%, FV = $6.0B / 1.7% = $353B market cap. This seems excessive. Using a more conservative anchor:

At 3.0% OEY (which gives OEY + Growth = 14%, a ~4pp spread over WACC), implied market cap = $6.0B / 3.0% = $200B = ~$718/share. This is too high.

At 4.0% OEY (OEY + Growth = 15%, ~5pp spread), market cap = $150B = ~$539/share.

At 5.0% OEY (OEY + Growth = 16%, ~6pp spread -- conservative), market cap = $120B = ~$431/share.

**OEY-based Fair Value range: $430-540 per share** (at OEY 4-5%)
