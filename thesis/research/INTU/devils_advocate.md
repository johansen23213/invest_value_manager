# Counter-Analysis: INTU (Intuit Inc.)

## Fecha: 2026-02-13
## Counter-Analysis Score: 14/19 (STRONG COUNTER)

> **CRITICAL FLAG FOR ORCHESTRATOR:** The thesis significantly understates two material risks: (1) SBC consumes ~39% of reported FCF, making the "5.4% OEY" headline metric misleading -- true OEY is ~3.7%, and (2) the per-seat pricing model across QuickBooks is structurally exposed to the SaaSpocalypse, which the thesis dismisses as "net positive for Intuit." These two factors combined reduce FV from $465 to ~$365-385 and push the appropriate entry to $270-310.

---

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The thesis presents Intuit as a Quality Compounder trading at a compelling discount. While the business quality is genuine -- QuickBooks' dominance and switching costs are real -- the thesis suffers from three systematic flaws: (1) it values the company on headline FCF that is inflated by $1.97B of SBC, producing a 5.4% OEY that is actually ~3.7% on an SBC-adjusted basis; (2) it dismisses AI disruption as "net positive" when Intuit is simultaneously paying $100M to the company most likely to disrupt it; and (3) it treats the 90/0 insider selling ratio as "mostly pre-planned" when the total absence of buying at -50% from highs is the most bearish signal insiders can legally send. The thesis survives, but the fair value should be $365-385 (not $465), the QS should be 70-72 (Tier B, not Tier A), and entry should be $270-310 (not $330-365).

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. "OEY 5.4% + Growth 11% = 16.4% -- very attractive for Tier A"

- **Evidencia en contra:** The thesis calculates OEY using headline FCF of $6.1B. But Intuit's SBC was $1.97B in FY2025 (source: MacroTrends, risk-identifier report). SBC is a real economic cost -- it dilutes shareholders and is functionally equivalent to paying employees in cash and then issuing shares to raise that cash. Adjusting FCF for SBC: $6.1B - $1.97B = $4.13B. Owner Earnings ex-SBC = $4.13B - $1.1B (maintenance capex) + $1.0B (depreciation addback) = $4.03B. True OEY = $4.03B / $111.3B market cap = **3.6%, not 5.4%**. True OEY + Growth = 3.6% + 11% = 14.6%, not 16.4%. This is still above WACC (9.7%) but the spread narrows from 6.7pp to 4.9pp -- significantly less attractive. Furthermore, when the thesis compares INTU favorably to ADBE (16.5%) and NVO (14.8%), it is making an apples-to-oranges comparison: ADBE's SBC is ~6% of revenue vs INTU's ~10%, meaning ADBE's FCF quality is materially better. The P/FCF ratio the market sees is 18x on headline FCF but 27x on SBC-adjusted FCF -- which is NOT cheap for a company with 40-45% seasonal revenue concentration.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee must recalculate the OEY-based FV using SBC-adjusted FCF. At 4.5% OEY target, FV = $4.03B / 0.045 / 278M shares = ~$322/share (not $480). Weight the OEY at 60% and adjust the blended FV downward by approximately 15-20%.

### 2. "AI is NET POSITIVE for Intuit, not a threat"

- **Evidencia en contra:** The thesis assigns only 20% probability that AI disruption materializes. This underestimates the speed of change. Key evidence:
  - **Altruist Hazel launch (Feb 10, 2026):** An AI platform that can read 1040s, pay stubs, and account statements, generating tax strategies in minutes. This triggered an 11% single-day drop in INTU. The thesis was written Feb 12 but does not mention this specific event.
  - **SaaSpocalypse ($285B wiped Feb 3):** The per-seat pricing model is under existential threat. Anthropic's Claude Cowork demonstrated autonomous task handling across business software -- if one AI agent replaces 10 seats, QuickBooks per-seat pricing collapses. The thesis does not address the per-seat exposure despite system MEMORY flagging this risk for all software positions (KC#7).
  - **Scale Venture Partners published research showing LLMs can "build TurboTax in a weekend"** -- this is referenced in the risk assessment but not in the thesis proper.
  - **The $100M OpenAI deal is DEFENSIVE, not offensive.** The risk-identifier correctly identified this: "Intuit is paying the disruptor to not disrupt them yet." The thesis frames it as "Intuit chose to PARTNER with AI" -- this is spin, not analysis.
  - **Wells Fargo downgrade (Jan 2026):** Cut from Overweight to Equal Weight specifically citing "elevated expectations" and AI disruption concerns. TD Cowen cut target to $658 citing "investor hesitation around AI impacts and terminal values."
  - **Probability reassessment:** AI disruption is not binary. The gradual erosion of per-seat pricing, commoditization of simple tax returns, and AI-native bookkeeping tools represent a continuum of degradation, not a single event. I estimate 35-45% probability of material impact within 3-5 years (vs thesis's 20%).
- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Increase bear case probability from 25% to 35%. Add explicit per-seat pricing erosion as Kill Condition #8. Increase WACC by 0.5pp to reflect heightened disruption risk. This alone reduces base FV by ~$30-40.

### 3. "Insider selling -- PARTIALLY pre-planned, PARTIALLY concerning"

- **Evidencia en contra:** The thesis admits 90 sales / 0 purchases over 6 months, $255M sold in 3 months, but then partially rationalizes it with "most sales under Rule 10b5-1 plans." Independent research confirms:
  - **Scott Cook sold $100M+ in the final days of 2025** (Dec 29-31). Two transactions: $50.5M on Dec 29 and $50.3M on Dec 30. He then sold another 1,402 shares on Dec 31. This is concentrated selling, not routine diversification.
  - **Cook's sales were at ~$668/share** (Dec 2025 prices) -- meaning he sold at prices 67% HIGHER than the current $398. He was selling aggressively at prices far above where the thesis recommends buying.
  - **Zero insider buying at -50%:** The thesis notes this is "notable" but does not assign appropriate weight. When a stock falls 50% and NOT A SINGLE insider buys, this is the most bearish signal management can legally send without breaking insider trading laws. If management believed the stock was undervalued at $400, the cheapest, most powerful bullish signal would be buying $50K of stock. They have not done so.
  - **CEO Sasan Goodarzi sold "only 3.6% of holdings"** per the thesis -- but 3.6% of his holdings is still millions of dollars in net selling at the top.
- **Severidad:** **HIGH**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee should treat the insider selling pattern as a -3 to -5 point QS adjustment under Capital Allocation. The thesis already applied -5 forward-looking but only partially attributed it to insider selling. This is a quantitative red flag that should increase the required MoS by 3-5pp.

### 4. "Mailchimp concern is valid but QUANTIFIED -- 6% of revenue, manageable"

- **Evidencia en contra:** The thesis quantifies Mailchimp at "~6% of revenue" and calls a potential impairment "manageable." Independent research reveals a more complex picture:
  - **Mailchimp was acquired for $12B in November 2021** at PEAK SaaS valuations. Revenue has declined in recent quarters. Seeking Alpha (Aug 2025): "Intuit Stock Keeps Overdelivering, But Mailchimp Doesn't."
  - **Excluding Mailchimp, GBS grew 20% and Online Ecosystem grew 25%.** Including Mailchimp, GBS grew only 16%. This is a 400bps drag, not the 150bps the thesis cites.
  - **Goodwill from acquisitions = $14B, ~76% of equity.** The risk assessment correctly identifies this but the thesis does not integrate it into the valuation. If Mailchimp requires even a partial impairment ($3-5B), it wipes out 1-2 quarters of GAAP earnings and signals capital misallocation.
  - **Email marketing is being commoditized by AI.** Competitors HubSpot and ConvertKit are eating into Mailchimp's base. The CFO acknowledges Mailchimp is "a near-term drag on growth."
  - **The thesis says "Mailchimp drags GBS growth by 150bps."** The actual figure from Q1 FY26 reporting shows a larger gap: GBS ex-Mailchimp grew 20% vs GBS total 16% = **400bps drag**, not 150bps. This is either a thesis error or selective citation.
- **Severidad:** **MODERATE**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Apply a $1-2B capital allocation penalty to the valuation (impairment risk discount). Increase the goodwill-related kill condition probability. If Mailchimp revenue declines >10% in FY26, trigger a thesis review.

### 5. "QS 75 Adjusted -- Tier A borderline"

- **Evidencia en contra:** The thesis adjusts QS from 77 (tool) to 75 (adjusted), a net -2 change. I believe the adjustment is insufficient:
  - **Market position +8 is generous.** While INTU is #1 in US DIY tax and US SMB accounting, the tool default of 0 reflects the tool's known limitation (market_position needs manual input). The +8 is reasonable for current share, but forward-looking, TurboTax's 60% share is under pressure from AI and QBO's 80% US share may face per-seat erosion. A forward-looking adjustment of +6 (not +8) is more appropriate.
  - **SBC distortion not reflected.** SBC at 39% of FCF means the "FCF Margin 32.3%" that scores 10/10 is inflated. FCF margin ex-SBC is approximately 21.9%, which scores 10/10 still but is materially different economically.
  - **ROIC trajectory distorted by acquisitions.** The tool shows 4 years: 10%, 12.2%, 15%, 17.2%. But this is RECOVERY from acquisition-inflated capital, not improvement. Pre-acquisition ROIC was 20%+. The current 17.2% is BELOW the pre-acquisition level. The ROIC persistence score should not improve as the company merely recovers from its own capital allocation decisions.
  - **Insider ownership 2.3% is LOW** for a founder-led company. Scott Cook is a director, not an employee. The CEO has been a net seller. At 2.3%, insiders have limited skin in the game.
  - **Forward QS reassessment:** +6 (market position, not +8) - 5 (ROIC forward/insider) - 2 (SBC quality) = net +(-1). QS Adjusted: 77 - 1 = **76, still borderline Tier A.** However, accounting for the per-seat pricing risk and AI disruption timeline, a further -4 to -6 points is warranted for forward-looking moat erosion, yielding QS 70-72 (Tier B).
- **Severidad:** **MODERATE**
- **Resolucion sugerida:** The committee should classify INTU as Tier B (QS 70-72, not 75) which changes the required MoS from 15-25% (Tier A precedent) to 20-30% (Tier B precedent). At Tier B, the entry calculation shifts materially downward.

## META-REFLECTION

### Dudas/Incertidumbres
- **SBC adjustment magnitude.** My correction treats 100% of SBC as a real economic cost. Some argue that buybacks offsetting dilution mean the net cost is lower. For INTU, buybacks mostly offset dilution (shares flat YoY) but don't CREATE per-share value -- they merely prevent destruction. I believe 100% deduction is appropriate but acknowledge the debate exists.
- **AI disruption timeline is inherently uncertain.** I assign 35-45% probability to material impact in 3-5 years, vs thesis's 20%. The truth could be anywhere. If AI disruption takes 7+ years, my adjustments are too harsh and the thesis FV is closer to correct.
- **Altruist Hazel's actual market impact.** The Feb 10 announcement caused an 11% drop in financial stocks, but Hazel is priced at $125/seat/month for advisors -- this is a B2B tool, not a consumer tax filing replacement. My concern is more about the NARRATIVE it creates than the direct competitive threat.
- **Mailchimp drag figure.** I found 400bps (GBS 16% vs ex-Mailchimp 20%) from Q1 FY26, but the thesis cites 150bps "management disclosed." I could not find the exact management quote for 150bps -- it may be from a different period or metric. This needs verification.

### Limitaciones de Este Analisis
- I could not access Intuit's full 10-K filing to verify exact SBC figures, goodwill breakdown by reporting unit, or segment-level FCF.
- I could not find specific QBO churn/retention data -- Intuit does not publicly disclose this. My inference of ">90% retention" follows the thesis and moat assessment, but it is an assumption.
- Per-seat pricing impact is theoretical at this point. No public data exists on how many QBO users are actually reducing seats due to AI automation. This risk is forward-looking and currently unquantifiable.
- The SaaSpocalypse is an ongoing event. Market conditions are changing daily. My analysis reflects data through Feb 13, 2026.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema
- **Create SBC-adjusted FCF as standard metric in quality_scorer.py.** For all software companies, headline FCF is misleading. This is the risk-identifier's suggestion #2 and I strongly endorse it.
- **Add per-seat pricing risk to the standard risk-identifier checklist** for all software/SaaS positions (ADBE, BYIT.L, INTU, PAYC, ROP).
- **Fix quality_scorer.py Net Debt detection.** Both the thesis and the risk assessment flagged that the tool shows "Net Cash" for INTU when reality is ~$2.4B net debt. This may be affecting other companies.
- **Develop a "correlated risk cascade" framework.** When multiple risks share a common trigger (AI advancement in this case), their probabilities should be modeled as correlated, not independent. Joint probability is higher than the product of independent probabilities.
