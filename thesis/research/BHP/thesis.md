**Modelo:** Diversified miner integrado - iron ore, copper, coal, potash (development).

- **Coal:** $5B (10%) - Queensland metallurgical coal, New South Wales energy coal

**Clientes:** China 50-60% iron ore exports, global copper/coal buyers.

**Competencia:** Rio Tinto (similar diversificado, 70% iron ore), Vale (iron ore puro Brasil), Glencore (trading + mining).

## Moat

**NARROW MOAT - Cost Leadership + Resource Quality**

**Ventajas sostenibles:**

1. **Cost leadership iron ore (similar RIO):**
   - WAIO costes bajos (no específico BHP vs RIO $22/ton, asumo comparable)
   - Integración vertical: mina → rail → puerto
   - Escala: 263MT/año = economies of scale

2. **Copper assets quality differentiation:**
   - **Escondida Chile:** #1 copper mine global (1MT/año), 50+ años reserves, grade 0.7% Cu (high-grade)
   - **Olympic Dam Australia:** Polymetallic (copper + uranium + gold + silver) = diversificación revenue stream
   - Long-life assets: Escondida/Olympic Dam 30-50 años mine life

3. **Diversificación commodity superior RIO:**
   - Copper 44% revenue = secular growth tailwind (EV, electrificación, AI data centers)
   - Iron ore 45% (vs RIO 70%) = menor China concentration risk
   - Balance: cyclical (iron ore down) offset por structural growth (copper up)

**Limitaciones moat:**

- **Commodities pricing power CERO:** Iron ore + copper = price-takers global markets
- **China concentration iron ore:** 50-60% iron ore exports China = similar RIO risk
- **Cyclicality:** Iron ore EBITDA $14B (FY25) vs $18B (FY24) = -22% por precio -18%
- **Copper growth límites:** Record production 2,017kt FY25, guidance 2026 flat-to-+5% = no hay ramp masivo short-term
- **Coal secular decline:** 10% revenue, long-term headwind transición energética

**Veredicto moat:** Narrow - Cost leadership iron ore + copper quality assets defendibles. Diversificación commodity mejor RIO PERO no elimina cyclical risk.
