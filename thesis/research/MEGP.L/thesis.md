## Business Understanding

### What ME Group Does

ME Group International (formerly Photo-Me International) operates the world's largest network of self-service vending kiosks across four product categories:

1. **Photo.ME (Photobooths)** -- 30,557 units, 53.8% of Group revenue
   - Biometric ID photos for passports, visas, driving licenses
   - "Fun" photos (photo strips, creative photos)
   - 30-60%+ market share by country across 18 countries
   - Revenue per booth: ~GBP 5,600/year
   - Manufacturing cost: ~GBP 6,500 per machine (1-year payback at ~30% cash ROI)

2. **Wash.ME (Revolution Laundry)** -- 6,956 units, 33.5% of vending revenue, 47.7% of EBITDA
   - Outdoor self-service commercial washing machines
   - Invented by ME Group in 2012, first mover in Europe
   - Placed at supermarket car parks, petrol stations, retail sites
   - Revenue per unit: ~GBP 13,000/year, EBITDA ~GBP 5,500/year
   - Installation cost: ~GBP 28,000 (declining), ~20% cash ROI year 1
   - 5-7 year contracts with high renewal rates
   - Growing +17.7% YoY (H1 2025), 1,145 net installations in FY2025

3. **Print.ME (Digital Printing Kiosks)** -- 4,471 units, 3.8% of revenue
   - Photo printing, canvas prints, personalised products
   - Stable, low-growth segment

4. **Other Vending** -- 6,579 units
   - Amusement kiosks, fresh juice machines, children's rides (primarily Japan)
   - Stable cash generator

**Total estate: 48,590 machines across 18+ countries.**

### Revenue Model

The model is predominantly **owned-machine vending**: ME Group manufactures, installs, and operates the machines, sharing revenue with site owners (supermarkets, transport hubs, government buildings). A smaller portion comes from equipment sales to third parties.

This is effectively a **distributed asset platform** with:
- Recurring revenue per machine (pay-per-use transactions)
- Site owner revenue shares (typically 15-30%)
- Manufacturing in-house (KIS factory, Grenoble -- founded by CEO Crasnianski in 1963)
- Capital deployed into new machines generating 20-30% cash ROI in year 1

### My Contra-Thesis

**1. "Selfie photos kill photobooths"** -- WRONG, regulation is going the OPPOSITE direction.

Germany mandated digital-only biometric passport photos from May 1, 2025, BANNING paper photos entirely. This is anti-spoofing legislation to combat identity fraud and morphing attacks. Self-uploaded photos had 15-25% rejection rates. The law REQUIRES either a certified photo studio or an approved self-service biometric kiosk (like Photo-Me/Fotofix). ME Group operates under the Fotofix brand in Germany and is one of the few certified providers.

**This is not a headwind -- it is a structural tailwind.** Other EU countries are expected to follow Germany's lead. The EU regulation on biometric ID documents (EU 2019/1157) mandates higher security standards, pushing countries toward controlled-environment photo capture. ME Group's installed base of 30,557 biometric-certified photobooths positions it as the primary beneficiary.

Evidence level: PRIMARY DATA (German law Personalausweisgesetz amendment, effective May 1, 2025).

**2. "Photobooths are legacy tech"** -- PARTIALLY TRUE, but misses the mix shift.

Photobooth unit count is stable (30,557 vs 30,708 YoY, -0.5%), not declining rapidly. Revenue per unit is stable. The segment generates GBP 82.7M revenue at 35.8% EBITDA margins. Meanwhile, laundry has grown from 18.3% to 33.5% of vending revenue in 5 years (2019-2025) and from 23.4% to 47.7% of EBITDA. The business is transforming from "photobooth company" to "self-service convenience platform." The market is pricing the old story, not the new one.

**3. "Small cap, no coverage"** -- THIS IS OUR EDGE.

Only 2 analysts cover MEGP.L. Both have BUY ratings with targets of 220p and 275p (mean 247p, +73% from current). Market cap GBP 538M is below institutional thresholds for many funds. This is exactly the type of neglected compounder where mispricing persists.

**4. "Revenue growth decelerating"** -- MISLEADING.

FY2024 revenue +3.4% includes (a) SEMPA divestiture headwind, (b) JPY depreciation impact, (c) photobooth printer disruption (2% impact). Constant currency growth was +5.9% ex-SEMPA. H1 2025 at +4.7% CC excluding SEMPA +5.9% CC. And FY2025 guidance of GBP 311-318M implies FY growth ~1-3.3% reported, but this includes continued FX headwinds and SEMPA base effect. The underlying laundry growth of +17.7% is the leading indicator, not the blended number.

## Competitive Position

### Photobooths

ME Group is the **global #1** in automated photobooths with 30,557 units across 18+ countries. Market share ranges from 30-60%+ depending on the country. The main competitive advantages are:

1. **Scale in manufacturing** -- In-house KIS factory in Grenoble produces machines at ~GBP 6,500 vs competitors who buy from third parties
2. **Site owner relationships** -- Decades-long partnerships with supermarkets, transport hubs, government buildings
3. **Data-driven redeployment** -- Telemetry data identifies underperforming locations for machine reallocation
4. **Regulatory certification** -- Certified biometric booths compliant with EU/national regulations; approval processes create barriers to entry
5. **Network effects** -- Consumers know the Photo-Me brand and seek out booths by name

**Threat assessment:** Smartphone passport photo apps are a real but overstated threat. The German anti-spoofing law (May 2025) explicitly BANS self-uploaded photos due to 15-25% rejection rates and morphing attack vulnerability. This is a structural regulatory tailwind that could expand across the EU. The risk is limited to "fun" photo usage, which is a small fraction of photobooth revenue.

### Laundry (Revolution)

ME Group is the **European #1** in outdoor self-service laundry kiosks and the inventor of the category (2012). With 6,956 Revolution units in operation (growing +16.8% YoY), ME Group has a near-monopoly in the outdoor/convenience laundry kiosk segment.

Key competitive advantages:
1. **First-mover advantage** -- Invented the outdoor laundry kiosk format; competitors are years behind
2. **Site owner relationships** -- Leverages existing photobooth site partnerships (supermarkets, petrol stations)
3. **Proprietary platform** -- Revolution brand with telemetry, app integration, and cashless payment
4. **Cost advantage** -- Installation cost declining from GBP 28,000 as manufacturing scales
5. **Renewal rates** -- Negligible churn on 5-7 year contracts

**Competition:** The traditional launderette market (coin-op stores) is the main alternative, not direct outdoor kiosk competitors. Revolution UK already outnumbers traditional launderettes (650+ units vs <600 launderettes). In Continental Europe, the penetration opportunity is massive (1 kiosk per 45,000+ people in most markets vs 1 per 11,400-19,900 in France/Ireland).
