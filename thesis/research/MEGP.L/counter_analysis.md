# Counter-Analysis: MEGP.L (ME Group International)

## Fecha: 2026-02-18

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The thesis survives scrutiny on business quality fundamentals (ROIC, unit economics, insider alignment) but contains **two material overstatements** and **one critical mischaracterization** that require correction: (1) the German anti-spoofing regulation is framed as a tailwind when primary evidence shows it is a **headwind** that bans traditional photobooth output, (2) the laundry "near-monopoly" narrative ignores Speed Queen's 1,500+ store European network, and (3) the FV of 200p relies on normalized FY2027E earnings two years forward, which is aggressive for a company in transition. After adjustment, my counter-FV is **165p**, representing a 17.5% correction from the thesis FV of 200p and still offering a meaningful 16.6% MoS at 141.6p -- which validates the opportunity but at tighter margins than the thesis claims.

**Verdict: MODERATE COUNTER**

---

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. "German anti-spoofing regulation is BULLISH for photobooths"

- **Thesis claim:** Germany mandated digital-only biometric photos from May 2025, which the thesis characterizes as "not a headwind -- it is a structural tailwind" and evidence for "anti-spoofing legislation" benefiting ME Group.

- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - PRIMARY DATA: The German Personalausweisgesetz amendment effective May 1, 2025 **bans paper photos from all sources including photobooths** for passport/ID applications. Citizens must use either (a) government self-service terminals inside Buergeramt offices provided by Bundesdruckerei GmbH (standardized EUR 6 fee), or (b) certified photographer studios transmitting digitally via cloud solution (Ringfoto, dm drugstore chain).
  - ME Group's existing street-level photobooths are NOT in the approved category. The regulation specifies "controlled environment" which means inside government offices or certified studios -- not supermarket lobbies.
  - ME Group's own year-end trading update (Nov 2025) confirms: "In Germany, regulatory changes requiring passport photos to be taken in the citizens' office or by certified photographers impacted revenue performance in H2." This is management acknowledging NEGATIVE impact, not positive.
  - ME Group stock fell 7% on the day this impact was flagged.
  - Photo.ME revenue was DOWN 4% in FY2025 (3% constant currency), with Germany specifically cited as a headwind.
  - The thesis claims ME Group operates under "Fotofix brand in Germany and is one of the few certified providers." However, the moat assessor flagged that the "Fotofix acquired by Bluesun" reference created uncertainty. My independent research confirms this is a FALSE ALARM -- the "Bluesun acquires Fotofix" refers to a Brazilian solar panel structure manufacturer called FotoFIX, completely unrelated to the German photobooth brand. ME Group Germany GmbH still operates Fotofix photobooths in Germany. However, this does NOT change the fact that their existing street-level booths are not compliant with the new digital-only mandate.

- **What the thesis gets wrong:** The thesis frames Germany as "anti-spoofing = controlled environment = photobooth tailwind." But "controlled environment" in the German law means GOVERNMENT offices and CERTIFIED studios, not self-service photobooths in train stations. The thesis confuses the general concept of "anti-fraud" with the specific implementation, which EXCLUDES traditional photobooths from the ID workflow.

- **What the thesis gets right:** ME Group IS deploying next-gen machines with QR code/digital upload capability (3,079 by FY2025 year-end). In France, they have the ANTS partnership for digital driving license photos. The question is whether they can convert their German installed base to government-certified digital terminals -- this is NOT guaranteed and is an execution risk, not a certainty.

- **Severidad: HIGH**
  - Germany is ~8-10% of photobooth revenue. Direct impact is manageable.
  - But the CONTAGION risk is the real threat: if France, UK, and other EU countries follow Germany's model, the entire photobooth-for-ID business is at risk.
  - The thesis presents this as a positive when evidence shows it is currently negative and the future is uncertain.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** Investment committee should reclassify Germany regulation from "bullish catalyst" to "execution risk with uncertain outcome." The bear case should include a scenario where 2-3 major markets adopt Germany-style rules without certifying ME Group's machines.

### 2. "Laundry near-monopoly in outdoor self-service"

- **Thesis claim:** ME Group has "near-monopoly in the outdoor/convenience laundry kiosk segment" with "no direct outdoor kiosk competitors."

- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - Speed Queen (Alliance Laundry Systems) has **1,500+ stores across 30 European countries**, growing at ~150 stores/year (1 new laundry every 2 working days). They reached 1,000 stores in 500+ cities in only 5 years.
  - Speed Queen is expanding aggressively in the same geographies ME Group targets: Italy, Spain, Portugal, Poland, France, Belgium, Ireland, Netherlands, Romania, Hungary, Croatia, Greece.
  - While Speed Queen operates primarily INDOOR franchise laundromats (not outdoor kiosks), the thesis's claim of "no competition" is misleading. Speed Queen serves the SAME end customer (people who need to do laundry conveniently) with a more premium, branded experience. The distinction between "indoor franchise" and "outdoor kiosk" is a format difference, not a market difference.
  - The thesis values ME Group's 6,956 laundry units against a TAM of "USD 5.2B European coin-operated laundry market." But ME Group's outdoor kiosk format is a SUBSET of this market, not the whole TAM. The addressable portion for outdoor kiosks specifically is much smaller.

- **What the thesis gets right:** ME Group's Revolution units occupy a specific niche (outdoor, supermarket car parks) that Speed Queen does not directly compete in. The 5-7 year site contracts with near-zero churn are genuine competitive advantages. The format difference (outdoor vs indoor) is real and meaningful for certain use cases.

- **Severidad: MODERATE**
  - The "near-monopoly" language overstates competitive position. ME Group has format leadership in outdoor kiosks but faces indirect competition from a much larger, well-capitalized player (Alliance Laundry Systems, private, $3B+ revenue).
  - However, the switching costs and site-level economics remain valid. The risk is not that Speed Queen steals existing sites, but that Speed Queen captures new sites that ME Group would have targeted.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** Reclassify competitive position from "near-monopoly" to "format leader with first-mover advantage in a contested market." Reduce laundry growth assumptions from 15-18% to 12-14% to account for competitive pressure in expansion markets.

### 3. "QS 80 (Tier A) is appropriate -- no adjustment warranted"

- **Thesis claim:** QS Tool score of 80 is accurate, no adjustment needed.

- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - **Market Position: 0/8 points** -- The tool gives 0/8 because it cannot auto-score market position. The thesis acknowledges this but argues "80 is appropriate anyway." But this means the QS is built on 80/92 available points, not 80/100. If we recalibrate: the effective score is 80/92 = 87% of available points. Whether you give 4-6 points for market position (justified: #1 in photobooths, leader in outdoor laundry) would push to 84-86 -- which doesn't change the tier.
  - **ROIC of 40% -- is it distorted?** The thesis reports ROIC-WACC spread of +32.3pp. This is extraordinary for an industrial company. However, ROIC can be inflated in asset-light or lease-heavy models. ME Group manufactures machines costing GBP 6,500-28,000 that operate on leased sites. The invested capital base may not fully capture the site relationships and operating infrastructure. That said, even GuruFocus independently calculates ROIC at 20-29% (varying by methodology), which still shows a massive WACC spread.
  - **FCF Margin only 10.6%** -- scores 5/10. This is genuinely depressed by growth capex, and the thesis explanation is valid. But the QS rewards TODAY's metrics, not projected ones. Scoring on projected FCF margin of 14-15% would be forward-looking, not current quality.
  - **Revenue CAGR 12.8% scores 8/10** -- This includes the post-COVID recovery bounce (FY2022 +21.2%). A more normalized 3-year CAGR (FY2023-FY2025) is approximately 5-7%, which would score 5/10 instead of 8/10, reducing QS by 3 points.
  - **EPS CAGR 35.4% scores 10/10** -- Same COVID recovery distortion. Normalized EPS growth is likely 8-12%, scoring 8/10, reducing QS by 2 points.

- **My adjusted QS estimate:**
  - Start: 80 (tool)
  - Market position add-back: +5 (clear #1 in both segments)
  - Revenue CAGR normalization: -3 (post-COVID distortion)
  - EPS CAGR normalization: -2 (post-COVID distortion)
  - Adjusted QS: **80** (coincidentally the same, but for different reasons)

- **Severidad: LOW**
  - The QS lands at approximately 80 even after adjustments because the overcounting on growth metrics roughly offsets the undercounting on market position.
  - Tier A classification appears justified, though at the low end of the range.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** Accept QS 80 but note it is at the floor of Tier A. If FY2025 results show continued photobooth decline accelerating or laundry growth slowing, a reassessment to 75 (Tier A floor) or even 72-74 (Tier B) is warranted.

### 4. "FV 200p is conservative" -- Valuation Sensitivity

- **Thesis claim:** FV 200p based on OEY (60%, 204p) + EV/EBIT (40%, 194p).

- **Evidencia en contra:**

  **OEY Method (204p):**
  - Uses FY2027E normalized FCF of GBP 50.2M -- this is 2 years forward, on projected 14% FCF margin (vs current 10.6%). This is aspirational, not normalized.
  - The discount back by 2 years at 8.5% only partially adjusts for the forward-looking nature.
  - Using CURRENT FCF of GBP 32.6M and the same OEY framework: Required mcap = GBP 32.6M / 5.5% = GBP 593M. FV = 593M / 378M shares = 157p.
  - The thesis method is valid IF the margin expansion materializes, but it is NOT "conservative."

  **EV/EBIT Method (194p):**
  - Uses 10.5x EV/EBIT multiple. The thesis justifies this as "at minimum 10x for a Tier A compounder with 40% ROIC."
  - Counter: This is a small-cap (GBP 538M) industrial company with an 83-year-old CEO, a core product in managed decline (photobooths), only 2 analysts, and a failed takeover process. UK small-cap mid-market M&A multiples average 5.3x EV/EBITDA (H1 2025 data). Converting to EV/EBIT basis, this implies approximately 7-8x EV/EBIT for typical small caps.
  - The thesis argues for a premium (10.5x) due to quality. Fair, but the premium should be moderate, not 40-50% above market.
  - Using 8.5x EV/EBIT (modest quality premium over market): Fair EV = 8.5x x GBP 78M = GBP 663M. Fair Equity = GBP 699M. FV = 185p.
  - Using 9x (moderate quality premium): FV = 195p.

  **DCF Cross-Check:**
  - The thesis's own DCF gives only 158p base case. This is more conservative than the OEY/EV/EBIT methods and arguably more honest about current cash flows.
  - DCF range: 115p-232p. The wide spread (74%) confirms high sensitivity to assumptions.

  **Failed takeover process:**
  - ME Group entered an "offer period" on June 18, 2025, exploring strategic options. Despite engagement with "several interested parties," the Board terminated discussions on December 5, 2025 because NO suitable offer was received.
  - This is highly significant: sophisticated buyers with access to private financial data chose NOT to acquire ME Group at prices around 150-180p. If the business were truly worth 200p+, private equity or trade buyers would have offered in that range. The failed sale process is market evidence that the valuation is NOT as compelling as the thesis suggests.

- **My Counter-FV Calculation:**

  Method 1: OEY on FY2026E (1 year forward, more conservative than FY2027E):
  - FY2026E FCF: ~GBP 40M (12% margin x GBP 335M -- 1 year out, partial margin expansion)
  - Required mcap = GBP 40M / 5.5% = GBP 727M
  - FV = 727M / 378M = 192p
  - Discount 1 year at 8.5%: 177p

  Method 2: EV/EBIT at 9x (moderate quality premium):
  - FV = (9x x GBP 78M + GBP 36M net cash) / 378M = GBP 738M / 378M = 155p

  Weighted (60/40): 177p x 0.6 + 155p x 0.4 = 106 + 62 = **168p**

  After rounding and cross-checking against DCF base (158p) and analyst low target (222p, which I discount as stale): **Counter-FV = 165p**

- **Severidad: HIGH**
  - FV 200p overstates by ~21% vs my 165p.
  - At 165p FV, MoS = 16.6% at current 141.6p -- still attractive for Tier A but NOT the "40% MoS" the thesis claims.
  - Bear case also needs adjustment: the thesis uses 7x for bear, but failed-sale-process evidence suggests 6.5-7x is more appropriate as there is no buyout floor.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** Committee should use FV range of 160-180p (center 170p) rather than 200p. MoS at 141.6p would be 13-27% (center 20%). This is adequate for Tier A but requires price discipline.

### 5. "Bear case 130p with only -8.7% downside"

- **Thesis claim:** Bear case FV 130p at 7x EV/EBIT. This implies "limited downside even if thesis fails badly."

- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - Bear case uses 7x EV/EBIT on FY2025E EBIT. But if photobooths decline accelerates AND laundry growth slows AND the CEO exits badly, revenue growth could turn negative and EBIT could compress.
  - A true bear case for a small-cap industrial in structural transition with key-man risk: 6x on compressed EBIT of GBP 65M (assuming photobooth decline -8% and laundry growth slows to +8%):
    - Fair EV = 6x x GBP 65M = GBP 390M
    - Fair Equity = GBP 426M (adding net cash)
    - Bear FV = 113p
    - Downside from 141.6p: **-20.2%**
  - The 52-week low is 130.2p, but the stock has tested this level recently, suggesting the market sees downside below 130p if results disappoint.

- **Severidad: MODERATE**
  - The thesis bear case is "base case in disguise" -- it assumes current EBIT holds even in the bear scenario.
  - A proper bear case should account for EBIT compression, not just multiple compression.

- **Resolucion sugerida:** Adjust bear case to 110-115p (accounting for EBIT compression). Downside risk is closer to 19-22%, not 8.7%.

## Conflictos con Otros Analisis

### Moat Assessment Conflicts

The moat assessor (independent) classified the moat as **NARROW** (16/25), while the thesis implies NARROW-to-WIDE (34/50, different scale). The key disagreement is on regulatory barriers:

- Moat assessor correctly identified the Germany regulation as having **MIXED** impact (can be positive or negative), while the thesis presents it as unambiguously positive.
- Moat assessor flagged a critical uncertainty: "I could not determine with certainty whether ME Group's existing Fotofix street-level booths qualify under the new 'controlled environment' law." My independent research CONFIRMS the moat assessor's caution -- street-level booths do NOT qualify.
- Moat assessor also noted the "Fotofix acquired by Bluesun" anomaly. I have resolved this: it was a **different company** entirely (Brazilian solar panel structure manufacturer), NOT the German photobooth brand. ME Group still operates Fotofix in Germany.

### Risk Assessment Conflicts

The risk-identifier rated Germany regulation as **CRITICAL** (Risk #1) and the overall risk score as **MEDIUM-HIGH**. The thesis, however, frames Germany regulation as a positive catalyst. This is a direct contradiction:

- Risk assessment: "traditional paper photos from photobooths are NO LONGER ACCEPTED...this effectively eliminates the traditional photobooth use case for ID photos in Germany"
- Thesis: "Germany mandated digital-only biometric passport photos from May 2025, BANNING paper photos entirely...This is not a headwind -- it is a structural tailwind"

The risk assessment's characterization is supported by primary data. The thesis's reframing is aspirational (assumes ME Group's next-gen machines will be certified) but not evidence-based.

### Succession: Factual Discrepancy

The risk assessment states "Vladimir Crasneanscki appointed Deputy CEO June 2025." However, my independent research shows Vladimir Crasneanscki was appointed as **Executive Director** in June 2025, not Deputy CEO. His current role is Managing Director, UK and Head of Investor Relations (appointed January 2024), elevated to the Board in June 2025. The "Deputy CEO" title was not confirmed in any primary source. This is a minor factual error but relevant for assessing succession readiness.

### Dudas/Incertidumbres

- I could not verify whether ME Group's next-gen digital photobooths are certified under the German digital-only regime. The company is deploying them (3,079 by FY2025 year-end), but certification status is unclear from public sources. This is the single most important datapoint for the photobooth segment and should be clarified from FY2025 annual results.
- The "FY2026 profit growth 3-5% vs 10% expectation" claim comes from a Stockopedia article (Nivel 2 source). I could not independently verify what the exact consensus expectation was prior to the November 2025 trading update. However, the stock dropping 7% on the day confirms the market viewed it negatively.
- Speed Queen's 1,500+ stores are primarily INDOOR franchise laundromats. The degree to which they compete directly with ME Group's OUTDOOR kiosks is debatable. They serve overlapping end customers but different use cases (planned visit vs. convenience/impulse). I may be overstating the competitive threat.
- I could not find detailed information on Vladimir Crasneanscki's background, age, or track record. The different surname spelling (Crasneanscki vs Crasnianski) suggests he may be a nephew or son-in-law rather than a direct son, which could have implications for family succession dynamics.

### Limitaciones de Este Analisis

- ME Group FY2025 annual results (Feb 27) will contain critical data I do not have: full-year balance sheet (receivables resolution), German market granular data, FY2026 explicit guidance, capital allocation plans, succession commentary. My analysis is based on H1 2025 interims and the November 2025 trading update.
- I could not access the ME Group H1 2025 interim results PDF directly (paywall/format issues). I relied on secondary summaries (Nivel 2 sources) for specific financial data points.
- The European outdoor self-service laundry market does not have reliable independent market size data. The "USD 5.2B coin-operated laundry" figure includes all formats (indoor, outdoor, commercial), making TAM claims imprecise.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema

- **Regulatory obsolescence risk should become a standard factor in risk-identifier.** The risk-identifier flagged this well for MEGP.L, but this should be a checklist item for any company whose revenue depends on government-mandated processes (ID photos, tax filings, regulatory compliance services). If a government changes the mandate, the revenue base can evaporate.
- **Failed M&A/sale processes should be flagged in thesis.** If a company enters a strategic review and fails to receive suitable offers, this is material negative evidence about private market value. The thesis should have mentioned this.
- **Forward normalization in OEY method needs guardrails.** Using FY2027E earnings in FY2026 creates 2 years of forward projection risk. Suggest a standard: OEY normalizes on T+1 maximum, with sensitivity shown for T+2.
