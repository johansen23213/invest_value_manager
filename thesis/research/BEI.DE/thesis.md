## Business Understanding

### Model de Negocio

Beiersdorf is a 142-year-old German consumer staples company focused on skincare and adhesive tapes. Two segments:

**Consumer (83% of sales, 80% of EBIT excl. special factors):**
- **NIVEA** (EUR 5.6B, 57% of group): World's largest skincare brand. Mass market. Deo, body care, lip care, face care. Organic growth +9.0% in 2024.
- **Derma / Eucerin+Aquaphor** (EUR 1.4B, 14%): "Dermocosmetics" — pharmacy-channel skincare. Became 2nd billion-euro brand in 2024. Growing +10.6% organic. Highest growth unit.
- **La Prairie** (EUR 0.5B, 5%): Ultra-luxury skincare (price points EUR 300-1,000+). China-dependent. Declining -6.2% in 2024 due to Greater China weakness. Stabilizing in Q3 2025 (+1.6%).
- **Health Care** (EUR 0.3B, 3%): Hansaplast/Elastoplast plasters. Stable +6.1%.

**tesa (17% of sales, 20% of EBIT excl. special factors):**
- EUR 1.7B in specialty adhesive tapes and systems for automotive, electronics, construction
- 7,000+ products, ~100 new adhesive solutions annually
- B2B with high switching costs (1,000+ solutions in automotive alone, 130+ tapes in an EV)
- Higher margin (16.3% EBIT margin) than Consumer (13.4%)
- Slow growth (1.9% organic in 2024)

**Revenue model:** Consumable/repeat purchase (skincare, tapes). High brand loyalty for NIVEA/Eucerin. Pharmacy channel for Derma creates professional endorsement moat.

### Unit Economics

- **Gross Margin: 58.5%** (2024) — expanding from 57.2% in 2021. +20.5pp vs sector median (38%). Strong pricing power signal.
- **Operating Margin: 13.6%** (2024, ex-special factors 13.9%). Below L'Oreal (~20%) but improving trajectory.
- **FCF Margin: 8.1%** (2024). This is the WEAKNESS. Improved from awful 2.8% in 2022, but still below quality compounder standards. Heavy investment cycle (capex EUR 437M in 2024, 1.6x depreciation).
- **R&D: 3.6% of revenue** (EUR 354M). Increasing — a POSITIVE sign for skincare innovation pipeline.
- **Capital intensity: Medium.** Capex/Revenue ~4.4%. Manufacturing + R&D facilities globally.

### Ownership & Governance

- **Maxingvest (Herz family): 53.2%** controlling stake. Michael and Wolfgang Herz.
- Prevented P&G hostile takeover in 2003 — family committed to independence.
- Steward ownership reduces short-term activism risk.
- Downside: minority shareholders have limited influence on capital allocation.
- EUR 500M annual share buyback program (started 2024, renewed 2025) — shareholder-friendly.

### Por Que Esta Barata

**Current price: EUR 106.50.** 52wH: EUR 137.70 (22.7% below). 52wL: EUR 87.02.

**Narrativa del mercado (why it's down 23% from high):**
1. **FY2025 growth deceleration:** Guidance cut from 4-6% organic to ~2.5%. Q3 2025 NIVEA went flat (+0.6% 9M organic). Skincare market decelerating, especially emerging markets.
2. **La Prairie drag:** -6.2% in FY2024, -7.2% in 9M 2025. Greater China + Hainan weakness in luxury skincare.
3. **Premium valuation history:** Stock traded at 30-35x P/E historically. Now at 27x — still not "cheap" by staples standards.
4. **Low FCF yield:** FCF margin 8.1% is below quality compounder standards. Market doesn't give premium for low cash conversion.

**Mi Contra-Tesis:**
1. **Growth deceleration is cyclical, not structural.** NIVEA gained market share even in Q3 2025 weakness. Derma unit growing +12.3% proves portfolio diversification works. September 2025 NIVEA bounced to +7.8% organic.
2. **La Prairie is stabilizing.** Q3 2025 returned to +1.6% growth after 2 years of decline. China market grew +3% in Q3. If China recovery materializes, this is upside optionality (EUR 500M revenue at potentially 30%+ EBIT margin).
3. **Margin expansion trajectory is intact.** From 11.8% EBIT in 2020 to 13.9% in 2024. Management guided +50bps for FY2025 Consumer segment (later trimmed to +20bps). Scale economics from Derma growth will continue.
4. **FCF is improving rapidly.** From EUR 249M (2022) to EUR 794M (2024) = 3.2x improvement. Capex cycle normalizing (1.6x D&A in 2024, down from 2.2x in 2022).

### Incertidumbres/Dudas
- **FCF margin projection uncertainty.** I project 9-10% base case FCF margin, but trailing 3yr average is only 5.1%. The FCF normalization rule was technically triggered (+490bps vs trailing). I relied on capex cycle normalization (1.6x D&A in 2024 matching 2021) as justification. This could be wrong if management re-accelerates capex.
- **La Prairie is hard to value independently.** At EUR 509M revenue declining 6-7%, it's unclear what EBIT margin this segment achieves. If luxury skincare is permanently impaired by China, this is a drag. If it recovers, it's significant upside.
- **QS adjustment of +5 for market position.** NIVEA is clearly #1 in skincare by brand. Beiersdorf as a company is #5 in the skincare market. I used +5 (position #3-5 in measurable TAM). A case could be made for +8 (NIVEA is #1), but I stayed conservative per serial acquirer market position precedent (max +3-5).
- **Receivables growing faster than revenue (12.1% vs 4.3%).** This could signal channel stuffing or aggressive revenue recognition. Worth investigating in R2 if stock reaches entry.
- **The WACC is problematic.** Beta 0.30 produces an unrealistically low Ke of 4.15%. I used 6.5% as a practical floor, but this is somewhat arbitrary. The DCF tool using 9.0% is too high. The "true" cost of capital is somewhere in between, making valuation range wider.

### Sugerencias para el Sistema
- **Consumer staples need a sector-specific WACC framework.** Low-beta defensive staples consistently produce WACC estimates below practical DCF thresholds. The tool default of 9% is too punitive for beta 0.3 companies. Consider allowing analyst override in the DCF tool.
- **Segment-level EBIT data would improve valuation.** For conglomerates like Beiersdorf (Consumer + tesa), a sum-of-parts approach would be more accurate but requires segment-level data that yfinance doesn't provide.
- **The consumer-staples.md sector view needs updating.** It does not include Beiersdorf/NIVEA as a major player. It should be added to "Para Analisis Profundo" or "Watchlist" now.
