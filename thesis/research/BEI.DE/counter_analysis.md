# Counter-Analysis: BEI.DE (Beiersdorf AG)

## Fecha: 2026-03-20

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The R1 thesis for Beiersdorf is structurally sound on quality but contains several material errors and stale assumptions that require correction before proceeding. The thesis was written at EUR 106.50 with a WATCHLIST verdict at EUR 85-90 entry. The stock has since dropped to EUR 83.66 following an 18% single-day collapse on March 3 upon FY2025 results and devastating 2026 guidance (flat-to-slightly-growing sales, margin decline). The thesis's quality assessment holds up reasonably well, but the growth trajectory, margin expansion narrative, and NIVEA competitive position are significantly weaker than the thesis assumed. The FY2025 actual results and 2026 guidance introduce new risks that the thesis did not anticipate. At EUR 83.66, the stock is now BELOW the R1 entry range -- but the fair value itself needs downward revision given the deteriorating fundamentals.

---

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. "Growth Deceleration is Cyclical, Not Structural" (THESIS CORE CLAIM)

- **Thesis assumed:** FY2025 organic growth of ~2.5% was a one-off transition year; normalized growth would return to 5% organic.
- **Actual FY2025 data:** Organic growth came in at 2.4% (in line with thesis), BUT the thesis expected this to be a trough. Instead:
  - NIVEA organic growth was only +0.9% -- the lowest in recent history
  - Management guided 2026 at "flat to slightly growing" organically -- WORSE than 2025
  - Q1 2026 specifically expected to be BELOW the full-year range (i.e., potentially negative organic growth)
  - Consensus expected ~3.7% organic growth for 2026; management guided effectively 0-1%
  - NIVEA lost market share every single month from February 2025 through January 2026 (20-120 bps monthly decline in Western Europe)
- **Evidence this is structural, not cyclical:**
  - The global skincare market growth has decelerated from 7% (2022-2024) to ~5% projected, with 54% of industry executives citing uncertain consumer appetite as greatest risk
  - NIVEA specifically is losing competitive position, not just slowing with the market
  - Management initiated a "recalibration" of NIVEA strategy that extends through 2027 -- this is not a one-quarter fix
  - The mass-market skincare segment faces structural headwinds from premiumization (consumers trading UP to dermocosmetics, not buying mass NIVEA facial care)
- **Severity:** **HIGH**
- **Resolution sugerida:** The base case growth assumption must be revised down from 5% organic to 2-3% organic for FY2026-2027, with recovery to 4-5% only from FY2028+. This directly impacts fair value by EUR 10-15.

### 2. "La Prairie is Stabilizing -- Q3 2025 Returned to +1.6% Growth"

- **Thesis assumed:** La Prairie was inflecting positive based on Q3 2025 data (+1.6% organic). FY2025 full year -4.5% was presented as the bottom.
- **Actual data post-thesis:**
  - FY2025 La Prairie: -4.5% organic decline (EUR 478M revenue, down from EUR 509M)
  - Q4 2025 La Prairie: +3.8% organic -- the thesis was directionally correct on Q4 inflection
  - BUT Q1 2026: La Prairie organic sales FELL -17.5% (massive decline on high comparison base)
  - Travel retail market declined by "double digits" in 2025
  - Hainan Island duty-free inventory had to be significantly reduced
- **Why this matters more than thesis suggests:**
  - La Prairie at EUR 478M represents only 4.8% of group revenue but was assigned upside optionality worth "+5-10% to FV"
  - A -17.5% Q1 2026 decline suggests the Q4 2025 "inflection" was a false positive or seasonal
  - China luxury skincare structural decline is deeper than the thesis assumed
  - Management is reducing Hainan inventory -- this suggests the channel was artificially loaded
  - La Prairie's EBIT contribution is unclear (thesis noted this uncertainty) but the segment is now a more material drag
- **Severity:** **MODERATE**
- **Resolution sugerida:** Remove La Prairie recovery as a bull case catalyst. The 25% probability Bull scenario (EUR 125 FV) assumed "La Prairie recovers fully, China boom" -- this is now extremely unlikely for 2026-2027 timeframe. Reprice Bull to EUR 115 or lower.

### 3. "EBIT Margin Expansion Trajectory is Intact" (13.9% --> 15.5% by FY2028)

- **Thesis assumed:** EBIT margin expanding from 13.9% (FY2024) toward 14.0% in FY2025, 14.5% in FY2026, and 15.5% by FY2028.
- **Actual FY2025 data:**
  - Group EBIT margin (ex-SF): 14.0% -- in line with thesis for FY2025
  - Consumer EBIT margin: 13.6% (up from 13.4% in FY2024 -- modest improvement)
  - tesa EBIT margin: 16.1% (down from 16.3% -- slight deterioration)
- **2026 guidance DESTROYS the trajectory:**
  - Management guided EBIT margin "slightly below" FY2025 levels for 2026
  - Raw material cost increases and unfavorable FX cited as margin headwinds
  - Oil at $98 WTI (Hormuz crisis) directly impacts Beiersdorf through plastic packaging, chemical inputs, and transportation costs
  - RBC Capital Markets specifically cited "margin squeezes from pricing dynamics and input costs"
  - The consensus had expected continued margin expansion; instead, margins will contract
- **Why this is critical:**
  - The EV/EBIT valuation method (60% weight) used 14.5x on EUR 1,385M normalized EBIT
  - If FY2026 EBIT margin declines to ~13.5% (slightly below 14.0%) on flat revenue (~EUR 9.9B), EBIT would be ~EUR 1,337M -- 3.5% below the R1 normalized EBIT
  - The margin expansion story was central to the re-rating thesis
  - With Hormuz crisis pushing oil to ~$100, input cost headwinds are MORE severe than when management issued 2026 guidance
- **Severity:** **HIGH**
- **Resolution sugerida:** Revise base case EBIT margin trajectory: FY2026 13.5-13.8%, FY2027 14.0%, FY2028 14.5%. The 15.5% target is at least 1-2 years further out than thesis assumed.

### 4. "QS Adjustment from 70 to 75 (+5 for Market Position) is Justified"

- **Thesis rationale:** Tool scored 0/8 for Market Position (manual/default). NIVEA is #1 global skincare brand. Beiersdorf is #5 globally in skincare. Adjustment of +5 for position #3-5 in measurable TAM.
- **Counter-evidence:**
  - The +5 adjustment moves Beiersdorf from Tier B (QS 70) to Tier A (QS 75). This is a TIER-CHANGING adjustment and deserves scrutiny.
  - NIVEA is losing market share every month for 12+ consecutive months. A brand actively losing competitive position should not receive maximum market position credit.
  - The market position score is meant to reflect defensibility and competitive strength. NIVEA's position is eroding, not strengthening.
  - The thesis compares to ACGL (+8 for insurer structural bias) but ACGL was gaining share, not losing it.
  - FCF margin at 8.1% (and likely to remain below 10% through 2027) is a genuine Tier B characteristic. Consumer Staples Tier A compounders (P&G, Colgate) have FCF margins of 15-20%.
  - At QS 70 (Tier B), the required MoS would be ~20-25% (per precedents), meaning entry price should be EUR 75-80, not EUR 85-90.
- **Counter-argument in favor of +5:**
  - NIVEA IS the #1 skincare brand globally by sales -- this is indisputable
  - Market share loss appears cyclical (strategy recalibration) not structural (brand impairment)
  - The tool gave 0/8 for a company that is clearly in the top 5 globally -- the adjustment corrects a tool limitation
- **Severity:** **MODERATE**
- **Resolution sugerida:** The +5 adjustment is defensible as a correction of tool error, but the committee should evaluate whether NIVEA's ongoing market share loss weakens the case for Tier A. If QS remains at 70 (Tier B), the entry price drops significantly. A compromise: accept QS 73-74 (Tier B, near boundary) and apply 15-20% MoS rather than 10-15%.

### 5. "FCF is Improving Rapidly -- FCF Margin Could Reach 9-10%"

- **Thesis assumed:** FCF margin improving from 8.1% (FY2024) to 8.5% (FY2025E), 9.0% (FY2026E), 10.0% (FY2028E). Capex cycle normalizing from C.A.R.E.+ investments.
- **Actual FY2025 data:**
  - Gross cash flow: EUR 1,075M (down from EUR 1,269M in FY2024 -- a -15.3% decline)
  - Capex: EUR 463M (UP from EUR 437M in FY2024 -- capex NOT normalizing)
  - R&D: EUR 365M (up from EUR 354M -- also increasing)
  - FCF calculation: EUR 1,075M - EUR 463M = ~EUR 612M implied operating cash flow less capex (rough estimate)
  - If FCF margin = FCF / Revenue, and revenue is EUR 9.9B, FCF margin appears to be ~6-7%, LOWER than the 8.1% in FY2024
- **Why this is concerning:**
  - The thesis projected improving FCF from EUR 794M to higher levels. Instead, gross cash flow declined 15%
  - Capex INCREASED, contradicting the "normalizing" thesis
  - FCF margin is the WEAKEST component of the Quality Score (scored only 2/10 points)
  - At 6-7% FCF margin, the Owner Earnings Yield calculation changes materially
  - Management's 2026 margin decline guidance suggests FCF margin will also contract
- **Severity:** **HIGH**
- **Resolution sugerida:** Revise FCF margin assumptions down. FY2025 actual FCF margin appears to be 6-7%, not the projected 8.5%. Base case FCF margin of 8-9% may not arrive until FY2028+. This significantly reduces the OEY-based fair value.

**Assessment on tesa "hidden gem":** The thesis characterizes tesa as a hidden gem with 16.3% EBIT margin and industrial switching costs. However, tesa grew only 1.8% organically in FY2025, with European sales declining -1.5%. The EBIT margin actually contracted slightly (16.1% vs 16.3%). Competitors include Henkel, Sika, Nitto, and 3M. While tesa has ~15% share in European automotive adhesives and genuine switching costs in industrial applications, calling it a "hidden gem" overstates the case. It is a solid, stable industrial business -- but at 1-2% growth, it does not add material upside optionality. It does provide portfolio stability and margin support, which is its real value. The thesis fairly acknowledges the low growth but oversells the hidden value narrative.

### Interpretacion:

**MODERATE COUNTER.** The thesis identifies a genuinely high-quality business (net cash, family ownership, strong brands, ROIC>WACC persistence) -- this quality core is CONFIRMED. However, the thesis's forward-looking assumptions are now materially stale:

1. **Growth is worse than assumed.** 2026 guidance is flat-to-slightly-positive (thesis assumed 5% normalized). This is a material deterioration.
2. **Margins are reversing.** 2026 EBIT margin guided BELOW FY2025. Hormuz oil shock adds further pressure the thesis did not consider.
3. **Fair value needs revision.** EUR 95-100 is too high given new data. Fair value is likely EUR 80-90 range, making the current price of EUR 83.66 closer to fair value than to a bargain.
4. **NIVEA competitive position is deteriorating.** 12+ months of market share loss is not a blip. This potentially triggers KC#1.
5. **The QS 70->75 Tier A uplift is borderline.** Given NIVEA's competitive struggles and sub-par FCF margin, QS 72-73 (Tier B) may be more appropriate.

**Why not STRONG COUNTER:** The business fundamentals remain solid in absolute terms -- net cash, family ownership preventing value-destructive M&A, Derma growing 11.7%, no balance sheet risk. The risks are primarily about PACE of returns, not permanent capital loss. Insider buying (EUR 20M by Herz family post-crash) suggests the controlling shareholder sees value. The EUR 750M buyback provides share count reduction support.
