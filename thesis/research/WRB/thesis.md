## Business Understanding

### Company Overview

W.R. Berkley Corporation is a US-based insurance holding company founded in 1967, headquartered in Greenwich, CT. It operates through 50+ decentralized operating units (the "Berkley model") across two segments:

1. **Insurance (87% of NPW):** Commercial lines, specialty, excess & surplus (E&S), workers' compensation, professional liability, cyber, marine, aviation. US focus with selective international.
2. **Reinsurance & Monoline Excess (13% of NPW):** Property catastrophe, casualty, specialty reinsurance. Smaller but higher-margin segment.

### Business Model

**Revenue generation:** Net premiums earned ($12.7B FY2025) + net investment income ($1.43B, record) + investment gains.

**The Berkley Model -- Decentralized Specialty Underwriting:**
- 50+ autonomous operating units, each with its own CEO, underwriting authority, and P&L
- Each unit is a specialist in a narrow niche (cyber, marine, professional liability, environmental, etc.)
- Central oversight provides capital allocation, reinsurance, investment management, actuarial support
- Units that underperform are restructured or shut down -- Darwinian discipline
- This model enables rapid entry into hardening niches and rapid exit from softening ones

**Why it works:**
- Specialists underwrite better than generalists (combined ratio 90.7% vs industry 98-99%)
- Decentralization attracts entrepreneurial talent (each unit head runs their "own" company)
- Rapid capital redeployment across 50+ units based on where margins are best
- Low expense ratio (28.3%) enabled by shared services infrastructure

## Valuation

### Method 1: P/B vs ROE (Primary -- 60% weight)

For insurers, P/B vs ROE is the most appropriate valuation method.

**Justified P/B = (ROE - g) / (Ke - g)**

Where:
- ROE = 21.2% (FY2025, sustainable given 5yr range 16-23%, use 18% normalized)
- g = terminal growth = 3% (premium growth + investment income growth)
- Ke = cost of equity = 6.5% (Rf 4.3% + Beta 0.36 x ERP 6% = 6.46%, round to 6.5%)

**Note on beta:** WRB's beta is 0.36 -- very low. This is consistent with specialty insurers (less correlated with market). However, using raw beta of 0.36 may understate risk. I will use two scenarios:

**Scenario A (raw beta 0.36):**
- Ke = 6.5%
- Justified P/B = (18% - 3%) / (6.5% - 3%) = 15% / 3.5% = **4.29x**
- Implied price = 4.29 x $25.72 BVPS = $110.34

**Scenario B (adjusted beta 0.65, more conservative):**
- Ke = 8.2% (4.3% + 0.65 x 6%)
- Justified P/B = (18% - 3%) / (8.2% - 3%) = 15% / 5.2% = **2.88x**
- Implied price = 2.88 x $25.72 = $74.07

**Scenario C (bear: ROE normalizes to 15%, beta 0.65):**
- Justified P/B = (15% - 3%) / (8.2% - 3%) = 12% / 5.2% = **2.31x**
- Implied price = 2.31 x $25.72 = $59.41

**P/B Method Summary:**

| Scenario | ROE Assumed | Beta | Ke | Justified P/B | Fair Value |
|----------|-----------|------|-----|---------------|------------|
| Bull (raw beta) | 18% | 0.36 | 6.5% | 4.29x | $110 |
| Base (adj beta) | 18% | 0.65 | 8.2% | 2.88x | $74 |
| Bear (adj beta, lower ROE) | 15% | 0.65 | 8.2% | 2.31x | $59 |

**Using adjusted beta as primary (more prudent):** P/B method FV = **$74**
