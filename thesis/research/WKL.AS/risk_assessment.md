## Top 3 Riesgos Criticos

### 1. AI Disruption is BROADER Than Thesis Estimates -- Claude Plugin is Just the Start

- **Categoria:** Fundamental / Valoracion
- **Descripcion:** The thesis correctly identifies that only 10-13% of EBIT (Legal & Regulatory) is directly vulnerable to LLM-based research disruption. But this framing may be too narrow. The Claude legal plugin does NOT just do legal research -- it automates contract review, NDA triage, compliance workflows, legal briefings, and templated responses. These are WORKFLOW tasks, not just information lookup. The thesis draws a clean line between "information" (vulnerable) and "workflow" (not vulnerable), but AI is increasingly crossing that line. Furthermore, H&R Block -- which our portfolio SOLD precisely because Intuit's 600 offices + $100M OpenAI investment breached its moat -- serves as a direct cautionary tale. WKL's tax business (CCH Axcess) faces competition from Thomson Reuters + Intuit ProConnect + OpenAI integration. The "AI battle" in CPA firms is already active (CPA Trendlines "Bot Wars" report, Nov 2025).
- **Evidencia:**
  - Claude Cowork legal plugin launched Feb 2, 2026 with contract review, NDA triage, compliance workflows -- these are WORKFLOW tasks the thesis claims are protected
  - H&R Block sold from our portfolio for AI moat breach -- WKL faces similar forces in tax
  - CCH Axcess user satisfaction 3.8/5.0 (below 4.2 average), only 65% would recommend (2025 tax software survey)
  - Microsoft Copilot already integrates with Thomson Reuters Westlaw/Practical Law
  - Thomson Reuters CoCounsel can answer complex tax questions in conversational language
  - Google DeepMind active in healthcare AI, though no direct evidence of UpToDate displacement yet
- **Probabilidad:** Media (40%) -- AI capabilities expanding faster than expected, but embedded workflow + regulatory barriers remain real
- **Impacto si materializa:** HIGH -- If AI disrupts 20-30% of revenue (not just 8-10% thesis estimate), FV drops to EUR 50-55 range (~20-25% downside from current). The thesis would need to reclassify from "bear = ~0% downside" to "bear = -15-25% downside"
- **Mitigante:** WKL is actively integrating AI (CCH Axcess Expert AI, UpToDate Expert AI). 84% recurring revenue with extreme switching costs provides time. Products are <1% of customer cost base. The question is speed of disruption vs speed of adaptation.
- **Kill condition?:** YES -- if WKL cloud software growth decelerates below 5% (indicating AI alternatives gaining traction in the workflow layer). Already identified in thesis as KC #2.

### 2. CEO Transition During Maximum Uncertainty + North America Growth Deceleration

- **Categoria:** Fundamental / Management
- **Descripcion:** Nancy McKinstry is retiring in February 2026 -- right NOW, at the worst possible moment for a leadership change. She grew market cap 10x over 20+ years and navigated every prior disruption. Her successor Stacey Caywood is an internal veteran (30 years) but has NEVER been group CEO. She inherits a company facing the biggest existential narrative (AI disruption) in its history, during a 63% stock decline, with a new Chief Strategy & Innovation Officer (Tejas Shah, appointed Jan 2026), all while the company needs to prove its AI strategy works. Meanwhile, North America organic growth decelerated from 6% (9M 2024) to 5% (9M 2025). This is concerning because NA is 63% of revenue. The thesis assumes 6% organic growth continues, but Q3 NA data suggests potential softness.
- **Evidencia:**
  - McKinstry retirement confirmed Feb 2026; Caywood appointed
  - Tejas Shah (former McKinsey) appointed EVP Chief Strategy & Innovation Officer Jan 2026 -- suggests management sees strategic urgency
  - 9M 2025 North America organic growth 5% vs 6% in 9M 2024
  - Health division decelerated (5% from 6%)
  - FCC decelerated (4% from 5% pro forma)
  - CPE decelerated (8% from 9% pro forma)
  - Only Legal & Regulatory accelerated (6% from 5%) -- but this is the division MOST at risk from AI
- **Probabilidad:** Media (35%) for execution stumble; Alta (70%) that transition causes strategic uncertainty for 12-18 months
- **Impacto si materializa:** MEDIUM-HIGH -- Poor strategic decisions during AI disruption could be irreversible. If growth decelerates to 4% under new CEO, thesis bear case becomes base case (FV EUR 68.88). If deceleration continues to 3%, FV drops below current price.
- **Mitigante:** Caywood is deeply experienced (led Health and Legal & Regulatory divisions). Internal promotion reduces disruption risk vs external hire. McKinstry involved in 2025-2027 strategic plan. CFO Kevin Entricken provides continuity (he bought $300K in stock Aug 2025).
- **Kill condition?:** Suggested -- If organic growth falls below 4% for 2 consecutive quarters under new CEO, reassess thesis entirely.

### 3. Thesis Bear Case Is NOT Conservative Enough -- Actual Downside Worse Than EUR 68.88

- **Categoria:** Valoracion
- **Descripcion:** The thesis bear case assumes 4% growth, 9.5% WACC, 2.0% terminal growth, yielding FV EUR 68.88 -- approximately current price (EUR 65.72). The thesis claims "downside from here in a reasonable bear scenario is approximately zero." This is DANGEROUSLY comforting. Here is why the actual bear case could be worse:
  1. **4% growth is not harsh enough.** If AI disrupts 20-30% of revenue over 5 years (vs thesis 8-10%), growth could go to 2-3%. Print is already declining 11%, non-recurring down 2%, licenses down 9%.
  2. **9.5% WACC may be too low for a disruption scenario.** In a genuine disruption, beta would increase from 0.85 to 1.0-1.2, pushing WACC to 10-11%.
  3. **The FRR divestiture removes EUR 123M of revenue** (completed Dec 2025) -- this is NOT in the thesis at all. The 2025 full-year results (Feb 25) will show a smaller revenue base.
  4. **Net debt is EUR 4.4B (2.2x), not the EUR 4.3B (2.2x) in the thesis.** EUR 1B in share buybacks at much higher prices (avg ~EUR 120-140) destroyed value in hindsight.
  5. **Stock was at EUR 82.38 on Feb 5, now EUR 65.72** -- continued decline post-thesis suggests the market knows something. The thesis was written at EUR 67.70 and the stock has fallen further.
  6. **FY2025 full-year results on Feb 25 are binary.** If they show H2 deceleration (which the divisional trends suggest), the stock could drop another 10-15%.
- **Evidencia:**
  - Thesis bear FV EUR 68.88 vs current EUR 65.72 -- stock already BELOW thesis bear
  - Non-recurring revenue declining across multiple categories
  - FRR divestiture (EUR 123M revenue) completed Dec 2025 not in thesis
  - Net debt increased from EUR 3.1B to EUR 4.4B in 9 months due to buybacks
  - Morgan Stanley downgrade: organic growth estimates cut to 5.7% (2025) and 6.1% (2026) from 6.8% and 6.6%
  - Price_checker shows stock at EUR 65.72, below thesis entry of EUR 67.70 and below thesis bear FV of EUR 68.88
- **Probabilidad:** Media (30-40%) that actual bear materializes
- **Impacto si materializa:** HIGH -- Realistic extreme bear (2% growth, 10.5% WACC, 1.5% terminal) = FV approximately EUR 45-50, implying 25-30% further downside from current.
- **Mitigante:** 9M adjusted operating profit up 15% and FCF up 17% in constant currencies shows the business is still very healthy operationally. P/E at 13.8x on current earnings provides value floor if earnings hold.
- **Kill condition?:** YES -- The thesis should add: "If stock remains below bear case FV (EUR 68.88) for 4+ weeks without positive fundamental catalyst, reassess whether market is pricing in something I cannot see."

## Additional Context: HRB Analogy Assessment

The thesis draws an implicit comparison with HRB (H&R Block), noting both face AI disruption in tax-adjacent services. This comparison is DANGEROUS for the following reasons:

**Similarities (valid):**
- Both serve tax professionals
- Both face AI competition from Intuit + OpenAI partnership
- Both have legacy software with switching costs

**Critical differences (thesis is correct here):**
- WKL serves PROFESSIONAL CPA firms (B2B embedded workflow); HRB serves CONSUMERS (B2C commoditized)
- WKL products are <1% of customer cost; HRB is the entire service
- WKL has regulatory certification barriers (e-filing); HRB's consumer tax prep has low barriers
- WKL retention >95%; HRB faces walk-in foot traffic decline

**But the thesis UNDERESTIMATES this:** Our portfolio sold HRB because Intuit 600 offices + $100M OpenAI investment breached the moat. The SAME Intuit (via ProConnect) competes directly with WKL's CCH Axcess for CPA firms. If Intuit's AI integration makes ProConnect significantly better, the switching cost barrier could eventually be overcome -- especially as younger CPAs who grew up with AI enter the workforce. The "Bot Wars" report (CPA Trendlines, Nov 2025) confirms this competitive dynamic is ACTIVE.
