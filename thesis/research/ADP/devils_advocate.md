# Counter-Analysis: ADP (Automatic Data Processing, Inc.)

## Fecha: 2026-02-18

## Resumen Ejecutivo

The thesis for ADP presents a Quality Compounder narrative: WIDE moat, 50% ROIC, 92% retention, with a temporary SaaSpocalypse-driven discount. **My adversarial analysis finds the thesis is MODERATELY vulnerable.** The Fair Value of $255 is inflated by approximately 20-25% due to: (1) over-reliance on OEY/multiple-based methods that assume mean-reversion to historical multiples while ignoring structural reasons for de-rating, (2) systematic under-weighting of the "Triple Squeeze" (flat PPC + rate cuts + AI headcount reduction) as correlated rather than independent risks, and (3) dismissal of insider selling patterns and rising short interest as "routine." The DCF tool independently confirms a FV range of $186-$231, not $255. **My adjusted FV is $195-210, with a recommended entry at $170.**

**CRITICAL FINDING:** CEO Maria Black has sold $9.5M in shares over the past 12 months with ZERO purchases. Combined with rising short interest (+28% MoM) and Jefferies downgrade to Underperform, the "smart money" signal is bearish while the thesis treats it as neutral.

## Asunciones Clave Desafiadas

### 1. "ADP is a Tier A Quality Compounder (QS 78)"

- **Thesis claim:** QS 70 tool, adjusted to 78 (+8 for market position) = Tier A.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - The +8 adjustment is reasonable (ADP IS #1), but the thesis ALSO acknowledges: (a) GM Premium 0/10 because ADP's 46% GM is 9pp below the sector median, (b) insider ownership 0/5 at 0.2%, (c) the moat-assessor notes the 50.7% ROIC is mechanically inflated by negative working capital from client float -- "the absolute ROIC level overstates the 'true' economic return vs peers with different capital structures."
  - Revenue growth is decelerating: 9.2% -> 6.6% -> 7.1%. The 7.1% bounce is partly float income recovery, not operational acceleration.
  - Paychex has 72% gross margin, Paycom has 82%. ADP's 46% is the worst among HCM peers. Even accounting for PEO dilution, this is structural -- it means a larger portion of ADP's revenue base is low-quality.
  - ROIC declined from 63.3% (FY2024) to 50.7% (FY2025) -- a 12.6pp drop in a single year. The thesis notes this but does not explain it. A 20% decline in ROIC is not consistent with "quality compounding."
  - The QS 78 maps to the LOWEST end of Tier A. Any further deterioration in growth or ROIC pushes it back to Tier B.
- **Severidad:** MODERATE
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Accept QS 78 as borderline Tier A, but apply Tier B MoS requirements (20-25%) rather than Tier A minimums (15%). This is a Tier A company with Tier B risks.

### 2. "FV $255 is supported by OEY, EV/EBIT, and DCF"

- **Thesis claim:** Weighted FV = $258, rounded to $255. OEY gives $268, EV/EBIT gives $263, DCF gives $225.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - **DCF tool independently says $186 (base), $199 (with thesis 6.5% growth), $231 (with 10% growth).** The thesis needs 10%+ FCF growth to justify $255, but the fundamental-analyst's own revenue growth assumption is only 6.5%. Where does the extra 3-4pp come from?
  - **OEY method circular reasoning:** The thesis uses a "target OEY of 3.8-4.2%" derived from where ADBE, LULU, and NVO trade. But those companies have HIGHER growth rates (ADBE 10%, LULU 8%, NVO 8-15%) and are NOT facing the Triple Squeeze. ADP's 6.5% growth rate warrants a HIGHER target OEY (lower FV), not comparable to faster-growing compounders.
  - **EV/EBIT method uses 22x** as "in-line with Paychex." But Paychex has 72% gross margins vs ADP's 46%. Paychex DESERVES a premium multiple. ADP at 22x with inferior gross margins is aggressive. A 18-20x multiple is more appropriate, giving EV/EBIT FV of $236-$263. The thesis acknowledges 18x gives $236 but then weights it 50/50 with 22x to get $263.
  - **DCF gets only 20% weight** precisely because it gives the LOWEST answer. This is a form of cherry-picking -- the method that is most conservative gets the least weight.
  - **Sensitivity analysis shows 79% FV spread** -- the thesis acknowledges this is HIGH but does not adjust its confidence accordingly.
  - The reverse DCF shows the market implies 8.1% FCF growth. The thesis claims this is "below historical 19.9%." But the 19.9% FCF CAGR is MISLEADING: it's calculated from a 4-year period (FY2022-FY2025) that includes: (a) COVID recovery bounce, (b) massive float income tailwind from rate hikes ($400M -> $1.3B), and (c) margin expansion from low base. **None of these tailwinds repeat going forward.** A normalized FCF growth rate of 7-9% is more realistic, which is CLOSE to what the market implies. The market may be pricing ADP correctly.
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Re-weight valuation with DCF at 40% weight (not 20%), OEY at 30%, EV/EBIT at 30%. Use conservative OEY (4.5% target, not 3.8%) and EV/EBIT (18-20x, not 22x). This gives adjusted FV of $195-$210.

### 3. "SaaSpocalypse risk is MODERATE (max -7% revenue impact)"

- **Thesis claim:** Per-employee pricing risk is lower for ADP than for SaaS because "the seat is the paycheck recipient, not a software user."
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - This distinction is real but the thesis UNDER-ESTIMATES the magnitude. The thesis quantifies a 10% headcount reduction as -7% of total revenue. But it ignores the SECOND-ORDER effect: if headcount drops 10%, float balances also drop ~10% ($3.7B less in client funds), reducing float income by ~$130M. The real impact of 10% headcount reduction is closer to -8-9% of revenue PLUS -10% of float income.
  - **January 2026 ADP Employment Report: private payrolls added ONLY 22,000 jobs**, far below the 45,000 consensus forecast (SOURCE: CNBC, Level 1 primary data). Professional and business services SHED 57,000 jobs. Manufacturing lost 8,000 jobs (every month since March 2024). The total would have been NEGATIVE without 74,000 education/health hires.
  - **January 2026 saw 108,000 job cuts announced -- the highest January total since 2009**, up 118% from January 2025 (SOURCE: Challenger, Gray & Christmas via Newsweek, Level 2).
  - 44% of companies cite AI as a driver of layoffs. 45% say AI has "partially reduced the need for new hires" (SOURCE: HR Executive, Level 2 survey data).
  - The thesis argues "you can't automate away the need to pay people." True, but you CAN automate away the PEOPLE themselves. If a company replaces 10 accountants with AI agents, ADP processes payroll for 10 fewer employees. Each agent does NOT receive a paycheck.
  - ADP's own PPC metric has been FLAT for the entirety of fiscal 2026. This is not a prediction -- it is ALREADY HAPPENING.
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Upgrade SaaSpocalypse from MODERATE to HIGH risk. Model bear case with PPC at -2% (not flat). Factor in float balance decline as second-order effect. The combined impact in a recession + AI scenario is -12-15% revenue, not -7%.

### 4. "Float income ($1.3B, ~100% margin) is a structural advantage"

- **Thesis claim:** Float income is "pure profit with zero incremental cost" and even in a zero-rate environment ADP earned $400-500M.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - The thesis underplays how much the CURRENT earnings profile depends on elevated rates. Float income is $1.3B at ~3.4% yield. At a 2.0% yield (which is where Fed Funds might go in a recession), float income drops to ~$750M -- a $550M reduction. That is approximately 10% of operating income at near-100% margin.
  - The laddered portfolio provides GRADUAL insulation, but it does NOT prevent the decline -- it merely spreads it over 2-3 years. Every maturing bond in the ladder gets reinvested at lower rates.
  - **The Fed is at 3.50-3.75%. Market expects 2 more cuts in 2026** (SOURCE: Bankrate, Level 2). If recession materializes, JPMorgan estimates up to 150bp in cuts (SOURCE: risk-identifier). Each 100bp = ~$350-400M revenue impact.
  - More critically, float income grew from ~$400M (near-zero rates) to $1.3B (+$900M) in 3 years. This $900M UPLIFT was almost pure profit. It boosted ADP's operating margin from ~23% to ~26%. **The margin "expansion" narrative in the thesis is substantially explained by float income recovery, not operational improvement.** If rates normalize to 2-2.5%, approximately half of the recent margin expansion reverses.
  - The thesis mentions this but then says "~6% of EPS" at risk. Actually, $1.3B at ~100% margin on a company with ~$5.4B operating income means float income is ~24% of operating profit. The EPS framing ($0.60/share or 6%) dramatically understates the operating profit dependency.
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Model float income sensitivity explicitly: base case $1.1B (2 cuts), bear case $750-800M (recession + aggressive cuts). Adjust operating margin projection to account for float income normalization. The "26% and expanding" margin story becomes "26% and at risk of compressing back to 24%."

### 5. "P/E 20.4x vs 10-year avg 29x means ADP is historically cheap"

- **Thesis claim:** ADP at 20.4x is pricing in "near-zero growth" and should mean-revert to 25-30x.
- **Evidencia en contra:**
  - **The 10-year average P/E of 29x was achieved during a period of (a) secular employment growth, (b) a rising rate cycle boosting float income, and (c) a tech/software premium narrative that ADP benefited from.** NONE of these conditions are guaranteed going forward.
  - ADP's revenue growth is decelerating: from high-single-digit to 6%. If growth settles at 5-6%, a 20x P/E is APPROPRIATE for a mid-single-digit grower with high quality -- not historically cheap. Compare: Paychex (similar growth) trades at 21x. Procter & Gamble (similar growth, similar quality) trades at 23x. Coca-Cola at 22x.
  - The 29x average includes years when ADP was growing 8-10% and rates were rising. A P/E mean-reversion assumption REQUIRES that the growth and macro conditions that supported 29x also revert. The thesis does not establish that this is likely.
  - **Jefferies explicitly flagged this: "22x P/E despite mid-single-digit growth" is overvalued, not undervalued.** Jefferies set an Underperform rating with a $230 target (SOURCE: Investing.com, Level 2 secondary analysis). The current price of $213 is BELOW Jefferies' bearish target.
  - **Alpha Spread intrinsic value analysis rates ADP as "slightly overvalued"** at current prices (SOURCE: Alpha Spread, Level 3 opinion but data-backed).
  - If ADP grows revenue at 6% and EPS at 9% (operating leverage + buybacks), a PEG ratio of 2.2x (20x / 9%) is rich. The S&P 500 PEG is ~1.8x. ADP is NOT cheap on a growth-adjusted basis.
- **Severidad:** HIGH
- **Resolucion sugerida:** Do NOT assume P/E mean-reversion to 29x. Use a sustainable P/E of 20-22x for a mid-single-digit grower. At 20x on $10.50 forward EPS, FV = $210. At 22x, FV = $231. This is consistent with the DCF range.

## Counter-Thesis: Why NOT to Buy ADP at $195 (or $185)

1. **The "Triple Squeeze" creates a correlated downside scenario where everything goes wrong simultaneously.** Flat PPC + rate cuts + AI headcount reduction are not independent risks -- they all worsen in a recession. January 2026 data (22K private payrolls, 108K job cuts announced) suggests this scenario is already materializing.

2. **The margin expansion narrative is substantially driven by float income recovery, which is REVERSING.** Operating margins went from 23% to 26% primarily because float income tripled ($400M to $1.3B). As rates normalize, this tailwind becomes a headwind. The "quality compounder with expanding margins" story becomes "mature business with normalizing margins."

3. **The FV of $255 requires assumptions that the DCF tool does not support.** At 6.5% growth and 9% WACC, the DCF gives $199. To reach $255, you need either 12%+ FCF growth (unsupported by fundamentals) or P/E mean-reversion to 25x+ (unsupported by current growth profile).

4. **CEO and insiders are SELLING, not buying.** 30 sales, 0 purchases, $9.5M net sold in 12 months. This is not "routine" -- this is a pattern of informed pessimism. When insiders only sell and never buy for 12+ consecutive months, that signals more than compensation diversification.

5. **ADP is a $213 stock that the DCF says is worth $186-$199.** At $195 entry, you are buying at or ABOVE fair value according to the most conservative methodology. The thesis reaches $255 only by weighting methods that assume historical multiple expansion. If the expansion never comes, you are buying an expensive stock.

## 🔄 META-REFLECTION

### Dudas/Incertidumbres
- **ROIC decline causation:** I flagged the 63.3% to 50.7% drop but could not determine the cause from available data. It could be: (a) working capital normalization post-COVID, (b) WorkForce Software acquisition diluting ROIC, (c) PEO margin compression, or (d) higher invested capital from float portfolio growth. Understanding this matters for whether it is one-time or structural.
- **Deferred revenue anomaly:** The 31.5% growth in deferred revenue is genuinely anomalous vs 7.1% revenue growth. The thesis and I both flag this but neither has confirmed the cause from primary sources (10-K/10-Q). If it represents real pre-paid contracts, it is bullish. If it is an accounting reclassification, it is meaningless. This could change the narrative.
- **PEO vs ES margin split:** I repeatedly reference ADP's consolidated 46% GM as inferior to peers, but I could not isolate the ES-only margin. If ES-only is 65-70%, then the GM comparison is misleading and my Desafio #2 is weaker. This needs verification from segment reporting.
